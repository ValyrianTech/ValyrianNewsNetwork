# China Advances 'Beautiful China' Vision Through Green Policy

China is intensifying its strategic commitment to environmental protection, marking a significant step in its long-term mission to build a "Beautiful China." This movement, centered on the concept of "Ecological Civilization," aims to harmonize economic development with ecological stability, ensuring the nation achieves its ambitious climate and conservation goals.

## Strengthening the Legal Framework

A cornerstone of this renewed focus is the recent submission of a draft environmental code to the National People's Congress (NPC) in March 2026. This legislative move is designed to provide a robust legal foundation for environmental governance, ensuring that ecological protection is integrated into the nation's judicial and regulatory frameworks.

According to [Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/politics/xxjxs/20260404/c01dc611d09d46959b28fcea870f8d06/c.html), the initiative is part of a broader effort to establish a permanent framework for ecological security. This legal architecture is expected to play a critical role in enforcing environmental standards and holding violators accountable as the country moves toward its 2035 modernization goals.

## The 15th Five-Year Plan and Carbon Targets

The upcoming 15th Five-Year Plan (2026-2030) is set to serve as a primary driver for this green transition. The plan is expected to include five specific key indicators focused on green and low-carbon development, providing measurable benchmarks for provincial and local authorities.

These indicators are vital for maintaining China's momentum toward its dual-carbon objectives: reaching peak carbon emissions by 2030 and achieving carbon neutrality by 2060. By embedding these targets into the national planning cycle, the government is signaling that ecological metrics will be as significant as economic growth metrics in the coming decade.

## Progress in Energy Transition

The shift toward sustainability is already yielding tangible results in China's energy sector. A significant milestone has been reached, with one-third of China's electricity now being sourced from green energy. This transition is a key component of the "Ecological Civilization" strategy, reducing reliance on fossil fuels and decreasing the carbon intensity of the industrial sector.

Furthermore, China has achieved "zero growth" in land degradation, a vital achievement for maintaining biodiversity and soil health. These developments reflect a transition from a model of highly resource-intensive growth to one of high-quality, sustainable development.

## Analysis: The Path to 2035

The integration of environmental law, long-term planning, and energy transition suggests that China is moving beyond rhetoric into a phase of structural implementation. The focus on "Ecological Civilization" is not merely an environmental policy but a fundamental component of China's "Socialist Modernization" by 2035.

The success of this vision will depend on the effectiveness of the 15th Five-Year Plan's implementation and the ability of the new environmental code to regulate a complex, multi-sector economy. As the energy mix continues to shift towards renewables, the economic implications for heavy industry and the global carbon market will be profound.

## Conclusion

As China continues to navigate the complexities of modernizing its economy, the "Beautiful China" initiative stands as a testament to its commitment to a sustainable future. With clear legislative steps and concrete energy milestones, the nation is actively constructing the foundations for a greener, more resilient civilization.
