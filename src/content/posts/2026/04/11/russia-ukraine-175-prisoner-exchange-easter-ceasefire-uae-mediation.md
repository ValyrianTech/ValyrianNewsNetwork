# Russia and Ukraine Swap 175 Prisoners Each in Major Easter Exchange

Russia and Ukraine exchanged 175 prisoners of war each on Saturday in one of the largest swaps since the conflict began, with the United Arab Emirates mediating the humanitarian exchange that coincided with a 32-hour Orthodox Easter ceasefire. According to the [Russian Ministry of Defense](https://tass.ru/armiya-i-opk/27076583), the "175 for 175" formula also saw the return of seven civilians from Russia's Kursk region who had been held in Ukrainian captivity.

The exchange marks the continuation of fragile humanitarian diplomacy amid ongoing military hostilities, demonstrating that communication channels remain open for prisoner returns even as fighting persists. Russian Human Rights Commissioner Tatyana Moskalkova personally greeted returning prisoners at the Belarus-Ukraine border, while Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy confirmed the swap from Kyiv's perspective.

## Context: UAE's Growing Mediation Role

The United Arab Emirates has emerged as a key neutral mediator in Russia-Ukraine prisoner exchanges since 2022, successfully facilitating eight such swaps in 2026 alone. This latest exchange brings the total number of prisoners returned through UAE mediation to over 6,300 since the conflict began, according to Emirati officials. The Gulf state has positioned itself as a humanitarian actor capable of bridging diplomatic divides, having also mediated a December 2022 prisoner exchange between the United States and Russia.

The timing of Saturday's exchange was particularly significant, occurring as a 32-hour Orthodox Easter ceasefire proposed by Ukraine and agreed to by Russia took effect. This marked the first officially agreed ceasefire between the two sides since the conflict began, creating a window for humanitarian gestures amid the religious holiday.

## Key Developments and Official Statements

According to the Russian Ministry of Defense statement, "On April 11, 175 Russian military personnel were returned from territory controlled by the Kyiv regime. In exchange, 175 prisoners of war of the Ukrainian Armed Forces were transferred. Also returned were seven citizens of the Russian Federation - residents of Kursk region - who were illegally detained by the Kyiv regime." The ministry confirmed that UAE mediation was instrumental in facilitating the return.

Russian Human Rights Commissioner Tatyana Moskalkova traveled to the Belarus-Ukraine border crossing point to personally welcome returning prisoners. [In her remarks to the returnees](https://tass.ru/obschestvo/27076409), she stated: "I welcome you to Belarusian soil! Today you have accomplished a heroic deed, having overcome such a great distance." Moskalkova checked on their wellbeing and inquired about conditions during their captivity.

The civilian component of the exchange involved seven residents from border areas of Kursk region - five women and two men from the Sudzhansky district, with the oldest being 91 years old. Kursk Governor Alexander Khinshtein [announced their return](https://tass.ru/obschestvo/27076631), stating: "Another 7 residents of the Kursk border area have been returned from Ukrainian captivity. These are the last residents of Kursk who were forcibly taken to Ukrainian territory." He noted that some returnees have health issues, including one woman who is blind, and promised full medical support.

International news agency [Reuters reported](https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/russia-ukraine-carry-out-pow-swap-defence-ministry-says-2026-04-11/) that the exchange occurred as both sides prepared to start the Easter ceasefire, with Ukrainian officials confirming the return of 175 Ukrainian soldiers alongside the seven civilians.

## Analysis: Humanitarian Diplomacy Amid Conflict

This exchange represents several significant developments in the Russia-Ukraine conflict:

**1. Sustained Mediation Channels:** The continued success of UAE-mediated exchanges demonstrates that third-party humanitarian diplomacy can function even when direct negotiations between Moscow and Kyiv remain stalled. The UAE's neutral positioning and established track record have made it an acceptable mediator to both sides.

**2. Civilian Humanitarian Concerns:** The inclusion of civilians in prisoner exchanges highlights ongoing issues with civilian detainees in conflict zones. The return of elderly and medically vulnerable individuals from border regions addresses specific humanitarian concerns that have drawn international attention.

**3. Ceasefire Coordination:** The alignment of the prisoner exchange with the Easter ceasefire suggests at least basic coordination and communication between the parties regarding humanitarian matters. While the ceasefire itself showed signs of strain with reports of continued drone strikes, the prisoner swap proceeded as planned.

**4. Regional Implications:** For border regions like Kursk that have experienced significant conflict and displacement, the return of civilians represents progress on local humanitarian priorities. Governor Khinshtein's direct involvement underscores how regional authorities are playing roles in prisoner return processes.

## Historical Context and Previous Exchanges

Saturday's exchange follows two major prisoner swaps in March 2026: a 200-for-200 exchange on March 5 and a 300-for-300 exchange on March 6. These large-scale swaps indicate that prisoner exchange mechanisms have become more established over time, despite fluctuations in frontline dynamics.

The Easter ceasefire itself represented a diplomatic breakthrough, being the first officially agreed pause in hostilities. Proposed by Ukraine and accepted by Russia with reciprocal expectations, it created a natural opportunity for humanitarian gestures during a significant religious holiday for both predominantly Orthodox Christian nations.

## What's Next: Future Humanitarian Prospects

The successful completion of this exchange amid the Easter ceasefire raises questions about whether similar humanitarian pauses could be arranged for future religious holidays or to facilitate civilian evacuations. While comprehensive peace negotiations remain distant, the functioning prisoner exchange mechanism provides a model for limited cooperation on humanitarian issues.

Key developments to watch include:
- Whether the UAE will expand its mediation role to other humanitarian issues
- If the Easter ceasefire leads to longer or more frequent humanitarian pauses
- How returned prisoners and civilians are reintegrated and receive medical care
- Whether this exchange creates momentum for further prisoner swaps

As the conflict enters its fifth year, the continued operation of humanitarian channels like prisoner exchanges represents one of the few areas where both sides demonstrate willingness to engage, offering glimmers of cooperation amid persistent hostilities. The return of 350 military personnel and seven civilians marks not just a numerical achievement but a testament to the persistence of humanitarian diplomacy even in the most challenging circumstances.
