# Police Issue Witness Appeal for Liège Jewelry Store Robbery

Belgian federal police have issued a public witness appeal to identify two armed robbers who targeted a jewelry store in central Liège last month, making off with a significant haul of gold, precious stones, and jewelry. The brazen daylight robbery occurred on March 27 and was captured in its entirety by the store's security cameras, providing investigators with clear footage of the suspects.

According to [RTBF](https://www.rtbf.be/article/un-appel-a-temoins-pour-retrouver-les-deux-braqueurs-d-une-bijouterie-de-liege-11708337), the federal police published the appeal on Saturday, April 11, seeking information about the armed robbery committed by two men in the mid-morning hours. The suspects demanded the opening of the store's safe under threat of firearms before escaping with what police described as an "important" haul.

## The Robbery: A Violent Few Minutes

The incident unfolded rapidly at the jewelry store located on En Neuvice street in Liège's city center. Surveillance footage shows the first suspect ringing the doorbell during morning business hours, his face visible except for dark sunglasses. After being admitted, he asked to see jewelry before suddenly producing a firearm and threatening a saleswoman.

"I first hear a scream, by the time I turn around, the saleswoman is taken by force and violently under the threat of a weapon," the store manager told [RTL](https://www.rtl.be/actu/regions/liege/il-emmene-la-vendeuse-de-force-sous-la-menace-dune-arme-violent-braquage-dans/2026-04-10/article/785400). The manager, who spoke anonymously, described the terrifying moments as the situation escalated.

The first suspect then demanded the door be opened for his accomplice. "At first, I think great, he wants to open the door, so he already wants to leave," the manager recounted. However, instead of leaving, a second suspect entered wearing a full-face motorcycle helmet, also armed.

## Suspect Descriptions and Escape

Police have released detailed descriptions of both suspects based on the surveillance footage. The first man, who entered with his face uncovered, is described as having a normal build, medium-length hair, a nascent beard and mustache, and wearing sunglasses with dark lenses. He had well-groomed hair and wore a leather jacket.

The second suspect, whose face was completely concealed under a motorcycle helmet, also had a normal build and wore black gloves. His helmet was blue and gray from the "Arai" brand.

After keeping staff and customers at gunpoint while forcing them to open the safe, the two men collected gold, precious stones, and jewelry before fleeing on a black motorcycle in the direction of Place du Marché. The entire robbery lasted only a few minutes, with police arriving quickly but finding the suspects had already escaped.

## Investigation and Witness Appeal

The Liège judicial police brigade is leading the investigation and has established multiple channels for potential witnesses to come forward. Authorities are asking anyone with information to contact investigators via the free phone number 0800 30 300 or by email at avisderecherche@police.belgium.eu.

RTBF reports that the robbery occurred in a jewelry store on En Neuvice, a street known for small businesses and shops in central Liège. The timing of the witness appeal—nearly two weeks after the robbery—suggests investigators have exhausted initial leads and are now seeking public assistance despite having clear surveillance footage.

## Psychological Impact and Security Review

The violent nature of the robbery has left a lasting impact on the store's staff. "We are organizing psychological support for each person," the manager revealed to RTL, highlighting the trauma experienced by employees during the incident.

The store is also reviewing its security measures in response to the robbery. "There is some concern about how to better protect ourselves in the future," the manager said. "It's certain that we will be much better protected."

## Broader Context of Crime in Liège

This robbery occurs amid other notable criminal incidents in the Liège region recently reported by local media. These include the interception of military cargo at Liège Airport bound for Israel and the posting of military personnel in front of Liège's synagogue for enhanced security—both occurring on April 10.

Jewelry stores remain frequent targets for armed robberies across Belgium due to the high value and transportability of their merchandise. The public witness appeal represents a standard police procedure in Belgium for serious crimes where community assistance might help identify suspects.

## What's Next

With clear surveillance footage of the first suspect's face, investigators hope the public appeal will generate leads that could break the case open. The footage provides unusual visibility for such crimes, as perpetrators typically conceal their identities more thoroughly.

Police are particularly interested in information about the distinctive blue and gray Arai motorcycle helmet worn by the second suspect and the black motorcycle used for the escape toward Place du Marché.

The case underscores ongoing security challenges for retail businesses in urban centers and the psychological toll such violent crimes take on employees. As the investigation continues, authorities emphasize that even small pieces of information from the public could prove crucial in apprehending the suspects and recovering the stolen valuables.
