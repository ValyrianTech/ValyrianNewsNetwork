# Swalwell Faces Sexual Misconduct Allegations, Democrats Withdraw Support

California Congressman Eric Swalwell, a leading Democratic candidate for governor, is facing multiple sexual misconduct allegations from four women—including one rape allegation from a former staffer—prompting key Democratic allies to withdraw endorsements and demand he end his campaign. The allegations, [reported by CNN](https://edition.cnn.com/2026/04/10/us/eric-swalwell-sexual-misconduct-allegations-invs) and the San Francisco Chronicle on April 10-11, 2026, have triggered a swift political crisis for the once-rising Democratic star, who strongly denies all accounts.

## Context and Political Stakes

Eric Swalwell, 45, represents California's 15th congressional district and has been a prominent figure in Democratic politics since his election in 2012. A former prosecutor and House Intelligence Committee member, Swalwell gained national attention as an impeachment manager in President Donald Trump's second trial. His campaign to replace termed-out Governor Gavin Newsom had been gaining momentum in recent polls, positioning him as a serious contender in California's competitive Democratic primary.

The timing is critical: voters will receive mail ballots next month for the June 2 primary election under California's top-two system, where the two highest vote-getters advance to the general election regardless of party. The allegations emerge as Swalwell faces increasing scrutiny in a crowded field where Democratic leaders fear a divided primary could cost them a spot in November.

## The Allegations and Corroborating Evidence

According to the CNN investigation, four women have come forward with accounts of sexual misconduct by Swalwell. The most serious allegations come from a former staffer who says Swalwell raped her in 2024 when she was heavily intoxicated after a charity gala in New York City. "I was pushing him off of me, saying no," the woman told CNN. "He didn't stop." She also alleges an earlier nonconsensual encounter in 2019 while still working for him, waking up naked in his hotel room after heavy drinking with no memory of what occurred.

Three other women allege various misconduct: one says Swalwell sent unsolicited explicit messages and nude photos; another alleges he kissed her and touched her leg without consent before she ended up intoxicated in his hotel room; a third says he sent unsolicited photos of his penis. CNN found corroboration for key elements of the women's claims, including family members and friends who confirmed they were told about incidents at the time, medical records showing STD and pregnancy testing after the alleged assault, and text messages describing the allegations shortly after they occurred.

## Immediate Political Fallout

The political reaction was swift and severe. As [AP News reported](https://apnews.com/article/california-governor-eric-swalwell-assault-allegations-aa1d13afe441be38d1d16f648e06d503), prominent Democratic supporters began withdrawing endorsements within hours of the reports. Senator Adam Schiff, a powerful California Democrat, said he was "deeply distressed" by the allegations and called for Swalwell to end his gubernatorial bid. Representative Jimmy Gomez, Swalwell's campaign chairman, resigned immediately, stating: "The congressman should leave the race now so there can be full accountability without doubt, distraction, or delay."

Major labor unions including the California Service Employees International Union (SEIU), California Teachers Association, and California Federation of Labor Unions suspended their support. House Democratic Leader Hakeem Jeffries called for a "serious and thorough investigation," while former House Speaker Nancy Pelosi suggested any investigation should occur "outside of a gubernatorial campaign." Governor Gavin Newsom, who has avoided endorsing in the race, stated: "As we continue to learn more, these allegations from multiple sources are deeply troubling and must be taken seriously."

## Swalwell's Response and Legal Threats

Swalwell has vehemently denied all allegations. "These allegations are false and come on the eve of an election against the front-runner for governor," he said in a statement to CNN. "For nearly 20 years, I have served the public—as a prosecutor and a congressman and have always protected women. I will defend myself with the facts and where necessary bring legal action."

His attorney sent cease-and-desist letters to two of the women, calling their accounts "false" and warning of potential legal action if they continue speaking out. The letters argued the women's claims were "undermined" by their "voluntary and cooperative relationship with Mr. Swalwell over the course of many years" following the alleged incidents. On social media, Swalwell stated: "These allegations of sexual assault are flat false. They're absolutely false. They did not happen, they have never happened, and I will fight them with everything that I have."

## Analysis: Political and Legal Implications

The allegations present a severe test for the Democratic Party, which has positioned itself as a champion of women's rights and the #MeToo movement. As [The New York Times noted](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/10/us/politics/eric-swalwell-allegations-california-governor.html), the party now faces the challenge of applying its principles consistently when one of its own prominent figures is accused. The situation echoes previous political scandals that ended careers, such as those of Al Franken and Andrew Cuomo, though Swalwell maintains his innocence.

Legally, the allegations could trigger multiple proceedings. While no formal police reports have been filed, the House Ethics Committee could investigate given Swalwell remains a sitting member of Congress. The cease-and-desist letters suggest potential defamation lawsuits, while the women could potentially file civil suits. The detailed corroborating evidence reported by CNN—including medical records, text messages, and witness accounts—could make this case particularly challenging for Swalwell to dismiss as politically motivated.

## What's Next for Swalwell and the Governor's Race

Swalwell said he would spend the weekend with family and provide an update "very soon," but the pressure to withdraw is mounting from all sides. According to [The Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2026/04/10/eric-swalwell-sexual-assault-allegations/), every other Democratic candidate in the governor's race has called for him to drop out, creating near-unanimous pressure within his own party.

The California governor's race now faces significant uncertainty. Swalwell's departure would reshape the Democratic primary, potentially advantage other candidates like Lieutenant Governor Eleni Kounalakis or former State Controller Betty Yee. For Swalwell personally, the allegations threaten not just his gubernatorial ambitions but his congressional career and political legacy.

As the story continues to develop, key questions remain: Will additional women come forward following these initial reports? Will formal complaints be filed with law enforcement or the House Ethics Committee? And can Swalwell's campaign survive the loss of major endorsements and staff resignations? The coming days will determine whether this political crisis becomes a career-ending scandal or a battle Swalwell can weather through denial and legal challenge.
