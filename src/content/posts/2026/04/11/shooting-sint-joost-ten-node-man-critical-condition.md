# Shooting in Sint-Joost-ten-Node Leaves Man in Critical Condition

A man is fighting for his life after a shooting in the Brussels municipality of Sint-Joost-ten-Node early Saturday morning. Police were called to the scene shortly after midnight following reports of gunfire in the Linnéstraat, according to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/04/11/schietpartij-schoten-sint-joost-ten-node-gewond-man-kritiek/).

The victim, whose identity has not been released, was seriously injured and transported to a hospital where he remains in critical condition. Emergency services provided first aid at the scene before rushing him to medical care. The federal police laboratory has examined the crime scene as investigators work to identify the perpetrators and determine the exact circumstances of the shooting.

## Location and Immediate Response

The incident occurred in the Linnéstraat, a residential street located between Brussels-North station and the Botanical Garden (Kruidtuin). Police Zone Brussels-North, which covers Sint-Joost-ten-Node along with the municipalities of Schaarbeek and Evere, responded to the emergency call. [BRUZZ](https://www.bruzz.be/actua/veiligheid/slachtoffer-levensgevaarlijk-gewond-na-schietpartij-sint-joost-2026-04-11) reported that "the person's condition is still critical," according to police statements.

Forensic teams from the federal judicial police conducted a thorough examination of the area throughout Saturday morning. The crime scene has since been reopened to the public, but the investigation into the shooting continues under the direction of the Brussels public prosecutor's office.

## Historical Context of Violence

This latest incident marks at least the third serious shooting in Sint-Joost-ten-Node in just over a year, raising concerns about persistent gun violence in the small Brussels municipality. 

On February 5, 2025, two people were injured in a shooting in the Weidestraat in the early morning hours. According to [VRT NWS reporting at the time](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/en/2025/02/06/two-injured-in-sint-joost-shooting/), both victims were hospitalized but not in critical condition, and the perpetrators managed to escape.

More recently, on January 30, 2026, a 50-year-old man died in another shooting in Sint-Joost-ten-Node. [De Standaard reported](https://www.standaard.be/binnenland/man-van-50-overleden-bij-schietpartij-in-sint-joost-ten-node/127617832.html) that the Brussels public prosecutor's office opened a murder investigation following that incident.

## About Sint-Joost-ten-Node

Sint-Joost-ten-Node (Saint-Josse-ten-Noode) is one of the 19 municipalities of the Brussels-Capital Region and holds the distinction of being the smallest municipality in Belgium by area, covering just 1.14 square kilometers. Despite its small size, it is one of the most densely populated municipalities in the country.

The municipality falls under Police Zone Brussels-North (Zone 5344), which also serves Schaarbeek and Evere. This integrated local police force is responsible for maintaining public safety across these three municipalities.

## Broader Crime Context in Brussels

The shooting occurs amid what appears to be a pattern of gun violence in various parts of Brussels. According to BRUZZ reporting, there have been multiple shooting incidents in the capital in recent months:

- April 1, 2026: A man with a gunshot wound was found in Kuregem (Anderlecht)
- March 26, 2026: A person was left in critical condition after a shooting in the Anneessenswijk
- March 25, 2026: A building facade was shot at near Simonis
- March 23, 2026: Police sought witnesses and camera footage for a shooting with a seriously injured victim in Kuregem

Police Zone Brussels-North has previously identified certain areas within its jurisdiction as "particularly problematic" for crime, though specific locations have not been publicly disclosed for operational reasons.

## Investigation Status and Public Safety Concerns

The Brussels public prosecutor's office is leading the investigation into Saturday's shooting. As of Saturday afternoon, no suspects have been identified or arrested, and the motive for the attack remains unclear.

With the victim in critical condition and the perpetrators still at large, there are immediate public safety concerns for residents of the area. The incident occurred in a residential neighborhood between two major landmarks—Brussels-North station and the Botanical Garden—which could impact public perception of safety in central Brussels.

The proximity in time between this shooting and the January incident (approximately 2.5 months) raises questions about law enforcement effectiveness in addressing gun violence in the municipality. However, police officials have not commented on whether they believe the recent shootings are connected.

## What Comes Next

Investigators will continue to pursue several lines of inquiry, including reviewing security camera footage from the area, interviewing potential witnesses, and analyzing forensic evidence collected from the scene. The public prosecutor's office typically does not comment on ongoing investigations to avoid compromising their work.

Medical professionals will continue to monitor the victim's condition, with updates expected from hospital officials rather than police sources to protect patient privacy.

Residents with information about the shooting are encouraged to contact Police Zone Brussels-North. The investigation represents another test for law enforcement in an area that has experienced multiple violent incidents in a relatively short period, highlighting ongoing challenges in combating gun crime in certain Brussels neighborhoods.
