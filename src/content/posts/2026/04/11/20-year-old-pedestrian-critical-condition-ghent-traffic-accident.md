# Pedestrian, 20, Critical After Ghent Traffic Accident

A 20-year-old pedestrian with Polish nationality is fighting for his life after being struck by a car on Gasmeterlaan in Ghent during the early hours of Saturday morning. According to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/04/11/voetganger-aangereden-gent-gasmeterlaan-levensgevaar/), the victim was transported to hospital in critical condition following the collision that occurred in the early morning hours of April 11, 2026.

The 20-year-old driver from Ghent tested negative for both alcohol and drugs, the East Flanders Prosecutor's Office confirmed. An expert has been appointed to investigate the precise circumstances of the accident, which marks the second serious incident on Gasmeterlaan in recent weeks.

## Incident Details and Emergency Response

The accident was reported to Ghent police in the early morning hours on Saturday, according to [Gazet van Antwerpen](https://www.gva.be/binnenland/wagen-rijdt-voetganger-aan-in-gent-slachtoffer-in-levensgevaar/145577968.html). The collision occurred at the end of Gasmeterlaan, past Spaarstraat in the Dampoort area of the city. Emergency services responded promptly, transporting the seriously injured pedestrian to a nearby hospital where he remains in life-threatening condition.

Gasmeterlaan was closed for several hours to allow for investigation and cleanup operations but has since reopened to traffic. The driver, also aged 20 and from Ghent, remained at the scene and cooperated with authorities.

## Investigation Underway

The East Flanders Prosecutor's Office (parket Oost-Vlaanderen) is overseeing the investigation into the accident. "Alcohol- and drug tests on the driver gave a negative result," the prosecutor's office stated. "An expert must now investigate the precise circumstances of the accident."

While impairment has been ruled out as an immediate factor, the investigation will examine other potential causes including speed, visibility, road conditions, and any possible mechanical issues with the vehicle. The involvement of the prosecutor's office indicates the seriousness with which authorities are treating the incident.

## Local Context: Second Accident on Gasmeterlaan

This latest incident follows another serious accident on Gasmeterlaan just weeks earlier, when a driver crashed into a balustrade further along the same street. That section remains cordoned off with barriers, highlighting ongoing safety concerns in the area.

Gasmeterlaan is located in Ghent's Dampoort neighborhood, an area that has seen significant traffic safety improvements in recent years including pedestrian and bicycle tunnels under the railway. However, residents and parents have continued to raise alarms about traffic safety, with calls for reduced speed limits on nearby Antwerpsesteenweg.

## Broader Traffic Safety Trends in Ghent

The accident occurs against a backdrop of historical traffic safety concerns in Ghent. According to a [2022 De Standaard report](https://www.standaard.be/nieuws/stad-telt-al-opnieuw-meer-verkeersongevallen/47627291.html), the city experienced nearly 400 serious traffic accidents with injuries in just the first four months of 2022—more than in pre-pandemic years. In more than 100 of those cases, drivers were under the influence of alcohol or drugs.

While these statistics reflect conditions from four years ago, they highlight persistent challenges in urban traffic safety that continue to inform current policy discussions. The Dampoort area specifically has been a focus of safety concerns, with local residents advocating for comprehensive traffic calming measures. While infrastructure improvements like pedestrian tunnels have been implemented, incidents like Saturday's accident suggest persistent vulnerabilities for those on foot.
