# Israel Faces Internal Tensions: Protests, Police Conduct, and Violence

Israel confronted significant internal divisions on April 3, 2026, as the Supreme Court debated protest freedoms during wartime, police faced scrutiny over an officer filmed pushing a protester, violence continued in Arab communities with a 22-year-old shot dead, and the IDF disciplined a reservist for photographing a Palestinian detainee holding an advertisement for his business. These interconnected developments reveal deep societal tensions between civil liberties and security concerns during ongoing military conflict.

## Supreme Court Weighs Protest Rights During Wartime

The Supreme Court heard urgent arguments on Friday regarding protest freedoms during Israel's ongoing military conflict with Iran, Operation "Roar of the Lion." According to [Haaretz](https://www.haaretz.co.il/news/politics/2026-04-03/ty-article/.premium/0000019d-51d7-da4e-a5df-5bdfcd280000), the court ordered the state to submit its position by Saturday at 11 AM regarding protests against the war at four locations across Israel.

Supreme Court President Isaac Amit criticized police handling of recent protests, stating, "The police are the ones who should come to the court and say, we know the law and the precedent... Protesters shouldn't have to beg to hold a protest." The state representative acknowledged that "freedom of protest and political expression, in the state's view, does not disappear during wartime. Protests should take place even during war, that's the essence of a democratic regime."

## Police Officer Incident Draws Public Scrutiny

A police officer recently promoted to intelligence officer for the Jerusalem district was filmed pushing a 60-year-old woman during a protest against death penalty legislation for terrorists. According to [Ynet](https://www.ynet.co.il/news/article/skc18stszg#autoplay), Officer Valentina Nemetz had been promoted just days before the incident, despite previous controversies involving her conduct.

In a 2019 case, Judge Eitan Cohen criticized police conduct after Nemetz's team arrested a Palestinian driver in East Jerusalem who was later acquitted. The judge noted, "The injustice in the police officers' behavior toward the defendant is particularly striking... The defendant did not drive recklessly backward, and did not endanger pedestrians or police officers." MMA fighter Haim Gozali also claimed she falsely accused him of attacking police at the Western Wall.

Police responded to the recent incident by stating, "We regret the choice to continue echoing a false presentation disconnected from reality... the '80-year-old woman' is actually a woman in her 50s." National Security Minister Itamar Ben-Gvir has expressed support for the officer.

## Violence Continues in Arab Communities

A 22-year-old man was shot and killed Friday evening in Jisr az-Zarqa, an Arab community in northern Israel. According to [Haaretz](https://www.haaretz.co.il/news/law/2026-04-03/ty-article/.premium/0000019d-53cf-da4e-a5df-5bdf18760000), Barkat Amash was pronounced dead at Hillel Yaffe Hospital in Hadera. Police assessed the background as criminal and opened an investigation.

The murder adds to a troubling pattern of violence in Arab communities. According to Haaretz tracking, since the beginning of 2026, 84 people have been murdered in Israel, with 76 of those victims coming from Arab society. This represents approximately 90% of all murders in the country during this period.

## IDF Disciplines Reservist for Inappropriate Photo

The Israel Defense Forces dismissed a reservist soldier who photographed a Palestinian detainee who was handcuffed, blindfolded, and holding a sign advertising the soldier's business. According to [Ynet](https://www.ynet.co.il/news/article/hyfuuwaswg), the soldier shared the photo on social media, where pro-Palestinian activists published both the image and the soldier's name.

An IDF spokesperson stated, "The IDF views the incident with great severity. Upon learning of the incident, the soldier was treated by the relevant commanders and was immediately suspended from reserve service." Military officials characterized it as "a very isolated incident that was dealt with immediately."

## Analysis: Interconnected Tensions in Wartime Israel

These four developments reveal interconnected tensions affecting Israeli society during wartime:

1. **Civil Liberties vs. Security**: The Supreme Court case highlights the fundamental tension between democratic rights and security concerns during military conflict. Attorney Oded Feller of the Association for Civil Rights warned, "Democracy doesn't die in one blow but in several small blows."

2. **Law Enforcement Conduct**: Both the police officer incident and the reservist case raise questions about appropriate conduct by security forces. The police incident occurred amid broader accusations of politicization and excessive force against left-wing protesters under Minister Ben-Gvir's leadership.

3. **Parallel Societal Divisions**: While political protests dominate headlines, Arab communities face different but equally serious challenges with violent crime. The government's ability to address this violence remains a significant domestic concern.

4. **Institutional Responses**: Different institutions have responded differently—the Supreme Court asserting rights, police defending actions, and the IDF taking disciplinary measures. These varied responses reflect institutional tensions within Israel's democratic framework.

## What's Next for Israeli Society

The Supreme Court's impending decision on protest guidelines could set important precedents for civil liberties during wartime. Police conduct investigations are likely for Officer Nemetz, while the government faces pressure to address violence in Arab communities more effectively.

Long-term implications include the impact on Israeli democracy during prolonged conflict, police-community relations particularly with protest movements, and military ethical standards. As Israel continues its military operations against external threats, these internal tensions reveal the complex challenges of maintaining democratic norms while ensuring security.

The coming days will test whether Israel's institutions can balance these competing demands while addressing the legitimate concerns of all segments of society during a period of national crisis.
