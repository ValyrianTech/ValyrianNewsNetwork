# Iranian, Lebanese Missiles Strike Northern Israel

Northern Israeli communities faced intensified missile attacks from both Iran and Lebanon on Friday, with direct hits reported in Kiryat Ata and Metula as regional tensions escalate. According to [Ynet](https://www.ynet.co.il/news/article/bj8v7bpi11g), an Iranian splitter missile caused extensive damage in Kiryat Ata, while Haaretz reported a separate missile from Lebanon struck a house in Metula.

## Context and Escalating Tensions

The attacks come amid growing regional instability, with Hezbollah claiming responsibility for 60 attacks in the past 24 hours alone, bringing their total operations since March 2 to 1,250. The current conflict began with a Hezbollah cross-border attack on March 2, 2026, prompting an Israeli ground offensive in southern Lebanon. Simultaneously, a joint US-Israeli offensive on Iran that began February 28 has triggered retaliatory strikes from Tehran targeting Israel, US bases, and Gulf countries.

Political tensions are also rising over Hezbollah's disarmament, with Israeli military officials stating that complete disarmament would require occupying all of Lebanon—a assessment that has sparked outrage among northern municipal leaders. US-Iran negotiations have reportedly reached a deadlock according to a Wall Street Journal report cited in the Haaretz live blog.

## Key Developments and Damage Assessment

### Kiryat Ata: Iranian Missile Impact

The Iranian splitter missile caused alerts throughout northern Israel and heavy damage in Kiryat Ata, with three fall sites identified. According to Ynet's detailed report, an apartment was reduced to rubble, parking lots and buildings sustained damage, and vehicles were burned. A 79-year-old man was lightly injured by stones thrown from the blast, while several people were treated for anxiety.

Residents described harrowing experiences. "It was a very loud boom, a great miracle that no one was hurt," said one resident. Aviv Zino, whose home and car were damaged, told Ynet: "Damage to the house, lots of glass everywhere. Look at my car." Haim Kaplon added: "We heard the alert and entered the shelter. There was a big explosion, see the destruction in the living room, bedrooms, kitchen."

Kiryat Ata Mayor Yaakov Peretz visited the main impact site and emphasized: "A very difficult scene, and there were others. Residents must follow Home Front Command instructions, that's how people are saved." Additional munitions from the splitter missile fell in Haifa with no casualties reported.

### Metula: Lebanese Missile Strike

Separately, a missile from Lebanon hit a house in Metula, [as reported by Haaretz](https://www.haaretz.co.il/news/politics/war-2023/2026-04-03/ty-article-live/0000019d-5030-d220-a9bf-73f531200000?liveBlogItemId=145826419#145826419). Metula Mayor David Azulai confirmed the direct hit but reported no casualties, explaining that the house was part of a vacation rental complex whose owners were currently outside the settlement. Damage was also reported to nearby houses and public infrastructure.

### Hezbollah's Claims and Broader Attacks

Hezbollah claimed responsibility for extensive operations, stating they had targeted six settlements in northern Israel: Kiryat Shmona (three times), Metula (twice), Even Menachem, Beit Hillel, Avivim, and Nahariya. The group also reported striking 40 gatherings of Israeli soldiers and vehicles (25 in southern Lebanon, 15 inside northern Israel), firing a surface-to-air missile at an Israeli helicopter over Ramieh, and achieving direct hits on two Merkava tanks and a D9 bulldozer.

Throughout the day, alerts were activated across northern Israel, with simultaneous UAV suspicion reported in the Kiryat Shmona area. An interception fragment also fell in the Haifa port area without causing casualties.

## Analysis: Political and Military Implications

The coordinated attacks from multiple fronts highlight Israel's complex security challenges in the north. The Iranian missile attack represents a direct escalation from Tehran, while Hezbollah's continued operations demonstrate the group's sustained capability despite Israeli military pressure.

The political dimension is equally significant. Northern municipal leaders have expressed outrage over Israeli military assessments that complete Hezbollah disarmament would require occupying all of Lebanon. This tension between military reality and political expectations creates additional pressure on the Israeli government to deliver security to northern communities.

Regionally, the deadlock in US-Iran negotiations adds to instability. According to [the Haaretz report](https://www.haaretz.co.il/news/politics/war-2023/2026-04-03/ty-article-live/0000019d-5030-d220-a9bf-73f531200000?liveBlogItemId=1988091277#1988091277), mediators told the Wall Street Journal that Iran has refused to meet with American officials in the coming days, stating that US demands are unacceptable. Turkey and Egypt continue to push for negotiations, considering alternative venues like Doha or Istanbul.

## What's Next: Regional Outlook

The immediate outlook suggests continued volatility along Israel's northern border. Hezbollah's demonstrated capacity for sustained operations indicates the conflict is far from resolution, while Iranian willingness to launch missiles directly at Israel marks a dangerous escalation.
