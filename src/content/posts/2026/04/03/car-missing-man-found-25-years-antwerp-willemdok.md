# Missing Man's Car Found After 25+ Years in Antwerp Dock

In a remarkable cold case development, the car belonging to a man who disappeared more than 25 years ago was accidentally discovered during a routine fire department diving exercise in Willemdok, near the MAS museum in Antwerp. The discovery, made on Thursday, April 2, 2026, has triggered a major recovery operation and reopened a long-dormant missing persons case that had baffled authorities for decades.

According to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/04/03/auto-willemdok-antwerpen-verdwijning-man/), divers from the Antwerp Fire Department initially noticed only the rear of the vehicle during their training exercise, with the rest buried deep in silt. Crucially, the license plate remained visible and readable, allowing police to quickly link the vehicle to a disappearance case from around the turn of the century.

## Context and Background

The discovery occurred in Willemdok, a marina in Antwerp's Het Eilandje district adjacent to the MAS (Museum aan de Stroom). The area has undergone significant transformation since approximately 2000 when the man disappeared—the iconic MAS museum wasn't yet built, and the former port area has since been redeveloped into a cultural hub. This urban evolution may explain why the vehicle remained undetected for so long despite the area's transformation.

The missing man, born in 1958, would be approximately 68 years old today. His identity has not been publicly released, but authorities confirmed the family was informed on Thursday when the car was discovered. "We know it's about a man from 1958," said Kristof Aerts, spokesperson for the Antwerp prosecutor's office. "It's a case that has been open for a very long time."

## Key Developments and Recovery Operation

A large-scale recovery operation began on Friday, April 3, involving multiple agencies including the Cel Vermiste Personen (Missing Persons Unit), Civiele Bescherming (Civil Protection), and Brandweer Zone Antwerpen (Antwerp Fire Department). Black screens were erected around the site to shield the operation from public view, and three boats had to be moved to facilitate the car's retrieval.

Joseph Andries, a witness living on a boat in Willemdok, observed the discovery firsthand. "A diver accidentally discovered a car under a boat. That started the whole thing, so to speak," he told VRT NWS. "They then asked to move 3 boats so they could retrieve the car from the water today. Then we helped."

The vehicle will now be extracted from the water for forensic examination. "The car will now be taken out of the water, then further investigation will follow," Aerts confirmed. It remains unclear whether the man's body is inside the vehicle, and authorities have not speculated on the circumstances of the original disappearance.

## Analysis and Implications

This discovery represents a significant breakthrough in one of Belgium's longer unresolved missing persons cases. The involvement of the specialized Cel Vermiste Personen unit indicates the seriousness with which authorities are treating this development. Cold cases like this one demonstrate how chance discoveries during routine operations can unexpectedly solve mysteries that have lingered for decades.

The location itself tells a story of urban transformation. Willemdok, once an industrial port area, has become a fashionable marina and cultural destination. The fact that a vehicle could remain submerged for over 25 years in what is now a heavily trafficked area highlights how waterways can conceal evidence indefinitely, with silt and sediment preserving vehicles and other evidence.

Forensic examination of the vehicle may provide crucial answers to the family who have waited over a quarter-century for closure. The condition of the car and any potential evidence inside could help determine whether this was an accident, foul play, or something else entirely.

## What's Next

The immediate focus is on safely recovering the vehicle and conducting a thorough forensic analysis. Authorities will examine the car for human remains, personal effects, and any evidence that might shed light on the circumstances of the disappearance. The license plate identification provides a solid starting point, but the investigation will likely expand to include re-interviewing witnesses from the original case and applying modern forensic techniques unavailable 25 years ago.

For the family, this discovery represents both hope and anxiety—hope for answers after decades of uncertainty, and anxiety about what those answers might reveal. For law enforcement, it's a reminder that cold cases are never truly closed, and that persistence combined with chance can sometimes break through even the most stubborn investigative barriers.

As the recovery operation continues at Willemdok, authorities urge anyone with information about the original disappearance to come forward. Meanwhile, the Antwerp prosecutor's office continues to monitor the situation closely, prepared to pursue any leads that emerge from this unexpected discovery.
