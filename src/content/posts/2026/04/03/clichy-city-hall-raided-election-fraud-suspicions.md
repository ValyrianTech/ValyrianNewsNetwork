# Clichy City Hall Raided Over Election Fraud Suspicions

French judicial police conducted a large-scale raid on Clichy city hall in the Hauts-de-Seine department on Friday afternoon, April 3, 2026, as part of an investigation into suspected proxy vote fraud during recent municipal elections. According to [Le Figaro](https://www.lefigaro.fr/faits-divers/soupcons-de-fraudes-aux-procurations-lors-des-elections-municipales-la-mairie-de-clichy-perquisitionnee-20260403), the operation follows formal complaints filed by defeated Socialist candidate Julie Martinez and comes less than two weeks after incumbent mayor Rémi Muzeau was narrowly re-elected with 51.83% of the vote.

## Context and Background

The raid represents a significant escalation in an electoral dispute that has raised questions about the integrity of voting procedures in the Paris suburb. Municipal elections in France allow voters to designate proxies to vote on their behalf through formal requests that require identity verification. The system, while designed to increase accessibility, has long been vulnerable to potential abuse.

Clichy, a politically sensitive suburb northwest of Paris, witnessed a particularly close election on March 22, 2026. Rémi Muzeau, representing the Les Républicains party under the divers-droite banner, secured re-election by a margin of just 731 votes—approximately 2.6% of votes cast. His opponent, Socialist candidate Julie Martinez, who had support from La France Insoumise, immediately challenged the results.

## Key Developments and Allegations

The investigation focuses on allegations that a system of false proxy votes prevented legitimate voters from casting ballots. According to reports, approximately 30 voters discovered that someone had already voted in their place using fraudulent proxy authorizations. In one particularly striking case, a family reportedly encountered the person using their proxy vote at the polling station.

The Nanterre prosecutor's office opened a formal investigation on Tuesday, March 31, examining potential charges including forgery (faux et usage de faux), violations of the electoral code, and identity theft. The investigation was triggered by an Article 40 signalement from the Commission de Contrôle des Opérations de Vote (CCOV), France's electoral oversight body.

Police are examining documents and computer systems seized during the raid to determine the extent of any potential fraud. A prefecture report had previously identified nine cases of "confirmed fraud," with allegations suggesting that proxy votes specifically targeted residents of the Berges-de-Seine neighborhood.

## Political Responses

Incumbent mayor Rémi Muzeau, a former parliamentary assistant to convicted former mayor Patrick Balkany, responded to the raid by stating, "I am satisfied with this perquisition. It's a normal investigative act that will shed light on the loser's accusations." He further suggested that Julie Martinez's campaign headquarters should also be searched.

Martinez, who filed her complaint on March 27 alleging "electoral fraud," "forgery," and "identity theft," expressed hope that the investigation would "provide probative elements explaining how people from working-class neighborhoods were prevented from voting because someone had voted before them with proxies." She also filed an appeal with the Cergy-Pontoise administrative court seeking to annul the election entirely.

The defeated candidate raised concerns about voter intimidation, noting that some affected voters were afraid to come forward with complaints due to fears about potential repercussions regarding public housing or employment.

## Broader Implications

The Clichy case highlights ongoing vulnerabilities in France's proxy voting system and raises questions about electoral integrity at the municipal level. Proxy votes reportedly doubled between the first and second rounds of voting in Clichy, a statistically unusual pattern that attracted investigators' attention.

This investigation occurs against a backdrop of heightened scrutiny of municipal election processes across France. The case echoes previous electoral fraud scandals in French municipalities and touches on fundamental questions about democratic safeguards.

Legal experts note that if substantiated, the allegations could lead not only to criminal charges but potentially to the annulment of the Clichy municipal election. Such an outcome would trigger a new election and represent a significant political setback for the Les Républicains party ahead of future national elections.

## Analysis and Forward Look

The timing of the raid—just days after the election—suggests authorities are treating the allegations with urgency. The involvement of judicial police rather than local authorities indicates the seriousness with which the case is being handled.

Several unanswered questions remain: How many proxy votes were potentially fraudulent? What specific evidence has been seized during the raid? What is the expected timeline for the investigation's conclusions? And perhaps most importantly, does this case represent an isolated incident or indicate broader vulnerabilities in France's proxy voting system?

The outcome of this investigation will have implications beyond Clichy, potentially influencing electoral reform discussions at the national level. As France prepares for future elections, including the 2027 presidential race, maintaining public confidence in voting procedures remains paramount.

For now, Clichy residents await clarity on whether their municipal election results will stand or whether democratic processes will require them to return to the polls. The investigation continues as authorities work to determine whether the narrow 731-vote margin that decided the election was influenced by fraudulent proxy votes.
