# China Reshapes Officials' View of Governance Achievements

China has launched a nationwide study campaign aimed at correcting officials' understanding of political achievements, shifting focus from short-term economic gains to long-term, sustainable development as the country enters its 15th Five-Year Plan period (2026-2030). The "Establishing and Practicing a Correct View of Political Achievements" campaign, initiated by the Communist Party of China Central Committee in late February, targets officials at county level and above with the goal of eliminating vanity projects and promoting people-centered governance, according to [official government reports](https://english.www.gov.cn/news/202603/25/content_WS69c3df45c6d00ca5f9a0a195.html).

## Context: From GDP Obsession to Comprehensive Governance

The campaign represents a formal institutionalization of President Xi Jinping's long-standing critique of GDP-focused evaluation that has dominated Chinese bureaucracy for decades. Xi's governance philosophy traces back to his experiences in Fujian Province in the 1980s, where he emphasized that ensuring access to basic necessities like light bulbs and soap for remote communities constituted genuine governance achievement.

"Development cannot rely on flashy gestures or grandstanding. Officials must roll up their sleeves and focus on real results," Xi stated in remarks published in Qiushi, the Party's flagship magazine, following the campaign's launch. This shift comes as China transitions from its successful poverty alleviation campaign, which lifted nearly 100 million people out of extreme poverty by 2020, to broader goals of common prosperity and modernization by 2035.

## Campaign Implementation and Key Components

Running from February to July 2026, the campaign aims to address what authorities describe as "misguided views on governance that often breed vanity projects, hidden risks, heavy burdens on local communities, and public discontent." According to [People's Daily coverage](http://politics.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0403/c461001-40694361.html), implementation has already begun across ministries, provinces, and state-owned enterprises with varied approaches:

- **Central Ministries:** The Ministry of Finance, Ministry of Commerce, People's Bank of China, and State Administration for Market Regulation have all launched study sessions and读书班 (reading classes) focusing on Xi's writings about political achievements.
- **Local Governments:** Regions like Yunnan's Lijiang, Heilongjiang's Jixi, Jiangsu's Huaian District, and Chongqing's Yuzhong District are moving "classrooms to the front lines" with practical sessions in communities and enterprises.
- **State-Owned Enterprises:** Companies like China Southern Power Grid, China Communications Construction Group, China National Petroleum Corporation, and Bank of Communications are integrating campaign themes with their operational goals.

## Emphasis on Practical Skills and Frontline Experience

A key theme emerging from the campaign is the need for officials to develop practical problem-solving abilities. [People's Daily commentary](http://opinion.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0403/c461530-40694372.html) emphasized that "creating practical achievements requires mastering the real ability to get things done," noting that "many will-do, do-good real abilities are precisely honed in the face of difficulties and challenges."

This focus on competence complements the campaign's political education components. As [Central Party School researcher Li Feng noted](http://opinion.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0403/c461530-40694371.html) in an article on democratic governance, the campaign represents part of broader efforts to "promote the in-depth development of whole-process people's democracy through good laws and good governance."

## Historical Models and Traditional Cultural Integration

The campaign strategically coincides with the Qingming Festival (Tomb-Sweeping Day), traditionally a time for honoring ancestors. State media has used this occasion to highlight historical model cadres like Jiao Yulu, Yang Shanzhou, and Gu Wenchang—officials celebrated for their selfless service during the mid-20th century. [A special feature](http://politics.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0403/c461001-40695014.html) published on April 3 recalled Xi's admiration for Jiao Yulu, the 1960s county Party secretary who worked tirelessly to combat natural disasters in Henan Province despite terminal illness.

This integration of traditional cultural elements with contemporary political education represents a deliberate strategy to ground the campaign in Chinese historical and cultural context, making its messages more resonant with both officials and the public.

## Addressing Dual Governance Problems

The campaign targets two opposite but equally problematic tendencies among officials:

1. **Recklessness:** Characterized by vanity projects, excessive debt accumulation, environmental damage, and "inflated statistics"—where local authorities use rebates to attract shell companies and create false economic growth.
2. **Inaction:** Manifested as risk-aversion, bureaucratic inertia, and fear of making mistakes—what Xi has criticized as non-acting "nice guys" and "fence-sitters."

To encourage responsible innovation while preventing abuse, the campaign reinforces Xi's "three distinctions" principle, which protects officials who make errors with good intentions from those who deliberately violate rules for personal gain.

## Connection to 15th Five-Year Plan and Long-Term Development

The campaign's timing is strategically aligned with the launch of China's 15th Five-Year Plan (2026-2030), the penultimate plan before the country's 2035 modernization goal. The plan includes 109 major projects focused on emerging industries and long-term foundations, requiring sustained commitment beyond typical political cycles.

Eduardo Regalado, a researcher at Cuba's International Policy Research Center, observed that "fostering a correct view on performance among officials has emerged as a key concept in the CPC's governance framework for the new era, and will help China transform its development model toward greater quality, efficiency, and equity."

In emphasizing the long-term perspective required for China's development, Xi has framed the national journey in generational terms. "The rejuvenation of the Chinese nation is a relay race, in which the baton must be passed on from one generation to another, with each generation striving to run its own leg well," Xi stated in the government report.

## Analysis: Institutionalizing Governance Norms

This campaign represents more than a temporary educational initiative—it aims to institutionalize new norms for bureaucratic evaluation and behavior. Key institutional changes include:

- **Multi-dimensional Evaluation:** Moving beyond GDP to include environmental protection, social stability, public satisfaction, and long-term sustainability.
- **Integration with Regular Systems:** Campaign outcomes are being incorporated into Party disciplinary inspections, audits, and performance assessment systems.
- **Protection for Reformers:** The "three distinctions" principle provides clearer guidelines for distinguishing between honest mistakes and intentional misconduct.

## What's Next: Implementation Challenges and Monitoring

As the campaign progresses through July, several challenges will test its effectiveness:

1. **Measurement Difficulties:** How to quantitatively assess "correct views" on political achievements when many valuable outcomes (like environmental protection or social stability) are inherently long-term and qualitative.
2. **Bureaucratic Adaptation:** Whether risk-averse officials will interpret the campaign as encouragement for inaction rather than responsible innovation.
3. **Local Implementation:** How consistently the campaign's principles will be applied across China's vast and diverse bureaucratic landscape.

The campaign's success will be measured not only by immediate educational outcomes but by its lasting impact on China's governance approach as the country navigates the complex challenges of the 15th Five-Year Plan period and its longer-term modernization goals.
