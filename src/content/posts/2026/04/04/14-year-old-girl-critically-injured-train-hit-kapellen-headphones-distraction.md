# 14-Year-Old Critically Injured by Train in Kapellen, Belgium

A 14-year-old girl is fighting for her life after being struck by a train at a railway crossing in Kapellen, Antwerp province, on Friday afternoon. Authorities suspect she may have been distracted by headphones and a mobile phone when the accident occurred Friday afternoon, according to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/04/03/ongeval-trein-voetganger-overweg-kapellen/).

The incident took place at the Koningin Astridlaan/Stationstraat railway crossing, where warning lights were red, barriers were down, and bell signals were sounding at the time of the accident. Emergency services rushed the critically injured victim to a nearby hospital with serious injuries.

## Conflicting Accounts of the Accident

Initial reports from railway infrastructure manager Infrabel suggested the girl attempted to cross the tracks despite the closed crossing. "The barriers were already down and the lights were red. This is a classic case we warn about so often," an Infrabel spokesperson told VRT NWS.

However, Kapellen Mayor An Stokmans (Anders.) presented a different scenario to [Gazet van Antwerpen](https://www.gva.be/regio/antwerpen/regio-antwerpen/kapellen/meisje-14-zwaargewond-na-aanrijding-met-trein-aan-overweg-in-kapellen-slagboom-was-naar-beneden/145150616.html). "The victim probably wanted to access the platform on the left side, but due to absent-mindedness, or because she was looking at her phone, she walked too far," Stokmans said.

## Witness Describes Distraction, Trauma

A car driver waiting at the crossing reported seeing the girl wearing headphones and possibly distracted by her mobile phone. "The driver of a car waiting at the crossing says the girl was wearing headphones and was possibly distracted by her mobile phone," Mayor Stokmans confirmed to reporters.

The witness was deeply traumatized by the incident, hearing the impact and requiring police support afterward. According to [HLN](https://www.hln.be/kapellen/meisje-14-zwaargewond-nadat-ze-gegrepen-wordt-door-trein-aan-overweg-in-kapellen-vermoedelijk-was-ze-door-haar-gsm-en-hoofdtelefoon-afgeleid~a388db5d/), the girl's mobile phone reportedly flew over the barrier upon impact.

## Emergency Response and Transport Disruption

Emergency services including police, firefighters, and medical personnel responded quickly to the scene. Despite the serious impact, the girl remained conscious after the accident. Firefighters assisted with evacuating passengers from the train, who were then transported by replacement buses.

Train traffic between Antwerpen-Centraal and Essen was halted for approximately two hours, with service gradually resuming in the early afternoon. Infrabel spokesperson Frédéric Petit told Gazet van Antwerpen that delays were expected to be resolved by the evening rush hour.

## Safety Infrastructure and Historical Concerns

The railway crossing in Kapellen is not considered particularly dangerous, and the municipality has built a pedestrian tunnel under the tracks specifically to ensure safety. "We have a pedestrian tunnel under the tracks to guarantee safety," Mayor Stokmans emphasized.

This incident highlights ongoing concerns about distraction near railway crossings. Belgian railway authorities have repeatedly warned about the dangers of mobile phone use and headphones at crossings, with similar incidents occurring nationwide. Online discussions following the accident have raised questions about whether barriers should cover the entire roadway width rather than the current partial barriers.

## Investigation and Forward Look

Police described the incident as "an accident due to confusion" and noted the investigation would rely on security camera footage to determine the exact circumstances. The girl's current medical condition has not been disclosed, though she was reported to be in critical condition after being transported to the hospital.

The accident has reignited public debate about mobile device safety near railways, with some online commentators calling for bans on headphones while walking or cycling near train tracks. As authorities continue their investigation, the focus remains on the victim's recovery and understanding how such accidents can be prevented in the future.

Railway safety campaigns have long emphasized the importance of staying alert near tracks, but this tragic incident in Kapellen serves as a stark reminder that technology distraction continues to pose serious risks in potentially dangerous environments.
