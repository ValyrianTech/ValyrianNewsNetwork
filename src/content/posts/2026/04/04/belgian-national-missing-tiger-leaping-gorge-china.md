# Belgian National Missing in China's Tiger Leaping Gorge

Belgian authorities have issued an official search notice for Hugo Huyghebaert, a 28-year-old national from Andenne, who has been missing since March 26, 2026, while traveling in China. The disappearance has triggered a coordinated effort between Belgian and Chinese police to locate the young man in one of the region's most challenging terrains.

## The Disappearance

According to reports from [Le Soir](https://www.lesoir.be/738919/article/2026-04-03/la-police-lance-un-avis-de-recherche-pour-un-belge-disparu-en-voyage-en-chine), Mr. Huyghebaert was last seen on the morning of March 26. He departed from the Haba Xiyinge guest house, located in the commune of Sanba in Shangri-La, Yunnan Province, with the intention of hiking in the Tiger Leaping Gorge (Saut du Tigre), a dramatic canyon carved by the Jinsha River, a major tributary of the Yangtze.

Despite the passage of several days, Mr. Huyghebaert has failed to make contact with family or friends. Following a request from the Namur Public Prosecutor, the Belgian police officially launched the search and disseminated a missing person's notice on April 3.

## Profile and Description

Authorities have provided a detailed physical description to aid in the search. Hugo Huyghebaert is 1.80 meters tall with a slim build, short curly brown hair, and a beard. At the time of his disappearance, he was wearing grey hiking gear and carrying a backpack protected by a green cover.

## The Risks of Tiger Leaping Gorge

The area where Mr. Huyghebaert vanished is known for its breathtaking scenery but is equally notorious for its extreme geography. The Tiger Leaping Gorge is characterized by steep vertical drops, rugged cliffs, and narrow paths that can be treacherous, especially for those venturing off the main tourist trails.

Disorienting fog and the sheer depth of the canyon system often complicate rescue operations. In such environments, a simple misstep or a loss of orientation can lead to critical situations, making the involvement of local Chinese authorities and their knowledge of the terrain essential for any recovery effort.

## International Coordination

Given the location of the disappearance, the case has required high-level diplomatic coordination. The Belgian Ministry of Foreign Affairs has confirmed that it is closely monitoring the situation and is working through diplomatic channels to support the on-ground investigation being conducted by Chinese authorities.

Cross-border missing person cases in remote areas present significant logistical challenges, including language barriers and the need for precise geolocation in regions where satellite coverage may be inconsistent. The collaboration between the Namur Public Prosecutor's office and international partners is currently the primary driver of the search.

## How to Help

Belgian and Chinese investigators are appealing to the public for any information that could lead to Mr. Huyghebaert's location. Anyone who has seen Hugo, communicated with him, or is planning to travel to the Shangri-La region and can provide a testimony is urged to contact the Belgian police.

Information can be submitted via email at **avisderecherche@police.belgium.eu** or by calling the toll-free number **0800/30.300**. 

As the search continues in the difficult terrain of Yunnan, the focus remains on gathering any lead that can narrow down the search area within the vast canyon system.
