# Astrid Coppens Wins Sunscreen Testing Lawsuit Against Testaankoop

Belgian cosmetics company Nomige, producer of media personality Astrid Coppens' sunscreen line, has won a significant legal victory against consumer organization Testaankoop. The Brussels Commercial Court ruled on April 3, 2026, that Testaankoop's testing methods for the RayDefense SPF50 sunscreen were "methodologically vulnerable" and ordered the organization to publish corrections across its platforms. The ruling highlights growing tensions between manufacturers and consumer testing organizations over scientific standards and public communication of product assessments.

## Background: The Sunscreen Controversy

The dispute originated in June 2025 when Testaankoop published test results claiming that Astrid Coppens' RayDefense SPF50 Tinted sunscreen provided only SPF 20 protection instead of the advertised SPF 50. According to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/04/03/cosmeticabedrijf-krijgt-gelijk-in-zaak-tegen-testaankoop-ze-he/), the consumer organization presented these findings as definitive, stating the product "did not meet the correct SPF" and advising consumers against its use.

Nomige, the Ghent-based cosmetics company behind the sunscreen line developed in collaboration with Astrid Coppens, immediately contested the results. "Our products are always carefully and thoroughly scientifically tested," said Barbara Geusens, bio-engineer and managing director of Nomige. "For this we rely on the independent and accredited laboratory Helioscience and we also follow internationally recognized ISO quality standards."

The company argued that Testaankoop's testing methodology was fundamentally flawed for assessing SPF 50 products, comparing it to "using a scale calibrated only to 50 kilograms to determine if something weighs more than 50 kilograms."

## Legal Proceedings and Court Ruling

Following the publication of Testaankoop's negative assessment, Nomige obtained an immediate publication ban via injunction in June 2025. The case then proceeded to the Brussels Commercial Court, where the central issue became the validity of the testing methodologies employed.

The court's ruling, delivered on April 3, 2026, found decisively in favor of Nomige and Astrid Coppens. According to the judgment detailed by [Het Nieuwsblad](https://www.nieuwsblad.be/media-en-cultuur/bv-en-co/astrid-coppens-krijgt-gelijk-in-rechtszaak-tegen-testaankoop/145189471.html), the court determined that "Testaankoop's results are based on vulnerable research methods" and that the organization "violated its duty of care by presenting questionable results as facts without sufficiently loyal and transparent representation of Nomige's serious objections."

Specifically, the court noted that Testaankoop's testing approach was inadequate for accurately determining SPF 50 protection levels. The methodology appeared designed primarily to detect lower protection levels rather than verify whether the product achieved its claimed SPF 50 rating.

## Court Orders and Penalties

The Brussels Commercial Court imposed several requirements on Testaankoop:

1. **Publication of Corrections:** Testaankoop must publish corrections on its website homepage, Facebook, TikTok, and Instagram accounts for 15 consecutive days.
2. **Link to Ruling:** The corrections must include a visible link to the court's full judgment.
3. **Financial Penalties:** Failure to comply will result in penalties of €1,000 per day, up to a maximum of €50,000.
4. **Court Costs:** Testaankoop must also cover the legal costs of the proceedings.

These measures must be implemented within 48 hours of formal notification of the ruling.

## Reactions from Both Sides

Nomige and Astrid Coppens expressed relief and vindication following the ruling. "We are very happy with this recognition. It has been a heavy year," Barbara Geusens told VRT NWS. "We stand and stand firm in our shoes, that has now been proven again," added Astrid Coppens in a statement to [HLN](https://www.hln.be/bv/rechter-geeft-astrid-coppens-gelijk-in-strijd-tegen-testaankoop-na-vernietigend-rapport-over-zonnecreme~a5f71eaa/).

Coppens emphasized the personal and professional impact of the controversy: "The claims that our sunscreen would not meet the correct SPF, and the devastating reviews that followed, hit us particularly hard. The damage was considerable."

Testaankoop, however, has announced it will appeal the decision. "We have taken note of the ruling, but we absolutely cannot agree with the reasoning and we will therefore appeal," said Ortwin Huysmans, spokesperson for the consumer organization. The organization maintains that it "stands 100 percent behind our tests" despite the court's findings.

## Product Rebranding and Market Context

Amid the legal battle, the sunscreen line has undergone rebranding. Originally launched as "Raylee" (a combination of Coppens' children's names Billie-Ray and Joey-Lee), the product is now marketed as "Leeray by Astrid." The premium-positioned sunscreen retails at €59 for a 20ml container, placing it at the higher end of the Belgian sunscreen market.

The case occurs within a broader context of increasing scrutiny on consumer testing methodologies. As products become more scientifically complex, the standards and transparency of consumer organization testing face greater examination. This ruling may establish important precedents for how testing organizations communicate potentially damaging findings about products, particularly when methodological questions exist.

## Implications for Consumer Protection and Business

The Brussels Commercial Court's decision carries significant implications for both consumer protection organizations and businesses in Belgium:

**For Consumer Organizations:**
- Raises questions about testing methodologies and their suitability for specific product claims
- Highlights the legal risks of presenting test results as definitive without adequate methodological rigor
- May prompt revisions to testing protocols and communication practices

**For Businesses:**
- Demonstrates that companies have legal recourse when disputing consumer organization findings
- Emphasizes the importance of maintaining rigorous, independently verified quality standards
- Shows that methodological challenges to testing can succeed in court

**For Consumers:**
- Underscores the complexity of product testing, particularly for scientifically advanced products
- Highlights potential conflicts between consumer protection goals and business interests
- Raises questions about how to interpret conflicting test results from different sources

## Looking Forward

As Testaankoop prepares its appeal, the case will continue to unfold in the Belgian legal system. The appeal process will provide further clarification on the standards expected of consumer testing organizations and the evidentiary requirements for product efficacy claims.

For Astrid Coppens and Nomige, the ruling offers an opportunity to rebuild brand reputation and move forward with their rebranded product line. "We are now finally ready to face a beautiful summer," Coppens remarked, signaling a desire to put the legal battle behind them.

The case represents a notable intersection of business, consumer protection, and scientific standards in Belgium's marketplace. As both sides prepare for the next legal chapter, the ruling serves as a reminder that even well-intentioned consumer protection efforts must withstand methodological scrutiny, particularly when their findings carry substantial commercial consequences.
