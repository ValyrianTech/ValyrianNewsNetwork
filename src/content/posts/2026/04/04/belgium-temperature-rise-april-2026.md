# Belgium Braces for Significant Temperature Spike Next Week

Belgium is set to experience a sharp increase in temperatures over the coming days, bringing an early taste of spring to the region. Following a period of instability and cooler weather, forecasts indicate a warming trend that will peak between April 7 and 8.

## From Rain to Radiance
The transition begins after a volatile weekend. Saturday and Sunday are characterized by shifting conditions; while some clearings appear, the weather remains humid with cloud cover and drizzle. According to [Le Soir](https://www.lesoir.be/738939/article/2026-04-04/forte-hausse-des-temperatures-voici-les-previsions-meteo-des-prochains-jours), the Royal Meteorological Institute (IRM) noted that Sunday will remain relatively cool, with temperatures between 10°C and 13°C and wind gusts reaching up to 60 km/h.

## The Warming Trend
The atmosphere shifts significantly starting Monday, April 7. The weather is expected to become calm and sunny, with temperatures climbing steadily. By Tuesday and Wednesday, the region will see a "remarkable softness" for the season. 

While some reports suggest a peak of 19°C, other data indicates temperatures could climb as high as 22°C. This discrepancy likely reflects the difference between national averages and local maxima in more sheltered areas.

## Outlook and Implications
This sudden meteorological shift is expected to impact both energy consumption and outdoor activities as residents pivot from winter gear to spring attire. However, the warmth will be short-lived.

The heatwave is predicted to break on Thursday, April 9, as rain returns to the country and temperatures drop back to a range of 9°C to 16°C. Residents are encouraged to make the most of the sunny window between Tuesday and Wednesday before the unstable weather returns.
