# Brussels Freezes Innovation Funding After 25% Budget Cut

The Brussels regional innovation agency Innoviris has suspended all new project funding for 2026 following a 25% budget reduction, sparking criticism from researchers and tech companies who warn the cuts could damage Brussels' competitiveness. According to an [official announcement](https://www.innoviris.brussels/news/funding-measures-2026) published on March 24, 2026, the agency's budget has been cut from €39 million in 2025 to €30 million for 2026.

"Considering the available financial resources for 2026, ongoing projects will continue to be funded in accordance with the signed decisions and agreements. However, no new projects can be funded in 2026," stated Innoviris. "No new calls will be launched in 2026, and all programs will be suspended in 2026."

## Context: Brussels' Budget Deficit

The funding freeze comes as part of broader austerity measures by the Brussels regional government led by Minister-President Boris Dilliès (MR). The government aims to reduce the region's budget deficit, which exceeds €1 billion.

As [reported by RTBF](https://www.rtbf.be/article/austerite-ou-responsabilite-la-region-bruxelloise-a-enfin-son-budget-11701426), the budget has sparked political controversy, with opposition parties labeling it an "austerity budget" while the government defends it as fiscal responsibility. "Moi je n'ai pas de problème avec ça, c'est une bataille de mots : austérité pour certains, rigueur pour d'autres, responsabilité aussi, en tout cas c'est un budget de nécessité," stated Dilliès.

Innoviris is Brussels' primary public agency financing research and innovation across universities and businesses. The agency had announced a €7.3 million investment in 46 AI projects on February 16, 2026—just two days after the government formation—though these were from 2025 calls.

## Immediate Impact

No new innovation projects will receive funding in Brussels during 2026, affecting universities, SMEs, and research institutions. According to [BRUZZ reporting](https://www.bruzz.be/actua/economie/onderzoeksfonds-innoviris-draait-geldkraan-dicht-voor-nieuwe-projecten-2026-03-27), the cabinet confirmed Innoviris must operate with 20% fewer resources this year.

"Het Brusselse onderzoeksfonds innoviris.brussels financiert dit jaar geen nieuwe projecten meer, omdat de Brusselse regering de middelen beperkt om de begroting op orde te krijgen," reported BRUZZ.

The suspension affects organizations that have previously benefited from Innoviris support, including Nationale Loterij, Orange, Institut Jules Bordet, Solidaris health fund, and Belga news agency.

## Political and Economic Implications

Over 100 researchers have signed open letters denouncing the "drastic budget cuts" that threaten Brussels' position as an innovation hub. Critics argue reducing innovation investment could have long-term negative consequences for competitiveness.

Supporters point to the need to address Brussels' budget deficit. The government emphasizes existing projects will continue, and applications already submitted may get funding in 2027, depending on resources.

[L'Echo reported](https://www.lecho.be/entreprises/tech-science/prive-de-20-de-son-budget-innoviris-ne-soutiendra-plus-de-nouveaux-projets-en-2026/10654818.html) the budget reduction represents a significant shift in Brussels' innovation policy, coming as other European regions increase R&D investments.

## Analysis: Short-term vs Long-term

The funding freeze highlights tension between immediate fiscal constraints and long-term innovation investment. While providing short-term savings, cuts risk undermining Brussels' innovation ecosystem.

Brussels has been positioning itself as a European AI and tech hub. The suspension could lead to:

1. **Brain drain**: Researchers may seek funding elsewhere.
2. **Project delays**: Promising initiatives may be postponed.
3. **Reduced competitiveness**: Brussels may fall behind other innovation hubs.
4. **Economic impact**: Lost economic activity, jobs, and investment.

The cuts raise questions about alignment with European innovation strategies like Horizon Europe.

## What's Next

The outlook for 2026 is bleak, but 2027 remains uncertain. Innoviris states applications "will continue to be processed and may be eligible for funding in 2027, depending on available resources."

Longer-term impact depends on:

1. **Budgetary developments**: Whether deficit targets are met without further cuts.
2. **Political priorities**: How government balances fiscal responsibility with innovation investment.
3. **Alternative funding**: Whether researchers can access European programs or private investment.
4. **Ecosystem resilience**: Whether key talent and organizations remain in Brussels.

The controversy reflects broader debates about public investment in innovation during fiscal constraint. As Brussels seeks to be a competitive innovation hub, today's decisions shape its economic future.

For now, Innoviris' message is clear: existing applications may qualify for 2027 funding, depending on resources. The innovation community must wait to see whether funding will be restored or further constrained.
