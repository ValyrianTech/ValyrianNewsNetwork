# Why Now Is a Good Time to Buy a Second-Hand Electric Car in Belgium

Belgium's second-hand electric vehicle (EV) market is experiencing significant growth, with prices dropping and battery technology proving more durable than many consumers expect. According to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/04/03/elektrische-tweedehandsauto-in-de-lift-maar-aandeel-blijft-klein/), electric cars accounted for 5.5% of used car sales in the first quarter of 2026—nearly 10,000 vehicles—up from just 2.7% in 2024. Experts suggest that current market conditions create an opportune moment for consumers to consider used EVs, citing "interesting prices" and reliable battery performance.

## Market Growth and Current Landscape

The used EV market in Belgium has shown steady progression, growing from 2.7% market share in 2024 to 3.9% in 2025 before reaching 5.5% in early 2026. Filip Rylant of Traxio, the Belgian Mobility Federation, notes that "you see steady progress" but adds that "companies are maintaining the climb, with more than half of the purchases." Private buyers remain relatively hesitant, accounting for less than half of used EV transactions despite favorable conditions.

Overall, Belgium's second-hand car market remains dominated by petrol vehicles (56%), followed by diesel (22.7%) and hybrids (15.3%). The total used car market saw nearly 182,000 sales in the first three months of 2026, with EVs representing the fastest-growing segment.

## Favorable Pricing and Supply Dynamics

Current market conditions are particularly advantageous for buyers. "It's an interesting price for what you get," Rylant told VRT NWS. The relative abundance of ex-lease electric vehicles entering the second-hand market, combined with still-modest demand, has kept prices competitive. Ivo Willems of charging station company Stroohm adds that "EVs that came to market 4 to 5 years ago also became cheaper," further enhancing the value proposition.

This pricing dynamic contrasts with neighboring Netherlands, where used EV sales surged 50% from February to March 2026, according to [NOS reporting](https://nos.nl/artikel/2608700-tweedehands-elektrische-auto-in-trek-door-dure-benzine-en-diesel). Dutch dealers report demand increases of 35-40% in recent weeks, suggesting Belgium may follow a similar trajectory as consumer awareness grows.

## Battery Health: Better Than Expected

One of the most significant barriers to used EV adoption—concerns about battery degradation—is being challenged by real-world data. Batteries in 3-4 year old electric vehicles typically retain 90% or more of their original capacity, with expected lifespans of 400,000-500,000 kilometers. Shared test results from Tesla user groups show vehicles averaging 90% battery capacity after approximately 200,000 kilometers.

"Battery tests show that the battery holds up well, buyers don't need to be afraid of that," Rylant emphasizes. He notes that modern batteries are protected by vehicle settings that prevent extreme charging states, extending their usable life. "Don't forget that engines of cars running on fossil fuels also wear out," he adds, putting EV concerns in perspective.

## Consumer Hesitation and Changing Attitudes

Despite favorable conditions, psychological barriers persist. "Private buyers remain hesitant to buy a second-hand electric car. Unjustly, according to Rylant. He believes a 'cultural shift' is needed," reports VRT NWS. However, experts detect changing attitudes, partly driven by high fuel prices during the ongoing energy crisis.

"Something is changing," Rylant observes. "That can make people think. Those who have solar panels can even drive for free if you charge at the right time." The normalization of EV ownership is also reducing anxiety. "I hear the word 'plug stress' less often," says Willems. "Everyone now knows someone who drives electric and that threshold has been lowered."

## Regulatory Developments and Testing Standards

Belgium is implementing measures to boost consumer confidence. Starting January 2026, the Car-Pass document for used vehicles will include a State of Health (SOH) percentage for EV batteries. This initiative, supported by Minister for Consumer Protection Rob Beenders and Minister for Mobility Jean-Luc Crucke, aims to provide transparent information about battery condition.

Michel Peelman, Car-Pass Managing Director, states: "For electric vehicles, the condition of the battery is at least as important as the mileage." Battery health tests, costing several tens of euros, can be performed by authorized dealers, the Flemish Automobile Association (VAB), or independent services like Aviloo, Moba, and Electrify.

## Expert Recommendations for Buyers

For consumers considering a used EV, experts offer several recommendations:

1. **Invest in a battery test:** While not yet standard practice, a State of Health assessment provides valuable information about remaining capacity and expected lifespan.
2. **Consider the total cost of ownership:** Lower fuel and maintenance costs can offset slightly higher purchase prices over time.
3. **Evaluate charging options:** Home charging capability, especially combined with solar panels, maximizes economic benefits.
4. **Check remaining warranty:** Many EVs retain substantial battery warranty coverage even as second-hand vehicles.

## Looking Ahead

The second-hand EV market in Belgium appears poised for continued growth. As more leased vehicles enter the used market and consumer education improves, market share is expected to increase further. The implementation of standardized battery health reporting through Car-Pass should address one of the key remaining barriers to adoption.

Rylant summarizes the opportunity: "It's an interesting price for what you get. Moreover, battery tests show that the battery holds up well." With prices favorable, battery technology proven durable, and regulatory frameworks improving transparency, the conditions for used EV adoption in Belgium have never been better.

As the energy transition accelerates and fuel prices remain volatile, second-hand electric vehicles offer Belgian consumers an increasingly attractive pathway to sustainable mobility—combining environmental benefits with economic sensibility.
