# China Digital Economy: AI Careers Boom, Delivery Record

China's digital economy is experiencing simultaneous acceleration across multiple sectors, with three key developments announced on April 2 highlighting the country's transition toward a technology-driven economic model. According to [Xinhua News Agency](https://www.news.cn/tech/20260402/6043ca224de3438fb9daef130f03e9bf/c.html), new AI professions are emerging as "robot teachers" train humanoid machines, while separate reports reveal record express delivery usage and a significant expansion of the digital yuan ecosystem.

## The Rise of 'Robot Teachers' and AI Professions

A new profession is taking shape in China's technology sector: humanoid robot data collectors, often called "robot teachers." These workers, many born after 2000, use VR headsets and controllers to physically teach robots through repetitive data collection. At Zhiji Robot's data collection center, young employees like Liu Genlin (born 2004) are training machines to perform tasks ranging from household chores to industrial operations.

"I applied for this position mainly because the humanoid robot market is very hot right now," Liu told Xinhua. "I wanted to see what data collectors actually do and where robots are headed."

The training process requires patience and precision. "I feel robots are like children," said fellow data collector Chen Xin. "Teaching them requires patience. Making mistakes once or twice in the lab doesn't matter, but ultimately we must ensure every piece of recorded data is high-quality and valuable."

Zhiji Robot, which officially launched its 10,000th general-purpose embodied robot "Yuanzheng A3" on March 30, has collected over 1 million high-quality data points covering home, catering, industrial, commercial, and office scenarios. Data Collection Manager Zhao Qiyan explained that each motion trajectory's joint angles and force are recorded "in a language computers can understand, allowing 'silicon-based life' to learn."

This development comes as China positions itself as a global leader in humanoid robotics, with 2025 considered the country's "first year of mass production" for such machines. Over 140 domestic manufacturers released more than 330 models last year, creating demand for the training infrastructure now being built across multiple cities including Beijing, Zigong, Wuhan, and Shanghai.

## Record Express Delivery Growth Reflects E-commerce Boom

Parallel to the AI profession boom, China's express delivery industry has reached new milestones. According to the State Post Bureau, annual per capita express delivery usage increased from 59 items in 2020 to 141 items in 2025—more than doubling in just five years.

Xu Huarong, Deputy Director of the Policy and Regulations Department at the State Post Bureau, [reported](https://www.news.cn/fortune/20260402/25ce9b1f2ec64def9299efe775d26c27/c.html) that postal express industry revenue grew from 1.1 trillion yuan in 2020 to 1.8 trillion yuan in 2025, representing over 10% annual growth. China maintained its position as the world's largest express delivery market for the 11th consecutive year, accounting for over 60% of global courier parcel growth during 2021-2025.

The figures reflect the continued strength of China's e-commerce ecosystem, driven by platforms like Alibaba, JD.com, and Pinduoduo. Live-streaming commerce and rural e-commerce expansion have been particularly significant growth drivers, with daily parcel volume peaking at 777 million items during the 2021-2025 period.

To support this growth while addressing environmental concerns, China issued over 30 safety and green standards during the period, including mandatory standards for packaging limits and heavy metal content. The 2025 revision of the "Express Temporary Regulations" added a dedicated chapter on快递包装 (express packaging), further strengthening regulatory oversight.

## Digital Yuan Ecosystem Expands with 12 New Banks

In the financial technology sector, the People's Bank of China announced a significant expansion of the digital yuan (e-CNY) ecosystem. On April 2, the central bank [added 12 new digital yuan business operation institutions](https://www.news.cn/fortune/20260402/bee415d240cc4628a054068b39cd540b/c.html), bringing the total number of operating institutions to 22.

The new operators include CITIC Bank, China Everbright Bank, Huaxia Bank, China Minsheng Bank, Guangfa Bank, SPD Bank, Zheshang Bank, Bank of Ningbo, Bank of Jiangsu, Bank of Beijing, Bank of Nanjing, and Bank of Suzhou. These institutions will commence operations after completing necessary business and technical preparations.

The expansion aims to "improve the inclusiveness of digital yuan services" and follows "market-oriented and rule-of-law principles," according to the central bank. This move represents the latest step in China's gradual rollout of its central bank digital currency, which began with pilot programs in 2020 and has since expanded to numerous cities and use cases.

## Strategic Implications and Economic Context

These simultaneous developments across AI, e-commerce logistics, and digital currency reflect China's broader "Digital China" strategy, which aims to position the country as a global leader in digital technologies. During the 14th Five-Year Plan period (2021-2025), China's digital economy grew to account for over 40% of GDP, with continued expansion targeted in current planning.

The emergence of "robot teachers" illustrates how technological advancement can create new professions even as it automates others. As Wang Feili, an industrial sector analyst with UBS Securities China, noted in a March 16 Xinhua article, "Even if the 'baby' is born smart, without real-world datasets to feed it, it cannot grow." China's investment in robot data collection centers addresses this critical bottleneck in AI development.

Meanwhile, the express delivery figures demonstrate the resilience of consumer demand and digital commerce despite broader economic challenges. The industry has undergone rapid automation at major sorting centers, yet continues to employ millions in delivery and logistics roles.

The digital yuan expansion carries both domestic and international implications. Domestically, it could challenge existing digital payment ecosystems dominated by Alipay and WeChat Pay. Internationally, it positions China at the forefront of central bank digital currency development, with potential implications for global financial systems and reduced dependence on dollar-dominated transactions.

## Looking Ahead

As these sectors continue to evolve, several questions remain. The net employment impact of AI and robotics—balancing job creation in fields like robot training against potential displacement in other sectors—will require careful monitoring. Digital yuan adoption among Chinese consumers, while growing, still faces competition from established payment platforms.

Data quality and diversity in robot training will be crucial to avoid algorithmic biases, while environmental sustainability concerns around packaging waste from express delivery growth demand continued innovation in green logistics.

What's clear from the April 2 announcements is that China's digital transformation is accelerating across multiple fronts simultaneously. The convergence of AI workforce development, e-commerce infrastructure growth, and financial technology expansion creates a powerful synergy that could reshape not only China's economy but also its position in global technological competition.

As one robot data collector trainee reflected in the Xinhua report: "I believe humans will still be the leaders of robots, and robots will remain under our control. After all, technological progress isn't meant to replace humans but to open up more creative space." This sentiment captures the balancing act at the heart of China's digital economy ambitions—harnessing technology's potential while maintaining human direction and control.
