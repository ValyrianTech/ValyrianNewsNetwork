# Brad Pitt Films in Brussels: Resident's Starstruck View

Hollywood actor Brad Pitt is filming scenes for his new movie 'The Riders' in Brussels, generating excitement among local residents and highlighting the city's appeal as an international filming location. According to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/04/02/opnames-brad-pitt-in-brussel-michiel-zit-plots-op-eerste-rij/), local resident Michiel found himself with a front-row view of the production from his apartment window near the Justice Palace, describing the experience as leaving him "completely starstruck."

## Film Production Details

'The Riders' is an adaptation of Tim Winton's 1994 novel, directed by Edward Berger who won four Academy Awards for 'All Quiet on the Western Front.' The film is produced by A24, with Pitt serving as both producer and lead actor. He plays Fred Scully, an Australian man whose wife disappears under mysterious circumstances. According to the plot summary, Scully plans to move to Ireland with his wife Jennifer, but when he picks up his family at the airport, only his 7-year-old daughter Billie is on the plane. The traumatized child cannot explain why she took the flight alone, and father and daughter embark on a search across Europe for Jennifer.

The cast also includes Camille Cottin, Julianne Nicholson, and Ulrich Thomsen. Previous filming has taken place in Ireland and Greece, with the European production schedule now moving through Belgium.

## Filming Timeline and Locations

The production arrived in Belgium in late March, beginning with scenes in Amsterdam before moving to Brussels. On Monday, March 30, Cinquantenaire Park (Jubelpark) was completely closed to the public throughout the day for filming. [The Bulletin](https://www.thebulletin.be/cinquantenaire-park-closed-brad-pitt-film-shoot) reported that "the police closed the park at the request of Visit Brussels" to "ensure the safety and tranquillity of the filming." All entrances were blocked at dawn, with visual barriers erected around the site.

The Autoworld museum, located within the park, had to welcome visitors through alternative entrances during the closure. A museum spokesperson told The Bulletin they were "not allowed to say anything about the film shoot," reflecting the production's tight security protocols.

Filming moved to an ice rink in Heverlee (near Leuven) on Tuesday, March 31, before returning to Brussels on Wednesday, April 1, for scenes near the Justice Palace. It was here that local resident Michiel witnessed the production from his apartment window.

## Eyewitness Account: "Completely Starstruck"

Michiel, a barista at Kaffabar in Brussels, told VRT NWS that residents received mysterious notices a week in advance about street evacuations for a fictional film. "The evening before, several trucks arrived and there was continuous security surveillance," he said.

"The next morning I look out my window and I see a gigantic crew, certainly 50 people with professional equipment," Michiel recounted. His housemate Victor, a superfan of Brad Pitt, had joked that it might be the actor's film. "So I spent the whole time looking out my window. And suddenly a beautiful taxi arrived and a chic Range Rover, and out stepped none other than the one and only Brad Pitt."

Michiel admitted, "Normally that doesn't affect me much, but now I was completely starstruck." He noted Pitt's appearance for the role: "He looked like a neglected, confused man."

The local resident also gained insight into film production efficiency. "Brad Pitt has a stand-in, a man who pre-plays the scene to set the lighting and frame the shot," he observed. "He had to walk up and down the Justice Palace slope 10 times. And then Pitt comes and after 2 takes it's in the can."

With hopeful hospitality, Michiel added, "I'm setting up an extra table for Brad, you never know."

## Public Reaction and Economic Impact

Crowds gathered at Jubel Park gates hoping to catch a glimpse of Pitt during filming. Fans Albane and Dorothée told VRT NWS they had "a little crush on him" and just wanted to see him for a minute. Social media platforms showed extensive coverage with fan videos and photos from various filming locations.

The production represents significant economic activity for Brussels, employing local crew, renting equipment, and utilizing local services. Visit Brussels, the city's tourism organization, facilitated filming permits and coordination with local authorities, highlighting Brussels' growing reputation as a filming destination.

This isn't Pitt's first filming experience in Belgium—he was last in the country in 2024 to film 'F1' in Spa. Brussels has recently hosted several international productions, including a TV series about the Salah Abdeslam manhunt.

## What's Next

Filming continues in Brussels, with the crew spotted at Greenwich brasserie in the city center on Thursday, April 2. The production is expected to move to additional European locations before completion.

'The Riders' joins a growing list of international productions filmed in Brussels, reinforcing the city's position in the global film industry. For local residents like Michiel, the experience provides a rare intersection of everyday life and Hollywood filmmaking—a starstruck moment in an otherwise ordinary week.

As film production continues to globalize, such interactions between international celebrities and local communities will likely become more common, blending global entertainment industries with local contexts in increasingly interconnected ways.
