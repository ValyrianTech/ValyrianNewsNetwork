# SpaceX Files for IPO, Could Be Largest Stock Offering Ever

Elon Musk's SpaceX has confidentially filed paperwork for an initial public offering (IPO) that could become the largest stock market listing on record, according to multiple financial news reports. The filing, submitted to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, could value the pioneering rocket company at approximately $1.75 trillion and potentially raise $50-75 billion when it goes public, likely in June 2026.

## Context and Background

Founded in 2002 by Elon Musk with the goal of reducing space transportation costs and enabling Mars colonization, SpaceX has revolutionized the aerospace industry with its reusable Falcon rockets. The company has remained privately held for 24 years despite frequent speculation about a public offering. In February 2026, SpaceX merged with Musk's artificial intelligence startup xAI, creating a combined entity valued at $1.25 trillion at the time.

SpaceX has become NASA's primary launch partner following the end of the space shuttle program in 2011 and has received over $24.4 billion from federal government contracts since 2008, according to [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/01/spacex-confidentially-files-for-ipo-setting-stage-for-record-offering.html). The company generated approximately $8 billion in profit on $15-16 billion of revenue in 2025, as [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/aerospace-defense/spacex-registers-take-rocket-maker-public-blockbuster-ipo-bloomberg-news-reports-2026-04-01/) reported in January.

## Key Developments

According to [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-01/spacex-is-said-to-file-confidentially-for-ipo-ahead-of-ai-rivals), SpaceX submitted its draft IPO registration confidentially to the SEC, putting it on track for a June listing. The company is reportedly targeting a valuation of around $1.75 trillion, which would make it potentially the largest IPO in history, surpassing Saudi Aramco's 2019 offering.

The confidential filing allows SpaceX to submit financial documents to regulators for review before making them public. The company will need to release a public filing at least 15 days before its IPO road show, as noted by CNBC.

SpaceX has scheduled an analyst day for April 21 and is offering an optional visit to xAI's "Macrohard" data center site in Memphis, Tennessee, on April 23, according to Reuters. A virtual session on May 4 will discuss financial models with banks' research analysts.

## Analysis and Implications

The SpaceX IPO represents a watershed moment for the commercial space industry and could revitalize the IPO market after years of subdued activity. Angelo Bochanis, data and index associate at Renaissance Capital, told Reuters that "investors could use a sum-of-the-parts analysis, but, like with Tesla, SpaceX's valuation could very much fluctuate wildly based off how much the public believes in Musk's vision."

Much of the $1.75 trillion valuation discussion centers on SpaceX's Starlink satellite internet business. Shay Boloor, chief market strategist at Futurum Equities, explained to Reuters that "Starlink is the only reason this valuation is defensible. This is going to be the recurring revenue engine" with 9 million subscribers, defense contracts, and its own data network.

A successful listing would make Elon Musk the first person to helm two separate trillion-dollar publicly traded companies (SpaceX and Tesla). However, the IPO comes amid market volatility due to the U.S.-Iran war and spiking oil prices. Reena Aggarwal, a professor of finance at Georgetown University, cautioned in a CNBC interview that "you can have a great company, with great fundamentals and a lot of investor interest — and an IPO can still flop if the markets have turned south, if there's too much volatility in the market."

The offering would provide retail and institutional investors their first opportunity to invest directly in SpaceX, which has been privately held since its founding. "It's not like five other companies like this will go public in the next five years," Aggarwal added. "Anyone who wants more exposure to Elon Musk — this is their opportunity to get in."

## What's Next

SpaceX's IPO filing marks the beginning of what could be a trio of mega-IPOs, with AI rivals OpenAI and Anthropic also reportedly considering large public offerings. The SpaceX listing would bring unprecedented public scrutiny and capital to the commercial space industry, potentially accelerating space exploration and commercialization efforts.

As a public company, SpaceX would face increased regulatory oversight and disclosure requirements. The company's ambitious projects — including the Starship system for Mars colonization, orbital AI data centers, and expansion of the Starlink constellation — would receive greater public attention and potentially more accessible funding.

The success of the SpaceX IPO could set a precedent for other large private companies considering public listings and demonstrate the maturation of the commercial space sector as a legitimate investment category. However, the offering's ultimate success will depend on market conditions, investor appetite for Musk's vision, and the company's ability to navigate the transition from private to public ownership while maintaining its innovative edge in the competitive space industry.
