# Knife-Wielding Man Arrested at Roeselare City Hall

A 37-year-old man of Afghan origin was arrested on Wednesday afternoon after entering the Roeselare city hall armed with a large knife and damaging property on the first floor. According to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/04/01/man-met-mes-stadhuis-roeselare/), the incident occurred during lunchtime outside regular opening hours when only staff were present, and no injuries were reported.

## Incident Details and Quick Police Response

The man entered the city hall atrium with the knife not initially visible, then proceeded to the first floor where he began waving the weapon and damaged a glass wall of a meeting room. A security guard immediately alerted police, who responded quickly and arrested the suspect without incident. [HLN reports](https://www.hln.be/roeselare/parket-vraagt-aanhouding-van-man-die-met-mes-zwaaide-in-roeselaarse-stadhuis~af01f817/) that approximately 10 staff members were in the reception area at the time, with no citizens present.

Police response was aided by officers being nearby after the Dwars door Vlaanderen cycling race that had just started in the area. The West Flanders prosecutor has requested detention for the suspect, who will appear before an investigating judge in Kortrijk on Thursday facing charges of threats, resistance, damage, and weapon possession.

## Staff Support and Security Measures

City officials have provided psychological counseling for affected employees following the traumatic incident. Veronique Degryse, the city's communication officer, stated in [Het Nieuwsblad](https://www.nieuwsblad.be/binnenland/politie-arresteert-man-die-met-mes-in-het-rond-zwaaide-in-stadhuis-bewaker-sloeg-meteen-alarm/144991983.html) that "the guard at the entrance immediately sounded the alarm" and emphasized the city's focus on aggression policies to help employees handle threatening situations.

Curd Neyrinck, police chief of Zone RIHO which covers Roeselare, noted that "the man could be arrested very quickly" thanks to the swift coordination between security personnel and responding officers. The city has sent internal emails to all staff informing them of available support services.

## Context and Broader Implications

Roeselare, a city in West Flanders with approximately 62,000 residents, has maintained security measures at its city hall including a permanent security guard at the entrance. The incident raises questions about public building security and knife violence prevention, particularly as the suspect's motive remains unclear according to all sources.

Public comments on the incident reflect broader societal concerns about weapon control and judicial responses to such threats. The successful arrest without injury demonstrates effective emergency response protocols, but also highlights the need for continued vigilance in public spaces.

## Legal Proceedings and Next Steps

The 37-year-old Afghan suspect is currently in custody and will face an investigating judge on Thursday. The prosecutor's office has opened a formal investigation into the incident, which caused significant disruption at the municipal building during lunch hours.

City officials have reassured the public that all necessary support is being provided to affected staff and that security protocols functioned as intended. The incident serves as a reminder of the importance of prepared emergency responses in public institutions and the value of trained security personnel in preventing escalation.

As the legal process moves forward, authorities will continue to assess the suspect's mental state and potential motives while ensuring appropriate support for city employees who witnessed the disturbing events.
