# Wang Yi Discusses Gulf Security with Bahraini Counterpart Amid Hormuz Tensions

Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi held a crucial telephone conversation on Thursday with Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) Chair and Bahraini Foreign Minister Abdullatif bin Rashid Al Zayani, focusing on escalating security challenges in the Middle East and specifically addressing navigation obstructions in the strategically vital Strait of Hormuz. According to [official Chinese reports](http://politics.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0402/c1001-40694164.html), the discussion centered on China's recently announced five-point peace initiative with Pakistan and potential coordination through the United Nations Security Council to address regional tensions.

## Context: Rising Gulf Tensions

The phone call comes amid what Bahrain's foreign minister described as "severe security challenges" facing Gulf countries, with particular concern over the Strait of Hormuz—a critical chokepoint through which approximately 20-30% of the world's oil shipments pass. The conversation marks continued Chinese diplomatic engagement in Middle Eastern affairs, following China's successful mediation of the Saudi Arabia-Iran rapprochement in 2023.

Minister Al Zayani, speaking as both Bahrain's top diplomat and current GCC Chair, initiated the call to brief Wang on Middle East developments. [Xinhua News Agency reported](https://www.news.cn/politics/leaders/20260402/c1b5a1fa869f47a18bfefe73a184dc53/c.html) that Al Zayani emphasized Bahrain's readiness to "leverage the UN Security Council to play a role in resolving the issue of shipping passage through the Strait" and expressed hope for strengthened coordination with China.

## The China-Pakistan Five-Point Peace Initiative

Wang Yi articulated China's stance of "opposing aggression and advocating for peace," while referencing the joint China-Pakistan five-point initiative announced days earlier. The initiative was jointly issued by Wang and Pakistani Foreign Minister Mohammad Ishaq Dar following their March 31 meeting in Beijing.

According to the [Chinese Foreign Ministry's official document](https://www.fmprc.gov.cn/eng/wjbzhd/202603/t20260331_11884511.html), the five points include:

1. **Immediate cessation of hostilities** and efforts to prevent conflict spread
2. **Commencement of peace talks** with commitment to peaceful resolution
3. **Protection of civilians and non-military targets**, including critical infrastructure
4. **Security of shipping lanes**, specifically calling for protection in the Strait of Hormuz
5. **Primacy of the United Nations Charter**, advocating for true multilateralism

Wang emphasized that "ceasefire and cessation of hostilities are the common aspiration of the international community" and that "UN Security Council actions should help ease tensions and bring war to an end to resume talks, rather than endorsing illegal acts of war."

## Strategic Implications

The discussion highlights Gulf states' increasing turn toward China as a diplomatic partner beyond traditional Western allies. Bahrain's initiative to coordinate with China through the UN Security Council suggests diversification of diplomatic approaches among GCC members.

The specific focus on the Strait of Hormuz indicates serious concerns about potential disruptions to global energy supplies. Any significant obstruction would have immediate economic consequences worldwide, directly impacting China's energy security as a major importer of Gulf oil.

China's positioning as a "responsible major country" ready to work with Bahrain to "safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of the Global South" aligns with Beijing's broader foreign policy narrative. This framing was evident in [English-language coverage](https://www.bignewsnetwork.com/news/278959507/1st-ld-writethru-chinese-fm-voices-readiness-to-work-with-bahrain-to-push-for-ceasefire-restore-peace), which quoted Wang's commitment to regional stability.

## UN Security Council Dynamics

Wang's comments position China against certain Western-backed resolutions while advocating for diplomatic solutions. As a permanent Security Council member with veto power, China's stance significantly influences Middle East discussions.

The foreign minister stated China "stands ready to work with Bahrain to push for the cessation of hostilities, restore peace, achieve lasting regional stability." This suggests potential Chinese support for Bahraini initiatives while maintaining Beijing's emphasis on non-interference.

## Historical Context

This phone call represents the second high-level discussion between Wang and Al Zayani in less than a month, following their March 9-10 conversation addressing Gulf tensions. The frequency indicates both urgency of regional concerns and established communication channels.

China's relationship with the GCC was formalized in a 2022 strategic partnership agreement, marking significant elevation of ties. Economic cooperation has expanded rapidly, with China becoming a major trading partner and investor in GCC countries.

## What's Next

Key questions include what specific measures Bahrain will propose at the UN Security Council, how China will operationalize its five-point initiative, and whether this coordination will translate into concrete diplomatic actions.

Regional observers will watch for Chinese diplomatic initiatives to facilitate Gulf talks, coordination at the United Nations, and whether China's positioning as a neutral mediator gains traction among Middle Eastern stakeholders.

The phone call reinforces China's active diplomatic posture in Middle Eastern affairs and highlights interconnected regional security, global energy markets, and great power diplomacy.
