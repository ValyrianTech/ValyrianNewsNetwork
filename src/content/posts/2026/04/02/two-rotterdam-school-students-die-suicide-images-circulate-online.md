# Two Rotterdam School Students Die by Suicide; Images Circulate Online

Two students from Emmauscollege, a secondary school in Rotterdam, died by suicide on Monday and Tuesday this week in a tragic incident that has shocked the local community. According to [NU.nl](https://www.nu.nl/binnenland/6391348/twee-leerlingen-van-school-rotterdam-overleden-beelden-slachtoffers-gaan-rond.html), both incidents occurred at the same apartment building in Capelle aan den IJssel, and images of the victims are reportedly circulating online, raising serious privacy and ethical concerns.

## Context and Timeline

The two students, both minors, attended Emmauscollege, a school with approximately 1,600 students located on the edge of Rotterdam. According to police statements reported by [NOS](https://nos.nl/artikel/2608799-twee-leerlingen-van-school-in-rotterdam-overleden-slachtoffers-gefilmd), the first student died on Monday, March 31, followed by the second student on Tuesday, April 1. Both incidents took place at the same location in Capelle aan den IJssel, with one victim coming from that municipality and the other from Rotterdam.

Police have confirmed the deaths were suicides and are investigating the circumstances. A police spokesperson told NU.nl that authorities are being "restrained with information" due to the sensitive nature of the situation. The exact connection between the two incidents beyond their attendance at the same school remains unclear.

## Official Responses

Local officials have expressed shock and offered support to the affected communities. Rotterdam's Education Alderman Said Kasmi met with the Emmauscollege director on Thursday to offer support. "We are deeply affected by the sad events concerning two students of Emmauscollege," Kasmi stated. "Our thoughts go out to the parents, fellow students, teachers and everyone affected by this."

Capelle aan den IJssel Mayor Joost Manusama issued an official statement on the [municipality's website](https://www.capelleaandenijssel.nl/burgemeester-manusama-over-overlijden-2-minderjarigen-uit-capelle-en-rotterdam), saying, "Words fail in the face of this drama. Our thoughts are with the families, friends, classmates and everyone who loved them. Their grief is great and intense." He added that the municipality is in close contact with police, schools, and affected neighborhoods, working with partners like the GGD and welfare organizations to provide support.

## School and Community Impact

The tragedy has deeply affected the school community and beyond. According to [AD](https://www.ad.nl/binnenland/grote-schok-na-overlijden-van-twee-leerlingen-van-een-school-politie-onderzoekt-aanleiding~a3937fdb/), Emmauscollege sent emails to parents warning about circulating images and videos of the incidents. The school explicitly asked parents to check their children's phones and delete any sensitive content "out of respect for the deceased and their relatives."

Support services have been mobilized across multiple institutions. Slachtofferhulp Nederland (Victim Support) is providing assistance to several schools and sports clubs in the region. At VV Nieuwerkerk football club, where one of the victims was a member, a support meeting on Tuesday attracted over 200 members, primarily young people.

Club chairman Hans Jeroense told AD, "The impact on our members and the board is enormous. I've been chairman for 25 years, but I've never experienced this." Training resumed on Wednesday with special guidance provided to the affected team.

## Ethical Concerns Over Image Circulation

A disturbing aspect of this tragedy involves bystanders filming the victims and sharing images online. A local police officer expressed outrage in an [Instagram post](https://www.instagram.com/p/DWkQZ9FCNyt/?igsh=MTBudzN2dDdhd2gxMg%3D%3D) on Monday, writing: "There lies someone fighting for his or her life and you think it's necessary to film that. And worse: you put it online. As if someone's suffering is something to share. As if it's entertainment."

The officer continued, "Stand still for a moment and think about what you're doing. Behind that image is a human being, a family, and real emotions... You've lost something: respect, humanity, and boundaries. And if you no longer feel that, that says everything about you."

This incident highlights growing concerns about the ethics of filming and sharing traumatic content on social media, particularly among young people.

## Analysis: Youth Mental Health and Social Media Ethics

This tragedy touches on two critical issues facing Dutch society: youth mental health and the ethical use of social media. Suicide remains a leading cause of death among Dutch youth aged 10-20, highlighting the need for continued attention to mental health support systems in schools and communities.

The circulation of images from traumatic incidents represents a relatively new ethical challenge. While curiosity about dramatic events is human nature, the instant recording and sharing capabilities of smartphones, combined with social media platforms' reach, create situations where privacy and respect for victims can be severely compromised.

Schools like Emmauscollege now face the additional challenge of managing digital fallout from tragedies, as evidenced by their email to parents about deleting sensitive content. This incident may prompt broader discussions about digital literacy, empathy education, and guidelines for responsible social media use during crises.

## Support Resources and Moving Forward

As the communities of Rotterdam and Capelle aan den IJssel grapple with this tragedy, support services remain available. The school is receiving advice from experts on how to support students who need to talk about what happened.

For those struggling with suicidal thoughts or concerned about others, resources include:
- **113 Suicide Prevention:** Chat via 113.nl or call 113 or 0800-0113
- **Slachtofferhulp Nederland:** Provides support to those affected by traumatic events
- **GGD and local welfare organizations:** Offering community-based support

The police investigation into the circumstances continues, though authorities emphasize the need for sensitivity and respect for the privacy of the grieving families. As Mayor Manusama noted in his statement, "Out of respect for the bereaved, restraint is appropriate. They deserve peace and space to mourn."

This tragic event serves as a somber reminder of the importance of mental health support for young people and the need for ongoing conversations about responsible digital citizenship in an age where personal tragedies can become public spectacle with a few taps on a smartphone.
