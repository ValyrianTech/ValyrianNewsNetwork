# China's Supreme Procuratorate Unveils 16 Key Tasks to Protect Public Welfare

BEIJING — According to [Xinhua News Agency](https://www.news.cn/legal/20260404/67f9cbfe8f154333b7d48c5c0f2f4e70/c.html), the Supreme People's Procuratorate (SPP) of China has announced its strategic deployment for the 2026 'Protecting People's Livelihoods' (检护民生) initiative. The comprehensive plan identifies 16 priority tasks designed to strengthen legal oversight across vital economic sectors, protect vulnerable populations, and resolve pressing social issues.

## Strategic Focus on Economic and Social Stability

The 2026 campaign is structured around three fundamental pillars aimed at utilizing prosecutorial supervision to support high-quality economic development and social governance. 

First, the SPP will intensify legal oversight in critical economic sectors. This includes strengthening civil supervision within the finance and real estate industries, a move seen by analysts as a way to manage legal risks during China's ongoing economic restructuring.

Second, the initiative prioritizes the protection of vulnerable groups. The SPP has committed to enhancing the legal rights of minors, women, the elderly, and persons with disabilities, ensuring that the legal framework actively supports those at the margins of society. This includes specialized efforts in protecting the rights of heroes, martyrs, and military dependents.

Third, the plan targets prominent livelihood issues to mitigate social friction. Key focus areas include food and drug safety, workplace safety, and the enforcement of labor rights. Additionally, the SPP is emphasizing the protection of ecological environments and natural resources, as well as promoting the rule of law in cyberspace.

## A Multi-Year Commitment to Legal Reform

This initiative is not an isolated development but a continuation of a sustained, multi-year commitment to 'prosecutorial supervision' as a tool for social governance. Since at least 2025, the 'Protecting People's Livelihoods' campaign has been a central pillar of the SPP's work, showing a consistent focus on addressing the "bottom line" of public welfare.

The expansion of these tasks aligns with China's broader mission of 'modernizing procuratorial work' to support 'Chinese-style modernization.' By targeting emerging challenges such as cybersecurity and strengthening traditional sectors like 'Sannong' (agriculture, rural areas, and farmers), the SPP is positioning itself as a proactive regulator. The plan also includes efforts to normalize the multi-channel resolution of social conflicts and disputes, as well as increasing judicial protection for cultural relics and heritage.

## Looking Ahead

As the SPP moves forward with these 16 tasks, the focus remains on using judicial intervention to resolve disputes and protect the 'bottom and line' of public welfare. The success of this initiative will be measured by the judiciary's ability to act as a check on administrative excesses and provide a stable legal environment for both citizens and economic actors alike.
