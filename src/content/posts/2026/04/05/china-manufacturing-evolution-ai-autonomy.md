# China’s Manufacturing Evolution: From Digitalization to AI-HDriven Autonomy

China has reached a significant milestone in its industrial transformation, completing a decade of intensive effort in intelligent manufacturing. After ten years of focusing on digitization and automation, the nation is now pivoting toward a new frontier: industrial autonomy. This strategic shift, characterized by the transition from simple automation to systems capable of self-learning and self-optimization, is set to be a cornerstone of China's industrial policy leading up to 2027.

## A Decade of Industrial Progress

Since the launch of the Manufacturing Powerhouse strategy in 2015, China has achieved remarkable advancements in the digital integration of its industrial sector. According to [Xinhua News Agency](https://www.news.cn/tech/20260                %s/546befd351a64d33b50854baa15bc540/c.html), the progress over the last ten years has been significant. Key achievements include a digital penetration rate of 89.6% among large-scale industrial enterprises and a 57.7% penetration rate for digital equipment.

China's leadership in advanced manufacturing is further evidenced by its role in hosting 45% of the world's 'Lighthouse Factories'—the international benchmark for highly automated production. Furthermore, the country now accounts for more than 50% of all new industrial robot installations globally, demonstrating a massive scale of technological adoption.

## The 2027 Mandate: 'AI + Manufacturing'

As China approaches the 15th Five-Year Plan, the focus is shifting from mere digitization to a specific, high-stakes mission: the 'Artificial Intelligence + Manufacturing' special action. Led by eight government departments, including the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT), this initiative has established concrete targets for 2027.

The mandate includes the deployment of 1,000 high-level industrial AI agents and the standardization of 500 typical industrial application scenarios. A primary driver for this acceleration is the pursuit of technological sovereignty. By fostering a 'secure and reliable supply' of core AI technologies, China aims to protect its manufacturing ecosystem from external supply chain disruptions and geopolitical volatility.

## The Strategic Shift toward Autonomy

The next generation of manufacturing is defined by the concept of 'Autonomy' (自主化). While previous iterations of automation focused on automating repetitive, manual tasks, the new era demands systems that possess advanced cognitive capabilities. This includes self-sensing, self-decision-making, self-execution, and self-optimization.

Experts suggest that this transition is not merely about efficiency but about building a robust, resilient industrial base. As noted by national manufacturing experts, the true 'newness' of this era lies in moving from single-point technological breakthroughs to the construction of a full, integrated industrial system. This shift toward flexible, distributed, and green manufacturing is expected to redefine the global industrial landscape.

## Looking Ahead

As China enters the second decade of this transformation, the success of the 'AI + Manufacturing' mandate will depend on the deep integration of AI into the very fabric of the factory floor. If successful, the deployment of industrial AI agents could cement China's position as a leader in the next wave of global industrial evolution, creating a manufacturing sector that is not only highly efficient but also intrinsically autonomous and secure.
