# NPC Vice Chairperson Wu Weihua Leads Delegation to Ethiopia

In a significant move to bolster bilateral relations, Wu Weihua, Vice Chairperson of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress (NPC) of China, led a high-level delegation to Ethiopia from April 3 to April 5, 2026. The visit, conducted at the invitation of the Ethiopian Federal Parliament, focused on deepening the "all-weather strategic partnership" between the two nations.

During the three-day diplomatic mission in Addis Ababa, the delegation held high-level talks with prominent Ethiopian leaders, including President Taye Atske Selassie, Speaker of the House of Peoples' Representatives Chafu, and Speaker of the Senate Agnehu Teshager, as reported by [Xinhua News Agency](https://www.news.cn/world/20260405/e813be28ca0e4ed3a4bdab32408143ef/c.html).

## Strengthening Strategic Ties

Wu Weihua emphasized that under the strategic guidance of both nations' leaders, China-Ethiopia relations have achieved fruitful results in practical cooperation. He underscored China's commitment to implementing the consensus reached by the leaders of both countries and the outcomes of the FOCAC Beijing Summit. A key focus of the discussions was the exchange of experience in national governance and the advancement of inter-parliamentary cooperation.

In addition to diplomatic alignment, the delegation promoted details of China's upcoming "15th Five-Year Plan" and highlighted China's policy of implementing zero tariffs on certain products from Africa, a move designed to stimulate trade and economic growth across the continent.

## Economic Cooperation and Shared Principles

Ethiopian officials expressed high appreciation for China's "Four Global Initiatives" and the economic benefits brought by the zero-tariff policy. The delegation's discussions also touched upon the continued support for Ethiopia's ongoing efforts regarding its integration into the global trading system.

Ethiopia reaffirmed its commitment to the "One China" principle and expressed a strong desire to expand cooperation across various sectors, particularly through legislative and multi-sectoral exchanges. This visit underscores the growing importance of parliamentary diplomacy in maintaining the momentum of China-Africa economic and political engagement.

## Future Outlook

As both nations look toward the future, the success of this visit signals a continued trajectory of strengthening bilateral ties. The focus remains on translating strategic agreements into tangible economic and legislative progress, ensuring that the partnership between China and Ethiopia continues to serve as a cornerstone of stability and growth in the region.
