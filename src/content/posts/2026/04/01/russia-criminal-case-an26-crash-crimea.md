# Russia Opens Criminal Case After An-26 Crash in Crimea

Russian authorities have opened a criminal investigation following the crash of an Antonov An-26 transport aircraft in Crimea, [according to](https://tass.ru/proisshestviya/26958863) the TASS news agency. Military investigative bodies initiated the case under Article 351 of Russia's Criminal Code for "violation of flight rules or preparation for them."

## Investigation Details

The Russian Investigative Committee confirmed the criminal probe into the aviation incident that involved 30 people onboard - 7 crew members and 23 passengers. The case was officially registered on April 1, 2026, with military investigative authorities taking lead responsibility for the investigation.

TASS reported the crash occurred in Crimea's mountainous terrain, though specific location details and timing remain undisclosed. The An-26, a Soviet-era twin-engine turboprop transport aircraft, has been involved in several incidents throughout its operational history.

## Aviation Safety Context

The investigation highlights ongoing aviation safety concerns in the region, particularly regarding military transport operations. The An-26 aircraft, first introduced in the 1960s, remains in service across various air forces despite its aging design.

Russian aviation authorities have faced increased scrutiny over military transport safety following several high-profile incidents in recent years. The current investigation will examine whether proper flight procedures were followed and if any equipment failures or maintenance issues contributed to the crash.

## Aircraft Background

The Antonov An-26 is a short-to-medium-range transport aircraft developed from the An-24 series. Known for its rugged design and ability to operate from unpaved runways, the aircraft has served numerous military and civilian operators worldwide since its introduction in 1969.

Despite its age, various upgraded versions of the An-26 continue to operate in several countries, primarily for military transport, cargo, and utility missions.

## Ongoing Developments

As the criminal case proceeds, investigators will work to determine the exact circumstances surrounding the crash. The outcome could potentially lead to criminal charges against those found responsible for any regulatory violations.

Transport safety experts emphasize that thorough investigations following such incidents are crucial for identifying systemic safety issues and preventing future accidents.

The incident serves as a reminder of the inherent risks in military aviation operations, particularly when operating older aircraft types in challenging terrain like Crimea's mountainous regions.
