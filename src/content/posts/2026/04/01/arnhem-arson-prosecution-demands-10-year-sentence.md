# Prosecution Demands 10-Year Prison Sentence for Arnhem Arson, Acquittal for Two Others

The Public Prosecution Service has demanded a 10-year prison sentence for the main suspect in the devastating March 2025 arson case in Arnhem's historic city center, while seeking acquittal for two other defendants. According to [NOS](https://nos.nl/artikel/2608650-om-eist-10-jaar-cel-voor-brandstichting-arnhem-vrijspraak-voor-twee-anderen), the prosecution argued that Koert H. (58) acted alone in setting the fire that destroyed multiple buildings, including historic monuments, while acknowledging insufficient evidence against Ricky N. (42) and Mark V. (31).

## Context: The Devastating Arnhem Fire

The case stems from a March 2025 incident in Arnhem's Varkensstraat where a fire started in a waste container and rapidly spread to adjacent buildings. The blaze resulted in the complete destruction of multiple historic structures—some designated as cultural monuments—and forced dozens of residents to evacuate their homes with only minutes to spare. According to municipal reports, the fire represented one of the most significant urban disasters in Arnhem's recent history, with damage estimates running into millions of euros.

The location in Arnhem's historic city center amplified both the damage and public concern. The Varkensstraat area contained buildings dating back centuries, with architectural features that were irreplaceable. The incident highlighted ongoing concerns about urban fire safety in historic districts across the Netherlands, where narrow streets and aging infrastructure can complicate emergency response efforts.

## Key Developments and Prosecution Position

During the March 31-April 1, 2026 court hearings at the Gelderland District Court, prosecutors presented their case against the three defendants. According to prosecution evidence, Koert H. "set fire alone and walked away when paper/cardboard began to glow" in the waste container. The prosecution emphasized that "miraculously no deaths or injuries occurred" despite the extensive damage.

For the two co-defendants, Ricky N. and Mark V., the prosecution acknowledged their presence at the scene but concluded that "it cannot be established that they made a significant contribution to the fire." Both men were released from pre-trial detention in October 2025, while Koert H. remains in custody awaiting the final verdict scheduled for April 24, 2026.

## Defense Positions and Courtroom Dynamics

During the two-day court proceedings, Koert H. maintained his complete innocence despite compelling evidence presented by prosecutors. Camera footage captured him uttering the words "Let's set that thing on fire" just moments before the blaze erupted—a statement he characterized as an offhand joke rather than criminal intent. "I did not set the fire," he repeatedly asserted during cross-examination, claiming the prosecution had misinterpreted both his words and actions.

For Ricky N. and Mark V., defense attorneys emphasized their clients' peripheral involvement and argued that mere presence at the scene did not constitute criminal behavior. Both men acknowledged being in the Varkensstraat area but denied any role in starting or encouraging the fire.

The prosecution's case relies heavily on multiple evidentiary pillars: the timestamped video recording, forensic analysis pinpointing the fire's origin to the specific waste container, and witness testimonies placing all three men near the ignition point. However, prosecutors carefully distinguished between direct involvement and circumstantial presence, concluding that only Koert H.'s actions met the threshold for criminal liability under Dutch arson statutes.

## Community Impact and Ongoing Consequences

The Arnhem arson case has inflicted lasting trauma on both individuals and the broader community. Twenty-eight formal damage claims have been filed by displaced residents and property owners, with many victims reporting ongoing psychological distress from the sudden loss of their homes and possessions. The emotional toll has been compounded by the destruction of historic buildings that held deep community significance and cultural value.

In response to the tragedy, Arnhem municipal authorities have implemented comprehensive safety reforms, including enhanced fire monitoring in historic districts, revised waste container placement regulations, and improved emergency response protocols for dense urban areas. These measures reflect broader national concerns about preserving historic urban centers while ensuring resident safety.

From a legal standpoint, the case exemplifies the complex evidentiary challenges in multi-defendant arson prosecutions. Dutch law requires clear demonstration of both actus reus (criminal act) and mens rea (criminal intent) for conviction, creating particular difficulties when multiple individuals are present at a fire's origin. The prosecution's nuanced approach—severe punishment for the primary actor while acknowledging insufficient evidence against others—demonstrates careful application of these legal principles.

## Analysis: Legal Precedents and Implications

This case represents a significant test for Dutch arson laws, which carry severe penalties—especially when fires endanger lives or cause extensive property damage. The 10-year sentence demand underscores the gravity with which authorities view urban arson cases, particularly those involving historic properties and residential areas.

The prosecution's position also demonstrates careful consideration of evidentiary standards. By seeking acquittal for the two co-defendants, prosecutors acknowledge the importance of distinguishing between presence at a crime scene and active participation in criminal activity—a distinction crucial to maintaining fairness in the justice system.

## What's Next: Awaiting the Final Verdict

With the prosecution's demands now formally presented, all parties await the court's final verdict scheduled for April 24, 2026. The decision will not only determine the fate of the three defendants but will also set important precedents for how Dutch courts handle complex urban arson cases involving multiple suspects.

The case has drawn significant public attention, reflecting broader concerns about urban safety, preservation of historic architecture, and the legal system's ability to address complex property crimes effectively. The April 24 verdict will provide closure to both the defendants and the Arnhem community affected by this devastating fire.
