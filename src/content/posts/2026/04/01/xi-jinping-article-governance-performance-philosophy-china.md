# Xi Jinping Publishes Article on Correct Governance Performance Philosophy

China's leader Xi Jinping has published a significant article in the country's leading political theory journal, emphasizing proper approaches to political achievement as the nation implements its 15th Five-Year Plan. The article, "Establishing and Practicing a Correct Understanding of Governance Performance," appears in the April 1 issue of Qiushi Journal and addresses systemic issues in official performance evaluation.

According to [Xinhua News Agency](https://www.news.cn/politics/leaders/20260331/0e007eccda254af5affe3c70b902851b/c.html), the article collects Xi's discourses on governance from December 2012 to February 2026. Published in China's flagship political theory journal, it serves as key guidance for officials nationwide.

## Context and Timing

The publication coincides with the first year of China's 15th Five-Year Plan (2026-2030), approved at March's Two Sessions meetings. This timing aligns with transitions of local leadership teams, creating opportunities to instill new governance philosophies.

The article follows a Party-wide campaign launched in late February to guide officials in establishing correct views of governance performance, running through July 2026.

## Key Principles Outlined

"The view on governance performance is a fundamental issue, concerning establishing the Party for the public good and governing for the people," Xi writes, according to the [Qiushi Journal English edition](https://en.qstheory.cn/2026-03/31/c_1172432.htm).

A central theme is the distinction between public and private interests. "Party consciousness plays a decisive role in establishing and practicing a correct understanding of governance performance," Xi states. "The fundamental measure of Party spirit is the distinction between public and private interests."

Xi declares that "Communists must remember that benefiting the people is the greatest political achievement," adding that work should be "for the benefit of the people, not for personal promotion or gain."

## Addressing Systemic Problems

The article targets "vanity projects," "image engineering," and data fabrication. Xi calls for improvements to performance evaluation to "prevent the vicious cycle in which the promotion of officials based on inflated figures would fuel data fabrication."

This acknowledgment represents a significant admission from China's leadership. The reference to "数字出官、官出数字" (officials promoted based on numbers, numbers created by officials) captures documented data manipulation by local officials.

As reported by [People's Daily](https://en.people.cn/n3/2026/0331/c90000-20442032.html), the article emphasizes that "high-quality development is the primary task" and "should be an important component in officials' understanding of governance performance."

## Integration with Economic Strategy

The governance philosophy connects to China's 15th Five-Year Plan, which emphasizes technological self-reliance and risk mitigation. The plan, analyzed by the [International Institute for Strategic Studies](https://www.iiss.org/online-analysis/online-analysis/2026/03/chinas-15th-five-year-plan/), comes at "a time of slowing economic growth, internal instability following last year's widespread anti-corruption campaign and global uncertainty."

Xi's article calls for officials to "implement the new development philosophy" and "improve performance evaluation mechanisms" to support strategic goals. The 15th Five-Year Plan reduces growth targets (from 5% to 4.5-5% of GDP annually) while increasing research spending (over 7% annual growth).

## Analysis and Implications

The article signals a concerted effort to address structural governance issues amid economic and geopolitical challenges. By emphasizing long-term development over short-term gains, leadership prepares for potentially slower but more stable growth.

The acknowledgment of data manipulation suggests recognition that previous evaluation approaches produced negative consequences. The call for "differentiated assessment systems" indicates understanding that uniform targets may not suit China's diverse regions.

The article attempts to reconcile centralized control with local initiative, providing clearer guidelines to reduce risk aversion while maintaining policy alignment.

## What's Next

The immediate focus is implementing the education campaign through July 2026, particularly among newly appointed officials. Observers will watch for changes to performance evaluation systems and whether "high-quality development" emphasis shifts how success is measured.

Longer term, success will be judged by impact on achieving 15th Five-Year Plan goals in technological innovation and economic restructuring. As China navigates domestic challenges and complex international environment, these principles face their test in practical implementation.
