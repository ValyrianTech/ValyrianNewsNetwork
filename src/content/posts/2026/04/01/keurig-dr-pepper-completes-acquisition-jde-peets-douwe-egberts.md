# Keurig Dr Pepper Acquires Dutch Coffee Giant JDE Peet's

American beverage company Keurig Dr Pepper Inc. (NASDAQ: KDP) has completed its $18.4 billion acquisition of Dutch coffee giant JDE Peet's N.V., making historic brands like Douwe Egberts fully American-owned. The deal, which gives KDP control of 96.22% of JDE Peet's shares, marks a major consolidation in the global coffee industry and ends the Amsterdam listing of one of Europe's largest coffee companies.

According to the [official press release](https://www.keurigdrpepper.com/keurig-dr-pepper-acquires-jde-peets-and-announces-rafael-oliveira-as-ceo-of-future-global-coffee-co/), the transaction creates a "global coffee powerhouse" that combines KDP's Keurig single-serve system with JDE Peet's portfolio of more than 50 coffee brands. The acquisition was finalized on April 1, 2026, following an offer period that began in August 2025.

## Historic Dutch Brand Changes Hands

Douwe Egberts, founded in 1753 in the Netherlands, represents one of Europe's most iconic coffee brands. Through JDE Peet's—formed in 2019 through the merger of Jacobs Douwe Egberts and American specialty coffee chain Peet's—the brand had maintained its Dutch roots despite previous ownership by German investment firm JAB. The company went public on Euronext Amsterdam in 2020.

As [NOS reported](https://nos.nl/artikel/2608656-ook-douwe-egberts-definitief-in-amerikaanse-handen), the acquisition means "JDE Peet's, het moederbedrijf van onder meer Douwe Egberts en L'OR Espresso, is volledig eigendom geworden van Keurig Dr Pepper" (JDE Peet's, the parent company of among others Douwe Egberts and L'OR Espresso, has become fully owned by Keurig Dr Pepper). This follows a broader trend of Dutch food brands transitioning to American ownership, including yesterday's announcement that Unilever is selling its food division to American spice company McCormick.

## Deal Structure and Timeline

The $18.4 billion all-cash transaction represents one of the largest food and beverage acquisitions in recent years. Keurig Dr Pepper [announced](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/keurig-dr-pepper-acquires-jde-peets-and-announces-rafael-oliveira-as-ceo-of-future-global-coffee-co-302731096.html) that it has acquired 96.22% of JDE Peet's shares, with remaining shareholders having until April 13, 2026, to tender their shares during a post-closing acceptance period.

As a result of the acquisition, JDE Peet's will be delisted from Euronext Amsterdam. The last trading day will be April 29, 2026, with delisting scheduled for April 30. This removes a significant European-listed company from the Amsterdam exchange, which had been JDE Peet's home since its IPO six years ago.

## Leadership Appointments and Future Structure

In a significant leadership announcement, KDP's Board of Directors has named Rafael Oliveira as Chief Executive Officer of its coffee operating unit and as CEO for the future Global Coffee Co. Oliveira, who has served as JDE Peet's CEO since November 2024, will continue in that role during the integration period while joining KDP's Executive Leadership Team.

"Our acquisition of JDE Peet's marks a defining step in our value creation strategy, and Rafa is the right choice to lead the combined coffee business and launch Global Coffee Co.," said KDP Board Chair Pam Patsley in the press release.

Current KDP CEO Tim Cofer will become CEO of the future Beverage Co. when the separation occurs. The company plans to split into two independent, U.S.-listed publicly traded companies by the end of 2026: a refreshment beverages company (Beverage Co.) and a global coffee company (Global Coffee Co.).

## Creating a Coffee Powerhouse

The combined coffee business brings together complementary strengths. KDP's Keurig system dominates the single-serve coffee market in North America, while JDE Peet's boasts a portfolio of leading brands including Jacobs, L'OR, Peet's, and of course, Douwe Egberts. Together, they will operate more than 40 manufacturing facilities worldwide and employ over 50,000 people globally.

[Food Business News reported](https://www.foodbusinessnews.net/articles/30084-keurig-dr-pepper-completes-deal-for-jde-peets) that "the spun-off coffee company will include brands like Keurig, Jacobs, L'OR and Peet's Global Coffee Co." The new entity will aim to "be the best coffee company in the world by combining global reach with local expertise," according to Oliveira.

## Broader Industry Implications

This acquisition represents the latest in a wave of consolidation in the global coffee industry, which has seen increasing competition from both traditional players and new entrants in the specialty coffee segment. The deal creates one of the world's largest pure-play coffee companies, with combined expertise across single-serve systems, ground coffee, beans, and ready-to-drink formats.

For the Netherlands, the acquisition continues a trend of iconic Dutch brands transitioning to foreign ownership. Just yesterday, Unilever announced the sale of its food division—including brands like Calvé, Cup A Soup, and Hellmann's—to American company McCormick. While the international headquarters for Global Coffee Co. will remain in Amsterdam, the ultimate ownership and stock market listing will be American.

## What's Next

The integration process will now begin in earnest, with Oliveira leading the combined coffee business toward the planned separation by year-end 2026. The separation timing will depend on achieving key milestones including appropriate leverage levels at each company and supportive market conditions.

For consumers, the companies have indicated that existing brands will continue to be available, with potential for innovation through combined research and development capabilities. The transaction brings together what Oliveira called "an incredible opportunity to create the future of coffee"—though market observers will be watching closely for any potential price implications resulting from increased industry consolidation.

As JDE Peet's prepares to leave the Amsterdam exchange after six years, the deal marks both the end of an era for Dutch ownership of one of its most historic food brands and the beginning of a new chapter in global coffee industry consolidation.
