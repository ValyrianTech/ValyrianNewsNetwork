# Iranian Official Criticizes Trump's Alleged Plan for Iranian Islands

Iranian military analysts have characterized former US President Donald Trump's alleged plan for ground entry to Iranian islands in the Persian Gulf as "among the most foolish scenarios," according to a [Tasnim News Agency report](https://www.tasnimnews.ir/fa/news/1405/01/12/3553974/trump-plan) published Wednesday. The criticism comes amid escalating tensions between Washington and Tehran centered around Kharg Island, Iran's primary oil export terminal.

## Context

The strategic standoff stems from Iran's reported closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a critical global oil shipping channel through which 20-30% of the world's oil passes. In response, the Trump administration has reportedly been considering military options, including using US troops to seize Kharg Island, which handles approximately 90% of Iran's crude oil exports.

According to the Tasnim analysis, "The plan for ground entry and attempt to seize Iranian islands is considered from a military perspective as a very ridiculous and high-risk action for America." The assessment comes from the Tasnim War Interpretation Group, which provides military analysis for the Iranian news agency.

## Key Developments

US forces conducted strikes on Kharg Island on March 13, 2026, targeting 90 military sites including naval mine storage facilities and missile storage bunkers, [according to a CNN report](https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/25/politics/iran-kharg-island-us-military-ground-troops). The Tasnim analysis notes that Iran subsequently began reinforcing the island's defenses with mines, traps, and additional air defense systems.

On March 25, 2026, [CNN reported](https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/28/middleeast/iran-war-strait-hormuz-arch-defense-intl-hnk-dst-ml) that the Trump administration was actively considering ground operations to seize the strategic island. The Iranian military analysis published April 1 represents Tehran's first official response to these reports.

The Tasnim analysis notes that "European countries are reportedly avoiding direct involvement in this conflict, stating 'this is not their war.'" Instead, the report suggests that "the burden of confrontation appears to be shifting to some Arab countries in the region."

## Military Analysis

From a tactical perspective, Iranian analysts argue that "land entry into such an arena is not only complex but also costly and high-risk, and could be considered one of the biggest potential risks for America in such a scenario."

The analysis highlights several strategic challenges facing any potential US operation:

1. **Geographic proximity**: Kharg Island's location near the Iranian mainland makes sustained occupation extremely difficult
2. **Defensive preparations**: Iran has reportedly been preparing defense scenarios for such situations for many years
3. **Military complexity**: The operation would require maximum operational capability with significant risk
4. **International reluctance**: Key allies appear unwilling to support prolonged ground operations

"Even if it is assumed that America could attempt to seize some points or islands," the analysis states, "the main problem is 'consolidation and maintenance' of them—a stage that is much more difficult than the seizure itself."

## Regional Implications

The escalating tensions have broader implications for regional stability. According to the Tasnim report, "Any expansion of the conflict could spread insecurity throughout the entire region."

Retired Admiral James Stavridis, former NATO Supreme Allied Commander, [told CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/28/world/video/are-ground-troops-in-iran-inevitable-a-former-nato-commander-weighs-in) that "Iranians are clever and ruthless. They will do everything they can to inflict maximum casualties on US forces" if ground operations proceed.

Gulf allies have reportedly been privately urging the Trump administration against prolonging the war with ground operations, recognizing the potential for broader regional destabilization.

## Strategic Considerations

The Iranian analysis draws a distinction between "creating security" and "creating insecurity," noting that while establishing security requires maximum capability and complete control, "disrupting security sometimes requires minimal disruption to affect the entire equation."

This perspective suggests that even if US forces could initially seize territory, maintaining control would prove extraordinarily difficult given Iran's geographic advantages and prepared defensive measures.

## What's Next

The situation represents one of the most significant escalations in US-Iran tensions in recent years, with potential global economic consequences due to the strategic importance of the Strait of Hormuz for oil shipments.

Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf, Speaker of Iran's Parliament, had previously warned against occupation attempts, with Iranian media reporting that Iran has prepared robust defensive measures.

As military planning continues to develop diplomatically, the international community watches closely whether diplomatic solutions can be found to prevent further escalation in this volatile region. The Iranian characterization of US plans as "foolish" reflects both confidence in defensive capabilities and recognition of the enormous risks involved in any ground operation against Iranian territory.
