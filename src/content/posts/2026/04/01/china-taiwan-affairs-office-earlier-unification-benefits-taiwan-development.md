# China's Taiwan Affairs Office Advocates Earlier Unification for Taiwan's Development

China's Taiwan Affairs Office (TAO) stated on Tuesday that earlier realization of cross-strait unification would be more beneficial for Taiwan's development and improve the quality of life for Taiwanese citizens. The statement came during a regular press conference where spokesperson Zhang Han responded to Taiwan's criticism of Beijing's unification proposals as having "limited appeal" to Taiwanese society.

## Context and Background

The remarks follow ongoing discussions about Taiwan's future relations with mainland China. According to [Xinhua News Agency](https://www.news.cn/tw/20260401/b135e4a9ffcd4ca7b1db5266b376dd62/c.html), the TAO had previously outlined infrastructure assistance proposals for post-unification Taiwan, including a round-island high-speed rail and east-west highways. Taiwan's Mainland Affairs Council (MAC) responded that such proposals were "overly focused on material benefits" with "limited appeal to Taiwanese society," arguing that cross-strait differences cannot be resolved through infrastructure projects alone.

## Key Statements from the Press Conference

During the April 1 press conference, TAO spokesperson Zhang Han emphasized that "national unification is an unstoppable historical trend." She stated, "Peaceful unification will create huge opportunities for Taiwan's economic and social development and bring tangible benefits to the vast number of Taiwanese compatriots. This is the greatest attraction."

Zhang specifically addressed Taiwan's criticism, saying, "We have done everything to promote the development of cross-strait relations to enhance the kinship and well-being of compatriots on both sides of the strait and realize their yearning for a better life." Her most direct statement came when she asserted, "The earlier unification is achieved, the more beneficial it is for Taiwan's development and the better life for Taiwanese compatriots."

The spokesperson criticized Taiwan's ruling Democratic Progressive Party (DPP), stating, "The Democratic Progressive Party authorities, for the sake of party interests, stubbornly pursue independence and resist unification, which will only deprive Taiwanese people of their right to pursue a better life." She called on Taiwanese citizens to "actively participate in the just cause of promoting the unification of the motherland and build our common homeland better."

## Analysis of Strategic Messaging

The TAO's emphasis on economic benefits and improved quality of life represents a strategic attempt to frame unification as a pragmatic issue rather than purely political. According to the [official transcript](https://www.gwytb.gov.cn/m/speech/202604/t20260401_12758540.htm) of the press conference, the messaging appears aimed at both Taiwanese citizens and the Chinese domestic audience, reaffirming Beijing's commitment to unification while attempting to make the case more appealing to Taiwanese society.

The timing of these statements is significant, coming ahead of a planned visit by Kuomintang (KMT) Chairperson Zheng Liwen to mainland China from April 7-12, 2026. This marks the first KMT chairperson visit in 10 years and suggests Beijing is attempting to influence political dynamics within Taiwan. The criticism of the DPP and praise for upcoming KMT visits indicates Beijing's preference for political forces in Taiwan that are more receptive to cross-strait dialogue.

## Broader Context and Regional Implications

Cross-strait relations have been strained since the election of DPP administrations in Taiwan, which Beijing views as pursuing independence. The Chinese government has consistently maintained that Taiwan is an inalienable part of China and has pursued unification as a long-term policy goal.

The press conference also addressed US-Taiwan relations, with the TAO warning against external interference in cross-strait affairs. As reported by [China News Service](https://www.chinanews.com.cn/gn/2026/04-01/10596432.shtml), Zhang Han stated that "Taiwan's future, the security and well-being of Taiwanese compatriots, always depend on the peaceful development of cross-strait relations and the unification of the motherland."

## What's Next

The upcoming KMT visit could signal potential warming in certain cross-strait exchanges, though fundamental differences between Beijing and Taipei remain. The emphasis on "earlier unification" suggests Beijing may be seeking to accelerate its timeline for cross-strait integration, though specific steps following this rhetorical emphasis remain unclear.

Regional actors will likely monitor these developments closely, particularly given broader tensions in the Taiwan Strait. The statements reinforce Beijing's consistent position on unification while attempting to make the case more appealing to Taiwanese citizens through economic arguments.

Key unanswered questions include how Taiwan's government will formally respond to these specific statements, what concrete steps might follow this emphasis on "earlier unification," and how the Taiwanese public will receive these messages amid ongoing discussions about cross-strait relations. The specific infrastructure projects and timelines envisioned in the TAO's proposals also remain to be detailed.
