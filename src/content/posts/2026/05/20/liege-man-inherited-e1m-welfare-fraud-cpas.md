# Liège Man Inherited €1M and Two Houses While on Welfare

A sexagenarian resident of Verviers, in Belgium's Liège province, has been convicted by the Liège Criminal Court for fraudulently receiving welfare benefits from the CPAS (Public Center for Social Welfare) for many years while concealing a substantial inheritance of two houses and over €1 million. The court sentenced him to 150 hours of community service and ordered him to repay €114,659.57 in wrongfully received benefits.

## Context

The case, reported by [La Libre Belgique](https://www.lalibre.be/belgique/judiciaire/2026/05/20/un-liegeois-qui-avait-herite-de-deux-maisons-et-de-plus-dun-million-deuros-beneficiait-du-cpas-je-suis-traine-dans-la-boue-souille-noirci-SII47NWGCBA4JB2B7JZWVPS7JU/), has reignited public debate in Belgium about welfare fraud detection and the effectiveness of asset verification procedures within the country's social welfare system. The CPAS is the Belgian public social welfare center system, operating at the municipal level to provide social assistance — primarily the Revenu d'Intégration Sociale (social integration income) — to individuals and families who lack sufficient resources.

To qualify for CPAS benefits, applicants must demonstrate they have insufficient income and assets. The case of the Verviers man raises serious questions about how such a significant change in financial circumstances went undetected.

## Key Developments

According to [DHNet](https://www.dhnet.be/regions/liege/2026/05/19/alors-quil-beneficiait-du-cpas-apres-le-chomage-le-sexagenaire-habitant-a-verviers-avait-herite-de-deux-maisons-et-de-plus-dun-million-deuros-QC2PEJSEBJAC5KRN54PQGJCZFE/), the man inherited the properties and funds from his father and his godmother. He was convicted of "faux et usage de faux" (forgery and use of forged documents), a criminal offense under Belgian law involving the fraudulent alteration of the truth in written documents.

The defendant was described as "particularly litigious" ("particulièrement procédurier") by the court. In a striking detail, the man had benefited from legal aid — funded by taxpayers — to defend himself throughout the proceedings, despite having concealed a fortune worth well over €1 million.

When confronted with the verdict, the convicted man told the court: "Je suis traîné dans la boue, souillé, noirci" ("I am dragged through the mud, sullied, blackened"), expressing a sense of victimization by the legal process.

## Analysis and Implications

The case arrives at a particularly sensitive moment for Belgium's social welfare system. In 2025, Belgium recorded a record 7,873 reports of social fraud, according to [21News](https://www.21news.be/onlyfans-influenceurs-travail-au-noir-la-belgique-intensifie-la-chasse-aux-fraudeurs/), with undeclared work accounting for approximately 63% of all reports. The federal platform for reporting social fraud, which has been in operation since 2015, has never registered such a high volume of denunciations outside the exceptional Covid period.

The case also comes amid heightened political scrutiny of the CPAS system. Belgian Prime Minister Bart De Wever made controversial statements about CPAS in March 2026 that were denounced as stigmatizing by CPAS federations, as reported by [La Libre Belgique](https://www.lalibre.be/belgique/societe/2026/03/19/les-cpas-denoncent-des-cliches-racoleurs-prononces-par-bart-de-wever-ces-propos-ont-profondement-choque-les-travailleurs-2EZES4AZ35HTVBQYQPMAZHMZNI/). Meanwhile, a separate high-profile scandal involving dysfunction and alleged fraud at the CPAS of Anderlecht in Brussels is currently under investigation by the Belgian Parliament.

Adding to the pressure, Belgium's 2026 unemployment reform — which began excluding job seekers after two years of benefits from January 2026 — has pushed thousands of newly excluded individuals toward CPAS centers, as detailed by [RTBF](https://www.rtbf.be/article/allocations-de-chomage-et-revenu-d-integration-sociale-cpas-quels-sont-les-montants-et-leurs-differences-11542850). The reform places the system under unprecedented strain as it struggles to balance accessibility with effective fraud prevention.

The fact that the defendant received a community service sentence rather than imprisonment reflects the Belgian legal system's preference for alternative sentencing, but the case has fueled public anger over perceived abuse of the social safety net.

## What's Next

The case leaves several questions unanswered. What specific forged documents did the man use to conceal his inheritance? How long exactly had he been receiving benefits? And crucially, whether the CPAS of Verviers will face any internal consequences for failing to detect the fraud over many years remains unclear. The case serves as a stark illustration of the challenges facing Belgian authorities as they seek to tighten welfare oversight while maintaining the system's fundamental mission of providing dignity and support to those in genuine need.
