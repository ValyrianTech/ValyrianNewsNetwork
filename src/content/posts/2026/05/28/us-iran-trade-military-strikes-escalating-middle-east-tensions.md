# U.S. and Iran Trade Strikes, Escalating Middle East Tensions

The United States and Iran exchanged military strikes on Thursday, significantly escalating hostilities in the Middle East and threatening to derail already fragile diplomatic negotiations aimed at ending the three-month-old war. Oil prices surged as markets reacted to the renewed conflict, with West Texas Intermediate crude rising approximately 3.78% to trade around $92 per barrel.

The exchange of fire marks the most serious breach of the temporary ceasefire agreed on April 8, 2026, and comes as both sides pursue dueling strategies of military retaliation and diplomatic outreach.

## The Strikes

According to [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2026/may/28/us-new-strikes-iran-israel-intensifies-attacks-southern-lebanon), U.S. Central Command forces shot down four Iranian one-way attack drones that posed a threat near the Strait of Hormuz. The U.S. also struck a ground control station in Iran's Bandar Abbas that was about to launch a fifth drone. A U.S. official described the actions as "measured, purely defensive, and intended to maintain the ceasefire."

In retaliation, Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) announced it had launched an attack on an "American airbase" in Kuwait. [CENTCOM reported](https://www.theguardian.com/world/live/2026/may/28/middle-east-crisis-trump-us-iran-war-lebanon-israel-strikes-latest-news-live) that Iran launched a ballistic missile toward Kuwait that was successfully intercepted by Kuwaiti forces, calling it an "egregious ceasefire violation." The IRGC warned of "more decisive" action should U.S. "aggression" continue.

## Regional Condemnation

Kuwait's foreign ministry issued a statement expressing its "strongest condemnation and denunciation of the criminal Iranian attacks that targeted the territory of the state of Kuwait with missiles and drones, in a dangerous escalation." The ministry noted that the escalation "comes at a time when earnest efforts are being exerted by a number of brotherly and friendly countries to reduce tensions," warning that the attacks "undermines diplomatic efforts."

The United Arab Emirates also condemned the attacks "in the strongest terms," describing them as "a flagrant violation of the sovereignty of Kuwait and a threat to its security and stability."

## Diplomatic Developments

Amid the military escalation, [Donald Trump circulated a draft peace agreement](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2026/may/28/donald-trump-shares-draft-iran-peace-agreement-with-israel-and-other-allies) for the war with Iran among allies including Israel. The draft proposes opening the Strait of Hormuz to commercial shipping, lifting the U.S. blockade of Iranian ports, and giving Iran access to up to $12 billion in frozen assets. The aim would be for commercial shipping to return to pre-war levels within 30 days, followed by negotiations on Iran's nuclear program lasting up to 60 days.

According to U.S. media reports, the U.S. and Iran have reached an agreement on a 60-day memorandum of understanding to extend the ceasefire and launch negotiations, but Trump has not yet given final approval, saying he needs "a few more days to think about it."

Pakistan's Foreign Minister Mohammad Ishaq Dar is set to fly to Washington to meet U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio as part of ongoing mediation efforts. China is pressing for the UN Security Council to ratify any agreement.

## The Strait of Hormuz Crisis

A central flashpoint remains the Strait of Hormuz, through which approximately 20% of the world's oil passes. Iran has asserted control over the waterway, requiring vessels to seek permission to pass. The IRGC navy stated that 26 commercial ships and oil tankers had been given permission in the past 24 hours, warning that "seeking permission is mandatory."

U.S. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent announced sanctions on Iran's Persian Gulf Strait Authority (PGSA) and threatened to shut down Iranian airlines' access to landing spots, refueling, and ticket sales. Bessent also warned Oman against facilitating any tolling system in the strait, stating the Treasury will "aggressively target any actors involved."

This follows Trump's threat on Wednesday to "blow up" Oman if it entered an agreement with Iran to manage shipping in the strait. Senior diplomats in Oman were reportedly shocked and furious at the threat.

## Iran's Position

At a UN Security Council meeting, Iranian Ambassador Amir-Saeid Iravani defended Iran's actions, stating that "Iran's actions are lawful and consistent with international law" and that Iran "could not allow such a critical waterway to be used as a corridor for hostile action."

Iranian officials have been careful to frame their actions as legitimate responses to U.S. ceasefire violations rather than an abandonment of the truce, suggesting Tehran is attempting to maintain diplomatic channels even as it retaliates militarily.

## Broader Regional Conflict

The U.S.-Iran exchange is unfolding against a backdrop of wider regional escalation. Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu ordered the IDF to occupy 70% of Gaza, up from 50%, and the IDF carried out a strike in Beirut targeting an IRGC-linked commander. Israel also broke all contact with UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres after the UN blacklisted Israel over allegations of sexual violence in conflict zones.

## Economic Implications

Oil prices remain highly volatile, with WTI crude trading around $92 per barrel. The three-month disruption to Strait of Hormuz shipping has caused the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market, with disruptions also affecting natural gas, fertilizer, aviation, and tourism industries.

## What's Next

The coming days will be critical. Pakistan's mediation efforts, Trump's final decision on the 60-day MOU, and the response from Iran's leadership will determine whether the fragile ceasefire can be salvaged or whether the region slides into a broader, more destructive conflict. The draft peace agreement faces multiple obstacles, including Israeli opposition to its deferral of firm nuclear commitments and the unresolved dispute over control of the Strait of Hormuz.
