# Virginia Bus Crash Kills 5, Injures 34 on Interstate 95

A passenger bus plowed into six vehicles on Interstate 95 in Stafford County, Virginia, early Friday morning, killing five people and sending 34 others to hospitals, according to [Virginia State Police](https://apnews.com/article/virginia-bus-crash-interstate-95-69a5cfdcfc5af71318422152365ad96e). The crash occurred at approximately 2:35 a.m. ET as traffic was slowing for an upcoming work zone on the southbound lanes near mile marker 146, close to the Quantico Marine Corps Base.

## The Crash

The preliminary investigation indicates that the bus failed to slow for traffic approaching a work zone and struck six vehicles. All five fatalities were occupants of the vehicles hit by the bus, police said. Three of the 34 injured remain in critical condition.

"The preliminary investigation indicates that traffic was slowing southbound for an upcoming work zone. A bus failed to slow for traffic and struck six vehicles," Virginia State Police said in a statement. It was not immediately known what the bus was being used for or how many people were aboard.

## Emergency Response

Mary Washington Healthcare received 19 patients from the crash scene. Seven were taken to the trauma center at Mary Washington Hospital in Fredericksburg, where five have been treated and discharged and two remain in critical condition. Twelve patients were taken to Stafford Hospital, all of whom have since been discharged in overall good condition, [according to the health system](https://www.wdbj7.com/2026/05/29/multiple-people-killed-dozens-injured-i-95-bus-crash/).

"All patients will continue to be cared for at both hospitals with meals and personal needs until they can be reunited with their belongings or other transportation can be arranged," Mary Washington Healthcare said in a statement.

## Investigation Underway

The Virginia State Police are leading the investigation, and charges are pending. Authorities have not yet released the identities of the five deceased victims, the bus driver, or the company operating the bus. The cause of the crash — whether driver fatigue, mechanical failure, distraction, or another factor — remains under investigation.

The National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) has deployed a "go-team" to conduct a parallel safety investigation into the crash, [CBS News reported](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/bus-crash-virginia-deaths-interstate-95/). The NTSB typically investigates major transportation accidents to determine probable cause and issue safety recommendations that can influence industry practices nationwide.

## Traffic Disruption

The crash caused significant disruption on the busy East Coast corridor. The southbound left shoulder and left lane remained closed through late Friday morning, with traffic backed up for approximately eight miles (12.8 kilometers), according to the Virginia Department of Transportation. Two additional crashes were reported in the vicinity of the original incident, compounding the delays for commuters and commercial traffic on one of America's most heavily traveled highways.

## Broader Context

Interstate 95 is a major north-south artery running along the East Coast, and the section through Stafford County is heavily traveled by commuters, long-haul truckers, and travelers. Work zone safety has been a persistent concern on Virginia highways, with the Virginia Department of Transportation implementing various measures including speed reduction signs, automated enforcement, and increased patrols.

While bus crashes on I-95 are not common, they tend to result in severe casualties due to the size and weight of buses relative to passenger vehicles. The NTSB's involvement signals the severity of Friday's crash and may lead to safety recommendations regarding bus driver fatigue management, work zone signage, or vehicle braking systems.

## What's Next

The investigation continues on multiple fronts. The Virginia State Police are pursuing potential criminal charges, while the NTSB will conduct a separate safety probe that could produce recommendations for preventing similar tragedies. Families of the deceased and injured parties may pursue civil litigation against the bus company, the driver, or other potentially responsible parties as the legal process unfolds.

This crash could also prompt renewed discussions about bus safety regulations, driver hours-of-service rules, and work zone safety protocols in Virginia and beyond. As investigators work to determine exactly what went wrong, the five lives lost serve as a stark reminder of the risks on America's highways.
