# Six Arrested, Four Officers Injured as Standard-Charleroi Violence Erupts

Violence erupted at the Stade de Sclessin in Liège on Saturday evening after the Belgian Pro League match between Standard Liège and Sporting Charleroi, leaving four police officers with minor injuries and resulting in six arrests. The clashes, which involved fans invading the pitch and throwing fireworks at players and each other, have reignited concerns about football-related violence in Belgium and prompted an investigation by the Belgian Football Association's disciplinary body.

According to [Het Laatste Nieuws](https://www.hln.be/belgisch-voetbal/vier-politieagenten-lichtgewond-en-zes-mensen-aangehouden-de-trieste-balans-nadat-hooligans-van-standard-en-charleroi-slaags-raken-met-spelers-en-elkaar~ad21dd47/), the trouble began after the final whistle when Charleroi goalkeeper Mohamed Koné provoked Standard supporters by waving a Charleroi supporters' flag and scarf. Multiple Standard fans — some wearing balaclavas — invaded the pitch to confront Koné, triggering a chain reaction of violence.

## How the Violence Unfolded

Charleroi had just secured a 2-0 victory in the Europe Play-offs, with goals from Jakob Napoleon Romsaas and Bernier, severely damaging Standard's hopes of European qualification. The tension of the high-stakes "Choc Wallon" (Walloon Derby) — played before a near-sell-out crowd — boiled over moments after the match ended.

As [Voetbalbelgie.be](https://www.voetbalbelgie.be/artikel/incidenten-standard-charleroi-na-provocatie-keeper) reported, Koné "laughed at the Standard fans, waving a scarf of Sporting Charleroi" and continued despite being called to order. The situation escalated rapidly when Charleroi fans broke out of their designated section to confront Standard supporters, with fireworks and flares thrown between both groups.

Sporting directors Marc Wilmots (Standard) and Nicolas Frutos (Charleroi) intervened in an attempt to calm the situation. The unrest continued outside the stadium, with fireworks thrown from Standard fans toward Charleroi supporters. Visiting supporter buses were only able to depart one and a half hours after the final whistle.

## Investigation Launched

The Bondsparket — the Belgian FA's disciplinary body — has opened an investigation targeting both clubs and all individuals involved. Ebe Verhaegen, the Belgian FA Prosecutor, confirmed that the investigation "is aimed not only at both clubs but also targets all involved (players, staff members, ...) who found it necessary to display provocative behavior and/or participate in the incidents." The Bondsparket has until Saturday to initiate disciplinary proceedings.

Supporters who invaded the pitch risk stadium bans and significant fines. Future "Choc Wallon" matches between Standard and Charleroi may be played without away fans.

## Government Condemnation and Legislative Response

Belgian Interior Minister Bernard Quintin condemned the events as "a disgrace for Belgian football." In a statement on Sunday, Quintin said those responsible must be identified and punished by their clubs and the authorities, including the judiciary.

"To combat such behavior, the federal government is tightening sanctions and extending the duration of stadium bans," Quintin said, reiterating his commitment to reforming Belgian football legislation. The minister has been working on the so-called "Quintin law" targeting dangerous associations, though the proposal was initially rejected by the Council of State and is being revised.

## A Pattern of Escalating Violence

Saturday's incident is the latest in a troubling series of football-related disorders in Belgium. Just nine days earlier, the Croky Cup Final between Anderlecht and Union Saint-Gilloise at the King Baudouin Stadium in Brussels saw flares thrown from the stands, nearly 300 seats destroyed, and a fire started in the Anderlecht section, as [L'Européenne de Bruxelles](https://leuropeennedebruxelles.com/020515/stadium-damage-flares-and-blaze-at-brussels-football-derby-raise-important-security-questions.html) reported.

Furthermore, the rivalry between Standard and Charleroi has a documented history of violence. In January 2026, 39 Charleroi supporters were arrested before the reverse fixture for attempting to attack Standard fan buses with pyrotechnics, as [Voetbalkrant.com](https://www.voetbalkrant.com/nieuws/2026-01-20/charleroi-supporters-probeerden-bussen-standard-fans-aan-te-vallen-voor-waalse-derby) reported.

## What's Next

The Bondsparket investigation will determine whether clubs face financial penalties, points deductions, or other structural sanctions. Mohamed Koné's provocative actions may also result in disciplinary action from the football authorities. The Belgian government's promised legislative reforms are expected to accelerate, with stricter sanctions and longer stadium bans on the horizon. The broader question remains whether these measures will be sufficient to address the systemic issues of stadium security and fan culture that continue to plague Belgian football.
