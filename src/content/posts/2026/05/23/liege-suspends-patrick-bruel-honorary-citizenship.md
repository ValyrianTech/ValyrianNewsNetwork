# Liège Suspends Patrick Bruel's Honorary Citizenship Amid Allegations

The Belgian city of Liège has temporarily stripped French singer and actor Patrick Bruel of his honorary citizenship title, responding to a wave of sexual assault and rape allegations that have emerged against the artist across multiple countries. Mayor Willy Demeyer announced the suspension on Friday, May 22, following a meeting of the city's College, emphasizing that the decision respects the presumption of innocence while acknowledging the gravity of the accusations.

## Context

Patrick Bruel, 67, was named an honorary citizen ("citoyen d'honneur") of Liège on November 6, 2022, during the Festival International du Film de Comédie de Liège (FIFCL). The honor recognized a deep, decades-long connection between the French star and the Belgian city — a relationship that began with a legendary concert at the Forum in Liège on May 17, 1990, when Bruel performed his new song "J'te l'dis quand même" alone at the piano to a spellbound audience. A commemorative plaque was inaugurated on Rue Pont d'Avroy to mark the occasion, as [reported by DH/Les Sports+](https://www.dhnet.be/regions/liege/2022/11/07/patrick-bruel-est-citoyen-dhonneur-de-la-ville-de-liege-G52I6HQK55EHNHNXABLMCF3CTQ/) at the time.

## Key Developments

The suspension was requested by the DéFi party in the Province of Liège, which sent an open letter to city authorities calling for the temporary withdrawal of the title "while justice does its work." According to [RTBF](https://www.rtbf.be/article/patrick-bruel-est-destitue-temporairement-de-son-titre-de-citoyen-d-honneur-a-liege-11728038), the party argued: "When a city publicly honors a person facing serious accusations, it sends a political signal. What coherence between the values displayed by Liège and the symbols it chooses?"

Mayor Demeyer, who made the decision in agreement with the College's PS-MR-Engagés coalition, stressed the delicate balance involved. "I made the decision to suspend this honorary citizenship status pending the evolution of the case and in respect of the presumption of innocence," he told RTBF. "Depending on how things evolve, we will see what happens." He added that it was "obviously difficult because, on one hand, we must respect the presumption of innocence and, on the other hand, we must take into account public opinion movements and the elements that have been brought to our attention."

[RTL Info](https://www.rtl.be/actu/regions/liege/liege-suspend-la-citoyennete-dhonneur-de-patrick-bruel/2026-05-22/article/789410) reported that Demeyer emphasized the city's longstanding commitment to both respect for women and the presumption of innocence. The College also voted on May 18 to adopt a "Charter of Respect for Values in the Cultural Domain," which will now be extended to future honorary citizens of Liège — institutionalizing ethical vetting for such honors.

The scale of allegations against Bruel is substantial. According to [RFI](https://www.rfi.fr/fr/france/20260520-accusations-de-viol-et-d-agressions-sexuelles-la-tourn%C3%A9e-de-patrick-bruel-sous-pression), approximately 30 testimonies and 12 formal complaints for rape and sexual assault have been filed against the artist in France and Belgium. Judicial investigations are ongoing in Paris, Saint-Malo, Brussels, and elsewhere. The most recent high-profile complaint came from French TV host Flavie Flament on May 15, who alleges Bruel raped her when she was 16. Other complainants include an actress, a journalist, and Daniela Elstner, director of UniFrance.

Bruel contests all allegations and continues to perform. His international tour, scheduled to begin in mid-June, has already faced cancellations in Quebec, and feminist collectives are planning boycotts across France, Belgium, Switzerland, and Canada.

## Analysis

The Liège decision represents a significant symbolic act, marking the first time the city has faced such a situation with an honorary citizen. The suspension — rather than permanent revocation — serves as a compromise, allowing the municipality to distance itself from Bruel without prejudging judicial outcomes. It also sets a precedent: the extension of the new values charter to future honorary citizens suggests Liège is formalizing a vetting process to prevent similar controversies.

The DéFi party framed the issue as one of institutional coherence, arguing that a city's honors must align with its stated values. Feminist activists, while welcoming the suspension, argue it is an insufficient step and call for Bruel's concerts to be canceled as well, pointing to broader patterns of delayed justice in the French-speaking world's #MeToo reckoning.

## What's Next

The suspension remains temporary, pending the evolution of the legal cases against Bruel. If judicial proceedings result in convictions, a permanent revocation is likely; if the allegations are disproven, the title could be restored. Meanwhile, Bruel's tour faces mounting pressure, and other municipalities may follow Liège's example. The newly adopted Charter of Values will shape how Liège approaches cultural honors going forward, potentially creating a template for other cities navigating similar dilemmas.
