# Man Arrested After Car Hits Seven Pedestrians in Derby

A 36-year-old man has been arrested on suspicion of attempted murder after a car struck and injured seven pedestrians in Derby city centre on Saturday evening. According to [BBC News](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn78v1yg7p1o), the incident occurred on Friar Gate, a busy street popular with students and filled with restaurants and bars.

Derbyshire Police confirmed that a black Suzuki Swift was driven into pedestrians, resulting in serious but non-life-threatening injuries to all seven victims. Counter-terrorism police have joined the investigation, though authorities emphasized this is "common practice for an incident of this nature" and officers are "keeping an open mind about the potential motives." The incident is not currently being treated as terrorism.

## Swift Police Response and Arrest

Police arrived at the scene within seconds after the collision, according to Chief Superintendent Emma Aldred, Area Commander for Derby. The suspect was arrested seven minutes later on a bridge crossing towards Pride Park, approximately half a mile from the incident location.

"This was a horrific incident that has understandably shocked the city," Aldred stated during a press conference at the scene on Sunday. "While none of those injured in the incident have suffered life-threatening physical injuries, the impact on them, I am sure, is just beginning to be felt."

The arrested individual, described as a Derby man originally from India who has lived in the UK for "a number of years," remains in police custody. He is also being held on suspicion of causing serious injury through dangerous driving, inflicting grievous bodily harm with intent, and dangerous driving.

## Witness Accounts of Chaotic Scene

Eyewitnesses described a chaotic scene following the collision. Mark Lucas, 47, who lives near Friar Gate, told the BBC he saw people lying injured on the road. "I ended up coming down with my son and I realised it was a big incident," he said. "People were over the floor and there were a lot of police and ambulances. No-one really knew what actually happened. We didn't know the car had run people over."

Shop worker Eren Celebi, 23, described hearing "screaming and shouting" after the collision. "I didn't see it exactly happen. I was serving some customers and I just heard a lot of screaming and shouting outside," he said. When he left his shop, he saw people lying on the pavement and main road, with some individuals running into shops while others tried to call for help.

## Emergency Services Response and Victim Care

The East Midlands Ambulance Service deployed three ambulance cars, seven ambulances, and a Hazardous Area Response Team to the scene. Victims were transported to Royal Derby Hospital and Queen's Medical Centre in Nottingham for treatment.

Police confirmed that "contrary to online speculation, no-one died in the incident" and emphasized there was no ongoing risk to the public. The road closure on Friar Gate, which stretched from the junction with Curzon Street/Cheapside up to Ford Street, was lifted in the afternoon on Sunday after forensic officers completed their initial evidence collection.

## Political and Community Response

Local political figures expressed shock at the incident in what is generally considered a safe city. Catherine Atkinson, Labour MP for Derby North, visited the scene and told the BBC: "Like many I remain really shocked. You don't expect this to happen in our city. I think that everyone is utterly stunned by what has happened."

Atkinson indicated she would raise the subject of cars being used as weapons in Parliament. "I am concerned and I will be raising this with government," she said. "We need to see if guidelines will be reviewed because we are seeing an increased amount of incidents where cars are being used as weapons."

Education Secretary Bridget Phillipson, speaking on BBC's Sunday with Laura Kuenssberg, said: "My thoughts are with anyone that's been affected by this, those who've been injured or hurt, and their family members at this really difficult time. The police are actively investigating. We don't believe there is wider cause for the public to be alarmed."

## Community Healing and Religious Support

The incident has prompted an outpouring of community support in Derby. Flowers were left at the scene on Friar Gate, and prayers were held at Derby Cathedral and churches across the city for the victims.

Rt Rev Malcolm Macnaughton, Bishop of Repton, said the incident had "shaken us all." "We like to think of our city as a safe place for everyone," he stated. "Our prayers... are with those injured and with all those who witnessed what happened. We pray also for those receiving treatment in hospital. May God bring peace and healing to our city."

Local businesses were also affected, with O'Dwyers Bar on Friar Gate announcing it would remain closed on Sunday. In a social media statement, the bar said: "We are deeply saddened by the horrific incident that took place on Friar Gate last night. Our thoughts are with all those injured, their families, and everyone impacted by what has happened. No-one should ever have to experience something like this on a night out in our city. Derby is a community, and today we stand together."

## Investigation Continues with Open Mind

Derbyshire Police continue to question the suspect while forensic officers examine the scene. The involvement of counter-terrorism specialists reflects standard protocol for serious incidents of this nature in the UK, though authorities maintain they have not classified the event as terrorism-related.

Police noted that releasing the ethnicity and nationality of suspects in high-profile cases follows National Police Chiefs' Council guidelines aimed at "reducing the risk to public safety where there are high levels of misinformation about an incident."

As the investigation progresses, the focus remains on supporting the seven injured victims and understanding the circumstances that led to Saturday evening's shocking events in Derby city centre.
