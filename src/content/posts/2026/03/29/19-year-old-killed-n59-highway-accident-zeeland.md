# 19-year-old woman killed in serious accident on N59 highway in Zeeland

A 19-year-old woman from Aagtekerke, Zeeland, died on Saturday evening in a serious chain collision involving three vehicles on the N59 highway between Zierikzee and Nieuwerkerk. Three other individuals were injured and transported to hospitals, with one requiring airlift by trauma helicopter, according to [NU.nl](https://www.nu.nl/binnenland/6390823/vrouw-19-omgekomen-bij-ernstig-ongeval-in-zeeland-drie-anderen-gewond.html).

## Incident details and emergency response

The accident occurred around 8:30 in the evening on March 28, 2026, on a segment of the N59 highway that has been identified in provincial traffic safety plans as having "insufficient traffic safety." Firefighters had to free four trapped individuals from the wreckage before they could be transferred to ambulance services for medical treatment.

Emergency services deployed a multi-agency response including police, fire department, ambulance services, and a trauma helicopter. The three injured victims were transported to different hospitals based on the severity of their injuries, though the exact conditions of those injured remain unclear.

The 19-year-old victim, who was from the Zeeland village of Aagtekerke with approximately 1,500 residents, died at the scene. Her identity has not been publicly released pending notification of family members.

## Road closure and traffic violations

Following the accident, authorities completely closed the N59 in both directions between Zierikzee and Nieuwerkerk to facilitate rescue operations and investigation. However, police reported that some drivers ignored the closure barriers and drove through the accident scene, creating additional safety risks for emergency personnel and cleanup crews.

In a statement via the Dutch public broadcaster NOS, police explicitly warned against such behavior: "Dat is NIET de bedoeling, men moet gewoon omrijden" ("That is NOT the intention, people should just take a detour"). Violating road closures during accidents can result in fines and legal consequences, in addition to endangering emergency responders.

## Investigation and historical safety context

Police are continuing their investigation into the cause of the chain collision, which remains unclear. The investigation was expected to continue through the night and possibly into the following days.

The accident occurred on a highway segment that has been the focus of safety concerns. According to provincial assessments, the N59 between Zierikzee and Bruinisse has relatively many accidents compared to its traffic volume. In October 2025, Rijkswaterstaat (the Dutch highway authority) installed raised center barriers on the N59 near Zierikzee and Nieuwerkerk specifically to reduce frontal collisions.

Both the N59 and nearby N57 highways have been experiencing increasing truck traffic, contributing to congestion and safety concerns in the region. The N59 receives low safety scores in provincial assessments, and various safety measures and infrastructure projects are planned or underway for the N57-N59 corridor.

## Geographic and community impact

Zeeland is a southwestern province of the Netherlands consisting of several islands and peninsulas, with a population of approximately 380,000. The N59 highway serves as an important regional route connecting communities throughout the province.

The tragedy represents a significant loss for the small, close-knit community of Aagtekerke and the broader Zeeland region. Local news coverage from regional outlets like PZC (Provinciale Zeeuwse Courant) provided more detailed accounts of the rescue operation than national news sources, highlighting the importance of regional journalism in covering local incidents.

## Emergency response analysis

The coordinated response demonstrated effective multi-agency collaboration:

1. **Police** managed traffic control, conducted the investigation, and handled public communications
2. **Fire department** performed technical rescue operations to free trapped victims
3. **Ambulance services** provided medical treatment and coordinated hospital transport
4. **Trauma helicopter** enabled critical care transport for the most severely injured patient

This incident highlights both the capabilities of Dutch emergency services and the challenges they face when public compliance with safety measures is lacking.
