# Kremlin: US-Israeli Iran Attack Has Devastating Global Consequences

Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov has issued stark warnings about the devastating consequences of the US-Israeli military operation against Iran, stating that the conflict has already caused severe damage to the world economy and regional stability while making former US President Donald Trump's proposed "Peace Council" initiative appear less relevant. According to [Kommersant](https://www.kommersant.ru/doc/8550309), Peskov emphasized that Russia is taking a cautious approach, waiting to see how the conflict unfolds before making any diplomatic commitments.

## Context: Trump's Peace Council Initiative

The "Peace Council" (also referred to as the "Board of Peace") was launched by former US President Donald Trump with a charter signing ceremony in Davos on January 22, 2026. Initially backed by 19 countries and later expanded to 26 states, the organization was ostensibly created to manage the Gaza sector following a peaceful settlement of the Israel-Hamas conflict. However, as Kommersant notes, the document doesn't explicitly mention Gaza, suggesting broader global ambitions for the initiative.

Russian President Vladimir Putin had been "studying every detail of the offer" according to earlier international reports, with the Kremlin expressing interest in the proposal while maintaining a characteristically cautious stance. The initiative represented one of Trump's signature foreign policy efforts during his second term, aiming to position the United States as a mediator in Middle Eastern conflicts.

## Peskov's Grave Assessment

In statements to both Kommersant and [TASS](https://tass.ru/ekonomika/26926099), Peskov delivered a sobering assessment of the ongoing conflict's impact. "We see extremely devastating consequences of this war for the world economy, for regional geopolitics," Peskov told TASS. "These consequences probably won't be short-term, they will have long-term effect."

The Kremlin spokesman's comments come amid escalating violence that began on February 28 when the United States and Israel launched a military operation against Iran. According to TASS, strikes hit major Iranian cities including Tehran, with the White House justifying the attack by citing missile and nuclear threats allegedly emanating from Tehran.

Iran's Revolutionary Guard Corps announced a large-scale retaliatory operation, attacking targets in Israel and striking objects in Bahrain, Jordan, Qatar, Kuwait, the United Arab Emirates, and Saudi Arabia. The conflict has reportedly resulted in the death of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei and other key Iranian leadership figures, marking one of the most significant escalations in the region in decades.

## Peace Council Now "Less Relevant"

Peskov directly linked the conflict's outbreak to the diminished relevance of Trump's Peace Council initiative. "While war continues in the Middle East, the 'Peace Council' created on the initiative of US President Donald Trump is less relevant," he stated, as reported by Kommersant.

When asked about Russia's potential participation in the Peace Council, Peskov responded: "We probably need to wait and see how this all ends." He added that President Putin has not yet made a decision about joining the organization, reflecting Moscow's wait-and-see approach to the rapidly evolving situation.

## Russia's Position and Regional Implications

Russia has consistently condemned the US-Israeli attacks as "an egregious violation of international law that led to heavy, tragic consequences for the entire Iranian people," according to TASS. This position aligns with Moscow's strategic partnership with Tehran, which has deepened in recent years through military cooperation, economic ties, and diplomatic coordination.

Peskov's warnings about long-term economic consequences come amid concerns about global energy markets, regional stability, and the potential for broader conflict escalation. The involvement of multiple Gulf states in the conflict—with targets struck across Bahrain, Jordan, Qatar, Kuwait, UAE, and Saudi Arabia—suggests the violence could destabilize the entire Middle East.

"Now we just need to be patient and watch what exactly the consequences will be," Peskov advised, emphasizing the need for caution in assessing the full impact of the conflict.

## Analysis: Strategic Implications and Global Economy

The Kremlin's statements reflect several strategic calculations. First, Russia positions itself as a responsible global actor concerned with stability while avoiding direct military involvement. Second, Moscow maintains its partnership with Iran—a key regional ally—while carefully managing relations with other Middle Eastern powers.

Economically, Peskov's warnings about "devastating consequences" for the world economy likely reference potential disruptions to global energy markets, increased military spending, and broader geopolitical uncertainty that could dampen international trade and investment. Russia, as a major energy exporter, has particular interest in stable oil and gas markets, though price volatility could offer both risks and opportunities.

Diplomatically, the conflict represents a setback for Trump's foreign policy agenda and complicates Russia's engagement with various Middle Eastern actors. The Peace Council initiative, which might have provided a platform for Russian diplomatic influence, now appears stalled by the very conflicts it was meant to address.

## What's Next: Russia's Cautious Path Forward

Russia's approach will likely remain cautious and pragmatic. The Kremlin will continue to condemn the attacks while avoiding actions that could directly embroil Russia in the conflict. Moscow may explore diplomatic channels to de-escalate tensions, potentially positioning itself as a mediator—a role it has played in other regional conflicts.

The future of the Peace Council initiative remains uncertain. Should the conflict de-escalate, Russia might reconsider participation, particularly if the organization's mandate expands beyond Gaza to address broader regional stability issues. However, with Peskov's assessment that the council is now "less relevant," any Russian engagement would likely require significant reformulation of the initiative's purpose and structure.

As the conflict continues to unfold, the international community will watch closely how major powers like Russia navigate the complex geopolitical landscape. Peskov's warnings serve as a reminder that the consequences of the US-Israeli operation against Iran extend far beyond immediate military outcomes, potentially reshaping Middle Eastern geopolitics and the global economic order for years to come.
