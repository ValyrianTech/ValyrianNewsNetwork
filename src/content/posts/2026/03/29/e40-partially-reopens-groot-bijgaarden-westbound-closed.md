# E40 Highway Partially Reopens in Groot-Bijgaarden, Westbound Closure Remains

Traffic authorities have partially reopened the E40 highway between Brussels and Aalst after earlier closures, with only the westbound lanes toward Ghent and Oostende remaining fully closed overnight on Sunday, March 29. The [Agentschap Wegen en Verkeer (AWV)](https://wegenenverkeer.be/nieuws/groot-bijgaarden-vanaf-zaterdagavond-28-tot-maandagochtend-30-maart-forse-hinder-op-e40) announced the adjustment following smoother-than-expected progress during Saturday night's infrastructure works. In the eastbound direction toward Brussels, two lanes remain open to traffic.

## Context: Critical Infrastructure Maintenance

The E40 is one of Belgium's most vital east-west transportation corridors, connecting the capital Brussels with major cities like Ghent, Bruges, and the coastal region. The Groot-Bijgaarden interchange represents a critical junction where the E40 meets the Brussels outer ring (R0/E19), handling approximately 62,000 vehicles per direction on an average weekend day.

The current closure facilitates three simultaneous infrastructure projects—a strategic "minderhinder" (less nuisance) approach where multiple necessary works are bundled to reduce overall disruption time. This coordinated effort includes the placement of six bridge girders for a new bridge in Dilbeek, renewal of bridge joints in the connecting ramp from Brussels outer ring to E40 toward Ghent, and application of a new asphalt layer over 1.6 kilometers on the E40 toward Ghent within the interchange.

## Key Developments and Schedule Adjustments

According to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/03/29/e40-in-groot-bijgaarden-zondagnacht-enkel-nog-afgesloten-in-rich/), the Saturday night works progressed more smoothly than anticipated, allowing authorities to reduce disruption for the final night of operations. "Doordat de werken afgelopen nacht vlotter dan verwacht verlopen zijn, kan de hinder komende nacht beperkt worden," the agency reported, translating to "Because the works last night went more smoothly than expected, the nuisance tonight can be limited."

The updated closure schedule now includes an earlier start time for the westbound closure. Instead of the originally planned 10 PM closure, the westbound lanes toward Oostende will close at 9 PM on Sunday. The Flemish Traffic Center continues to advise motorists to avoid the wider area if possible and check real-time traffic information before traveling.

For those who must travel through the region, official detours route traffic via the Brussels ring (R0) and Ninoofsesteenweg (N8) through Ninove and Denderhoutem back to the E40 at Aalst. Alternative recommendations include taking the E313 via Antwerp for traffic from Hasselt to Ghent, or using the R0 and A12 for Brussels to Ghent travel.

## Analysis: Responsive Traffic Management

The partial reopening demonstrates responsive traffic management based on real-time progress assessment—a notable shift from rigid planning to adaptive operations. This flexibility benefits both commuters and the regional economy by minimizing unnecessary disruption while ensuring essential infrastructure maintenance continues.

The bundling of three major projects represents significant investment in Belgium's transportation infrastructure. While causing short-term inconvenience, this approach aims to reduce long-term cumulative impact on this vital corridor. The coordination requires meticulous planning to ensure worker safety, motorist safety, and technical feasibility across different contractors and project timelines.

Public communication has been consistent across sources, with [HLN](https://www.hln.be/dilbeek/e40-in-groot-bijgaarden-zondagnacht-enkel-nog-afgesloten-in-richting-van-oostende~a01acfdb/) reporting similar factual information regarding closure times and traffic advice. This consistency helps prevent confusion among motorists and ensures public safety messages reach broad audiences.

## What's Next and Conclusion

If remaining works proceed according to plan, the E40 is scheduled to fully reopen by 5 AM on Monday, March 30. The connecting ramp from the Brussels outer ring to the E40 toward Ghent/Oostende will also reopen at that time, restoring normal traffic flow through this critical transportation hub.

The successful adjustment of closure plans based on actual work progress sets a positive precedent for future infrastructure projects. It demonstrates that with careful monitoring and responsive decision-making, authorities can balance necessary maintenance with minimizing public inconvenience—a crucial consideration for maintaining public support for essential infrastructure investments.

Motorists planning Sunday evening travel toward Ghent or Oostende are strongly advised to consult the [Flemish Traffic Center website](http://www.verkeerscentrum.be) for the latest route advice and consider train travel as an alternative where possible. The temporary disruption, while significant, represents short-term investment in long-term transportation reliability for one of Belgium's busiest corridors.
