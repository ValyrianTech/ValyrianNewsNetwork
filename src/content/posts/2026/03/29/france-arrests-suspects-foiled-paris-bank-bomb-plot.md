# France Arrests Two More Suspects in Foiled Paris Bank Bomb Plot

**PARIS** — French anti-terrorism prosecutors announced on Sunday the arrest of two additional suspects in connection with a foiled bomb attack targeting Bank of America's Paris headquarters, expanding an investigation that has raised concerns about proxy operations targeting U.S. interests across Europe.

The latest detentions bring to three the number of individuals in custody after French police intercepted a suspect in the early hours of Saturday morning as he attempted to ignite a homemade explosive device outside the bank's offices in the capital's upscale 8th arrondissement, just streets from the Champs-Élysées.

## The Intercepted Attack

According to statements from Interior Minister Laurent Nunez, a police patrol assigned to protecting sensitive sites spotted two individuals placing and attempting to ignite an improvised explosive device at approximately 3:30 a.m. (0130 GMT) on March 28. Officers moved in immediately, arresting one suspect at the scene while the second fled, though both were subsequently apprehended according to [France 24 reports](https://www.france24.com/en/france/20260329-two-new-suspects-arrested-over-foiled-bank-of-america-bomb-plot-in-paris).

"One individual was trying to light an improvised explosive device made with a container likely containing hydrocarbons and a crude ignition system, while another was filming," Nunez told BFM TV on Saturday evening. He described the device as rudimentary but potentially lethal, crediting officers with preventing its ignition.

The anti-terrorism prosecutor's office confirmed it had taken over the investigation, examining "attempted damage by fire or other dangerous means in connection with a terrorist undertaking" and a "terrorist criminal conspiracy." Both the Paris judicial police and France's domestic intelligence service, the General Directorate for Internal Security (DGSI), are involved in the probe.

## Recruitment via Social Media

Investigators have uncovered disturbing details about the plot's origins. According to a police source cited by France 24, the initial suspect claimed he had been recruited through the Snapchat messaging app to carry out the bombing in exchange for 600 euros (approximately $692). The suspect was allegedly moments from igniting the five-liter fuel-based device with a lighter when officers intervened.

A separate police source told AFP that an accomplice appeared to be documenting the crime, stepping back during the placement of the explosive "apparently to take a photo or video of the crime with his mobile phone."

## Expanding Investigation

The [U.S. News & World Report](https://www.usnews.com/news/world/articles/2026-03-29/france-detains-two-more-suspects-over-foiled-paris-bank-of-america-attack) detailed that the two new suspects were taken into police custody on Saturday night as part of the investigation launched on March 28. The custody of the first suspect, identified as a minor, has been extended. Under French counter-terrorism laws, suspects can be held for up to 96 hours without charge, with further extensions possible under judicial oversight.

In a statement to Reuters, the prosecutor's office said it was investigating "attempted destruction by fire or other dangerous means, as well as the manufacture, possession and transport of an explosive or incendiary device."

## Suspected International Dimensions

Minister Nunez pointed to possible Iranian involvement while cautioning that no conclusions had been reached. "In this type of conflict, you have a number of Iranian services that are likely to carry out actions such as these through proxies," he said. "There is a significant suspicion, but it is for the investigation to determine."

The Iranian embassy in France declined to comment on Nunez's remarks when contacted by Reuters.

Nunez characterized the suspects as "common-law" offenders acting as paid intermediaries, describing a known modus operandi involving proxies recruited to carry out attacks. He noted authorities had identified similarities with incidents in several European countries, including the Netherlands, Belgium, Britain and Norway, where improvised devices have targeted sites linked to U.S. interests.

## Bank Response and Security Context

A spokesperson for Bank of America, whose U.S. headquarters is in Charlotte, North Carolina, told AFP that the bank was "aware of the situation and were in communication with the French authorities."

The foiled attack comes amid heightened security alerts across Europe following escalating tensions in the Middle East. "Since the outbreak of the war in the Middle East, European countries have been on high alert for potential attacks on Iranian dissidents, Jewish places of worship and U.S.-Israeli assets," noted France 24's reporting.

Minister Nunez hailed the police intervention on social media platform X, praising the "speedy action by the police officers, given the current international situation."

## Ongoing Investigation

As of Sunday, the investigation continues with all three suspects in custody. The anti-terrorism prosecutor's office has not released the identities of those detained, nor specified when they might be formally charged.

The incident represents one of the most serious terror threats in Paris since the 2015 attacks that killed 130 people, highlighting ongoing vulnerabilities in European capitals despite enhanced security measures implemented over the past decade.
