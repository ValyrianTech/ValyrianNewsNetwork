# Dutch F-35s Conduct Historic Trilateral Exercises with Japan and US

The Royal Netherlands Air Force (RNLAF) is conducting its first-ever fighter exercises from Japan's Misawa Air Base, deploying F-35A stealth fighters in trilateral drills with Japanese and American forces. The exercises, running from March 23 to April 3, 2026, mark a significant expansion of Dutch military engagement in the Indo-Pacific region and demonstrate deepening security cooperation between European and Asian allies amid growing regional tensions.

According to [NOS](https://nos.nl/artikel/2608171-luchtmacht-oefent-in-japan-met-f-35-s-afschrikking-als-doel-vechten-als-nodig-is), the Dutch contingent includes five F-35A fighter jets and one A330 MRTT tanker aircraft that traveled over 10,000 kilometers from the Netherlands to northern Japan. The exercise, dubbed "Kazaguruma Guardian 26" (Windmill Guardian 26), involves the Japan Air Self-Defense Force (JASDF) and United States Air Force, with American forces contributing both F-35s and F-16s.

## Strategic Context and Historical Significance

This deployment represents the first time Dutch combat aircraft have operated from Japan and marks the RNLAF's initial F-35 deployment to the Western Pacific region. The exercises are part of the Netherlands' Indo-Pacific strategy implementation, which was formally adopted in 2020 to protect economic interests in a region where approximately 20% of world trade passes through the Taiwan Strait.

The Dutch Ministry of Defense [stated](https://www.defensie.nl/actueel/nieuws/2026/03/23/luchtmacht-traint-jachtvliegoperaties-met-japanners-en-amerikanen) that "Japan and the Netherlands have been loyal partners for years. For both countries, peace and stability are not self-evident. For the Netherlands, Japan is an important partner in the Indo-Pacific because it is a democratic and friendly country with a lot of influence in the region."

Japan is currently undergoing its largest military buildup since World War II in response to deteriorating regional security. China and Russia have increased their presence in seas around Japan and Taiwan, while North Korea regularly fires missiles toward Japanese territory. Japan's search for diverse security partnerships beyond the United States has led to increased cooperation with European nations perceived as politically stable partners.

## Exercise Objectives and Operational Details

André Steur, Commander of Dutch Air and Space Forces, emphasized the dual nature of the exercises: "The goal of the exercise is deterrence, but if things go wrong we must be able to fight as one team," he told NOS. The training focuses on air combat maneuvers and ground target attacks, with interoperability between the three nations' F-35 fleets being a key objective.

Lieutenant Colonel Pascal Smaal, leader of the Dutch detachment in Japan, highlighted the operational efficiency of the deployment: "We were able to do this with relatively few people and equipment because we can borrow a lot from the Japanese and use their airfield. The cooperation is going well. It shows that with such a small group of aircraft we are very agile and can be deployed anywhere in the world."

The Japanese Ministry of Defense had [announced](https://www.mod.go.jp/asdf/news/20260303e.pdf) the upcoming trilateral exercise in early March, noting the importance of enhancing allied interoperability in the region. The exercise follows similar European deployments to Japan, including Italian F-35s in 2023-2024 and British F-35Bs in 2025.

## Geopolitical Implications and Strategic Analysis

The trilateral exercises occur against a backdrop of complex regional dynamics. Dutch Ambassador to Japan Gilles Beschoor Plug explained in December 2025 that "the war in Ukraine, North Korea's troop deployment to Russia and the potential impact of South China Sea disruptions on international trade highlight how much regional security developments have become globally interconnected," as [reported](https://militarywatchmagazine.com/article/us-dutch-japanese-f35-trilateral-stealth) by Military Watch Magazine.

Ambassador Plug added: "Given the growing threat to international peace, stability and the rules-based order, Japan, the Netherlands, as well as our allies and partners, need to help each other to tackle these challenges."

The exercises carry particular significance given the Netherlands' nuclear capabilities. Dutch F-35s are nuclear-capable under NATO's nuclear sharing agreement, making this deployment part of a broader Western effort to maintain air dominance in the region. The timing is notable as it follows Russia's deployment of Su-57 fifth-generation fighters to its Far Eastern bases near Japan.

From Japan's perspective, the exercises represent an important diversification of security partnerships. Relations with the United States have been strained under the Trump administration, with the president complaining that partner countries like Japan contribute too little to joint defense. Japan's limited regional allies—its relationship with South Korea remains volatile—make European partnerships increasingly valuable.

## Economic and Security Interdependence

Economic considerations underpin the Netherlands' strategic engagement in the Indo-Pacific. Commandant Steur noted the direct connection between regional stability and European security: "We must also contribute to stability here. If tensions rise here, the consequences are directly felt in Europe, and therefore also in the Netherlands."

The Netherlands and Japan signed an action plan in 2025 for increased military cooperation, reflecting their shared interest in protecting maritime trade routes. With 20% of global trade passing through the Taiwan Strait, disruptions in the Indo-Pacific would have immediate economic consequences for European economies.

## Looking Ahead: Future Cooperation and Regional Stability

The Kazaguruma Guardian exercises represent more than routine military training—they signal a fundamental shift in how European nations engage with Asian security architecture. As NATO members increasingly pivot toward Indo-Pacific concerns, such deployments demonstrate the alliance's global reach and commitment to maintaining a rules-based international order.

Future developments to watch include whether these trilateral exercises become a regular pattern, how China and Russia officially respond to increased European military presence in the region, and whether other European NATO members will follow with similar deployments. The Netherlands-Japan defense cooperation is likely to expand beyond exercises, potentially encompassing joint patrols, intelligence sharing, and coordinated responses to regional crises.

As regional tensions continue to evolve, the strategic partnership between the Netherlands and Japan—bridging Europe and Asia—will play an increasingly important role in maintaining stability in the Indo-Pacific and protecting the interconnected economic and security interests of democratic nations worldwide.
