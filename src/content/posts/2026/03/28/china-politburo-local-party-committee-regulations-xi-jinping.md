# China Updates Local Party Rules Under Xi's Leadership

BEIJING, March 28, 2026 – China's top political leadership has approved updated regulations governing local Communist Party committees, reinforcing centralized authority while emphasizing a "correct view of political performance" for officials. The Political Bureau of the Communist Party of China Central Committee, chaired by President Xi Jinping, convened on March 27 to review the "Work Regulations for Local Committees of the Communist Party of China," signaling a significant shift in how local governance will be evaluated during the country's 15th Five-Year Plan period.

## Context: Recalibrating Governance Expectations

The updated regulations arrive at a pivotal moment in China's development trajectory. As the country enters the first year of its 15th Five-Year Plan (2026-2030) – the penultimate plan before its 2035 modernization goal – the leadership is emphasizing quality over quantity in governance. The "correct view of political performance" concept, championed by Xi Jinping since his early political career in Fujian Province, represents a fundamental recalibration of how officials' achievements are measured.

According to analysis from The Independent Uganda, this concept prioritizes "people's well-being and values long-term, tangible results" over short-term economic gains or visible infrastructure projects. The regulations come amid a broader Party-wide study campaign launched in February 2026 that aims to correct misguided views on governance that have historically led to vanity projects, hidden risks, and public discontent.

## Key Directives from the Politburo Meeting

The 24-member Politburo, China's top decision-making body, issued several key directives during the March 27 meeting. According to the official report published by People's Daily, local Party committees must "resolutely uphold the authority and centralized, unified leadership of the CPC Central Committee with Comrade Xi Jinping at the core."

However, the regulations also acknowledge the need for local flexibility. While demanding strict implementation of central decisions, the guidelines encourage local committees to "consider local conditions and enhance initiative and creativity" in their work. This dual mandate – absolute loyalty to central authority combined with localized innovation – represents the core tension the new regulations aim to manage.

Specific requirements outlined in the meeting include:
- Promoting "high-quality development with solid efforts"
- Focusing on improving people's well-being
- Safeguarding national security and social stability
- Establishing and practicing the "correct understanding of what it means to perform well"
- Refining decision-making rules and strengthening oversight mechanisms
- Advancing full and rigorous self-governance of the Party

## Analysis: Political and Economic Implications

The updated regulations carry significant implications for China's political system and economic development model.

**Political Centralization Continues:** The emphasis on upholding the authority of the CPC Central Committee with Xi Jinping at its core continues a trend of power concentration that has characterized Xi's leadership since 2012. As noted in the research, this reinforces "the centralized leadership of the CPC Central Committee with Xi Jinping at its core."

**Performance Evaluation Reform:** The shift from GDP-focused evaluation to broader metrics including people's well-being, environmental protection, and long-term sustainability represents a fundamental change in how local officials are assessed. Cuban researcher Eduardo Regalado, cited in the research, views this concept as "key to China's transformation toward greater quality, efficiency, and equity in development."

**Economic Restructuring Alignment:** The regulations align with the 15th Five-Year Plan's emphasis on cultivating new industries and emerging sectors. With a GDP growth target of 4.5-5% for 2026 and 109 major projects planned, the focus on "high-quality development" rather than raw growth figures signals a maturing economic approach.

**Anti-Corruption and Governance Improvement:** The meeting's emphasis on "exercising power in compliance with laws and regulations" and "advancing full and rigorous self-governance" continues China's ongoing anti-corruption campaign while addressing governance quality at local levels.

## Historical Precedents and Models

President Xi has frequently cited historical figures as models for contemporary governance. Jiao Yulu, a county Party chief in the 1960s known for his selfless dedication to poverty alleviation, represents the ideal of people-centered governance that the new regulations seek to institutionalize.

The regulations also build on China's recent success in eliminating extreme poverty, now shifting focus toward "common prosperity" – reducing inequality while maintaining economic growth. This transition requires local officials to balance multiple, sometimes competing, priorities while maintaining political loyalty.

## International Perspectives and Reactions

International analysts have noted the strategic timing of these regulations. The Mercator Institute analysis, referenced in the research materials, observes that "China's five-year plans act as a compass for Party cadres, signaling how they should work and what they need to achieve."

The South China Morning Post analysis raises questions about implementation challenges, particularly how local officials will balance the "dual mandate of absolute loyalty to central authority with the need for local initiative."

## Implementation and Forward Look

The updated regulations will now be disseminated to provincial, municipal, and county-level Party committees across China. Implementation will be closely watched as it affects thousands of local officials responsible for governing China's vast and diverse regions.

Key areas to monitor include:
1. **Measurement Mechanisms:** How the "correct view of political performance" will be quantified and evaluated in practice
2. **Local Adaptation:** How different regions will interpret and implement the balance between central directives and local innovation
3. **Economic Impact:** Whether the emphasis on quality over quantity affects regional development patterns
4. **Governance Outcomes:** Whether the regulations successfully reduce formalism, corruption, and vanity projects at local levels

As China navigates the complex challenges of the 15th Five-Year Plan period – including economic restructuring, environmental protection, and social stability – these updated regulations for local Party committees represent a crucial governance framework. Their implementation will significantly influence China's development trajectory toward its 2035 modernization goals while testing the Party's ability to maintain both centralized control and localized effectiveness in governance.
