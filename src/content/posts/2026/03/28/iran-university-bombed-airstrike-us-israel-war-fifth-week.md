# Iran University Bombed in Airstrike as US-Israel War Enters Fifth Week

**Tehran University of Science and Technology** was bombed in overnight airstrikes early Saturday, marking a significant escalation in the **fifth week** of the US-Israel conflict with Iran. The university, also referred to as the University of Future Scientists, reported **severe damage** to research and teaching buildings but **no casualties** among students or staff, according to [Maariv](https://www.maariv.co.il/news/military/article-1302217).

The attack on academic infrastructure comes amid a **major escalation** of strikes across Iran, with simultaneous attacks on **water reservoirs**, **broadcasting towers**, **industrial parks**, and **steel factories** throughout the country. The conflict, which began on **February 28, 2026**, has now entered its **28th day** with no clear end in sight.

## Context: A Month of Escalating Conflict

The war between the United States, Israel, and Iran began with coordinated strikes on February 28 and has since expanded across the Middle East. What started as targeted military operations has evolved into **broader economic warfare** and **infrastructure targeting**, with both sides inflicting significant damage while attempting to avoid full-scale ground invasion.

According to [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/rubio-sees-us-action-iran-completed-weeks-airstrikes-rumble-2026-03-28/), the conflict has already **killed thousands** and created the **largest-ever disruption** to global energy supplies, with the **Strait of Hormuz** effectively closed to shipping. The strategic waterway normally carries about **20% of global oil** and liquefied natural gas supplies.

## Key Developments: University Attack and Broader Strikes

The attack on Tehran University of Science and Technology occurred in the **early hours of Saturday**, March 28. Fighter jets reportedly **attacked within the campus**, causing what the university described as "severe damage to central research and teaching buildings." In an official statement, the institution strongly condemned the attack, calling it a violation of **international law** and the **principles of humanity**.

"Despite the severe damage to central research and teaching buildings, fortunately the enemy's cruel missile strike did not lead to loss of human life," the university stated via Iranian news agencies Fara and Entekhab, as reported by Maariv.

Despite the absence of casualties on campus, the attack created **"great panic and fear"** in surrounding neighborhoods, particularly affecting patients at a hospital adjacent to the university grounds.

The university strike was part of a **broader wave of attacks** across Iran that same night. Other targets included:

- A **water reservoir** in Haftgel city, Khuzestan province
- A **broadcasting tower** in Khuzestan province
- The **Esteklal industrial park** in western Tehran
- The **headquarters of the Iranian Maritime Industries Organization**
- **Steel factories** in Khuzestan and Isfahan provinces
- A **heavy water facility** in Khondab
- A **yellow cake production plant** in Ardakan

## Military Statements and Regional Expansion

The **Israeli military** confirmed conducting extensive airstrikes against Iranian infrastructure throughout Tehran, stating it had targeted **ballistic missile production and storage facilities** and hit **over 100 targets** in Iran since Friday. In a separate statement, Israel reported hitting **over 170 targets** in Lebanon, where conflict with Iran-backed Hezbollah has intensified.

Meanwhile, **Yemen's Houthi rebels** launched their **first missile attack on Israel** since the war began. The attack, which caused no casualties or damage, signals potential expansion of the conflict to threaten **global shipping lanes** beyond the already-closed Strait of Hormuz.

**US Secretary of State Marco Rubio** stated that military operations were expected to be concluded in **"weeks, not months"** and echoed President Donald Trump's calls for European and Asian countries to help secure free passage through the Strait of Hormuz. The US is deploying **thousands of Marines** to the region, with the first contingent arriving Friday.

## Iranian Response and Civilian Impact

**Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian** warned that Iran would **"retaliate strongly if our infrastructure or economic centers are targeted."** In a message to regional countries, he added: "To the countries of the region: If you want development and security, don't let our enemies run the war from your lands."

The conflict has taken a significant toll on Iranian civilians. According to [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/27/guns-in-the-streets-as-us-israel-intensify-month-long-attacks-across), **nearly 2,000 people** have been killed since February 28, with residential areas, hospitals, schools, and civilian vehicles affected. The country has experienced a **complete civilian internet shutdown** for nearly a month—the longest recorded in Iran's history—affecting over **90 million people**.

A Tehran resident described the psychological impact to Al Jazeera: "You still can't help but get that sinking feeling in your gut for a moment, not knowing whether you will be next when you hear the jets flying over."

## Economic Warfare: Targeting Iran's Industrial Base

The simultaneous attacks on Iran's **steel industry** represent a strategic effort to cripple the country's economy and military supply chain. Iranian steel production has been **severely damaged**, potentially causing **billions in losses**. The steel factory in Khuzestan province—Iran's second-largest producer—has **halted production completely**, with operations expected to take considerable time to resume.

This economic targeting comes as Iran already faces **approximately 70% inflation** and severe economic pressure from years of international sanctions. The combination of infrastructure damage and internet blackout has created what analysts describe as **"economic warfare"** designed to pressure the Iranian government.

## Analysis: Strategic Significance of University Targeting

The attack on Tehran University of Science and Technology represents several strategic developments:

1. **Escalation to Academic Targets:** Previously, strikes focused on military, nuclear, and industrial infrastructure. Targeting a university signals willingness to strike **scientific and educational facilities**, which Iran claims violates international norms.

2. **Psychological Impact:** While causing no casualties, the attack in the heart of Tehran creates fear and demonstrates the reach of airstrikes into the capital's core institutions.

3. **Research Disruption:** The university likely conducts research with both civilian and potential military applications. Disrupting this research could have longer-term implications for Iran's scientific and technological development.

4. **Broader Economic Strategy:** Combined with attacks on steel factories and other industrial targets, the strikes appear designed to **cripple Iran's economic base** and military supply chain simultaneously.

## Diplomatic Efforts and Forward Look

Despite the intensifying conflict, **diplomatic efforts continue**. Pakistan is hosting talks with **Saudi Arabia, Turkey, and Egypt** on Sunday to seek regional de-escalation. Iranian President Pezeshkian spoke with Pakistan's Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif ahead of the meeting.

However, there appears to be **no immediate breakthrough** in sight. The US has given Iran an **additional 10 days** to respond to demands to open the Strait of Hormuz, extending a previous deadline.

As the conflict enters its second month, key questions remain:
- Will international diplomatic efforts yield results?
- How long can Iran's economy withstand current pressure?
- Will the conflict expand further with Houthi involvement threatening additional shipping lanes?
- What will be the long-term impact on Iran's scientific and educational infrastructure?

The attack on Tehran University of Science and Technology serves as a stark reminder that in modern conflict, **educational and research institutions** are no longer considered immune from targeting—a development with profound implications for academic freedom and international norms in wartime.
