# Dutch Government Proposes Doctorate Titles for HBO Graduates

The Dutch government has announced plans to introduce two new doctorate titles that would allow graduates of universities of applied sciences (HBO) to obtain doctoral degrees for the first time. The proposal, unveiled by Education Minister Rianne Letschert (D66), would create Professional Doctor (PD) and Engineering Doctor (EngD) titles, marking a significant shift in the Netherlands' binary higher education system.

According to the [official government announcement](https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/actueel/nieuws/2026/03/30/wetsvoorstel-masterstudenten-kunnen-ook-doctor-worden-op-hbo), the Professional Doctor title would be awarded after 4-6 years of practice-oriented research at HBO institutions, while the Engineering Doctor would be a 2-year program focused on technological design at universities. Both programs currently exist as pilots but lack official recognition as doctorate degrees.

## Context: Breaking the Binary Divide

The Netherlands has maintained a strict separation between research universities (WO) and universities of applied sciences (HBO) since the 1960s. While WO institutions have traditionally held the exclusive right to award doctorate degrees (PhD), HBO schools have focused on vocational education and practical training. This proposal represents the most significant challenge to that division in decades.

Minister Letschert emphasized the societal value of practice-oriented research in her statement: "Praktijkgericht onderzoek is van grote waarde om ons oplossingen te leveren voor maatschappelijke problemen, en is vaak direct toepasbaar voor bijvoorbeeld bedrijven. Ik vind het belangrijk om zulk onderzoek echt te waarderen." ("Practice-oriented research is of great value in providing solutions to societal problems and is often directly applicable to businesses. I believe it's important to truly value such research.")

## The Proposal Details

The Professional Doctorate would differ from traditional PhD programs in its orientation toward practical applications. As [NOS reports](https://nos.nl/artikel/2608363-kabinet-wil-dat-hbo-ers-ook-doctorstitel-kunnen-halen), where university doctorates focus on theoretical, fundamental research under professor supervision, HBO doctorates would emphasize collaboration with industry and practical problem-solving.

Maurice Limmen, chairman of the Association of Universities of Applied Sciences (Vereniging Hogescholen), welcomed the proposal, stating: "Het professional doctorate leidt mensen op die samen met bedrijven en instellingen innovaties tot stand brengen in een beroepspraktijk die razendsnel verandert door technologische ontwikkelingen zoals artificiële intelligentie." ("The professional doctorate trains people who, together with companies and institutions, create innovations in a professional practice that is changing rapidly due to technological developments such as artificial intelligence.")

## Legal Process and Timeline

The proposal requires amending the Higher Education and Research Act (WHW) and follows a multi-step legislative process. As [RTL Nieuws details](https://www.rtlnieuws.nl/nieuws/politiek/artikel/5583125/promoveren-op-het-hbo-kabinet-wil-doctorstitel-openbreken), the internet consultation period began on March 30, 2026, followed by advice from the Council of State, and then parliamentary approval in both the House of Representatives (Tweede Kamer) and Senate (Eerste Kamer).

If approved, the first candidates could begin their PD or EngD trajectories in the 2027-2028 academic year. The PD pilot program, which started in 2023 through collaboration between the Association of Universities of Applied Sciences, the Ministry of Education, and the Regieorgaan SIA, has already enrolled candidates in seven domains including health & wellbeing, technology & digitalization, and energy & sustainability.

## International Context and Rationale

The Dutch proposal aligns with international trends where professional doctorates have gained recognition in countries like the United Kingdom, Australia, and the United States. As [DutchNews.nl reports](https://www.dutchnews.nl/2026/03/dutch-plan-to-launch-two-new-doctorates-pd-and-engd/), the move responds to OECD and Rathenau Institute findings that highlight the Netherlands' need for more advanced research capabilities to address complex societal challenges.

The government's rationale emphasizes economic and educational equity. Practice-oriented research addresses immediate industry needs while providing HBO graduates with academic recognition equivalent to their university counterparts. The proposal also fulfills a commitment in the coalition agreement to strengthen practice-oriented research.

## Potential Controversies and Criticisms

Despite broad support from HBO institutions, the proposal faces potential criticism. Some academics worry about devaluing traditional doctorate degrees and blurring distinctions between HBO and WO institutions. Quality assurance mechanisms and international recognition remain key concerns that the legislation must address.

RTL Nieuws notes that critics fear the value of the doctorate title could be undermined if HBO institutions gain awarding powers. The legislation specifies that PD programs must maintain the same difficulty level as traditional PhDs but with different orientation—a challenging balance to achieve in practice.

## Implications for Dutch Education

The introduction of HBO doctorates would represent a milestone for the Dutch education system, offering new pathways for advanced professional development. For students, it creates additional career options beyond traditional academic routes. For industry, it provides access to highly qualified professionals with both theoretical knowledge and practical research skills.

The move also signals a broader trend toward recognizing diverse forms of knowledge production and expertise. As technological change accelerates and societal challenges grow more complex, the ability to conduct rigorous, applicable research becomes increasingly valuable across all sectors.

## What's Next

The legislative process will unfold over the coming months, with parliamentary debate expected later in 2026. Educational institutions are already preparing for potential implementation, with HBO schools developing research capacities and university faculties considering how to integrate EngD programs more formally.

International observers will watch closely as the Netherlands potentially becomes a model for other countries with binary education systems. The success of the Professional Doctorate could influence global approaches to recognizing practice-oriented research and expanding access to advanced academic qualifications.

As Minister Letschert described it, the recognition of HBO doctorates represents "een missend puzzelstukje" ("a missing puzzle piece") in valuing all forms of research that contribute to societal progress. Whether this piece fits perfectly into the Dutch educational landscape will depend on careful implementation and ongoing evaluation of the new doctorate programs' quality and impact.
