# Russian Attacks Target Ukrainian Infrastructure, Causing Casualties and Power Outages

Russian forces conducted coordinated attacks on Ukrainian civilian and critical infrastructure across multiple regions on March 31, 2026, resulting in civilian casualties, significant damage to essential facilities, and widespread power disruptions. The strikes targeted residential areas, railway infrastructure, and energy systems in what Ukrainian officials describe as a continued strategy of targeting civilian infrastructure in the ongoing war.

## Coordinated Attacks Across Regions

In the northeastern city of Chuhuiv, Kharkiv region, a Russian drone struck a non-operating enterprise, injuring four people including a civilian woman and three police officers. According to [Chuhuiv Mayor Galina Minaeva](https://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2026/03/31/8027979/), the attack damaged a critical infrastructure object, a children's institution, and approximately 15 private houses, mostly affecting windows and roofs. Part of the city was left without electricity, with most areas expected to have power restored within two hours.

Further south in central Ukraine, Russian forces attacked Poltava overnight, killing one person and injuring four others, including two children. The [Ministry of Internal Affairs of Ukraine](https://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2026/03/31/8027949/) reported that a multi-story building was damaged, forcing rescue workers to evacuate residents. The attack also hit an infrastructure object and caused fires in open areas that were subsequently extinguished.

## Railway Infrastructure Targeted

In Slovyansk, Donetsk region, Russian forces struck railway infrastructure, damaging railway objects, a locomotive depot, and partially breaking windows in the station building. According to [Minister Oleksii Kuleba and Ukrzaliznytsia press service](https://epravda.com.ua/infrastruktura/vorog-atakuvav-zaliznichnu-infrastrukturu-slov-yanska-ye-poraneni-819857), four locomotive depot workers were injured, with one female worker in serious condition. All injured were hospitalized, and railway workers were moved to shelters during the attack.

This attack continues a pattern of targeting Ukraine's railway system, which has suffered approximately 5,000 attacks since Russia's full-scale invasion began in February 2022. Ukrainian authorities have implemented electronic warfare modules to protect trains from unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) attacks, but comprehensive protection requires dozens of modules per train along the 30-60 kilometer frontline depth.

## Widespread Power Disruptions

The morning attacks resulted in partial power outages across five Ukrainian regions: Kharkiv, Donetsk, Dnipropetrovsk, Zaporizhzhia, and Poltava. [Ukrenergo, Ukraine's national energy company](https://epravda.com.ua/energetika/znestrumlennya-vnaslidok-obstriliv-situaciya-v-regionah-ukrajini-na-31-bereznya-819852), reported that emergency restoration work was underway wherever security conditions allowed. The company noted that electricity consumption had increased by 2.7% compared to the previous day, partly due to cloudy weather and rain reducing the efficiency of household solar power systems.

Ukrenergo urged residents to practice economical electricity consumption during evening peak hours, limiting the use of powerful electrical appliances. Despite the disruptions, the company stated that planned power outages were not scheduled for March 31.

## Official Responses and Damage Assessment

Chuhuiv Mayor Galina Minaeva provided detailed assessment of the damage in her city, stating: "Unfortunately, information about victims is coming in. Currently, we know about varying degrees of injuries to a civilian woman and three police officers. A critical infrastructure object, a children's institution, and about 15 private houses (mostly windows and roofs) have been damaged."

The Ministry of Internal Affairs confirmed the Poltava casualties, reporting: "At night, the enemy attacked Poltava. A multi-story building was damaged, rescuers evacuated residents. Four people were injured, including two children. One person died."

Ukrenergo emphasized its restoration efforts, stating: "Emergency restoration work is already underway wherever security conditions allow. Energy workers are doing everything possible to restore power to all consumers as quickly as possible."

## Pattern of Infrastructure Targeting

The March 31 attacks demonstrate several consistent patterns in Russian military strategy against Ukraine:

1. **Multi-Region Coordination**: Attacks occurred simultaneously across geographically dispersed regions, stretching Ukrainian emergency response capabilities.
2. **Critical Infrastructure Focus**: Railway, energy, and residential infrastructure were all targeted, indicating a comprehensive approach to disrupting civilian life and military logistics.
3. **Timing for Maximum Impact**: Attacks were timed during morning hours to maximize disruption to daily activities and emergency response.
4. **Civilian Impact**: All attacks resulted in civilian casualties or significant disruption to civilian life, consistent with patterns that international observers have described as potential war crimes.

## Broader Context and Implications

Russia's systematic targeting of Ukrainian civilian infrastructure has been a consistent feature of the conflict since the full-scale invasion began in February 2022. The railway system has been particularly vulnerable, with attacks intensifying in recent months—over 10% of all railway attacks have occurred in the past several months alone.

The geographical spread of the March 31 attacks is notable: Kharkiv region in the northeast near the Russian border, Poltava in central Ukraine (previously less frequently attacked than frontline regions), Donetsk region on the active eastern frontline, and the southeastern industrial and energy regions of Dnipropetrovsk and Zaporizhzhia.

## Short-term and Long-term Consequences

In the short term, the attacks create immediate humanitarian needs for injured civilians and displaced residents, strain emergency and medical services, and require rapid restoration of power and critical services. The medium-term implications include continued vulnerability of Ukrainian infrastructure to Russian attacks, increased need for enhanced air defense and electronic warfare protection, and economic impacts from disrupted transportation and energy systems.

Longer-term considerations involve the strategic importance of protecting critical infrastructure for Ukraine's defense and economy, the need for international support for infrastructure protection and reconstruction, and the ongoing documentation of potential war crimes related to attacks on civilian infrastructure.

## Looking Forward

As emergency services continue restoration work and medical teams treat the injured, Ukrainian authorities face the ongoing challenge of protecting critical infrastructure while maintaining essential services. The pattern of coordinated, multi-region attacks suggests that Russia continues to prioritize disruption of civilian life and critical systems as part of its military strategy.

The international community continues to monitor these developments, with particular attention to the protection of civilian infrastructure under international humanitarian law. Meanwhile, Ukrainian energy workers, railway personnel, and emergency services demonstrate remarkable resilience in responding to attacks and restoring essential services under difficult conditions.
