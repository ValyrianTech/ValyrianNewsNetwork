# Zelenskyy Warns of Trump Pressure for Donbas Withdrawal as EU Chief Visits Kyiv

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy has expressed concern that the Trump administration sees only one way to stop Russian President Vladimir Putin: withdrawing Ukrainian forces from the Donetsk region. In an interview with [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/03/30/zelensky-russia-iran-war-ukraine) published Monday, Zelenskyy revealed that recent negotiations between Ukrainian delegates and Trump's special envoys yielded no results, and he fears Washington will resume pressure for territorial concessions after the Iran conflict concludes.

Meanwhile, European Union foreign policy chief Kaja Kallas arrived in Kyiv on Tuesday morning, accompanied by foreign ministers from Germany, Poland, Latvia, Lithuania, Italy, and Bulgaria. The visit, which coincides with the fourth anniversary of the Bucha massacre liberation, signals continued European support for Ukraine amid growing diplomatic tensions with the United States.

## Context: Stalled Peace Talks and Shifting Alliances

The diplomatic developments come as peace talks between Ukraine and Russia remain suspended due to the U.S.-Israeli war with Iran, which has diverted international attention and resources from the Ukrainian conflict. According to [Ukrainska Pravda](https://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2026/03/31/8027927/), Zelenskyy stated that the Trump administration is pressuring Ukraine to withdraw its forces from the Donetsk region as a condition for U.S. security guarantees.

"I am convinced that President Trump and his team want to end the war," Zelenskyy told Axios. "My concern is that they see only one way to do this. They do not see another way to stop Putin except withdrawing Ukrainian forces from our territory. But why should we pay for this? We are not aggressors."

## U.S. Response and Diplomatic Tensions

U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio has denied Zelenskyy's claims, calling them "a lie" during a March 27 press conference. Rubio clarified that the U.S. position is not advocating for Russian demands but explaining them to Ukraine, with the decision ultimately resting with Kyiv. He emphasized that security guarantees would only activate after the war ends, not as a precondition for territorial concessions.

The disagreement highlights growing tensions in U.S.-Ukraine relations under the Trump administration. Zelenskyy revealed that a senior Ukrainian delegation met with Trump's envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner in Miami ten days ago, but the talks produced no results. He warned that after the Iran conflict concludes, the U.S. administration will resume pressure on Ukraine to make territorial concessions to end the war with Russia.

## European Solidarity Amid Uncertainty

Against this backdrop of strained transatlantic relations, EU diplomacy chief Kaja Kallas's visit to Kyiv represents a significant show of European solidarity. According to [Ukrainska Pravda](https://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2026/03/31/8027941/), Kallas was greeted by Ukrainian Foreign Minister Andrii Sybiha and EU Ambassador to Ukraine Katarina Maternova upon her arrival.

"Such strong European presence on this day demonstrates that justice for this and other Russian atrocities is inevitable," Sybiha stated, referring to the Bucha massacre anniversary. The delegation is scheduled to visit Bucha, where Russian troops killed more than 400 civilians in early 2022 according to Ukrainian officials and rights groups.

Kallas pledged continued EU support: "Europe stands by your side. We will keep providing military, financial, energy, and humanitarian support." Her visit comes as a 90-billion-euro EU loan for Ukraine remains blocked by Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orban over disputes about Russian oil transit via Ukraine's Druzhba pipeline.

## Analysis: Diverging Approaches to Ending the War

The contrasting diplomatic approaches reveal fundamental differences in how Western powers view the path to peace in Ukraine. The Trump administration appears focused on achieving a quick resolution, potentially through territorial concessions, while the European Union emphasizes continued military and economic support alongside accountability for Russian war crimes.

Zelenskyy's comments also shed light on how the Iran conflict is affecting the Ukraine war. He told Axios that Russia is "winning" from the Middle East conflict, benefiting from surging oil prices and loosening U.S. sanctions while potentially facing reduced Western military assistance to Ukraine.

"I see only benefits for Russia from the war with Iran continuing," Zelenskyy said, expressing concern that weapons supplies could be diverted to the Middle East and that rising fuel costs due to oil price increases would further strain Ukraine's economy.

## What's Next: Diplomatic Challenges Ahead

The diplomatic developments set the stage for several critical challenges in the coming months:

1. **Post-Iran Conflict Pressure**: Zelenskyy's warning suggests the Trump administration will intensify pressure for territorial concessions once the Iran conflict concludes, potentially creating a major diplomatic crisis in U.S.-Ukraine relations.

2. **European Unity Test**: Kallas's visit demonstrates European solidarity, but overcoming Hungarian opposition to Ukraine aid and maintaining consensus among 27 member states will be crucial for sustained support.

3. **Peace Process Revival**: With peace talks currently suspended, any resumption will occur in a dramatically changed geopolitical landscape, with Russia potentially strengthened by oil revenues and Western attention divided.

4. **Security Guarantee Negotiations**: The fundamental disagreement over whether U.S. security guarantees should be linked to territorial concessions remains unresolved and will likely dominate future diplomatic discussions.

As Ukraine enters its fifth year of conflict, the diplomatic landscape appears increasingly complex, with traditional alliances strained and new geopolitical realities emerging. The coming months will test whether Western unity can be maintained in the face of diverging approaches to ending Europe's largest armed conflict since World War II.
