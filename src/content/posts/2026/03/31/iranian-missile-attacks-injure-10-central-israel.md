# Iranian Missile Attacks Injure 10 in Central Israel

At least 10 people were injured in central Israel on Tuesday morning when Iran launched missile attacks targeting multiple urban areas, including Bnei Brak, Petah Tikva, Givat Shmuel, and Tel Aviv. According to [Ynet](https://www.ynet.co.il/news/article/bk82ylfjzx#autoplay), the attacks occurred in the morning hours of March 31, 2026, with sirens sounding across the Gush Dan, Shfela, and Sharon regions around 10 AM local time. The strikes represent a significant escalation in the ongoing conflict between Israel and Iran.

## Casualties and Injuries

Israeli emergency services reported 10 people injured in the attacks, all in mild or light condition. The casualties included six people in Bnei Brak: a 39-year-old woman, a 24-year-old woman, a 6-year-old boy, a 3-year-old girl, and two 1.5-year-old infants. In Petah Tikva, four people were injured including a 67-year-old man, a 27-year-old man, and two additional people suffering from smoke inhalation. Emergency responders also treated three anxiety cases and several minor injuries from people running to shelters.

The attacks caused significant property damage across multiple locations. In Bnei Brak, a synagogue was damaged and a residential building sustained extensive damage. Petah Tikva saw a garage hit and multiple vehicles burned. Givat Shmuel reported a vehicle on fire, while Tel Aviv had three impact sites including one near the coastal promenade where vehicles were destroyed.

Yechiel Miller, a United Hatzalah medic who arrived at the scene in Bnei Brak, described the damage: "This involves extensive damage caused to a residential building. With the help of other medics, I provided assistance at the scene to two women and four children and infants who were lightly injured."

## Civilian Accounts and Response

Civilian accounts from the attack sites paint a picture of sudden terror and destruction. "I was at work," said Sagi Malka, a resident of Petah Tikva. "My pregnant wife was here with our daughter. Fortunately we were in a protected space. We were in the shelter. It really saves lives. We couldn't believe it would happen. There was a very unusual noise, a crazy explosion. All the cars went up in flames. Let's hope for good news."

Ronen Yehezkel, who went to a shelter in Petah Tikva with his wife and young children, recounted: "There was an alarm, we went straight to the shelter. There was a serious boom, flames and fire. My wife started choking, having a panic attack. All the cars burned. The house was slightly damaged - the shutters and windows. Thank God we came out okay."

Israeli emergency services responded quickly to the attacks. Magen David Adom (Israel's national emergency medical service) provided medical treatment at the scenes, while United Hatzalah volunteers assisted with first response. The Israeli Home Front Command confirmed "reports of damage" in central Israel, and police reported falling munitions fragments in the Tel Aviv area.

## Attack Details and Weaponry

The weapon used in the attacks was described in the [Ynet report](https://www.ynet.co.il/news/article/bk82ylfjzx#autoplay) as a "fragmenting missile" or "cluster bomb," though specific missile types have not been officially confirmed. Some missiles were reportedly intercepted approximately one hour after the initial attacks, though details about Israel's Iron Dome or other defense system performance remain unclear.

The attacks raise questions about Israel's air defense capabilities against direct Iranian missile strikes. While Israel has invested heavily in multi-layered missile defense systems including Iron Dome, David's Sling, and Arrow, the effectiveness of these systems against large-scale Iranian missile attacks remains a critical consideration for Israeli security planners.
