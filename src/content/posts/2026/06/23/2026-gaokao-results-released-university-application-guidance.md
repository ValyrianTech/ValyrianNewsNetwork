# 2026 Gaokao Results Released; Universities Guide Applicants

China began releasing the results of its 2026 National College Entrance Examination, known as the Gaokao, on June 23, with Shanghai becoming the first province to publish scores. Approximately 12.9 million candidates registered for this year's exam, a decrease from 13.35 million in 2025, according to [China Daily](https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202606/03/WS6a1fcb7ea310d6866eb4c3b6.html). As millions of students and their families now turn their attention to the critical task of submitting application preferences (志愿填报), universities across the country are offering expert guidance on how to navigate this high-stakes decision.

## Staggered Results Release Across Provinces

The results are being published on a staggered schedule through late June. Following Shanghai's release on June 23, scores in Tianjin, Inner Mongolia, Heilongjiang, Fujian, and Henan will become available on June 24. The peak release day falls on June 25, when over 20 provinces including Beijing, Jiangsu, Shandong, Hubei, Guangdong, and Sichuan will publish results. Zhejiang follows on June 26, with Liaoning, Ningxia, and Xinjiang releasing scores in late June, as reported by [CCTV](https://news.cctv.com/2026/06/23/ARTI3rXGGTorv3vmF3z9rvaC260623.shtml).

## Cloud Consulting Week Offers Digital Guidance

To assist students and parents during this period, the Ministry of Education's "Sunshine Gaokao" information platform is hosting a "Cloud Consulting Week" from June 22 to 28. According to the [Ministry of Education](https://www.moe.gov.cn/jyb_xwfb/gzdt_gzdt/s5987/202606/t20260617_1440974.html), the platform provides text-based Q&A sessions from 9:00 to 17:00 daily, along with video livestreams where universities present their admissions policies and answer questions in real time. The initiative is part of the broader "2026 Gaokao护航行动" (Escort Action) designed to support students through the application process.

## Expert Advice: Balancing School, City, and Major

In a special feature by [Xinhua News](https://education.news.cn/20260623/3ee2ab744ee1474ab2a6545d701570ec/c.html), admissions officers from leading Chinese universities shared their insights on one of the most challenging questions facing applicants: how to balance the competing priorities of university reputation, city location, and choice of major.

Yao Chang, an admissions officer at Peking University, advised students not to blindly follow popular majors. "Choices that match your own talents, personality, and interests are more conducive to long-term development," Yao said. "Students who truly develop well are mostly those who follow their hearts and match their strengths."

Niu Guowei, Director of the Admissions Office at China Agricultural University, offered a structured framework he called "using the ruler of time to measure." His approach breaks down the decision into three time horizons:

- **Short-term (undergraduate years):** Prioritize school over city over major, as higher-tier institutions offer superior resources, faculty, and flexibility to change majors.
- **Medium-term (graduate studies):** Prioritize school over major over city, as strong institutional platforms enhance graduate school competitiveness.
- **Long-term (career development):** Prioritize major over school over city, as the chosen field ultimately determines career trajectory and earning potential.

"In the long run, what determines the ceiling of your career value is definitely the track your major is on," Niu emphasized.

Zhang Rui of Northeastern University urged students to view "hot majors" with caution. "The popularity of majors is closely related to the social environment and economic development conditions," Zhang said, recommending that students consult faculty, current students, and admissions offices to understand the真实 (real) nature of academic programs.

## Application Periods and Fraud Warnings

Multiple provinces have announced their志愿填报 schedules. Beijing's undergraduate application period runs from June 27 to July 1, while Jiangsu and Hebei have set theirs from June 28 to July 2. Shanghai's undergraduate stage, including comprehensive evaluation batches, is scheduled for July 1-2.

Authorities have also issued warnings about heightened fraud risks during the post-exam period. Common scams include fake "early score checking" links sent via SMS, fraudulent admission letters, and claims of special admission channels. Officials emphasize that all official score checking is free and only available through provincial education examination authority websites and verified government service platforms.

## A Demographic Shift

The decline in test-takers from 13.35 million to 12.9 million reflects China's broader demographic challenges, though the number remains enormous by global standards. The 2026 exam also featured over 14,000 candidates with disabilities who received special accommodations, including Braille exam papers for visually impaired students.

## Looking Ahead

As the application process unfolds over the coming weeks, the choices made by 12.9 million students will shape not only their individual futures but also the next generation of China's workforce. With universities emphasizing flexibility through expanded transfer policies, dual-degree programs, and interdisciplinary curricula, students are being encouraged to view their decisions not as final destinations but as starting points for lifelong learning and adaptation.
