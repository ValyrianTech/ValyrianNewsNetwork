# Teenager in Life-Threatening Condition After Antwerp Park Stabbing

A 19-year-old man is fighting for his life after a brutal stabbing in Antwerp's Rivierenhof park on Monday evening, with police arresting nine suspects in connection with the attack. The incident has sent shockwaves through the local community and reignited concerns about youth knife violence in Belgium's second-largest city.

The victim sustained multiple stab wounds during the attack, which occurred around 19:20 local time on June 22 in the provincial domain of Deurne. He was rushed to hospital in critical condition and, as of the latest updates, remains unable to be questioned by investigators, according to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/06/22/jongeman-19-in-levensgevaar-na-steekpartij-in-antwerpse-rivier/).

## The Attack and Immediate Response

According to Wouter Bruyns, spokesperson for Antwerp police, the incident began as a dispute between Afghan youths that quickly escalated. "The victim, a 19-year-old young man, sustained stab wounds and was taken to hospital in life-threatening condition," Bruyns told reporters. "It wasn't immediately clear what had happened. A number of witnesses reported that a fight had likely broken out."

Police arrested eight suspects on Monday evening — six adults and two minors — with a ninth suspect taken into custody on Tuesday morning. Some of the suspects also sustained injuries during the altercation. The forensic laboratory conducted on-site evidence collection at the park, and investigators are now working to determine each suspect's role in the attack.

"During the evening we arrested 6 adult and 2 minor suspects. They too had sustained injuries," Bruyns said. "Now we must investigate who had what role in the incident — who instigated and who inflicted the wounds."

## Possible Extortion Link

Authorities are investigating whether extortion may have been a motivating factor in the attack. "It could be related to an extortion case, but that's not yet entirely clear to us," Bruyns explained, as reported by [HLN](https://www.hln.be/antwerpen/jongeman-19-in-levensgevaar-na-steekpartij-in-rivierenhof-politie-zoekt-verdachte-n~aae0da76/).

The investigation remains in its early stages, with police working to piece together the sequence of events and the relationships between those involved.

## Related Incident in Merksem

Earlier that same evening, around 18:00, a separate fight broke out in the Merksem district of Antwerp. One person was lightly injured, possibly with a knife, and two suspects — one minor and one adult — were arrested. According to Bruyns, a long-running dispute is believed to be at the root of that conflict. It remains unclear whether the two incidents are connected.

## Broader Context: Youth Knife Violence in Belgium

This stabbing is the latest in a series of violent incidents involving young people in Antwerp's public parks. In September 2025, an 18-year-old Afghan man, Samir Ahmadi, was fatally stabbed in Antwerp's Stadspark during a fight between groups of youths, resulting in the arrest of seven minors. More recently, in January 2026, six people were wounded — two critically — in a stabbing near the Operaplein during a pro-Kurdish demonstration.

Despite these high-profile incidents, federal police statistics show that the number of stabbings in Belgium has remained relatively stable in recent years, with 707 incidents recorded in 2023. However, as [VRT NWS reported](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2025/09/29/steekpartij-mol-cijfers-jeugdrechter/) in a background analysis, experts and police chiefs report a growing perception that more young people are carrying knives.

Criminologist Stefaan Pleysier of KU Leuven notes there is no clear increase in stabbing incidents in available statistics, despite public perception to the contrary. However, advocate Chantal Van den Bosch reports seeing more young clients involved in knife incidents, observing that youths carry knives for a "false sense of security" that has the opposite effect. Antwerp juvenile judge Michiel Steel has noted that Afghan youths are more frequently observed carrying weapons due to feeling vulnerable, and has highlighted the role of social media in escalating conflicts, as documented by [KU Leuven](https://www.law.kuleuven.be/home/onderzoek/nieuws-onderzoek/2025-2026/stefaan-pleysier-over-steekincidenten-met-jongeren).

## What's Next

The victim's life-threatening condition means this case could become a homicide investigation if he does not survive. With nine suspects in custody — including minors — the case will involve both adult criminal court and juvenile justice procedures.

As the investigation continues, the incident is likely to fuel ongoing political debate in Belgium about youth violence, knife crime, and public safety in urban spaces. For the residents of Antwerp, it marks yet another troubling episode in a pattern of violence that has left many questioning what can be done to prevent the next tragedy.

The police investigation remains ongoing, with authorities working to determine the precise motive and the degree of each suspect's involvement.
