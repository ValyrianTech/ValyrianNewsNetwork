# Belgian Man Caught Smuggling Woman Under Bouquet of Flowers in Montenegro

A 34-year-old Belgian man has been arrested at the Montenegro-Bosnia and Herzegovina border after attempting to smuggle a 34-year-old Moroccan woman across the checkpoint by hiding her under luggage and a large bouquet of flowers. The unconventional smuggling attempt was uncovered during a routine border inspection, and both individuals are now in custody.

## The Discovery

The incident occurred at the Vraćenovići-Deleuša border crossing, where the Belgian driver, identified as M.A.A., was attempting to leave Montenegro and enter Bosnia and Herzegovina. During a detailed vehicle inspection, Montenegrin border police discovered the Moroccan woman concealed in the narrow space between the front and rear seats, covered by suitcases and a large bouquet of flowers, according to [Het Laatste Nieuws](https://www.hln.be/nieuws/bizarre-ontdekking-in-montenegro-belg-34-smokkelt-vrouw-onder-grote-bos-bloemen~ad882fcf/).

Authorities confirmed that the woman, a Moroccan national of the same age as the driver, did not possess a visa for Montenegro and had no legal entry into the country on record. The Police Directorate of Montenegro stated that a routine check quickly escalated when officers noticed irregularities in the vehicle's interior.

## Arrest and Charges

The Basic State Prosecutor’s Office in Nikšić was immediately notified of the discovery. Upon the prosecutor’s order, the Belgian driver was arrested on suspicion of human smuggling and illegal border crossing, as reported by [Kurir.rs](https://www.kurir.rs/region/crna-gora/10013943/uhapsen-belgijanac-na-granici-crne-gore-pokusao-da-prokrijumcari-marokanku-skrivenu-u-automobilu). [Informer.rs](https://informer.rs/hronika/region/1120867/krijumcarenje-zena-belgijanac-crna-gora-hapsenje) further detailed that the woman was found covered by both luggage and a significant quantity of flowers, confirming the unusual nature of the concealment method.

Both individuals remain in custody as the investigation continues. The Belgian man faces serious criminal charges that could carry a significant prison sentence under Montenegrin law.

## An Unconventional Method

The use of a large bouquet of flowers as a concealment tool has drawn international attention due to its highly unusual nature. Law enforcement experts suggest that the improvised method points to an amateur, opportunistic smuggling attempt rather than a sophisticated criminal network operation. The fact that both the smuggler and the smuggled person are the same age has also raised questions about their possible relationship, which remains under investigation.

## Broader Context: Smuggling in the Western Balkans

While this particular case is remarkable for its bizarre method, it highlights the ongoing challenges of human smuggling in the Western Balkan region. According to the 2025 Organised Crime Index, the Western Balkans remain a key corridor for human trafficking and migrant smuggling in Europe, as reported by [Balkan Insight](https://balkaninsight.com/2025/11/10/western-balkans-remains-key-smuggling-corridor-in-europe-report/).

Frontex has reported significant declines in irregular border crossings in recent years—a 78% drop in 2024 and a further 42% in 2025—but smuggling networks continue to adapt. In November 2024, a German-led EU operation dismantled a major criminal network involved in migrant smuggling along the Balkan route.

Montenegro, while not central to migrant flows, plays a role in regional illicit markets, particularly through its coastal ports. The Vraćenovići-Deleuša crossing itself is a relatively new joint border facility between Montenegro and Bosnia, with construction completed only in early 2025.

## What’s Next

The investigation is ongoing, with several key questions remaining unanswered. It is unclear whether the Belgian man was acting alone or as part of a larger network, what the intended final destination for the Moroccan woman was, and how she originally entered Montenegro. The Belgian government has not yet publicly commented on the case.

As legal proceedings unfold in Montenegro, this bizarre incident serves as a reminder that smuggling attempts can take many forms—some far more creative than others. The case has captured public attention not only for its unusual method but also for the human story behind an increasingly complex regional challenge.
