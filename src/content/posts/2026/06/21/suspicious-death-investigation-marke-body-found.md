# Suspicious Death Investigation Underway in Marke After Body Found on Street

A passerby discovered the lifeless body of a Polish man on a residential street in Marke, a sub-municipality of Kortrijk in West Flanders, on Saturday afternoon, prompting the public prosecutor's office to open a suspicious death investigation. The discovery was made in the late afternoon on the Rode Dreef, where the man was found near a house undergoing extensive renovations.

## Discovery and Initial Response

According to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/06/21/marke-pool-dood-gevonden-verdacht-overlijden/), a passerby spotted the lifeless body on the street and immediately alerted emergency services. The public prosecutor's office treated the death as suspicious from the outset, deploying the forensic laboratory and a forensic medical examiner to the scene to investigate the cause of death.

The Rode Dreef was closed to traffic for approximately five hours as forensic teams conducted their examination. Photographer Joerie Dewagenaere captured images of emergency services remaining at the location late into the evening.

## Victim and Location

The deceased is a Polish national, though authorities have not publicly released his identity pending notification of next of kin. The body was found at a property on the Rode Dreef that has been undergoing extensive renovations for several weeks, carried out by Polish workers, according to local news outlet [Mandelnieuws.be](https://www.mandelnieuws.be/Artikels/mandelnieuws/marke-verdacht-overlijden).

"Extensive renovations have been underway at that house for several weeks by Polish workers," Mandelnieuws.be reported. "The victim is also said to be Polish, but the public prosecutor's office is still in the dark about the exact circumstances."

[KW.be](https://kw.be/nieuws/samenleving/overlijden/voorbijganger-treft-poolse-man-dood-aan-op-straat-in-kortrijkse-deelgemeente-marke-onderzoek-naar-verdacht-overlijden/) confirmed the details, reporting that the precise circumstances were not immediately clear but that authorities immediately treated the case as a suspicious death.

## Investigation Status

The public prosecutor's office in Kortrijk has launched an inquiry to determine the circumstances surrounding the death. The forensic laboratory and a medical examiner have been appointed to conduct analyses aimed at establishing the cause of death. As of Sunday, June 21, no further details have been released regarding potential suspects or witnesses.

In Belgian law, a "verdacht overlijden" (suspicious death) designation means that the circumstances suggest the possibility of a criminal act — such as homicide or manslaughter — as opposed to a natural death or accident. The involvement of the forensic lab indicates authorities are treating the case with a high degree of seriousness.

## Context and Unanswered Questions

Marke is a quiet residential sub-municipality of Kortrijk in the province of West Flanders, making this an unusual incident for the area. Several key questions remain unanswered as the investigation enters its early stages:

- **Cause of death:** Was it homicide, an accident, or natural causes? The suspicious designation suggests authorities have reason to believe it may not be a natural death.
- **Connection to the renovation work:** Was the victim one of the Polish workers renovating the house, or was he otherwise connected to the property?
- **Witnesses:** Were there any witnesses to the incident, and have neighbors provided any information?

Polish workers are commonly employed in the construction and renovation sectors in Belgium, often working on short-term or project-based contracts. The presence of Polish workers renovating the house in Marke is consistent with broader labor migration patterns within the Belgian construction industry.

## What's Next

The investigation remains ongoing. The forensic medical examiner's findings will be crucial in determining whether the case proceeds as a criminal investigation. Authorities have not announced whether an investigating judge has been appointed, which would indicate the case is being treated as a potential homicide.

Further updates are expected once the forensic analysis is complete and next of kin have been notified. The Polish consulate in Belgium may also become involved as the identification process continues.
