# Belle Perez Returns to Stage After Husband's Death

Belgian singer Belle Perez (50) made an emotional return to the stage on Friday night, performing her first major concert since the unexpected death of her husband, Wouter van der Horst, six months ago. Taking the stage at "De Foute Party" organized by Qmusic at Flanders Expo in Ghent, Perez received a thunderous standing ovation from the crowd before telling them, "It feels good to be on stage again."

## A Painful Loss

Wouter van der Horst, a 43-year-old Dutch jeweler and Perez's husband of nearly a decade, died suddenly on December 16-17, 2025, after suffering a brain aneurysm at home. According to [Het Laatste Nieuws](https://www.hln.be/bv/echtgenoot-43-van-belle-perez-onverwacht-overleden-wouter-cijferde-zich-graag-voor-haar-weg-die-twee-waren-echt-een-topkoppel~a13a1ce5/), he was rushed to hospital but could not be saved. The tragedy struck just one day after the family had celebrated the ninth birthday of their son, Ellia.

The couple, who married on June 16, 2016, arriving by helicopter at the Herenthout town hall, were described by their inner circle as a "power couple" who loved each other deeply. Van der Horst, who preferred to stay out of the spotlight, was Perez's steadfast partner through the highs and lows of her career.

## Music as Therapy

In the months following her husband's death, Perez canceled her scheduled Christmas concerts and retreated from public life to grieve. In an Instagram post shared days after the tragedy, she wrote: "Gone so suddenly, that our hearts cannot yet follow. Life changed in one breath. What remained was love and the terrible loss."

But as [RTL Boulevard](https://www.rtl.nl/boulevard/artikel/5621586/belle-perez-eerst-weer-podium-overlijden-man) reported, Perez gradually found the strength to return to what she knows best. "I'm still going through a difficult period, but I realize more than ever that music is therapy," she said ahead of the performance. "That's why I've decided to start singing again."

## The Comeback

The performance at De Foute Party — an annual event featuring popular Flemish artists performing upbeat hits — marked a significant milestone in Perez's grieving process. She opened her set with "Que viva la vida," one of her signature Latin-pop songs, and delivered a string of her greatest hits to an enthusiastic audience.

Addressing the crowd, Perez said: "Dear people, it feels good to be on stage again. Tonight we'll let the music speak and hopefully it will resonate. For me it certainly does." For the rest of the evening, she let her music do the talking, making no further mention of the tragedy that has marked her family's life.

The event also featured performances by other well-known Flemish artists including Ingeborg, Natalia, Danzel, K3, Laïs, and De Romeo's, who were joined by Q-dj Vincent Fierens and Gerben Tuerlinckx.

## A Community's Support

The article from Het Laatste Nieuws quickly became one of the most-shared stories on the platform, with readers flooding the comments section with messages of support. Comments described Perez as a "strong lady" and welcomed her back to the stage, with many readers sharing personal stories of their own experiences with loss.

## What's Next

According to reports, Perez is scheduled to perform at the Afro-Latino festival this summer, suggesting a gradual return to her performance schedule. The coming months carry emotional weight — they would have included her 50th birthday celebrations and the 10th anniversary of her marriage to Wouter, milestones she had been looking forward to before his death.

For now, Perez's return to the stage serves as a powerful reminder of the healing power of music and the resilience of the human spirit. As she continues to navigate life after an unimaginable loss, her fans — in Belgium and beyond — will undoubtedly be cheering her on every step of the way.
