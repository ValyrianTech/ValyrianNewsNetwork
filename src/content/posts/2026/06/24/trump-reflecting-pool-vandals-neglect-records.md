# Trump Blames Vandals for Reflecting Pool Crisis; Internal Records Tell Another Story

President Donald Trump has blamed vandals for the rapid deterioration of the newly renovated Lincoln Memorial Reflecting Pool, claiming that "sick, deranged people" used knives to cut a gash in the pool's sealant and poured corrosive chemicals into the water. But internal records obtained by [The New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/06/23/us/trump-reflecting-pool-green-peeling.html) and reporting from multiple news outlets paint a starkly different picture — one of rushed no-bid contracting, inadequate maintenance, and natural environmental conditions.

## The Renovation and Its Problems

The reflecting pool, a centerpiece of the National Mall since 1922, was closed for renovations earlier this year. Trump awarded a no-bid contract worth approximately $14.7 million to Atlantic Industrial Coatings, a Virginia-based company with no prior federal contracts, to repaint the pool "American flag blue" — a dark navy shade officially called "Old Glory Blue." The project was originally announced at $1.8 million, according to [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/jun/22/what-happened-dc-reflecting-pool-vandalism-arrest-algae) and The New York Times.

A second no-bid contract worth $1.7 million for a water filtration system was awarded to Greenwater Services, owned by a trust led by John J. Cafaro, a Trump donor and neighbor of Mar-a-Lago, as reported by The New York Times and Snopes.

The pool reopened on June 6, 2026. Within days, algae blooms turned the water green, and the blue coating began peeling off the bottom, with chunks floating to the surface.

## Trump's Vandalism Claims

Trump first blamed vandals on June 19, posting on Truth Social that someone had created a gash in the pool's sealant. Over successive posts, the alleged gash grew from 250 feet to 300 feet to 350 feet. He also claimed vandals poured "corrosive and destructive chemicals" into the water.

"I can't help it if somebody goes in with a knife and starts hacking it up," Trump told reporters in the Oval Office on June 22, according to The Guardian.

Despite repeated claims, Trump has not provided any photographic or video evidence of vandalism. [Snopes](https://www.snopes.com/news/2026/06/24/trump-vandals-reflecting-pool/) reported that the White House and Interior Department did not respond to requests for evidence. Reporters from The Washington Post who inspected the reflecting pool on June 21 found no signs of vandalism, and at least eight officers patrolling the site also found no evidence.

## The Arrests

Six people have reportedly been arrested in connection with the pool. However, reports indicate those arrested were tourists or curious individuals who touched or retrieved pieces of peeling paint from the water — not vandals causing damage.

Among them is David Hearn, a 67-year-old three-time U.S. Olympic canoe racer, who was arrested on June 19 after reaching into the pool to examine a piece of the peeling coating. He was detained for five hours and faces a misdemeanor charge of destruction of government property. "I didn't vandalize anything," Hearn told [the BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c8d26051vv2o). "I didn't destroy or break or peel anything."

## Expert Analysis: A Natural Phenomenon

Scientists and aquatic ecology experts have confirmed that the algae bloom is a natural phenomenon — "new pond syndrome" — caused by shallow, stagnant water in summer conditions. The renovation disturbance accelerated the process.

"It's called 'new pond syndrome,'" Steve Goodale, a swimming pool specialist known as "Swimming Pool Steve," told [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/19/nx-s1-5863044/dc-reflecting-pool-algae-green-trump). "It's a known thing that happens when you take a natural, clear body of water like this that sits in an open air environment and you try to start it up, very often you end up with green water almost immediately."

Rosalina Stancheva Christova, a professor of aquatic ecology at George Mason University, confirmed to NPR that the algae (genus Desmodesmus) is not toxic or harmful, but is "growing in excessive amounts" due to the pool's excellent conditions for algae growth: strong sunlight and shallow, stagnant water.

## Internal Records and the Hydrogen Peroxide Treatment

National Park Service workers poured hydrogen peroxide into the pool to kill the algae. Snopes noted that hydrogen peroxide can also act as a paint remover, though it's unclear if the amount used was sufficient to cause the peeling.

The New York Times obtained internal records that do not indicate the peeling blue coating and algae blooms were caused intentionally. The documents point to maintenance failures and rushed work.

## Political Fallout

The story has become a flashpoint in the broader political landscape. Senator Adam Schiff (D-CA) criticized the administration on Truth Social, writing: "President Trump wasted your tax dollars on a poor paint job for the Reflecting Pool, pouring millions down the drain. Now he's wasting more money detaining tourists who take a close look at the botched repairs."

The controversy unfolds ahead of the nation's 250th anniversary (Semiquincentennial) celebrations on July 4, 2026, as part of a broader pattern of Trump-ordered renovations to Washington landmarks that have drawn scrutiny over cost overruns, no-bid contracting, and potential conflicts of interest.

## What's Next

It remains unclear whether Trump will ever provide evidence of vandalism, or whether charges against the six arrested individuals will be pursued or dropped. The pool may need to be fully drained and repainted again, adding to the cost. Meanwhile, questions persist about whether there will be any investigation into the no-bid contracting process that awarded millions to companies with personal ties to the president.

As the July 4th celebrations approach, the reflecting pool — meant to be a symbol of national pride — has instead become a symbol of the tensions between preservation, politics, and accountability.
