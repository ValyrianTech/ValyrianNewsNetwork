# Southern China Braces for Heavy Rainfall as Flood Season Intensifies

Southern China has entered a concentrated period of heavy rainfall, with six provinces and regions facing severe downpours and potential flooding as the annual flood season intensifies. Jiangxi province activated a Level IV emergency flood response on Saturday as authorities warned of localized extreme rainstorms threatening transportation, agriculture, and public safety across the affected areas.

## Widespread Rainfall and Emergency Response

According to [China Weather Network via Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260613/d42592150b8d4474a374e89b1f3157d5/c.html), the concentrated heavy rainfall period has begun in earnest, with rainfall significantly intensifying on June 13. The scope of heavy to torrential rain is expanding across Guangxi, Guangdong, Hunan, Jiangxi, Zhejiang, and Fujian, with some areas expected to experience extreme downpours.

[Jiangxi province](https://www.news.cn/local/20260613/f98e70cef409484cb1958d7809567314/c.html) took early action by activating its Level IV emergency flood response on the morning of June 13, as announced by the Jiangxi Flood Control and Drought Relief Headquarters. The provincial authority urged residents to exercise caution near mountainous areas, river channels, and low-lying regions, warning of potential flash floods, geological disasters, and urban waterlogging.

The activation follows a broader national response. On June 12, the National Flood Control Headquarters activated a Level IV emergency flood response for seven provinces — Guangdong, Guangxi, Jiangxi, Fujian, Zhejiang, Guizhou, and Hunan — and dispatched two working groups to assist with flood prevention efforts, [CCTV News reported](https://news.cctv.com/2026/06/12/ARTI4mG1QGPO1WYvfasSh3HF260612.shtml).

## Rivers at Risk and Pre-Season Context

China officially entered its annual flood season on June 1, 2026. The Ministry of Water Resources has warned that precipitation in parts of southern China is projected to exceed average levels by 10 to 20 percent during June, [according to ECNS.cn](https://www.ecns.cn/cns-wire/2026-06-02/detail-ihffcfqs3229707.shtml). Rivers connected to Dongting Lake and Poyang Lake in the middle reaches of the Yangtze River, as well as the Xijiang and Hanjiang rivers in the Pearl River basin, could see water levels exceed flood warning thresholds.

This year's flooding follows an already turbulent pre-season period. Multiple episodes of extreme rainfall hit southern and central China throughout April and May 2026, with over 200 millimeters recorded in 12-hour periods in parts of Guangdong and Guangxi. The overlapping rainfall patterns with already saturated ground significantly elevate the risk of flash floods, landslides, and urban waterlogging.

## Evacuations and Agricultural Threats

Nearly 10,000 residents were evacuated in Guizhou after torrential rains caused flooding, [Carbon Brief reported](https://www.carbonbrief.org/china-briefing-11-june-2026-tech-clampdown-extreme-weather-provinces-energy-plans/), citing China Daily and Bloomberg. Flood-response measures have also been activated in Hunan and Guangxi.

Southern China is a key producer of rice and other staple crops, and the heavy rainfall poses significant risks of crop damage from waterlogging and soil erosion. Expected further rains could inundate crops and damage rice fields, raising concerns about potential food price impacts in the coming months.

## Climate Change Context

Meteorologists have attributed the unusually early and intense rainfall to shifting weather patterns associated with global warming. The People's Daily has noted that "against the backdrop of intensifying global warming, northern China is seeing a clear trend of more frequent and severe extreme weather." Bloomberg reported that meteorologists attribute the pattern to broader weather changes in China linked to global warming.

## Temperature Impacts and Outlook

The heavy rainfall is expected to bring temperature drops across affected regions. Cities in Hunan, Jiangxi, and Zhejiang will see temperatures fall from above 30°C to around 25°C, while Beijing and Tianjin will experience highs around 27°C. Continued heavy rain is forecast through at least June 15 across southern China.

## What to Watch

As the rainfall event progresses, authorities will be monitoring for specific damage and casualty figures, the impact on China's agricultural output and food prices, and how the concurrent heatwave in northern China affects overall disaster management capacity. The situation underscores the growing challenges China faces as climate change intensifies extreme weather patterns across the country.
