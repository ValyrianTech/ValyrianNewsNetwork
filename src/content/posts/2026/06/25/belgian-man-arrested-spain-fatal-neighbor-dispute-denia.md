# Belgian Man Arrested in Spain After Fatal Neighbor Dispute

A 68-year-old Belgian man has been arrested in the Spanish coastal town of Dénia, Alicante, after a long-running neighbor dispute turned deadly on Wednesday afternoon. The suspect allegedly pushed his 43-year-old Spanish neighbor during a heated argument, causing the victim to fall backward and strike his head and neck on the ground, according to [Het Laatste Nieuws](https://www.hln.be/nieuws/belg-68-opgepakt-in-spaanse-badplaats-na-burenruzie-met-dodelijke-afloop~ae4b55977/).

## The Incident

The fatal altercation occurred in the late afternoon on Wednesday, June 24, 2026, in the parking area of the Paloví urbanization in Les Deveses, a residential zone near Dénia on Spain's Costa Blanca. According to initial police findings cited by multiple Spanish media outlets, the Belgian suspect pushed his neighbor hard during a physical confrontation that escalated from a verbal argument.

The victim fell backward and struck his head and neck against the ground. An emergency medical team (SAMU) arrived at the scene and attempted to resuscitate him but could only confirm his death, as [Telecinco](https://www.telecinco.es/noticias/sucesos/20260624/empuja-vecino-discutian-provoca-muerte-golpearse-cuello-suelo-denia_18_019540929.html) reported.

## Confession and Arrest

In a notable development, the suspect himself contacted emergency services and admitted his involvement at the scene. [Infobae](https://www.infobae.com/espana/2026/06/24/la-policia-nacional-detiene-a-un-hombre-de-68-anos-por-causar-presuntamente-la-muerte-de-un-vecino-de-43-anos-en-denia/), citing the Spanish state news agency EFE, reported that the alleged aggressor alerted emergency services and acknowledged the facts. A female neighbor also called the 112 emergency number after witnessing the incident.

Following a body recovery and scene reconstruction conducted by the National Police on Wednesday evening, the Belgian suspect was arrested and transferred to police cells at the Dénia National Police station.

## History of Conflict

The two neighbors had been in conflict for years over cohabitation issues, according to [Cadena SER](https://cadenaser.com/comunitat-valenciana/2026/06/25/muere-un-hombre-de-43-anos-tras-una-pelea-con-un-vecino-en-una-urbanizacion-de-denia-radio-denia/). While the exact trigger for Wednesday's fatal argument has not been publicly disclosed, investigators confirmed that longstanding disagreements between the two men had previously led to altercations.

## Legal Proceedings

The suspect is expected to appear before a judge on Friday, June 26, 2026. Authorities are awaiting autopsy results from the Institute of Forensic Medicine in Alicante to determine the exact cause of death, though initial hypotheses suggest the victim died from the impact of the fall.

Under Spanish law, the suspect could face charges of homicide or manslaughter, depending on the autopsy findings and the determination of intent. The fact that the suspect called emergency services and confessed may be considered a mitigating factor in the judicial process, as [El Español](https://www.elespanol.com/alicante/marina-alta/20260625/detenido-causar-muerte-vecino-discusion-denia/1003744299270_0.html) noted.

## Broader Context

Dénia, located approximately 50 kilometers north of Benidorm on the Costa Blanca, is a popular tourist destination with a significant expatriate community, including many Belgian and other European retirees. The incident has drawn particular attention in Belgian media due to the nationality of the suspect.

The case echoes a similar incident referenced by HLN, in which a 54-year-old Belgian man was imprisoned in France for the murder of a retired couple following a dispute over a barking dog, highlighting a pattern of fatal neighbor disputes involving Belgian nationals abroad.

## What's Next

The investigation remains open as authorities await forensic results to confirm the precise cause of death. The suspect's name has not been publicly released, and no official statements from Belgian consular services in Spain have been issued at this time. The case will proceed through the Spanish judicial system, with the initial court appearance expected on Friday.
