# Bidens Return to Public Stage Amid Stage 4 Cancer Revelation

Former President Joe Biden is stepping back into the public spotlight, appearing at a sold-out Democratic Party dinner in Sioux Falls, South Dakota, even as his wife reveals he is living with stage 4 metastatic prostate cancer. The 83-year-old former president delivered the keynote address at the South Dakota Democratic Party's McGovern Day Dinner on June 5, 2026, at the Best Western Plus Ramkota Exhibit Hall, marking his most prominent public appearance since leaving office in January 2025.

The re-emergence comes as Dr. Jill Biden, in a series of media interviews promoting her memoir "View from the East Wing," has disclosed intimate details about her husband's health, revealing that he was diagnosed with an aggressive form of prostate cancer in May 2025 that had already spread to his bones. According to [The New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/06/06/us/politics/biden-speech-sioux-falls-trump.html), the former first couple's return to public life has been met with mixed reactions within the Democratic Party.

## A Candid Health Disclosure

In an interview on the Today Show on June 1, Jill Biden told host Craig Melvin that her husband's cancer had metastasized, fundamentally changing his prognosis. "I think if he had just been diagnosed with prostate cancer, that's one thing, because that can be cured," she said. "But the fact that it metastasized to his bones, that makes it a whole different story. So I think Joe will live with cancer 'till the rest of his life."

Speaking further at a book event with political commentator Ana Navarro on June 6, Jill Biden elaborated on the former president's current condition. "He keeps his schedule, but he's slowed down," she said. "I mean, stage four cancer is — and he's 83 — so, I think the mix of everything and the medications that he's taken has made life a little more difficult these days."

Biden underwent five weeks of radiation therapy, traveling from Delaware to Philadelphia for treatment, and remains on hormone therapy and specialized medications. He also underwent surgery for skin cancer in September 2025. Jill Biden described her role as her husband's primary caretaker, managing his medications, doctor's appointments, and diet.

## The McGovern Day Appearance

Biden's speech at McGovern Day — an annual event named after the late Senator George McGovern, the 1972 Democratic presidential nominee — drew a sold-out crowd. He shared the stage with Democratic candidates including U.S. Senate candidate Julian Beaudion, U.S. House candidate Nikki Gronli, and gubernatorial candidate Dan Ahlers. South Dakota Democratic Party Chair Shane Merrill called Biden's visit a "privilege."

The appearance marked Biden's first visit to South Dakota since 2012, when he attended McGovern's funeral as Vice President. Despite his health challenges, Jill Biden noted that her husband remains active, traveling on Amtrak, attending veterans' ceremonies, and making speeches.

## Political Implications and Party Tensions

Biden's return to public life arrives at a delicate moment for Democrats, who are preparing for the 2026 midterm elections while still grappling with the aftermath of the 2024 presidential loss. Some party members have expressed discomfort with the former president's re-emergence, preferring to focus on future leadership rather than revisit the Biden era.

Jill Biden has been candid about the lingering wounds from the 2024 campaign. She recounted that after Biden's disastrous June debate performance against Donald Trump — during which he appeared confused and spoke with a raspy voice — her husband told her, "I have no choice," before dropping out of the race in July 2024. She admitted she still has not spoken to former House Speaker Nancy Pelosi, whom she holds responsible for the pressure campaign that pushed Biden to withdraw.

## A Legacy in Transition

For now, the Bidens are navigating a profoundly personal chapter in the public eye. Jill Biden's memoir offers an unvarnished account of the final year of the Biden presidency, including the health struggles that were not publicly known at the time. The former president, meanwhile, continues to engage with the party he led, even as Democrats chart a course toward an uncertain future.

As the 2026 midterms approach, the question of how the party balances honoring Biden's legacy with the need for new leadership remains unresolved. What is clear is that the Bidens — online, in bookstores, and at a Best Western in Sioux Falls — are not stepping quietly out of the public consciousness.
