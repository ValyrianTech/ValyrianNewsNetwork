# Gunman Kills 4 at Detroit Suburb Home, Surrenders

LIVONIA, Mich. — A 25-year-old man fatally shot four people at a home in this quiet Detroit suburb on Tuesday afternoon, killing his parents, his brother, and his brother's girlfriend before surrendering immediately to police, authorities said.

The suspect emerged from the house on the 1900 block of Rensellor Road with his hands raised and fingers interlocked, telling officers he had shot his family members, according to [Livonia Police Chief Tom Goralski](https://apnews.com/article/livonia-michigan-four-dead-ba4451967bdcf4bfa2358c84a1a18b28). He was taken into custody without incident, and a weapon was recovered at the scene.

## The Victims

Officers responding to reports of gunshots just after 5:30 p.m. on June 9 discovered two victims in the backyard and two more inside the home. All four were pronounced dead at the scene.

The deceased were identified as:
- **Sterling Pierce**, 58, the suspect's father
- **Holly Kimball**, 53, the suspect's mother
- **Tanner Pierce**, 22, the suspect's brother
- A 21-year-old woman, Tanner Pierce's girlfriend, whose name has not been released

[FOX 2 Detroit](https://www.fox2detroit.com/news/livonia-mass-shooting-leaves-four-dead-one-arrested-area-7-mile-inkster-roads) reported that three of the four victims were family members of the suspect, while the fourth was in a relationship with one of the victims. The two men and two women all died at the scene from gunshot wounds.

## Investigation Underway

Chief Goralski told reporters that the suspect had a "contentious relationship" with his parents, though he cautioned that investigators do not yet know whether that was a factor in the attack.

"We don't know why this happened," Goralski said. "Whether that had anything to do with what occurred, we don't know yet."

Livonia police had no record of prior calls for service at the home, suggesting no known history of domestic disturbances at the address. The suspect's name has not been released by authorities as the investigation continues, and police have not disclosed whether the weapon used in the shooting was legally obtained.

## A Community in Shock

Livonia, a suburban city of approximately 93,000 residents located about 20 miles northwest of Detroit, is generally regarded as a safe, middle-class community. The mass shooting has left residents shaken and searching for answers.

"This is a tragic event for our city," Chief Goralski said, as reported by the Associated Press.

Local residents described the neighborhood on Rensellor Road as a quiet, family-oriented area where such violence is virtually unheard of. The shooting has prompted an outpouring of grief and support from the community, with neighbors leaving flowers and messages at the scene.

## Broader Context

The incident adds to a troubling pattern of family-related mass shootings in the United States. Family annihilations — in which a perpetrator kills multiple relatives — represent a distinct subset of mass violence that often involves domestic strife, mental health issues, or financial pressures. According to research on mass shootings, family-related incidents account for a significant portion of multiple-victim homicides each year.

The suspect's immediate surrender is somewhat atypical in such cases, where perpetrators sometimes flee or turn the weapon on themselves. His cooperation with authorities may aid investigators in determining a motive, though the investigation remains in its early stages. Experts note that family annihilations are often preceded by identifiable warning signs, including financial stress, relationship breakdowns, or untreated mental illness.

[CBS Detroit via Yahoo News](https://www.yahoo.com/news/videos/four-people-killed-livonia-shooting-030800047.html) confirmed that one suspect was taken into custody and a weapon was recovered from the scene. Officials have stated there is no ongoing threat to the public, and the neighborhood is considered safe.

## What's Next

Authorities are expected to file charges against the suspect in the coming days, with potential counts including four counts of first-degree murder. The suspect remains in custody as the investigation continues.

Key unanswered questions include:
- What was the specific motive behind the attack?
- Does the suspect have a history of mental health issues or prior violent behavior?
- How was the weapon obtained, and was it legally purchased?
- Why was the brother's girlfriend, who was not a direct family member, also targeted?

The investigation remains active, and police have urged anyone with information to come forward. The community of Livonia is left mourning as it grapples with one of the most violent incidents in the city's recent history, a tragedy that has shattered the peace of this suburban Detroit community. As the investigation unfolds, residents and officials alike are hoping for answers that might help make sense of a senseless act of violence.
