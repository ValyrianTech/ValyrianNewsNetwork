# China Overhauls Housing Provident Fund in Major Policy Expansion

China has unveiled its most significant overhaul of the Housing Provident Fund (HPF) system in decades, proposing a draft revision that dramatically expands how the country's 200 million-plus contributors can use their housing savings. The reforms, announced by the Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development (MOHURD) on June 5, mark the third revision of the HPF regulations since their inception, following updates in 2002 and 2019, as [reported by CCTV News](http://m.cnwest.com/tianxia/a/2026/06/07/23370041.html).

## What Is Changing

The revised draft expands permitted withdrawal scenarios from six to nine categories, adding three new allowances: renovation of self-occupied housing, property management fees, and a catch-all clause for "other housing consumption situations approved by the State Council." Rental withdrawal criteria have also been simplified from requiring rent to exceed a specified proportion of household income to simply requiring proof of rent payment, according to [People's Daily](http://finance.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0610/c1004-40737472.html).

Perhaps most significantly, the draft explicitly allows individual businesses, part-time workers, and other flexible employment personnel to voluntarily participate in the HPF system — a move that could extend housing security to China's estimated 200 million gig economy workers, delivery drivers, and freelancers.

## Three Major Transformations

Wang Lixin, a reporter for Securities Daily writing in People's Daily, characterized the reforms as representing three fundamental shifts: from buying to maintaining homes, from local to mutual recognition, and from employees to gig workers.

**From Buying to Maintaining:** The expansion of HPF usage to cover renovation and property management fees creates what analysts call a "full-chain" support system for housing consumption, covering purchase, rental, renovation, and maintenance. For households without immediate purchase plans, this effectively unlocks idle savings for everyday housing costs.

**From Local to Mutual Recognition:** The draft mandates strengthening digital capabilities to promote cross-regional, cross-departmental business coordination and mutual recognition of HPF contributions and loans. This addresses a long-standing pain point for China's mobile workforce, who have often struggled to use HPF funds when working outside their home province. Nanjing has already expanded its cross-regional loan coverage to the entire Anhui Province, as detailed by People's Daily.

**From Employees to Gig Workers:** The formal inclusion of flexible employment workers addresses what experts have long identified as a critical gap in China's housing security net. According to the [China Index Academy via 36Kr](https://36kr.com/p/3843948897536257), as of end-2024, 36 cities had piloted HPF participation for flexible workers, with over 1 million such workers contributing and 240,000 having used HPF for renting or home purchases.

## Expert Analysis

Wu Jing, Director of Tsinghua University's Real Estate Research Center, told CCTV News that the revision expands coverage in response to changes in employment structure. "This adjustment primarily responds to changes in the employment structure," Wu said, noting that the extension of withdrawal purposes to renovation and property management fees makes HPF support "more comprehensive and multi-dimensional."

However, Wu also cautioned about implementation challenges. "Different cities have vastly different levels of usable HPF funds. If withdrawal purposes are expanded too broadly or too quickly, especially with a one-size-fits-all approach ignoring local differences, it could impact the normal operation of HPF in some cities," he told CCTV News.

## Local Implementation Underway

Guangzhou became the first major city to issue implementing regulations following the national draft. On June 8, the Guangzhou HPF Management Center released new loan measures effective June 9, including new loans for construction, renovation, and major repair; relaxed eligibility for foreign nationals; an increased loan calculation coefficient (from 8x to 10x account balance multiplier); and extended loan terms up to five years past retirement age, as [reported by 36Kr](https://36kr.com/p/3844336426650115).

Nationwide, the pace of HPF policy adjustments has been remarkable. According to the China Index Academy, over 250 HPF-related policy adjustments have been implemented nationwide in 2026 alone, with more than 180 localities optimizing withdrawal policies and over 130 expanding the scope of allowable uses.

## Broader Economic Significance

The reforms come amid China's broader efforts to stabilize its real estate market and boost domestic consumption. By unlocking HPF funds for renovation and maintenance, the policy effectively converts idle savings into active consumption. The China Index Academy noted that deepening HPF reform will be "one of the key priorities of government work in 2026 and the coming years," as reported by 36Kr.

The public comment period on the draft revision runs until July 5, 2026, after which the final regulations are expected to be published. Individual cities will then develop their own implementation细则, creating what analysts expect to be significant variation across different regions.

## What to Watch For

Key questions remain: How will individual cities balance expanded withdrawal options with fund sustainability? Will the voluntary participation model for flexible workers achieve meaningful coverage rates? And what impact will these changes have on China's commercial housing loan market? The answers will shape the effectiveness of what is arguably the most significant reform to China's housing security system in a generation.
