# Navigation Warning: Military Training in Beibu Gulf

Chinese maritime authorities have issued a navigation warning announcing a scheduled military training exercise in parts of the Beibu Gulf, also known as the Gulf of Tonkin. The training is set to take place on June 22 from 12:00 to 14:00 Beijing time, according to [CCTV News](https://news.cctv.com/2026/06/20/ARTIf5yuUSJxm3KM4XPZuEQD260620.shtml).

## Warning Details

The Beihai Maritime Safety Administration, operating under the China Maritime Safety Administration, issued the warning on June 20. All vessels are prohibited from entering the designated training area during the two-hour exercise window. The [Beijing Daily](https://news.bjd.com.cn/2026/06/20/11817164.shtml) reported the warning as early as 08:27 Beijing time, citing the China Maritime Safety Administration website.

## Routine Activity

Navigation warnings for military training in the Beibu Gulf are issued frequently and routinely by Chinese authorities. Similar warnings have been issued multiple times throughout 2026, including live-fire exercises in January and multi-day training sessions in March. The short two-hour duration and standard phrasing indicate this is a focused, limited training evolution rather than a major military exercise.

## Regional Context

The Beibu Gulf is a strategically important semi-enclosed sea bordered by southern China and northern Vietnam. Despite being a maritime boundary zone, China and Vietnam maintain a robust framework of cooperation in the gulf, including joint naval patrols, coast guard operations, and fishery conservation efforts. The two countries have conducted 40 joint naval patrols and 30 joint coast guard patrols, with bilateral agreements governing the delimitation of the gulf dating back to 2004.

## No Geopolitical Significance

There has been no international reaction or concern regarding the announced training, which would be expected for a significant military development. The warning was widely republished by Chinese state media outlets including Xinhua Daily, CNR, and Guangming Daily, but all simply relayed the same basic information without additional commentary or analysis.

## What to Watch

While the specific type of training — whether naval gunnery, anti-submarine warfare, or other maneuvers — has not been disclosed, the limited duration suggests a routine operational evolution. Civilian maritime traffic and fishing activities in the region may face minor disruptions during the two-hour window on June 22.
