# Chen Zhongwei Appointed Jiangsu Party Secretary-General

Chen Zhongwei, the newly appointed member of the Jiangsu Provincial Party Standing Committee, has been named Secretary-General of the Provincial Party Committee and Secretary of the Provincial Organs Work Committee, according to updated information published on the Jiangsu Provincial Party Committee News Network on June 26, 2026. The appointment marks the latest step in a steady career ascent for the 57-year-old official, who has held key posts in work safety regulation, emergency management, and local government leadership across Jiangsu Province.

## Context

The Secretary-General of a provincial party committee is a senior leadership position responsible for coordinating the daily operations of the provincial party apparatus, managing communications, overseeing administrative affairs, and ensuring the implementation of party decisions. The role also typically includes serving as Secretary of the Provincial Organs Work Committee, which oversees party-building work within provincial government organs and institutions.

Chen Zhongwei's appointment to this influential coordinating role comes just ten days after the Central Committee of the Chinese Communist Party approved his elevation to the Jiangsu Provincial Party Standing Committee on June 16, as reported by [Jiaohuidian News](https://www.zgjssw.gov.cn/yaowen/202606/t20260616_8578597.shtml). His rapid assumption of the Secretary-General portfolio signals strong confidence from higher leadership.

## Key Developments

According to [The Paper](https://www.thepaper.cn/newsDetail_forward_33457831), a major Chinese news outlet, Chen Zhongwei was born in June 1969 in Chaozhou, Guangdong Province. He holds a graduate degree from the Provincial Party School and is a member of the Chinese Communist Party.

His career trajectory reflects a typical path for senior Chinese officials, with progressive advancement through regulatory, provincial department, and local government roles:

- **Regulatory foundation:** Chen spent many years in work safety regulation, serving as Deputy Director of the Jiangsu Provincial Administration of Work Safety and as a Party Leadership Group member of the Jiangsu Coal Mine Safety Supervision Bureau.
- **Provincial department leadership:** In February 2018, he became Director and Party Secretary of the Jiangsu Provincial Administration of Work Safety, later concurrently serving as Director of the Jiangsu Coal Mine Safety Supervision Bureau. Following China's 2018 institutional reforms, he was appointed Director of the newly formed Jiangsu Provincial Department of Emergency Management.
- **Local government experience:** In December 2020, Chen was transferred to Suqian City as Deputy Party Secretary and Acting Mayor. He was elected Mayor in January 2021, appointed Party Secretary of Suqian in March 2023, and promoted to Vice Governor of Jiangsu Province in July 2024.

His official profile on the [Jiangsu Provincial Party Committee website](https://www.zgjssw.gov.cn/baodaoji/chenzhongwei/) now lists him as a member of the Jiangsu Provincial Party Standing Committee, Provincial Party Committee Secretary-General, and Secretary of the Provincial Organs Work Committee.

## Analysis

Chen Zhongwei's appointment carries several notable implications. First, the Secretary-General position is a critical coordinating role within the provincial party apparatus, and his appointment to this post immediately upon joining the Standing Committee — in less than two years since his promotion to Vice Governor — represents a relatively rapid advancement that suggests strong backing from higher leadership.

Second, Chen brings a distinctive background to the provincial leadership. Unlike many senior officials who come from economic management, propaganda, or organizational department backgrounds, Chen's expertise lies in work safety regulation and emergency management. This experience could influence Jiangsu's approach to disaster preparedness and public safety governance.

Third, his successful tenure as Party Secretary of Suqian — a prefecture-level city in northern Jiangsu — likely contributed to his promotion. Local leadership performance is a key factor in cadre advancement within the Chinese political system, and his track record in Suqian appears to have positioned him well for provincial-level responsibilities.

## What's Next

As Secretary-General, Chen Zhongwei will be responsible for the smooth functioning of the Jiangsu Provincial Party Committee's daily operations, a role that places him at the center of the province's political coordination. The Jiangsu Provincial Party Standing Committee, which now includes Chen as its newest member, remains the top decision-making body for one of China's most economically significant provinces.

Observers will watch for any policy directions or administrative priorities that may reflect Chen's background in emergency management and public safety. His appointment also completes a significant leadership reshuffle within Jiangsu's party structure, with implications for how the province navigates its policy agenda in the second half of 2026 and beyond.
