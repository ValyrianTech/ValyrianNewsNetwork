# Fatal Scooter Dispute in Charleroi: Man Drowned in Sambre River

A young man drowned in the Sambre River in Charleroi, Belgium, during the night of Thursday 25 to Friday 26 June 2026, after a dispute over a suspected stolen scooter escalated into a deadly confrontation. The victim was pursued, beaten, and thrown into the river near the Pont de la Résistance in central Charleroi, according to the [Charleroi prosecutor's office](https://www.hln.be/binnenland/ruzie-over-vermoedelijk-gestolen-step-loopt-fataal-af-man-geduwd-in-samber-en-verdronken~a54c7793a/).

## The Incident

The altercation began when two men argued over an electric scooter that was allegedly stolen. According to the public prosecutor's office, the victim — who was driving the scooter — was accused of having stolen the vehicle and was pursued by an individual who wanted to recover it. The suspect caught up with the victim near the Pont de la Résistance, a central bridge spanning the Sambre River in Charleroi.

[RTBF](https://www.rtbf.be/article/charleroi-il-poursuit-un-conducteur-de-trottinette-le-roue-de-coups-avant-de-le-jeter-dans-la-sambre-la-victime-est-decedee-11747863) reported that a fight broke out between the two men, during which the suspect struck the victim multiple times before pushing him into the Sambre River. The young man drowned in the water. The Charleroi prosecutor's office confirmed the details to local press, stating that the confrontation began as a verbal argument before escalating into a chase and then a physical assault.

## Emergency Response

Firefighters and divers from the Hainaut-Est rescue zone were dispatched to the scene and recovered the victim's lifeless body from the river. Emergency services had arrived in force after receiving reports of the altercation, but were unable to save the man. The recovery operation drew attention from passersby in the central Charleroi area, as rescue teams worked through the early morning hours.

## Victim and Suspect

The victim has not yet been identified. According to the prosecutor's office, he was staying illegally in Belgium, which may be complicating efforts to establish his identity and notify next of kin. Local media, including [L'Avenir](https://www.lavenir.net/regions/charleroi/charleroi/2026/06/26/un-homme-meurt-noye-dans-la-sambre-a-charleroi-apres-une-altercation-pour-une-trottinette-P4KPXMRLG5AMDNTLEZVK4YGI5E/), confirmed that the victim was a young man, though his exact age has not been disclosed.

The suspect, who allegedly pursued the victim to recover the stolen scooter, has not yet been apprehended. It remains unclear whether authorities have identified the individual, and the investigation is ongoing. The prosecutor's office has not released any description of the suspect at this stage.

## Investigation

The Charleroi prosecutor's office has launched a full investigation into the circumstances of the incident. Authorities are working to determine the exact sequence of events and to locate the suspect. As [RTL Info](https://www.rtl.be/actu/regions/hainaut/un-homme-noye-dans-la-sambre-durant-la-nuit-de-jeudi-vendredi-apres-une/2026-06-26/article/792816) reported, no arrests have been made as of the time of reporting. Investigators are likely reviewing CCTV footage from the area and speaking with potential witnesses to piece together what happened.

## Broader Context

Electric scooters have become increasingly common in Belgian cities as a popular mode of personal transport, but scooter theft is a known issue in urban areas. This incident highlights how a seemingly minor dispute over property can escalate into deadly violence with tragic consequences. The Pont de la Résistance, where the attack took place, is a central location in Charleroi, raising questions about public safety in the city's core.

This is not the first fatal incident on the Sambre in Charleroi. In September 2024, a man died after jumping into the river to rescue a woman who had been pushed into the water by a suspect. In August 2016, an 8-year-old boy fell from a pleasure boat and drowned in the Sambre, leading to an investigation into his parents. The river, which flows through the center of Charleroi, has been the site of several tragic events in recent years.

## What's Next

The investigation continues as authorities work to identify the victim, locate the suspect, and establish the full circumstances of the altercation. Questions remain about whether the scooter was actually stolen or if the confrontation was based on a misunderstanding, as well as what charges the suspect will face once apprehended. The case has drawn significant public attention across both Dutch- and French-language media in Belgium, reflecting widespread concern over the escalation of street-level disputes into fatal violence. The Charleroi prosecutor's office has urged anyone with information to come forward as the search for the suspect continues.
