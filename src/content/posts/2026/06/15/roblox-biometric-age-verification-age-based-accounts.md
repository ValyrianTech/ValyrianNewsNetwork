# Roblox Launches Biometric Age Verification for Safer Accounts

Roblox has launched a biometric age-verification tool that uses AI-powered facial analysis to place users into age-based accounts, marking a significant escalation in the platform's efforts to protect children online. The system, which went live globally in June 2026, was unveiled in an exclusive demonstration at Roblox's San Mateo headquarters, where [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/inside-roblox-age-verification-efforts-rcna346973) observed an 11-year-old's fake mustache fail to fool the technology.

## The New Age-Based Account System

Starting this month, users are automatically placed into one of three account tiers based on their verified age. Children ages 5 to 8 are assigned to **Roblox Kids** accounts, which disable all messaging by default and restrict access to games with "minimal" or "mild" content maturity labels. Users ages 9 to 15 enter **Roblox Select** accounts, which gradually introduce chat functions and allow access to games rated up to "moderate" content maturity. Users 16 and older retain access to the full suite of Roblox games and communication features, as [NBC News reported](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/roblox-age-based-accounts-children-teens-rcna331582) in April.

The system builds on a broader age-check requirement rolled out in January 2026, which mandated that all users complete either a biometric scan or government ID upload to access chat functions. According to Roblox, 73% of age-checked daily active users were under 18, with 35% under 13 as of Jan. 31.

## Accuracy and Privacy Concerns

Roblox's AI-powered technology, developed through third-party vendor Persona, estimates ages based on facial structure and claims accuracy within 1.4 years of a child's exact age. Eliza Jacobs, Roblox's vice president of safety product policy, told NBC News that "as compared to picking your birthday on a drop-down list, it's much more accurate. Ticking a box to say you're 13 or older, it's not enough anymore."

However, the system has faced significant criticism. [WIRED reported](https://www.wired.com/story/robloxs-ai-powered-age-verification-is-a-complete-mess/) in January that the rollout was "a complete mess," with users being misidentified — children placed in adult categories and adults placed in children's categories. Users have tricked the system using avatars, photos of celebrities, and drawn-on facial features. WIRED also found age-verified accounts for minors as young as 9 being sold on eBay for as little as $4.

Roblox says biometric data is deleted immediately after processing, and users who are misidentified can provide government IDs to correct their age. Parents can also manually adjust linked accounts. The company uses a system of "continuous age estimation" that monitors signals like time of day, game types, and chat language to detect discrepancies and request re-verification.

## Legal Pressure and Financial Fallout

The safety push comes as Roblox confronts unprecedented legal scrutiny. At least nine states have sued the company over child safety issues, with three — Nevada, Alabama, and West Virginia — reaching settlements totaling approximately $35.2 million, as [NBC News reported](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/roblox-alabama-settlement-attorney-general-rcna341128). More than 150 family lawsuits have been consolidated into multidistrict litigation in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California, alleging the platform failed to prevent grooming and assault.

The financial impact has been severe. Daily active users dropped from approximately 152 million in Q3 2025 to 132 million in Q1 2026, contributing to an 18% single-day stock decline on May 1, 2026, according to [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/01/roblox-rblx-stock-child-safety-earnings.html). Roblox slashed its full-year 2026 bookings forecast from $8.28-$8.55 billion to $7.33-$7.6 billion, citing safety measures as a headwind.

CEO David Baszucki defended the changes on CNBC's Squawk Box, saying: "Part of what we're rolling out with age check, we believe, is the real, right long-term way to build this platform."

## Developer Community Friction

In May 2026, Roblox enacted new publishing requirements for developers, mandating ID verification, two-factor authentication, and a financial deposit — either through a paid Roblox subscription or a one-time fee of 1,000 Robux per game — to publish games available to players under 16. The changes have created friction among the platform's 24,500-plus game developers, many of whom are independent hobbyists.

Jacobs acknowledged the tension: "We're trying to build the appropriate friction to make sure that developers that are publishing to our community of millions of users are invested in that publishing and acting in good faith."

## Industry Implications and What's Next

Roblox's approach could become a template for other social platforms facing similar regulatory pressure, particularly as Britain recently unveiled a sweeping ban on social media for under-16s and U.S. states pursue stricter online child safety laws. However, the biometric verification approach intensifies the ongoing debate between child safety advocacy and digital privacy rights.

Later this year, Roblox plans to transition to the International Age Rating Coalition (IARC) framework, a globally recognized content rating standard used for digital games and apps. The company has also established a Community Safety Council, a global group of Roblox creators who meet monthly to advise on safety measures.

"We have a long-term vision of safety and civility on the platform that we're building towards that we believe in," Jacobs said. "It's OK if some people are not always happy about that."

As the multidistrict litigation continues and more states pursue investigations, the question remains whether Roblox's aggressive safety overhaul will restore trust — or whether the user and developer exodus will accelerate.
