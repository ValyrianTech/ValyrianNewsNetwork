# Ex-Footballer Karel Snoeckx in Coma After Road Rage Attack in Turnhout

A road rage incident in Turnhout, Belgium, has left former professional footballer Karel Snoeckx (53) in a coma after a violent physical assault on Saturday afternoon. Two pedestrians — a man and a woman — were arrested at the scene, with the male suspect formally detained by an investigating judge on Sunday, according to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/04/verkeersagressie-turnhout/).

## The Incident

The altercation occurred around midday on July 4 at the intersection of Steenweg op Oosthoven and Hogestraat in Turnhout, Antwerp province. Snoeckx, who runs a calf farm in Retie with his wife Lindsy, had been visiting the local market with his family before stopping to let pedestrians cross the road.

According to Lindsy Snoeckx, her husband parked the car and approached a group of pedestrians in a friendly manner to ask why they had reacted aggressively after crossing behind his vehicle. "He went to those people in a friendly way to ask what the problem was and why they reacted that way," she told [HLN](https://www.hln.be/turnhout/man-die-ex-profvoetballer-karel-snoeckx-53-in-coma-sloeg-na-verkeersagressie-is-aangehouden-hij-hield-hem-ook-in-een-wurggreep~ab18de92/). "So he didn't do anything wrong, just asked that question."

What began as a verbal exchange quickly escalated into a brutal physical assault. "The man grabbed Karel and started hitting and kicking him. On his head and chest. He also put him in a chokehold," Lindsy said.

## Rescue and Aftermath

Snoeckx's daughter, who is 22 weeks pregnant, and her husband — both trained nurses — immediately began CPR. The resuscitation efforts lasted approximately 45 minutes. A bystander named Yan (49), who heard the commotion while unloading groceries, provided first aid, bringing water and ice and holding the victim's hand as he drifted in and out of consciousness.

"He squeezed my hand each time, so he kept coming to a bit. But then he would fade again. I really hope he makes it," Yan told [HLN](https://www.hln.be/turnhout/ik-hoorde-gillen-en-roepen-en-toen-zag-ik-de-man-al-op-grond-liggen-buurtbewoonster-49-biedt-hulp-bij-zwaar-geval-van-verkeersagressie~a84ce14f/).

Emergency services transported Snoeckx to hospital in critical condition. The forensic lab conducted evidence collection at the scene, and the road was closed for over an hour. The family's vehicle was seized for investigation, with police seeking dashcam footage.

## Judicial Outcome

Lieselotte Claessens of the Antwerp Public Prosecutor's Office confirmed the seriousness of the incident. The two suspects — a man and a woman — were brought before an investigating judge on Sunday. The male suspect was formally detained, while the female suspect was released under conditions.

## Victim's Condition

As of Sunday, July 5, Snoeckx remains in a coma. His wife reported that he had a restless night but briefly moved his head. Doctors have increased his medication to ensure he gets sufficient rest. "We are devastated with grief. Karel is gentleness itself," Lindsy said. "He is my first love. We've been married for 28 years and together for 34. He is my best friend, my dear husband, my everything."

## A Notable Football Career

Karel Snoeckx enjoyed a distinguished professional football career spanning over 15 years. He played for Lierse SK from 1991 to 1997, winning the Belgian league championship in 1997, and later returned for four more seasons. He also played for Lokeren, Germinal Beerschot — with whom he won the Belgian Cup in 2005, scoring in the final against Club Brugge — as well as Beveren, Hoogstraten, Lille, and White Star Schorvoort. In 1996, he received a call-up to the Belgian national team.

## Broader Context

The incident has shocked the community of Turnhout and reignited public debate about road rage in Belgium. The VIAS institute, the country's road safety authority, has documented numerous cases of traffic-related violence in recent years. The investigation remains ongoing, with authorities working to determine the exact sequence of events and whether additional charges will be filed.

## What's Next

Snoeckx's family remains at his bedside, hoping for recovery. "It should have been a beautiful day with our daughter and son-in-law, but it became hell," Lindsy said. The football community has also taken note, with many former teammates and clubs expressing support. The legal process will continue as investigators analyze evidence and determine the full scope of charges against the detained suspect.
