# Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce Marry at Madison Square Garden

Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce were married on July 3 at Madison Square Garden in New York City, in what the Associated Press described as "the apex celebrity wedding" of the decade. Actor and comedian Adam Sandler officiated the ceremony, which was attended by approximately 1,000 guests from the worlds of entertainment, sports, and politics.

According to [USA Today](https://www.usatoday.com/story/entertainment/music/2026/07/03/taylor-swift-wedding-travis-kelce-married-msg/88828948007/), the couple's representative Tree Paine confirmed the marriage. The wedding was announced to the public via illuminated billboards outside Madison Square Garden reading "JUST&T MARRIED" — a play on the couple's first initials.

## A Ceremony Like No Other

The couple opted out of a traditional wedding party, instead choosing Swift's brother Austin Swift as her man of honor and Jason Kelce, Travis's brother and podcast co-host, as best man, as reported by the [Associated Press](https://www.boston.com/culture/entertainment/2026/07/03/taylor-swift-and-travis-kelces-madison-square-garden-wedding-starts-soon/). The bride and groom wore custom Christian Dior Haute Couture designed by creative director Jonathan Anderson, with shoes by Christian Louboutin and Cartier jewelry for Swift.

Sandler, a longtime friend of the couple, was described by the AP as "an increasingly warm and paternal cultural figure with age." Kelce appeared in Sandler's 2025 sequel "Happy Gilmore 2," and Sandler appeared on the Kelce brothers' "New Heights" podcast last year.

## The Venue and Its Significance

Madison Square Garden holds deep personal significance for Swift. She headlined her first MSG show during the "Fearless" era in 2009, celebrated her 30th birthday there in 2019, and attended the 2026 NBA Finals at the venue just weeks before the wedding, as [BBC News](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/c982ry2pen3o) reported. The arena's enclosed, windowless structure offered exceptional privacy and security for the high-profile event.

The New York Police Department closed 7th Avenue between 30th and 34th Streets and restricted pedestrian access in the surrounding blocks. Guests arrived in a parade of black SUVs that pulled into a white tent erected along 31st Street, shielding their identities from the crowds of fans and media gathered outside.

## The Guest List

The wedding drew an extraordinary cross-section of celebrities. From Hollywood: Steven Spielberg, Tom Hanks, Tom Cruise, Jennifer Lopez, Hugh Grant, and Ethan Hawke. From music: Paul McCartney, Stevie Nicks, Ed Sheeran, and Selena Gomez. From sports: Tom Brady, Michael Strahan, Chiefs head coach Andy Reid, and teammates including Kareem Hunt.

According to [TMZ](https://www.tmz.com/2026/07/04/taylor-swift-travis-kelce-wedding-celebrities-exit-madison-square-garden/), guests partied into the early morning hours, with some departing around 10 PM and others — including Selena Gomez, Bradley Cooper, and Gigi Hadid — leaving in the wee hours of Saturday.

## Charitable Giving

Ahead of the wedding, Swift and Kelce donated $26 million to more than 20 charities, as reported by [Variety](https://variety.com/2026/music/news/taylor-swift-travis-kelce-donate-26-million-charity-wedding-1236800905/). The donations spanned New York-based organizations including the Food Bank For NYC and City Harvest, the Rhode Island Community Food Bank near Swift's Watch Hill estate, Children's Mercy Hospital in Kansas City, and national groups including Dolly Parton's Imagination Library, the ASPCA, and Feeding America.

## A Love Story for the Ages

The couple's relationship began in summer 2023 after Kelce publicly expressed disappointment on his "New Heights" podcast that he had failed to give Swift a friendship bracelet at her Eras Tour concert at Arrowhead Stadium. Swift reached out, and they began quietly dating. Their first public appearance together came on September 24, 2023, at a Chiefs game against the Chicago Bears.

Kelce proposed on August 10, 2025, at his home in Leawood, Kansas, after the couple recorded an episode of "New Heights" in which Swift announced her 12th studio album, "The Life of a Showgirl." The engagement was announced publicly on August 26, 2025.

## What's Next

The wedding date was chosen strategically — falling during Independence Day weekend, a holiday Swift has long celebrated at her Rhode Island estate, while also giving Kelce time before reporting to Kansas City Chiefs training camp later this month. As the couple begins their married life, questions remain about where they will honeymoon and whether Swift will change her name — details the couple has not yet disclosed.
