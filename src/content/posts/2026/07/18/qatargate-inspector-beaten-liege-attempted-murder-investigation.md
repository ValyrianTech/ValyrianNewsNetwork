# Qatargate Inspector Beaten: Liège Opens Murder Probe

An inspector from Belgium's Central Office for the Repression of Corruption (OCRC) — one of the last remaining investigators on the Qatargate case — was severely beaten in early June while walking near a pasture in Hesbaye, Liège province. The Liège public prosecutor's office has opened a judicial investigation for attempted murder, according to [La Libre Belgique](https://www.lalibre.be/belgique/judiciaire/2026/07/18/une-inspectrice-du-qatargate-passee-a-tabac-le-parquet-de-liege-ouvre-une-enquete-pour-tentative-de-meurtre-2D7AHS6W4RBVBAFZPN3LOVRVB4/).

## The Attack

The victim, an inspector with the OCRC — a specialized unit of the federal police — was attacked while walking near a pasture in the Hesbaye region. The assailant or assailants inflicted extremely violent blows, particularly to the face, resulting in a work incapacity that continues to this day.

In the wake of the assault, the Liège public prosecutor's office (Parquet du Procureur du Roi de Liège) opened a judicial investigation for "tentative de meurtre" (attempted murder) and appointed a forensic doctor. As of the current stage of the investigation, no suspect has been identified, and all leads remain open.

## Uncertain Motive

Crucially, the investigation has not yet been able to establish a direct link between the attack and the victim's professional activities. The Federal Prosecutor's Office, which oversees the Qatargate case, confirmed it has not opened a separate file related to these facts, as [L'Avenir reported](https://www.lavenir.net/actu/belgique/politique/2026/07/18/le-parquet-enquete-sur-une-tentative-de-meurtre-contre-une-policiere-du-qatargate-elle-avait-ete-passee-a-tabac-lors-dune-promenade-HJRG6SZ6H5GCFCKFJAGC6ZE3HE/).

Despite this official caution, the context is deeply troubling. The victim is described by La Libre Belgique as "not an ordinary police officer" but rather "one of the last investigators in charge of the explosive Qatargate case" — a highly sensitive corruption probe that has already seen security incidents targeting those involved.

## A Pattern of Targeting?

This is not the first time that officers working on the Qatargate investigation have appeared to be targeted. In February 2024, a chief inspector filed a complaint for attempted murder by poisoning following an incident in a Brussels bar. According to [The Brussels Times](https://www.brusselstimes.com/2236085/investigations-end-regarding-qatargate-probes-alleged-poisoning), that case was closed after the National Institute of Criminalistics and Criminology (INCC) and hospital examinations found no evidence of poisoning.

L'Avenir noted that "this is not the first time that police officers in charge of this sensitive case appear to have been targeted," underscoring a worrying pattern that raises questions about the safety of those pursuing one of Europe's largest corruption investigations.

## Background: The Qatargate Scandal

Qatargate is one of the most significant corruption scandals in recent European history. The investigation, code-named "Mezzo," began in July 2022 after Belgian State Security received intelligence about alleged bribery of European Parliament members by Qatar and Morocco. As [Wikipedia documents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qatar_corruption_scandal_at_the_European_Parliament), the scandal erupted publicly in December 2022 when Belgian police conducted coordinated raids across Brussels, seizing approximately €1.5 million in cash and arresting several high-profile figures, including former Greek MEP and European Parliament Vice-President Eva Kaili.

## Prior Warnings Ignored?

A companion investigation by La Libre Belgique reveals that Belgium's State Security Service (VSSE) warned federal police as early as July 2022 about risks to Qatargate investigators. According to confidential documents obtained by [La Libre](https://www.lalibre.be/belgique/judiciaire/2026/07/18/services-secrets-marocains-logiciels-espions-et-intimidations-les-enqueteurs-du-qatargate-ont-ils-ete-mis-en-danger-RI7TCBVR45GK7IQ6P4N7RAVDYU/), "no measures or almost none were implemented" to protect the investigators despite these warnings.

The shadow of Moroccan intelligence services has long hung over the case, with reports of alleged spyware use and intimidation tactics against those working on the investigation.

## Broader Implications

The attack on the OCRC inspector raises profound questions about the protection of those investigating high-level corruption. The fact that the victim is described as "one of the last" investigators on the case suggests the team has dwindled — potentially due to the pressures and risks involved.

For the Belgian justice system, questions will likely intensify about witness and investigator protection protocols. For European institutions, the incident serves as another reminder of the ongoing fallout from a scandal that continues to undermine public trust in the European Parliament's integrity.

## What's Next

The investigation remains in its early stages, with no suspects identified and the motive still unclear. All eyes will be on whether the Federal Prosecutor's Office eventually opens its own file — a move that would signal a confirmed link between the attack and the Qatargate probe. For now, the Liège prosecutor's office continues its work, and the question of who attacked the inspector — and why — remains unanswered.
