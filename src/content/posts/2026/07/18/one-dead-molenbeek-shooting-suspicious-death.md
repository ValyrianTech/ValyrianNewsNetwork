# One Dead in Molenbeek Shooting; Police Probe Possible Link to Suspicious Death

A man was killed in a shooting in Sint-Jans-Molenbeek, Brussels, in the early hours of Saturday morning, in what authorities believe may be the latest incident in an ongoing wave of drug-related violence plaguing the municipality. Police are also investigating whether the shooting is connected to a separate suspicious death reported in the same area.

## Fatal Shooting in Zwart Paardstraat

Emergency services were dispatched to the Zwart Paardstraat (rue du Cheval Noir) in Molenbeek at approximately 00:22 local time on Saturday, 18 July 2026, following reports of a shooting. According to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/18/verdacht-overlijden-in-molenbeek-in-nacht-van-vrijdag-op-zaterda/), the Brussels fire brigade sent a MUG (Mobile Urgency Group) and an ambulance to the scene.

Walter Derieuw, spokesperson for the Brussels fire brigade, confirmed that a person was declared dead upon their arrival. "Around 00:22, a MUG and an ambulance were dispatched. A person died on the scene," Derieuw told reporters.

[Het Laatste Nieuws](https://www.hln.be/brussel/een-dode-bij-schietpartij-in-molenbeek-slachtoffer-zou-actief-geweest-zijn-in-drugsmilieu~ab478c90/) reported that the victim was a man believed to have been active in the drug milieu, citing the French-language news site Sudinfo. HLN's later update stated more explicitly that the victim is believed to be a dealer. The victim's identity has not yet been officially released, pending notification of next of kin.

## Authorities Tight-Lipped on Investigation

The Brussels public prosecutor's office has declined to provide further details at this stage, a standard practice in the early phases of a homicide investigation. The local police zone Brussels-West, contacted by [DH Net](https://www.dhnet.be/regions/bruxelles/2026/07/18/un-deces-suspect-a-molenbeek-dans-la-nuit-de-vendredi-a-samedi-clone-ZX7LTDNEFBGEHM32AJDO2EZDAM/), has also declined to comment on the ongoing investigation.

A key question facing investigators is whether this shooting is linked to a separate suspicious death that occurred in the same Brussels municipality overnight. While the story summary indicates authorities are exploring a possible connection, individual media reports have not yet provided specific details about this second incident, suggesting it may be a developing aspect of the case.

## A Pattern of Drug-Related Violence

The shooting marks the latest in a series of violent incidents that have plagued Molenbeek, a Brussels municipality with a well-documented history of drug-related crime. During the summer of 2025, the area experienced a significant surge in shootings, with approximately 13 incidents recorded between June and September — accounting for roughly half of all shootings in Brussels during that period.

In response, police announced in September 2025 that [14 suspects had been arrested and 14 firearms seized](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2025/09/25/verdachten-opgepakt-en-wapens-in-beslag-genomen-politie-geeft-o/) in connection with the shootings in the Brussels-West police zone, which covers Molenbeek, Ganshoren, Jette, Koekelberg, and Sint-Agatha-Berchem. Despite these efforts, the violence has persisted.

A community meeting held in Molenbeek in August 2025 laid bare the deep frustration and fear among residents. Acting mayor Amet Gjanaj (PS) addressed concerns about the territorial drug violence, which local authorities have described as a "territoriumstrijd" (territorial struggle) between drug gangs operating as "multinationals" reaching far beyond Brussels. According to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2025/08/30/buurtvergadering-molenbeek-over-drugsgeweld-stelt-niet-gerust-g/), residents expressed feeling abandoned, with one stating: "Our mailbox is used by dealers as storage for drugs. I try to stay optimistic, but I'm afraid for my life." Another resident reported that a bullet had struck his living room in October 2023.

## Broader Challenges

Molenbeek's struggles with drug violence are compounded by broader structural issues. Acting Mayor Gjanaj acknowledged during the August 2025 meeting that the police zone is 150 officers short of its required strength. A "hotspot strategy" was announced in March 2024, with four hotspots identified in Molenbeek alone, and public prosecutor Julien Moinil has declared a crackdown on drug gangs.

The municipality, which gained international notoriety after the 2015 Paris attacks due to several perpetrators' connections to the area, has long grappled with high unemployment, poverty, and organized crime. The bilingual (French/Dutch) administrative environment of the Brussels-Capital Region adds further complexity to policing and governance.

## What to Watch For

As the investigation into Saturday morning's shooting continues, several questions remain unanswered. The victim's identity has not been confirmed, and no suspects have been publicly identified or arrested. The possible connection between the shooting and the separate suspicious death in Molenbeek will be a key focus for investigators.

With the prosecutor's office maintaining its silence for now, further details are expected to emerge as the investigation progresses. The incident serves as a stark reminder that, despite arrests and crackdowns, the underlying drug violence in Molenbeek remains a persistent and deadly challenge for both authorities and residents.
