# Chongqing Landslide: 8 Dead, 34 Missing as Rescue Teams Battle Elements

A catastrophic mountain collapse in Pengshui County, Chongqing, has claimed eight lives and left 34 people missing, as Chinese authorities mobilize one of the region's largest disaster response operations. The collapse, which struck at 9:10 AM local time on July 17, destroyed multiple residential buildings along the Wujiang River in Hanjia Street, sending approximately 18,000 cubic meters of rock crashing into the neighborhood below.

## Early Warnings and Narrow Escapes

The disaster did not strike without warning. Local resident Hou Kaiyun, who has lived in the area for 48 years, spent the night of July 16 listening to rocks tumbling from the mountainside. At 6:34 AM on July 17, he called community leader He Guangxue to report falling rocks. By 8:00 AM, more than 60 residents had been evacuated from the core danger zone, according to [Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260717/3c956c353fc7492fa5f9fbc0352516c7/c.html).

Hou Kaiyun and 60-year-old neighbor Xu Zhaoquan climbed the mountain at 8:11 AM, photographing cracks in the rock face measuring 10 to 20 centimeters wide. "The stones weren't rolling down the slope — they felt like they were being forced out, bursting out," Hou later told reporters from [The Paper](https://www.thepaper.cn/newsDetail_forward_33611203).

Despite the early warnings, the scale of the collapse overwhelmed evacuation efforts. "That rock was too big. The buildings were like tofu — it was over in an instant," Hou recalled.

## Massive Rescue Mobilization

China's central government responded swiftly. The National Development and Reform Commission allocated 30 million yuan (approximately $4.1 million) for emergency recovery, as reported by [Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260718/a90982e2f4254cb29e34510fd0c71830/c.html). The Ministry of Finance and Emergency Management jointly allocated an additional 50 million yuan for rescue and relief operations.

A total of 872 rescue personnel and over 1,100 pieces of equipment have been deployed to the site. The operation is divided into three zones: the left and right sides of the collapse center and the river surface below. Rescue teams are using life detection instruments, search dogs, and a combination of mechanical excavation and manual searching.

Chongqing activated its Level I geological disaster emergency response, while the national government activated a Level II geological disaster response. Vice Premier Zhang Guoqing led a working group to the scene, and President Xi Jinping issued instructions emphasizing scientific rescue efforts.

## Eyewitness Accounts of Survival

Ge Haibin, a father of two, received an evacuation call at 8:44 AM while preparing baby formula for his infant daughter. He quickly gathered his children and fled on a motorcycle. "We were the last ones when the buildings collapsed," said Peng Di, a community grid worker who evacuated residents door-to-door.

By 6:30 PM on July 17, rescue teams had pulled 18 people from the rubble. Ten were hospitalized, with injuries ranging from minor to critical, but all are reported in stable condition. However, 34 people remain unaccounted for as of the latest official count.

## Geological Factors and Ongoing Risks

Geologists classify the event as a large-scale collapse, measuring approximately 60 meters long, 30 meters high, and 10 meters thick. The largest single rock dislodged weighed an estimated 3,000 cubic meters. The collapse triggered a secondary explosion when falling rocks struck a fireworks storage warehouse — an explosion that was a consequence, not a cause, of the disaster.

Pengshui County is one of Chongqing's most geologically vulnerable areas, with 461 registered geological hazard sites across 37 townships. In 2025 alone, the county recorded 106 geological incidents. Experts warn that 5,000 to 6,000 cubic meters of unstable rock remain on the mountainside, posing a risk of secondary collapse. Round-the-clock slope radar monitoring has been installed.

## Rescue Challenges and Community Response

Heavy rainfall began around 11:00 PM on July 17, temporarily suspending rescue operations. Teams resumed work on the morning of July 18 using a combined mechanical and manual approach. A national medical expert team from Beijing Jishuitan Hospital has arrived to support the injured.

The local community has rallied in support. Zhou Jiasheng, a food delivery rider, organized fellow riders to assist. "Making money isn't important right now. They need support, and we know the roads best," he said.

Over 1,100 residents have been evacuated from the surrounding area, with those displaced housed in hotels and temporary shelters. Authorities have distributed relief supplies including bedding, clothing, food, and medicine.

## Looking Ahead

As rescue operations continue into their second day, the focus remains on locating the 34 missing individuals despite challenging conditions. The narrow workspace, massive boulders, unstable rock formations, and intermittent heavy rainfall present formidable obstacles. Pengshui County Mayor Ren Xujiang, speaking at a press conference on the evening of July 17, pledged that every effort would be made to find the missing and support affected families.

The disaster underscores the persistent geological threats facing communities in China's mountainous southwestern regions, where development increasingly encroaches on unstable terrain.
