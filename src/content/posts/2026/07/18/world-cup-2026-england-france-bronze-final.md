# World Cup 2026: England and France Clash in Bronze Final

England and France meet at Miami Stadium in Miami Gardens, Florida on Saturday evening for the 2026 FIFA World Cup third-place play-off, a match that carries significant emotional weight as it marks the final game of Didier Deschamps' 14-year tenure as France manager. Both sides enter the contest after painful semi-final defeats — France lost 2-0 to Spain, while England squandered a late lead to fall 2-1 to Argentina.

## A Farewell to Deschamps

Deschamps, one of only three men in history to win the World Cup as both a player (1998) and manager (2018), will take charge of his 187th and final match for Les Bleus. His 121 wins are more than any other head coach in France's history, according to [BBC Sport](https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/live/c5yz7egp0pvt).

"In my head I know it is my last match," Deschamps said in his final pre-match press conference. "I don't want anybody to cry. The end is near but life goes on." He added that the match carries a duty despite the disappointment: "It is not a friendly. It is a third-place playoff. The players, staff and I have the duty to reach this last objective."

His likely successor is Zinedine Zidane, the 54-year-old World Cup winner who has been out of management since leaving Real Madrid in 2021. French Football Federation president Philippe Diallo has indicated he already knows the name of Deschamps' successor, as [The Athletic](https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/live-blogs/france-vs-england-live-updates-world-cup-2026-score-result/vysP98726i9z/) reported.

## England's Semi-Final Heartbreak Continues

England's defeat to Argentina added another chapter to their painful history of semi-final exits. There have only been two instances this century where a team scored first in a World Cup semi-final but did not advance — and both involved England (2018 against Croatia and 2026 against Argentina). England's tournament record over the past five major tournaments reads: beaten semi-finalists (2018 World Cup), beaten finalists (Euro 2020), beaten quarter-finalists (2022 World Cup), beaten finalists (Euro 2024), and beaten semi-finalists (2026 World Cup).

Manager Thomas Tuchel has insisted he wants to continue leading England through to Euro 2028. "100 per cent. There is still enough to improve, and I am more than happy to do that," he said, as reported by The Athletic. FA chief executive Mark Bullingham has expressed full support for the German coach.

## The Golden Boot Subplot

The third-place play-off also carries significance in the Golden Boot race. Kylian Mbappe and Argentina's Lionel Messi are tied on eight goals each, with Messi leading on assists (four to three). Goals in the bronze final count toward the award, meaning Mbappe has a chance to overtake his Paris Saint-Germain former teammate. The last player to win the Golden Boot by scoring in a third-place match was Italy's Salvatore Schillaci in 1990 — against England.

England's Harry Kane and Jude Bellingham, both on six goals, would need hat-tricks to have a realistic chance of claiming the prize.

## The Third-Place Debate

The match has reignited debate about whether the bronze final serves a purpose in modern football. France defender Ibrahima Konate was blunt in his assessment, telling BBC Sport: "No one wanted to play for third place." Deschamps acknowledged the sentiment, saying: "England does not want to play this game and neither do we. But here we are."

Supporters of the fixture argue it provides opportunities for fringe players. Manchester United's Kobbie Mainoo has yet to play a minute at the tournament, while France's 2018 World Cup winner N'Golo Kante, 35, is also yet to feature and could make his final World Cup appearance.

## What to Watch For

With both managers potentially rotating their squads, the match offers a chance to see emerging talents and a final glimpse of an era-defining manager. For England, a bronze medal would represent their second-best ever finish at a men's World Cup. For France, victory would send Deschamps out as a winner — a fitting end for the manager who restored Les Bleus to the pinnacle of world football.

## Tactical Questions

France's semi-final performance raised concerns. They failed to register a first-half shot against Spain for the third time this tournament and managed their lowest expected goals (0.31 xG) in a World Cup match on record. Kylian Mbappe failed to register a shot on target for only the second time in 21 World Cup appearances — the other occasion was against England in the 2022 quarter-final.

England, meanwhile, had just 12% possession during the 30-minute period when they led 1-0 against Argentina — the lowest ever by a team when leading in a World Cup match for at least ten minutes in the last 60 years, according to match statistics. Thomas Tuchel's switch to a back five was criticized as "way too soon" and "did not work."
