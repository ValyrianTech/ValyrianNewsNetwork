# Tomorrowland Brings Surge in Bookings to Mechelen Hotels

As Tomorrowland, the world-renowned electronic music festival, kicks off its 2026 edition in Boom, Belgium, hotels in the nearby city of Mechelen are reporting a dramatic surge in bookings. International festivalgoers from across the globe are filling the city's accommodations to near 100% capacity, bringing both vibrant atmosphere and significant economic benefits to the historic Dijlestad.

## Festival-Driven Demand

Tomorrowland 2026 takes place over two consecutive weekends — July 17-19 and July 24-26 — at De Schorre park in Boom. Located just 10 to 15 kilometers from the festival site, Mechelen has become a prime base for international visitors seeking accommodation during the event.

According to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/17/mechelen-hotels-tomorrowland-het-anker-elisabeth-zomer/), hotels in Mechelen are seeing 60 to 70 percent of their occupancy during these weekends come from festivalgoers. Kristina Loriers, chairwoman of the Mechelen Hotel Association and owner of Hotel Het Anker, confirmed that approximately 70 percent of all Mechelen hotel rooms will be booked by international festival visitors over the next two weekends.

Ivo Van Itterbeeck, owner of Hotel Elisabeth in Mechelen, reported that his establishment is operating at full capacity. "Our occupancy rate is currently 100 percent. We are fully booked," he told VRT NWS. The hotel, like many others in the city, has seen a significant shift toward leisure travelers as international chains have entered the market.

## A Global Gathering

The festival's international appeal is reflected in the diversity of guests arriving in Mechelen. This year, hoteliers report an unusually high number of visitors from South America, alongside returning guests from previous editions. "The first festivalgoers have just left for Boom and it's always a fabulous fashion show," Van Itterbeeck remarked.

Loriers echoed the sentiment, noting that the atmosphere in Mechelen's hotels is electric. As [Made in Mechelen](https://www.made-in.be/mechelen/mechelse-hotels-kijken-uit-naar-twee-weekends-tomorrowland-festivalgangers-zorgen-voor-leuke-sfeer/) reported, she described how "people walk around as if they're at the festival itself and they're all in a good mood."

## Economic Significance

The festival's impact extends well beyond atmosphere. July and August are traditionally quieter months for Mechelen's approximately 15 hotels, which historically rely on business travelers for about 80 percent of their occupancy. The two Tomorrowland weekends provide a critical revenue injection during what would otherwise be a slow summer period.

According to a study by Beacon Economics cited by [Trends/Knack](https://trends.knack.be/nieuws/tomorrowland-voegt-706-miljoen-euro-toe-aan-vlaamse-economie/), a prior edition of Tomorrowland contributed an estimated €70.6 million to the Flemish economy, with €19 million in direct visitor spending on transport, accommodation, and local retail. Of that direct spending, €14.5 million came from visitors outside Flanders. The festival itself spent an additional €17.2 million on local businesses, bringing total direct economic input to €36.2 million.

The festival supports nearly 300 direct jobs and 454 total full-time equivalent positions in Flanders. Total fiscal revenues amount to approximately €15.5 million, including €3.3 million in VAT on ticket sales alone. Earlier estimates by organizers had placed the broader economic impact at around €275 million for Flanders and Brussels combined.

## Operational Realities

While the influx is welcome, it also presents unique challenges. Loriers noted that night receptionists have their hands full with guests who lose key cards, cannot find their rooms, or misplace rental bikes. Most festivalgoers rely on taxis to reach Boom, though hotel bike rental services are also popular.

Looking ahead, the Mechelen Hotel Association is exploring the possibility of organizing its own shuttle service to Boom starting in 2027, aiming to provide a more convenient transport option for guests.

## Part of a Broader Festival Ecosystem

Tomorrowland is not the only major event driving bookings for Mechelen hotels. Rock Werchter, held a week earlier, and the local Maanrock festival also generate significant demand, creating a sustained period of high occupancy throughout July.

Despite the late nights and logistical challenges, hoteliers welcome the energy that festivalgoers bring. "Tomorrowland always creates a very nice atmosphere in our hotels," Loriers told Made in Mechelen. "It's not just fun — it's also good for business."

## What to Watch For

As the second Tomorrowland weekend approaches (July 24-26), Mechelen's hotels are expected to remain at or near full capacity. The planned shuttle service for 2027 could further strengthen the city's position as the premier accommodation hub for festival attendees, while the broader economic ripple effects continue to benefit local businesses throughout the region.
