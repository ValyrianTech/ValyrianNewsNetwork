# DeepSeek Hits $52B Valuation in Landmark Funding Round

Chinese artificial intelligence startup DeepSeek has raised approximately 50 billion yuan ($7.4 billion) in its first-ever external funding round, achieving a post-money valuation of over 350 billion yuan ($52 billion), according to [Caixin Global](https://www.caixinglobal.com/2026-07-17/deepseek-reaches-52-billion-valuation-in-round-backed-by-tencent-catl-102465358.html). The round, backed by tech giant Tencent and battery manufacturer CATL, marks a watershed moment for China's AI industry and positions DeepSeek as one of the world's most valuable AI startups.

## The Funding Round

Tencent Holdings Ltd. invested 10 billion yuan ($1.48 billion), becoming the largest external investor, while CATL committed 5 billion yuan. Other institutional investors included NetEase Inc., JD.com Inc., and IDG Capital, each contributing 3 billion yuan, among 12 total institutional participants in the round.

Notably, DeepSeek founder Liang Wenfeng personally contributed 20 billion yuan to the round, demonstrating his commitment to maintaining strategic control over the company he founded in 2023. According to the [Straits Times](https://www.straitstimes.com/business/deepseek-preparing-for-ipo-filing-as-soon-as-end-2026), Liang's combined ownership stake stands at approximately 91%, giving him extraordinary control unusual for a startup at this valuation.

## How the Details Emerged

The funding details came to light through an unusual source — a regulatory filing by Anhui Korrun Co. Ltd. (300577.SZ), a Chinese luggage manufacturer whose subsidiary invested 40 million yuan in a private-equity fund that indirectly holds a 0.0114% stake in DeepSeek. As [Caproasia](https://www.caproasia.com/2026/07/17/china-ai-startup-deepseek-raised-7-4-billion-cny-50-billion-in-first-funding-round-at-52-billion-valuation-in-2026-june-deepseek-ai-model-built-with-6-million-funding-founded-in-2023-invested/) reported, the disclosure offered a rare glimpse into the private company's finances.

## DeepSeek's Remarkable Trajectory

Founded in 2023 and initially backed entirely by Liang's hedge fund High-Flyer Quant, DeepSeek quickly gained global attention for developing AI models that rival Western counterparts like OpenAI's GPT series at a fraction of the cost. The company's open-source approach and cost-efficient model development challenged the prevailing narrative that cutting-edge AI requires massive capital expenditure on par with U.S. tech giants.

DeepSeek's success has been particularly significant in the context of U.S.-China technology competition. Despite U.S. export controls on advanced semiconductors, the company demonstrated that Chinese AI firms can compete at the highest level. The company has now launched a sweeping recruitment drive to at least double the size of every department, hiring for roles in full-stack development, algorithms, and AI systems.

## IPO Plans and Next Steps

DeepSeek has begun preparations for an initial public offering on Shanghai's STAR Market, China's Nasdaq-style tech board, with a potential filing as early as late 2026 targeting a 2027 listing. The IPO would be one of the most anticipated Chinese tech listings in recent years.

Remarkably, even before closing this round, DeepSeek is reportedly seeking a new funding round at a $71 billion pre-money valuation — a 37% increase from the $52 billion valuation achieved just weeks earlier. This rapid appreciation signals intense competition among investors to secure access to DeepSeek's growth story.

## Ownership and Control

As of July 2026, Liang Wenfeng held 60.19% of DeepSeek via Ningbo Cheng'en and 31.01% directly, with Hangzhou Chengli — a special-purpose vehicle created for the financing — holding 8.52%. China's National Artificial Intelligence Industry Investment Fund, a state-backed vehicle spearheading Beijing's AI ambitions, owns approximately 0.28%. The company's registered capital has increased to 16.45 million yuan.

This ownership structure gives Liang extraordinary control over the company's direction, allowing him to prioritize long-term research goals over short-term shareholder returns. He has reportedly told potential investors that DeepSeek will continue developing open-source AI models while pursuing the broader goal of achieving artificial general intelligence.

## Strategic Implications

The participation of Tencent — which is simultaneously developing its own AI capabilities — reflects a "bet on both sides" strategy common in China's competitive AI landscape. CATL's involvement as a battery manufacturer investing in AI highlights how the technology is viewed as a foundational asset with cross-sectoral applications. The round validates that Chinese AI companies can attract massive institutional investment despite ongoing U.S. chip export restrictions.

DeepSeek's rise has been closely watched in the context of U.S.-China technology competition. The company's open-source models and cost-efficient approach challenged the narrative that cutting-edge AI requires massive capital expenditure on par with U.S. tech giants. Its success has been seen as evidence of China's ability to compete in the AI race despite export controls on advanced semiconductors.

Liang Wenfeng, now worth an estimated $36 billion according to the Bloomberg Billionaires Index, has signaled that DeepSeek will prioritize groundbreaking AI research over short-term commercialization. The company is also expanding into agentic AI, tapping a wave of enthusiasm for software that can carry out tasks without human intervention.

## What to Watch

Key questions remain about how DeepSeek will deploy its $7.4 billion war chest. The hiring spree suggests aggressive expansion, but specific product roadmaps remain unclear. Whether U.S. chip export controls will affect DeepSeek's ability to scale its computing infrastructure is another critical factor, as AI labs worldwide race to secure data center capacity.

The company's planned STAR Market IPO in 2027 would be a landmark event for Chinese tech listings, potentially rivaling the Ant Group IPO in scale and attention. The valuation the public markets ultimately assign will serve as a bellwether for investor confidence in China's AI sector. The answers to these questions will shape not only DeepSeek's future but also the trajectory of China's broader AI ambitions.
