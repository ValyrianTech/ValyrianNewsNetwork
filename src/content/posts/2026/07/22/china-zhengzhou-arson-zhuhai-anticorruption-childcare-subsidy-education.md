# Zhengzhou Arson, Anti-Corruption Probe, and Policy Updates

Chinese authorities have confirmed that a deadly fire at the Hongda International Auto Plaza in Zhengzhou was deliberately set as arson, while a former political advisor in Zhuhai has been placed under investigation for alleged disciplinary violations. In separate developments, the government has extended the deadline for infant childcare subsidy applications, and officials have reported significant progress in the nation's quality education system.

## Zhengzhou Auto Plaza Fire Ruled Arson

On July 22, the Zhengzhou Public Security Bureau Jinshui Branch announced that the fire at the Hongda International Auto Plaza on June 19 was deliberately set. According to [Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/local/20260722/e9186a4ad0a241468dc1e56a89f385d0/c.html), the blaze broke out in the evening in the Jinshui District, resulting in two deaths, two injuries, and significant property damage.

Investigators determined that a suspect surnamed Lu (陆某) started the fire due to a business dispute. The suspect has been arrested and approved for detention on arson charges, with the case remaining under further investigation. The announcement came more than a month after the incident, reflecting the time required for forensic investigation.

## Former Zhuhai Official Under Investigation

In a separate anti-corruption development, Zhang Yisheng (张宜生), a former Party member and full-time standing committee member of the Zhuhai Municipal Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC), is under investigation for suspected serious disciplinary and legal violations. [CCTV News](https://news.cctv.com/2026/07/22/ARTILd9iIfavJkUdsMTHN0eJ260722.shtml) reported that the Guangdong Provincial Commission for Discipline Inspection and Supervision is conducting the investigation.

The probe, announced on the Central Commission for Discipline Inspection (CCDI) website, continues China's long-running anti-corruption campaign targeting officials across various levels of government. Guangdong Province, where Zhuhai is located in the Pearl River Delta, has seen numerous such investigations in recent years.

## Childcare Subsidy Application Deadline Extended

In a move aimed at supporting families, the National Health Commission and the Ministry of Finance have jointly extended the application deadline for first-time childcare subsidies for children born between 2022 and 2024. As reported by [People's Daily](http://health.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0722/c14739-40765436.html), the new deadline is December 31, 2026, for first-time and second-time applications.

The policy builds on China's landmark "Childcare Subsidy System Implementation Plan" released in July 2025, which provides 3,600 yuan (approximately $500 USD) per child per year from January 1, 2025, until the child reaches age 3. This applies regardless of whether the child is the first, second, or third in the family. The subsidy has been described as the first large-scale, universal, direct cash subsidy for people's livelihood since the founding of the People's Republic.

Eligible families can apply through Alipay or WeChat mini-programs, provincial government service platforms, or in person at township or street offices.

## Quality Education Achievements Reported

China has built the world's largest basic education system, reaching the average level of high-income countries, according to the National Basic Education Work Conference held in Beijing on July 22. [CCTV News](https://news.cctv.com/2026/07/22/ARTIRhMFt8yMU2OXXqM8jh3v260722.shtml) reported that the nation now has 430,000 primary and secondary schools and kindergartens, serving 220 million enrolled students with 15.77 million full-time teachers.

Key metrics show substantial progress: the nine-year compulsory education consolidation rate has reached 96.1%, the high school gross enrollment rate stands at 92%, and free preschool education has benefited over 14 million children. The internet access rate in primary and secondary schools has reached 100%, and the proportion of students exempted from entrance exams near their place of residence exceeds 99.5%.

According to national quality monitoring data from 2023 to 2025, approximately 80% of students meet curriculum standards in mathematics, Chinese, English, and science, with 55% reaching excellent or good levels. Education officials also reported that physical health indicators are improving, with obesity and myopia rates showing signs of being curbed. Schools across the country have implemented two hours of daily physical activity, with officials noting that "sweat on the body, light in the eyes" is becoming a reality for students.

Deputy Director of the Ministry of Education's Basic Education Department, Zhu Dongbin, stated that the next phase will focus on building a fair and high-quality basic education system, including expanding free preschool education, exploring the extension of compulsory education, and increasing the supply of general high school places.

## Looking Ahead

These four developments highlight the breadth of China's domestic policy agenda, spanning public safety, anti-corruption enforcement, social welfare expansion, and education modernization. The childcare subsidy extension and education reforms align with China's strategic priorities of addressing demographic challenges and building human capital, while the anti-corruption investigation signals continued vigilance in governance. The Zhengzhou arson case, meanwhile, underscores the intersection of commercial disputes and criminal law in China's rapidly urbanizing economy.
