# French Ecology Minister Barbut Resigns Over Pesticide Vote

French Minister of Ecological Transition Monique Barbut announced her resignation on Wednesday, 22 July 2026, following the definitive adoption by Parliament of an emergency agricultural bill that allows the conditional reintroduction of two banned pesticides. Describing the government's decision as "one environmental setback too many," Barbut said she would present her resignation to President Emmanuel Macron.

## A Controversial Bill

The emergency agricultural bill (loi d'urgence agricole) was definitively adopted by the National Assembly and Senate on Tuesday, 21 July. The most contentious provision allows the National Agency for Food, Environmental and Occupational Health Safety (Anses) to conditionally reintroduce two pesticides — acetamiprid and flupyradifurone — for struggling agricultural sectors including hazelnuts, sugar beets, apples, and cherries, as reported by [La Libre Belgique](https://www.lalibre.be/international/europe/2026/07/22/la-ministre-francaise-de-la-transition-ecologique-monique-barbut-demissionne-apres-le-vote-sur-les-pesticides-le-recul-environnemental-de-trop-SKO6FAYOQRH47HXOTECVRINCBE/).

France banned acetamiprid in 2016 under the precautionary principle, going further than European Union regulations which still permit its use. Scientific studies continue to raise concerns about the effects of both pesticides on human health, bee populations, and biodiversity.

The bill passed with 294 votes in favor and 224 against. It was supported by all far-right and right-wing deputies and a majority of the centrist bloc, while one-third of the centrist bloc voted against or abstained — including former Prime Minister Élisabeth Borne and former Ecology and Agriculture Minister Agnès Pannier-Runacher.

## Barbut's Resignation

In a LinkedIn post announcing her departure, Barbut wrote: "Contrary to the commitments made to me, the government prevented debate on the removal of this measure, which was also desired by deputies. This reversal constitutes... the environmental setback too many."

Barbut, a former president of WWF France and a career environmental professional with no prior political experience, had previously threatened resignation in January 2026 over hydrocarbon exploration in French overseas territories and again in late June over budget cuts to her ministry. She served approximately nine months after being appointed in October 2025.

According to [La Libre Belgique's related analysis](https://www.lalibre.be/international/europe/2026/07/21/france-une-majorite-qui-se-dechire-et-une-ministre-qui-demissionne-PGN2FCI3ABB6VBM627CCUDUMSQ/), Barbut told her inner circle on Tuesday that she had no other option but to resign, stating: "Politics is not my world, and if this is what it's about, it will never be."

## A Revolving Door for Ecology Ministers

Barbut becomes the eighth French ecology minister to leave office since Emmanuel Macron became president in 2017, following Nicolas Hulot (who resigned live on radio in 2018), François de Rugy (2019), Barbara Pompili (2022), and others. This instability reflects the deep tension between France's agricultural sector — the largest in the European Union — and its environmental commitments.

The revolving door reinforces the perception that the ecology portfolio in France has become a "poisoned chalice" — a position where principled environmental stands inevitably clash with the government's economic and agricultural priorities.

## Political Fallout and What's Next

The vote revealed deep fractures within Macron's governing coalition. Agnès Pannier-Runacher denounced a "passage en force" and a "denial of democracy," while Agriculture Minister Annie Genevard (Les Républicains) expressed satisfaction with the bill, arguing that science — not politics — should decide on pesticide use.

Environmental groups have pushed back. François Veillerette, spokesperson for Générations Futures, pointed to over 70 recent studies showing negative effects of flupyradifurone on biodiversity and more than 50 on acetamiprid toxicity, arguing that the science already exists.

Several left-wing parties have announced they will refer the law to the Constitutional Council, echoing the 2025 "Duplomb law" precedent, when a similar attempt to reintroduce acetamiprid was censored by the Council after over 2 million French citizens signed a petition against it.

## Analysis and Implications

Barbut's resignation marks a significant political fallout at a sensitive time. With France experiencing record heatwaves and wildfires in summer 2026, the timing underscores the growing tension between environmental protection and agricultural competitiveness.

The government's approach — leaving the decision to Anses rather than legislating directly — is widely seen as an attempt to bypass the constitutional obstacles that blocked the 2025 Duplomb law. However, questions remain about Anses' independence, as some of its administrators previously denounced a project of "subordination" by the executive and agricultural lobbies.

As the 2027 presidential election approaches, this episode may further mobilize — or demobilize — green voters in France. The key question now is whether President Macron will accept Barbut's resignation and who will become the ninth ecology minister under his presidency.
