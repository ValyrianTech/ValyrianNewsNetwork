# Mark van Bommel Set to Lead Belgium Into a New Era

The Royal Belgian Football Association (RBFA) is on the verge of appointing Dutch former international Mark van Bommel as the new head coach of the Red Devils, succeeding Frenchman Rudi Garcia. The appointment signals a decisive shift as the federation seeks a "builder" to lead the national team beyond its celebrated golden generation.

According to [La Libre Belgique](https://www.lalibre.be/sports/football/coupe-du-monde/2026/07/22/le-successeur-de-rudi-garcia-sera-un-batisseur-pret-a-tourner-la-page-de-la-generation-doree-des-diables-YNANU7ZLKVHCNIKP4CYXIG2QKU/), negotiations with the 49-year-old are in their final stages, with all parties agreeing on a contract running through Euro 2028. Financial terms have been finalized, and discussions regarding his coaching staff — which will likely include a Belgian member — are well advanced.

## A Swift Transition After World Cup Heartbreak

The succession process has moved with remarkable speed. Barely a week after Belgium's quarter-final elimination at the 2026 FIFA World Cup in Los Angeles — a narrow 2-1 defeat to eventual champions Spain — the RBFA announced on July 20 that Garcia's contract would not be renewed. Within hours, Van Bommel had emerged as the clear frontrunner.

As [L'Avenir](https://www.lavenir.net/fil-info/sports/2026/07/21/mark-van-bommel-va-sengager-avec-les-diables-rouges-jusquen-2028-clone-GTT46L65R5E2TBAATZ6I6M6O5U/) reported, technical director Vincent Mannaert conducted an extensive telephone consultation process with his network of contacts, and Van Bommel was the favorite from day one. The Dutchman was already at the door of the national training center in Tubize before Garcia's departure was officially announced.

## Van Bommel's Turbulent Coaching Career

Van Bommel brings a mixed but impressive coaching résumé. His most notable achievement came with Royal Antwerp, where he led the club to a historic domestic double in the 2022-2023 season — the club's first Belgian Pro League title in 66 years, coupled with the Belgian Cup. He departed Antwerp in May 2024 after reaching a second consecutive cup final and has remained without a club by choice, waiting for the right project.

However, his earlier managerial stints have been less consistent. At PSV Eindhoven, he started with a record 14 consecutive Eredivisie wins but was sacked in December 2019 with the team in fourth place. A brief tenure at VfL Wolfsburg in 2021 ended after eight winless games, marred by a cup disqualification for using six substitutes instead of the permitted five.

As [DH Les Sports+](https://www.dhnet.be/sports/football/diablesrouges/2026/07/21/mark-van-bommel-va-sengager-avec-les-diables-rouges-jusquen-2028-UMCIWVJPHRFEPOIXCZNS7YFMRM/) noted, Van Bommel is described as tactically strong and skilled in human resources management — precisely the qualities that were reportedly lacking in Garcia's final months.

## The Language Question

One potential point of contention is language. Van Bommel, a Dutch speaker, does not currently speak French, which may draw criticism in French-speaking Belgium. However, as [RTBF](https://www.rtbf.be/article/mark-van-bommel-candidat-numero-un-a-la-succession-de-rudi-garcia-a-la-tete-des-diables-rouges-11760761) reported, he has expressed a strong intention to learn French and already speaks Dutch, English, German, Italian, and Spanish. Notably, Garcia did not speak Dutch during his tenure, which had also drawn criticism from Flemish media.

## A New Cycle for Belgian Football

The appointment of a "builder" signals the federation's intent to develop a new competitive cycle. Belgium's golden generation — featuring Kevin De Bruyne, Romelu Lukaku, and others — reached its peak with a third-place finish at the 2018 World Cup. By 2026, most of these players have retired or are approaching the twilight of their careers.

Van Bommel's experience with youth development, including stints with PSV's U19 team and as assistant coach of the Netherlands U17 side, positions him well to integrate emerging talents. The long-term target is clear: build a competitive squad capable of challenging at the 2030 World Cup.

## What's Next

The RBFA has not yet made an official announcement, but multiple Belgian media outlets report that only a few minor details remain to be finalized. An official confirmation is expected imminently. Van Bommel's first task will be to assess the current squad, identify the core of the post-golden generation team, and begin preparations for the qualification campaign for Euro 2028 — the tournament that will define his initial mandate.

For Belgian football, this marks the end of an era and the beginning of an uncertain but hopeful new chapter.
