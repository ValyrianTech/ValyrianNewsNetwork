# China Issues Heavy Rain Warning for 16 Provinces

China's National Meteorological Center (NMC) issued a Yellow Alert for heavy rainstorms on Wednesday, warning that 16 provinces and regions — including the Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei area — will experience heavy to severe rainfall over a 24-hour period. The alert, effective from 08:00 on July 22 to 08:00 on July 23, urges residents to prepare for potential flooding and travel disruptions, as reported by [Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260722/3ad69a4583fd4da99c16a216e7b0a4ca/c.html).

## Scope of the Warning

The initial warning, issued at 06:00 on July 22, covers a vast swath of Chinese territory stretching from Inner Mongolia in the northeast to Xinjiang in the northwest, and from Hebei in the north to Guangdong and Guangxi in the south. Affected areas include Inner Mongolia (Northeast), Hebei (East and South), Beijing (East), Tianjin, Shanxi (South), Shandong (Northwest), Henan (North and South), Anhui (Central), Jiangsu (Central and North), Jiangxi (North), Hubei (East), Hunan (West), Guangxi (West and East), Guangdong (West and East), Yunnan (South), and Xinjiang (Yili River Valley).

At 10:00, the NMC updated the warning, extending the forecast period to 14:00 July 22 – 14:00 July 23, and adding Liaoning (Southwest), Tibet (Southeast), and Heilongjiang (Northwest) to the affected areas, according to [China Weather Network](https://news.weather.com.cn/2026/07/4726847.shtml). The expansion of the warning zone within hours of the initial alert underscores the dynamic nature of the storm system.

## Severe Localized Rainfall and Convective Threats

In specific areas — including southeastern Hebei, northwestern Shandong, central-northern Jiangsu, central Anhui, eastern Hubei, eastern Guangxi, and western Guangdong — rainfall may reach 100 to 200 millimeters, classified as extremely heavy rain. The storms are expected to be accompanied by short-duration heavy rainfall with hourly intensity of 20 to 50 millimeters, locally exceeding 80 millimeters, as well as thunderstorm gales and hail.

These conditions pose significant risks for urban flooding, particularly in cities with drainage systems that may be overwhelmed by such intense downpours. The combination of extreme hourly rainfall rates and the potential for hail and damaging winds elevates the threat level beyond that of a typical rain event.

## Government Response

The Ministry of Transport has maintained a **Level 3 (Tertiary) Defense Response** for heavy rainfall, the third-highest in China's four-tier emergency response system. On the morning of July 22, the ministry conducted targeted video dispatches with transportation departments in Guangdong and Hubei provinces, two of the regions expected to face the most severe rainfall.

According to [CCTV News](https://news.cctv.cn/2026/07/22/ARTIjFulVhU5FROEnDSTHFos260722.shtml), the ministry required relevant transportation departments to maintain a high level of vigilance, closely monitor areas with heavy rainfall and high-risk zones with continuous rainfall, strengthen coordination, and ensure the implementation of proactive defense measures including response, inspection, and control. The ministry stated it will adjust the defense response level in a timely manner based on changes in weather warnings.

## Broader Context: 2026 Flood Season

This rainstorm event fits within a broader pattern of intensified extreme weather in China during 2026. According to the Ministry of Water Resources' flood prevention work conference held in February 2026, the hydrological and meteorological outlook for the 2026 flood season (June–August) was assessed as generally unfavorable, with more flooding than drought expected, particularly in northern China.

The China Meteorological Administration's flood season consultation on March 27, 2026, confirmed that extreme weather events in 2026 are trending toward normalization — no longer "once-in-decades" events but increasingly frequent occurrences. The North China Plain and Huang-Huai region are expected to experience frequent short-duration heavy rainstorms, with single-day rainfall potentially matching entire monthly averages.

## Implications and What to Watch For

The inclusion of the Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei region — China's political and economic core — in the warning zone signals potential major disruptions to road, rail, and air travel. Major cities including Beijing, Tianjin, Shijiazhuang, Jinan, Zhengzhou, Hefei, Nanjing, Wuhan, and Guangzhou are within or adjacent to affected areas, placing significant stress on urban drainage systems. The affected regions also include major agricultural zones in the North China Plain and the Yangtze River basin, where heavy rainfall could damage crops during the critical summer growing season.

Residents are advised to monitor local warnings and take precautions against potential flooding and travel disruptions as the storm system develops. Whether the warning will be upgraded to Orange or Red — indicating severe or extreme risk — depends on how the storm system evolves over the coming hours.
