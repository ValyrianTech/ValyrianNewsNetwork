# Former Zhuhai Political Advisor Zhang Yisheng Under Investigation

Zhang Yisheng, a former senior political advisor in the southern Chinese city of Zhuhai, has been placed under disciplinary review and supervisory investigation for alleged "serious violations of discipline and law," according to an announcement published by [CCTV News](https://news.cctv.com/2026/07/22/ARTILd9iIfavJkUdsMTHN0eJ260722.shtml) on Wednesday.

## Investigation Details

The investigation is being conducted by the Guangdong Provincial Commission for Discipline Inspection and Supervision, as reported on the website of the Central Commission for Discipline Inspection (CCDI) and the National Supervisory Commission. Zhang, who served as a former member of the Party Leadership Group and full-time Standing Committee member of the Zhuhai Municipal Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC), is suspected of conduct that violates both Party discipline and state law.

The standard formulation of "serious violations of discipline and law" indicates that the case may involve both internal Party disciplinary infractions and potential criminal offenses under the National Supervision Law.

## Broader Anti-Corruption Campaign in Guangdong

Zhang's case is the latest in a sustained anti-corruption drive in Guangdong Province, China's most populous province and economic powerhouse. According to a report by the [21st Century Business Herald](https://m.21jingji.com/article/20260719/herald/4f4c63055368285784e1f35545b80682.html), Guangdong recorded 1,463 officials voluntarily surrendering to authorities in the first five months of 2026 alone — a 14.6% year-on-year increase — reflecting the continued pressure of the "three non-corruptions" strategy encompassing deterrence, prevention, and education.

Zhuhai, a major city in the Pearl River Delta, has seen a notable concentration of anti-corruption investigations in 2025-2026. High-profile cases include Guo Yonghang, the former Party Secretary of Zhuhai who later served as Guangzhou Party Secretary and Guangdong Provincial Political Consultative Conference Vice Chairman, who was placed under investigation in March 2026. According to [China News Weekly](https://c.m.163.com/news/a/KP68I40B0514BE2Q.html), Guo's case is reportedly linked to his tenure in Zhuhai, particularly his role in promoting state-owned enterprise reforms.

Other Zhuhai officials investigated this year include Guo Caiwu, a member of the Zhuhai Municipal Standing Committee and Head of the United Front Work Department, as well as Pan Ming, a former Vice Chairman of the Zhuhai Municipal CPPCC who was investigated in 2025 and had his retirement benefits revoked. [Lianhe Zaobao](https://www.zaobao.com.sg/news/china/story20260328-8807325) reported that the investigations into Zhuhai officials suggest a broader anti-corruption sweep in the city.

## Institutional Context

The investigation operates under the framework of China's National Supervision Law and the Chinese Communist Party's disciplinary regulations. The Zhuhai Municipal CPPCC is a political advisory body that functions under the leadership of the CCP, and investigations into CPPCC officials are part of the broader anti-corruption campaign that covers all Party and state organs.

## Analysis and Implications

Zhang Yisheng's case adds to a growing list of officials in Zhuhai who have faced scrutiny in recent years, suggesting a sustained anti-corruption push in the city. Guangdong Province, as one of China's wealthiest and most economically strategic regions, has seen particularly intense anti-corruption efforts as part of the nationwide campaign that has been a hallmark of governance under the current leadership.

The investigation raises several outstanding questions, including the specific nature of the allegations against Zhang, his career history prior to joining the Zhuhai CPPCC, and whether his case is connected to the broader investigations of other Zhuhai officials.

## What to Watch For

Observers will be watching for further details on the allegations against Zhang Yisheng and whether the case leads to formal charges or prosecution. The investigation also signals continued anti-corruption scrutiny within Zhuhai's political advisory bodies, and may provide further insight into the scope of the anti-corruption campaign in Guangdong Province.
