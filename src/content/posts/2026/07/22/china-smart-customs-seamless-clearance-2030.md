# China Unveils Smart Customs Vision for Seamless Clearance by 2030

China's General Administration of Customs (GAC) announced on Wednesday an accelerated push to upgrade the nation's smart customs systems, setting a target for over 90% of ports to be equipped with intelligent inspection equipment by 2030, according to [CCTV News](https://news.cctv.com/2026/07/22/ARTI5MrIKNM8QE6KtKccGGNt260722.shtml). The initiative aims to make "seamless clearance" — where supervision is both ubiquitous and non-intrusive — the new normal for travelers and businesses alike.

## Robust Trade Growth Sets the Stage

Speaking at a State Council Information Office press conference, GAC Director Sun Meijun revealed that China's foreign trade grew 16.9% year-on-year in the first half of 2026, with exports rising 13.4% and imports surging 22.1%. Sun described the momentum as "strong and robust," noting that the country is seeing simultaneous improvements in trade volume and quality, a growing number of trading partners, and a shift toward greener and more innovative exports.

"This year is the first year of the '15th Five-Year Plan,'" Sun said. "The momentum is strong, the growth is robust, and the start is good." However, she also acknowledged mounting headwinds, including rising unilateralism, protectionism, and severe disruptions to global supply chains.

## Four Pillars of Customs Modernization

The GAC outlined four priority areas for the "15th Five-Year Plan" period (2026–2030): better supervision, higher security, greater convenience, and stronger anti-smuggling enforcement.

On supervision, the GAC plans to combine institutional innovation with technology empowerment, including revisions to customs laws and the rollout of risk-based classification management. A key innovation is "embedded along-the-flow supervision," where customs requirements are integrated into the production and supply chain processes of importers and exporters. The agency also aims to expand multimodal transport "one-document" clearance to streamline logistics.

On security, the GAC will maintain a declaration compliance rate above 95% and build a comprehensive "big risk control" system covering all domains and stages of customs operations.

On convenience, the agency will upgrade port infrastructure — both hardware and software — while advancing the development of the Hainan Free Trade Port and comprehensive bonded zones. The "Smart Customs" Partner Plan will deepen international cooperation on standards, procedures, and mutual recognition.

On anti-smuggling, the GAC will continue "National Sword" special operations and international joint enforcement campaigns such as "Mekong Dragon" and "Operation Thunder."

## AI and Big Data at the Core

Central to the modernization is the integration of artificial intelligence, big data, and intelligent equipment into customs operations. "By 2030, over 90% of ports will be equipped with intelligent inspection equipment," Sun said. "AI + Big Data + Intelligent Equipment will widely empower customs operations, making supervision services both 'ubiquitous' and 'non-intrusive,' making 'seamless clearance' the new normal."

The vision builds on proven successes. At Tianjin Port, a major northern hub, smart customs initiatives have already delivered measurable results. A "Laboratory Testing System Interconnection" pilot reduced sample submission form creation time by over 70% for ore inspections, according to a report from [China News Network](https://www.022tianjin.cn/2026/0114/236355.html). "Smart Telecommunication Quarantine" for ships saved an average of 15,000 yuan in berth fees and one hour per vessel across 180 applications. At Tianjin Binhai International Airport, "Dual Pre-inspection" and "One-time Inspection" models cut customs clearance time by approximately 60%.

## International Cooperation and BRICS Leadership

China is also positioning its smart customs initiative as a platform for global cooperation. The BRICS Smart Customs Demonstration Center, which opened in Xiamen in July 2025, serves as a hub for training, capacity building, and sharing best practices among developing nations, as reported by the [Emirates News Agency](https://www.wam.ae/zh/article/15nmpoe-金砖国家"智慧海关"示范中心在厦门启用). The center features a multilingual online platform and a curriculum covering customs operations, technology applications, and cultural exchange.

Sun emphasized the importance of deepening international collaboration: "We will strengthen international cooperation in import and export product standards, customs procedures, inspection and quarantine, and enterprise mutual recognition, expanding new spaces for digital trade, green trade, and cross-border e-commerce."

## Implications for Global Trade

The announcement comes at a time of significant disruption to global trade order. By accelerating smart customs upgrades, China aims to enhance its competitiveness as a trading nation while strengthening supply chain resilience. The focus on digital trade and cross-border e-commerce aligns with China's broader strategy to lead in the digital economy.

However, challenges remain. Implementation disparities between coastal and inland ports, data security concerns, and the need for international interoperability will test the ambitious timeline. The success of the Smart Customs Partner Plan will ultimately depend on the willingness of other nations to adopt compatible standards and systems.

## What's Next

With the 2030 target now set, attention will turn to the specific technologies, budget allocations, and legal revisions required to realize the vision. The GAC's ability to deliver on its promise of seamless clearance — balancing efficiency with security — will be closely watched by trading partners and global businesses alike.
