# Pronoun Dispute at UCF Leads to Police Standoff on Bodycam

Newly released bodycam footage from February 2025 shows a confrontation between University of Central Florida (UCF) campus police and transgender student Jarrett Vick that escalated from a pronoun dispute into a physical altercation involving pepper spray and a Taser. The video, released 17 months after the incident by the YouTube channel Inside the Blue TV, has reignited national debate over transgender rights, campus policing, and the use of force by law enforcement.

## Background of the Incident

On February 5, 2025, UCF campus police responded to reports of a "verbal altercation" between Vick and several juveniles on the Orlando campus. According to [Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trans-college-student-melts-down-over-pronouns-new-viral-bodycam-footage-just-say-she), which first reported the story widely on July 22, 2026, officers arrived to find Vick engaged in a heated exchange.

Bodycam footage shows the interaction quickly intensifying. When an officer referred to Vick using male pronouns, Vick responded emphatically: "She! She! Just say the right pronoun!" The officer later corrected himself, saying, "OK, her. I'm going to spray her," before deploying pepper spray.

## Escalation and Arrest

The confrontation escalated as Vick resisted attempts to be handcuffed. At one point, Vick appealed to a university administrator named Josh, saying, "Can you please just tell them to use the right pronouns? Please, you know it's causing me a lot of distress."

According to police, Vick threw a closed fist, grabbed an officer's microphone, and kicked an officer during the struggle. Officers ultimately deployed a Taser to subdue Vick, who was then arrested and charged with battery on a law enforcement officer, disorderly conduct, and resisting an officer with violence.

Vick pleaded not guilty to the charges. Orange County court records list the cases as closed following a court appearance on January 16, though the specific disposition — whether charges were dismissed, reduced, or resolved through a plea or diversion program — has not been publicly clarified.

## Legal and Political Context

The incident unfolds against the backdrop of Florida's contentious political climate regarding LGBTQ+ rights. Since 2022, Florida has enacted laws restricting transgender participation in sports, access to gender-affirming care for minors, and bathroom access. The officer's threat to invoke the Baker Act — Florida's mental health hold law — has also drawn scrutiny, raising questions about how campus police handle students in emotional distress.

A UCF Police Department spokesperson confirmed that Vick was enrolled at the university in spring 2025 but is not a current student and did not graduate, according to Storyful.

## Differing Media Interpretations

The incident has been framed differently across the political spectrum. Conservative outlets such as Fox News and RedState characterize the confrontation as a "pronoun meltdown" by an entitled student who resisted lawful police orders. Fox News' headline described "Trans college student melts down over pronouns in new viral bodycam footage."

Other outlets, including Cultura Colectiva, have pushed back against the "arrested for pronouns" framing, noting that Vick was charged based on physical actions — allegedly striking officers and resisting arrest — that occurred after the pronoun exchange. The [YouTube video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7lX24RKQQM4) has accumulated over 490,000 views in five days, with social media comments sharply divided along ideological lines.

## Analysis and Implications

Legal experts note that while Vick was not arrested solely for requesting correct pronouns, the pronoun dispute was the flashpoint that escalated a routine police call into a physical confrontation. Civil rights organizations have documented a pattern where misgendering by law enforcement can escalate routine interactions with transgender individuals, and the question of whether officers could have de-escalated by using Vick's requested pronouns remains a central point of debate.

The 17-month delay between the incident and the release of the bodycam footage is also notable. The video was released by Inside the Blue TV, a channel specializing in police bodycam footage, entering a different political and social media landscape than when the incident originally occurred.

## What's Next

The case highlights ongoing tensions at the intersection of transgender rights, campus policing, and free speech in Florida and beyond. With the legal case now closed and Vick no longer enrolled at UCF, the broader questions raised by the incident — about de-escalation training, pronoun usage by law enforcement, and the role of bodycam footage in shaping public narratives — remain unresolved. The university has not publicly disclosed whether any internal review was conducted following the incident.
