# Kompany Leads 60-Man Training Session at Bayern Munich

Vincent Kompany, the Belgian head coach of Bayern Munich, kicked off the 2026/27 pre-season with a training session unlike any in the club's history. On Tuesday, July 21, the double-winning manager oversaw approximately 40 players and 20 coaching staff — roughly 60 people — on a single pitch at the Säbener Straße training ground, an event described by German tabloid [Bild](https://www.bild.de/sport/fussball/fc-bayern-vincent-kompany-trainiert-mit-60-mann-auf-dem-platz-saisonauftakt-der-profis-6a5f76882937e4865893e9d5) as "never seen in the history of Bayern."

## Context: A Skeleton Crew and a Golden Opportunity

The unprecedented scale of the session was born out of necessity. Bayern Munich was the second-most represented club at the 2026 FIFA World Cup in North America, with 17 players participating in the tournament. Those players — including Manuel Neuer, Joshua Kimmich, Harry Kane, and Michael Olise — were still on post-tournament holiday, leaving Kompany with a skeleton crew for the start of pre-season.

Only six senior professionals were available: reserve goalkeeper Sven Ulreich (37), new signing Tom Bischof (21), loan returnee Arijon Ibrahimovic (20), and transfer-listed players João Palhinha (31), Sacha Boey (25), and Armindo Sieb (23). Rather than scale back the session, Kompany turned a logistical challenge into a development opportunity. He called up more than 30 players from Bayern's U23 and U19 squads, effectively transforming the training into a high-stakes talent showcase, as reported by [HLN](https://www.hln.be/bundesliga/nooit-gezien-in-de-clubgeschiedenis-vincent-kompany-leidt-gigantische-training-bij-bayern-munchen-en-staat-met-liefst-60-man-op-een-oefenveld~a71e26f5/).

## The XXL Casting Session

The 80-minute session featured notable youth talents including Maycon Cardozo (17), Wisdom Mike (17), Guido Della Rovere (19), Felipe Chávez (19), and defensive prospect Filip Pavic (16). According to [90min.de](https://www.90min.de/kompany-mit-dicker-uberraschung-beim-trainingsauftakt-des-fc-bayern), Kompany "welcomed an incredible 60 persons and essentially organized an XXL casting session for all those who want to prove themselves in front of the Belgian."

Kompany was assisted by U23 head coach Dante (42), the former Bayern and Standard Liège defender, and U19 head coach Leonhard Haas (44), along with their respective staff. The Belgian coach emphasized communication during the session, telling the assembled players: "Even though we are many and even though we are many young players: The most important thing is communication! Talk to each other — that is the most important thing in football!"

The session was watched closely by Bayern's sporting leadership, including Sportvorstand Max Eberl (52), Sportdirektor Christoph Freund (49), and new Campus-Chef Michael Wiesinger (53), who observed from the executive offices.

## Analysis: A Philosophy in Action

This training session reveals several important aspects of Kompany's management style. His decision to fill the training ground with youth players rather than cancel or postpone the session demonstrates a genuine commitment to developing homegrown talent. It also sends a clear cultural message: under Kompany, opportunity at Bayern is based on merit, and the gap between the academy and the first team can be bridged.

The timing is significant. Kompany enters his third season at the helm having secured the domestic double in 2025/26 — the Bundesliga title and the DFB-Pokal — making him the first Bayern coach since Hansi Flick to achieve that feat, as noted by [Bavarian Football Works](https://www.bavarianfootballworks.com/bayern-munich-dfb-pokal/206474/vincent-kompany-becomes-the-first-coach-since-hansi-flick-to-win-the-double-with-bayern-munich-pokal).

Adding to the complexity, the Säbener Straße training ground is undergoing major renovation works, including construction of three new training pitches, expansion of professional player facilities, and renovation of the fan shop, as detailed by [FCB Inside](https://fcbinside.com/2026/07/20/major-construction-project-on-saebener-strasse-bayern-boss-outlines-modernization-plan/). The XXL session unfolded amid this construction site, with Kompany delivering his instructions over the noise of building work. Bayern CEO Jan-Christian Dreesen has described the project as creating "even better conditions for our sporting success."

Injured players Serge Gnabry (31, adductor tear) and Lennart Karl (18, muscle bundle tear) trained individually in rehabilitation, while Bryan Zaragoza (24) was absent due to a knee inflammation.

## What's Next

Bayern's first pre-season friendly is scheduled for July 25 against 3. Liga side SV Wehen Wiesbaden. The World Cup participants are expected to return gradually over the coming weeks, with the full squad anticipated by August 8 after the club's Asia tour from August 1-8.

For the youth players who participated in Tuesday's session, the message from Kompany was clear: the door to the first team is open for those who walk through it. The session served not only as a practical solution to a pre-season staffing challenge but as a statement of intent — under Kompany, Bayern's future will be built from within as much as through the transfer market.

As the 2026/27 Bundesliga season approaches, all eyes will be on whether any of the young talents who impressed during the XXL casting session earn a place in Kompany's first-team plans.
