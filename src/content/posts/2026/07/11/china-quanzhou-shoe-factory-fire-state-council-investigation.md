# Quanzhou Factory Fire Kills 28; State Council Launches Special Investigation

A catastrophic fire at a shoe factory in Quanzhou, Fujian Province, has killed 28 people, prompting the State Council Safety Committee to launch a special investigation as China's leadership balances urgent safety enforcement with a broad policy agenda on digital infrastructure, flood control, and economic development.

The fire broke out around midday on July 9 at the Huite Shoes Co., Ltd. factory in Chendai Town, Jinjiang City — the heart of China's footwear manufacturing hub known as "China's Shoe Capital." Of the 239 people inside the five-story building at the time, 213 were evacuated, but 28 perished. Two victims died after being transported to hospital, while 26 were initially listed as missing before being confirmed dead.

## The Fire and Its Aftermath

According to [Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/20260711/dec4bb5f27434288b773aea44aa26791/c.html), the State Council Safety Committee has decided to supervise the investigation of the fire and dispatched an on-site supervision team led by the National Fire and Rescue Administration to Fujian. The committee has called for a thorough investigation to determine the cause, identify management failures, and propose accountability measures.

The fire originated in the first-floor stamping workshop. Workers reported that a small electrical fire at the main switch had initially been extinguished with fire extinguishers, but minutes later a sudden deflagration engulfed the building. "As soon as I went downstairs, I heard a huge explosion from the first-floor workshop, then saw flames spreading upward," worker Lin Qiao told reporters. "Fire instantly filled the entire factory building."

Firefighting efforts involved 183 firefighters, 35 fire trucks, and a helicopter, but rescue operations were hampered by narrow village roads, high temperatures, and shifting winds. The blaze burned for approximately five hours before visible flames were extinguished.

At a press conference on July 10, Quanzhou Mayor Cai Zhansheng bowed in apology. "The Quanzhou Municipal Party Committee and Government express deep condolences to all victims and sincerely apologize to all victims' families and to the whole society," he said. Seven company personnel have been detained and the company's accounts frozen as investigations proceed.

## Prior Safety Violations

The tragedy is particularly striking because a routine fire safety inspection just two days earlier — on the evening of July 7 — had identified violations at the factory, including debris piled in the first-floor corridor and an electric fan placed near a distribution box. The factory, which produces sports shoes using highly flammable materials such as mesh fabric, sponge, and adhesives, operated under a piece-rate wage system that incentivized workers to continue through lunch breaks — a factor that may have contributed to the high casualty count.

Chendai Town alone produces over one billion pairs of sports shoes annually, and the broader Jinjiang footwear cluster exceeds RMB 300 billion in scale. The region hosts major brands including Anta, Xtep, and 361°. The "three-in-one" building design — combining production, storage, and dormitory functions — has long been identified as a safety hazard in the area.

## State Council Meeting Addresses Policy Priorities

On the same day as the fire investigation was announced, Premier Li Qiang chaired a State Council executive meeting in Beijing, as [reported by People's Daily](http://politics.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0711/c1024-40758286.html). The meeting addressed six major policy areas, reflecting the government's dual focus on development and risk management.

The meeting opened with flood control and disaster relief, as China enters its summer flood season. The State Council called for heightened vigilance, improved monitoring and early warning systems, and coordinated disaster response to protect lives and property.

On digital infrastructure, the meeting called for "moderately advanced deployment" of next-generation communication networks and computing power networks to accelerate Digital China construction. The council also addressed modern logistics, calling for infrastructure improvements and multimodal transport development to reduce logistics costs.

In a forward-looking agenda item, the meeting discussed nurturing emerging pillar industries through full-chain development, basic research investment, and support for multiple technology pathways. It also approved the National Fitness Plan (2026-2030), which aims to expand fitness facilities, develop campus sports, and promote health industry innovation.

## Analysis: Development and Safety as Two Sides of the Same Coin

The simultaneous focus on the Quanzhou fire investigation and the State Council policy meeting underscores a central challenge for China's leadership: maintaining rapid economic development while ensuring adequate safety enforcement. The fire exposed gaps between inspection and enforcement — violations were identified but not remedied in time.

Online reaction to the mayor's apology has reflected public anger, with some calling for accountability beyond the company level. The State Council's decision to directly supervise the investigation signals the seriousness with which Beijing views the incident, and may presage a nationwide safety inspection campaign targeting high-risk industries.

## What to Watch

As investigations continue, several questions remain: What was the exact cause of the fire? Will local government officials face criminal charges for inadequate oversight? And will the piece-rate wage system — which critics say prioritizes production over safety — face regulatory scrutiny? The answers will shape not only the aftermath of this tragedy but also the future of workplace safety enforcement across China's manufacturing sector.
