# Gansu Landslide Buries 33, Rescue Teams Save 17 in Dangchang County

A massive landslide struck Rencang Village in Dangchang County, Gansu Province, in the early morning of July 7, 2026, burying 33 people. Within approximately four hours, rescue teams had successfully pulled 17 survivors from the debris, while search efforts continued for the remaining 16 individuals, according to [Xinhua News Agency](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260707/e7771cbdefd749bfba5dd94ade5def99/c.html).

## Context and Background

Dangchang County is located in the mountainous southern region of Gansu Province, where the Minshan Mountain system meets the West Qinling Mountains at the edge of the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau. The area is highly susceptible to geological disasters, a vulnerability exacerbated by the 2008 Wenchuan earthquake, which significantly loosened rock formations and increased the frequency of rain-induced landslides and debris flows.

The landslide comes during what Chinese authorities had warned would be a severe 2026 flood season. In April, Gansu provincial officials predicted above-average precipitation, noting that rainfall in the first months of 2026 was already 40% above the multi-year average. The broader region is currently experiencing widespread severe weather, including a reservoir dam failure in Guangxi on July 6 and deadly tornadoes in Hubei.

## Rescue Operations

Within hours of the disaster, a multi-agency emergency response was mobilized. According to [China News Service](https://www.chinanews.com.cn/sh/2026/07-07/10654185.shtml), departments including emergency management, fire services, medical teams, public security, transportation, power, and communications all deployed personnel to the scene.

"After the disaster, multiple departments organized rescue forces, rapidly carrying out rescue operations, making every effort to search for trapped persons, properly relocating and安置 affected residents, and strictly preventing secondary disasters," China News Service reported, citing CCTV.

The rapid response proved effective: within hours of the landslide, 17 of the 33 buried individuals had been rescued — a survival rate exceeding 50%. Rescue teams were observed walking into the landslide site to begin their work, as documented in photographs published by Xinhua.

## Broader Disaster Context

This landslide is occurring amid a period of intense and widespread severe weather across China. On July 6, the Liulan Reservoir in Guangxi experienced a dam failure, flooding downstream villages. Meanwhile, Hubei Province was hit by severe convective weather producing tornadoes that claimed eight lives. Multiple regions across the country are currently experiencing flooding and geological disasters as the annual flood season peaks.

Gansu Province had conducted a comprehensive flood disaster defense drill on June 29, 2026 — just one week before this incident — testing provincial, city, and county-level emergency response capabilities. This preparedness appears to have contributed to the swift multi-agency coordination observed in the Dangchang County response.

## Analysis and Implications

The successful rescue of more than half of those buried within four hours reflects China's well-established disaster response framework, which emphasizes multi-agency coordination and rapid mobilization. President Xi Jinping has repeatedly issued instructions on flood prevention and disaster relief, prioritizing the protection of life and property.

However, with 16 people still unaccounted for, the situation remains critical. The landslide's location in a remote mountainous village presents logistical challenges for rescue teams, including difficult terrain and the risk of secondary landslides. No official statement has yet confirmed the cause of the landslide, though heavy rainfall during the flood season is a likely trigger.

## What to Watch For

As rescue operations continue, key developments to monitor include the fate of the 16 still-buried individuals, any official confirmation of casualties, the scale of evacuations in the surrounding area, and the extent of damage to homes and infrastructure. The broader flood season situation across China — already marked by multiple concurrent disasters — will also remain under close observation.
