# Banks Rush to Tap Hong Kong's Yuan and Gold Market Expansion

Global and Chinese financial institutions are rapidly positioning themselves to capitalize on a sweeping package of financial market reforms in Hong Kong, as Beijing moves to cement the city's status as the world's leading offshore yuan hub and a major international gold trading center. The coordinated initiatives, announced July 7, include 11 measures to deepen Hong Kong's fixed-income, currency, and offshore yuan markets alongside the trial launch of a long-planned Gold Central Clearing System.

## A Comprehensive Reform Package

The People's Bank of China (PBOC), the Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA), and the Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) jointly unveiled the 11 measures at the 2026 Fixed Income and Currency and Bond Connect Summit. According to [Caixin Global](https://www.caixinglobal.com/2026-07-10/banks-move-fast-as-hong-kong-expands-yuan-and-gold-market-links-102462542.html), the package represents the most significant push yet to strengthen Hong Kong's financial infrastructure, backed by explicit support in China's 15th Five-Year Plan.

PBOC Governor Pan Gongsheng said at the summit that the yuan's global use is "moving beyond trade settlement into investment, financing and reserve management," signaling Beijing's ambition to deepen the currency's international role through Hong Kong's markets.

## Key Fixed-Income and Currency Measures

Six of the 11 measures target fixed-income and currency markets. The Southbound Bond Connect annual net investment quota has been raised from 500 billion yuan to 800 billion yuan ($117.8 billion), with the program's reach extended to include Macao's bond market. The China Foreign Exchange Trade System (CFETS) and Hong Kong Exchanges and Clearing Ltd. (HKEX) will jointly develop an electronic fixed-income and currency trading platform.

HKEX is set to launch five-year offshore Chinese government bond futures on August 3, providing foreign investors a critical hedging tool. As [Caixin Global reported](https://www.caixinglobal.com/2026-07-08/china-moves-to-deepen-hong-kongs-bond-and-offshore-yuan-markets-102461811.html), overseas investors held 3.2 trillion yuan of Chinese bonds by the end of May 2026, underscoring the growing demand for yuan-denominated assets.

Regulators also plan to accept onshore Chinese government and policy-bank bonds held through Northbound Bond Connect as collateral by Hong Kong Futures Clearing Co. and SEHK Options Clearing House within 2026. Such bonds already account for 17% of margin collateral at OTC Clearing Hong Kong.

## Offshore Yuan Market Expansion

Five measures focus specifically on deepening Hong Kong's offshore yuan market. The RMB Business Facility quota has been raised from 200 billion to 500 billion yuan ($73.6 billion), effective July 10, with the participant base expanding from 14 to over 40 banks. New borrowing tenors of 9 months, 2 years, and 3 years have been added to meet diverse demand.

In a significant step for regional currency cooperation, the PBOC signed a cross-border local-currency settlement arrangement with Bank Indonesia and the HKMA, with direct trading between offshore yuan and Indonesian rupiah targeted by the end of 2026. Similar arrangements with other Asian economies are under consideration.

The HKMA is also studying a seven-day offshore yuan liquidity tender mechanism via repos and exploring the issuance of short-term offshore yuan debt instruments to fill gaps at the short end of the yield curve.

## Gold Central Clearing System Launches

Simultaneously, Hong Kong commenced the trial operation of its Gold Central Clearing System, operated by the Hong Kong Precious Metals Central Clearing Company Limited (HKPMCC), a wholly government-owned entity. As detailed in the [Hong Kong Government press release](https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202607/07/P2026070700223.htm), the system features a comprehensive Clearing Rulebook, a central ledger for settlement activities, and efficient settlement for gold meeting international standards.

Financial Secretary Paul Chan emphasized the strategic importance of the launch, stating: "The National 15th Five-Year Plan incorporates explicit support for Hong Kong in establishing a commodity trading ecosystem. The commencement of the trial operation of the gold central clearing and settlement system today marks a significant step forward in developing Hong Kong's gold trading infrastructure."

## Banks Execute First Gold Trades

Major financial institutions moved swiftly to utilize the new system. HSBC completed six HAU gold trades on the first day of operations. Standard Chartered completed seven gold transactions with clients including Chow Sang Sang, Haitong International Securities, JD Technology, and Shanghai Pudong Development Bank.

Bill Winters, Group Chief Executive of Standard Chartered, said: "Gold is becoming increasingly important as investors and central banks seek diversification in a more uncertain environment. At the same time, the centre of gravity of the gold market is shifting towards Asia, creating new opportunities for international hubs such as Hong Kong." The bank [confirmed](https://www.sc.com/en/press-release/standard-chartered-completes-seven-gold-transactions-through-hong-kongs-gold-central-clearing-and-settlement-system/) it completed its first batch of transactions through the new system.

## Eight Supporting Initiatives for the Gold Ecosystem

The government unveiled eight complementary initiatives to build a comprehensive gold trading ecosystem. These include the initial phase of Delivery Connect with the Shanghai Gold Exchange, a new HAU price ticker developed with Bloomberg, and plans to expand total storage capacity to over 2,000 tonnes within three years. The Airport Authority Hong Kong is developing vaulting facilities on a thousand-tonne scale, while HSBC is expanding its gold storage to 200 tons.

Tax incentives are being explored, with an amendment bill introduced to LegCo to expand qualifying investments for funds and family offices to include precious metals. The Insurance Authority is coordinating with international, mainland, and local insurers for specie insurance, with a dedicated hotline launching within July 2026.

## Strategic Implications

These measures represent the most comprehensive package of financial market reforms for Hong Kong since the introduction of Stock Connect and Bond Connect. The explicit inclusion of Hong Kong's commodity trading ecosystem in the 15th Five-Year Plan signals Beijing's long-term strategic commitment to developing the city as a global financial hub.

The gold clearing system, combined with the HAU benchmark, positions Hong Kong to potentially challenge London and Shanghai in gold price discovery during Asian trading hours. Meanwhile, the yuan internationalization push is shifting from trade settlement toward investment, financing, and reserve management functions.

## What to Watch Next

Market participants will be watching several key developments in the coming months. HKEX is set to launch five-year offshore Chinese government bond futures on August 3. The FDR007 will be added as a Swap Connect reference rate from Q4 2026. The Mandatory Provident Fund Schemes Authority is expected to submit an amendment bill in Q4 2026 to relax restrictions on MPF fund structure, potentially enabling gold ETF investments.

Secretary for Financial Services and the Treasury Christopher Hui summed up the vision: "Our goal is to build a scalable and integrated platform with trusted capabilities in clearing, connectivity, price discovery, risk management, storage, and insurance for global participants."
