# Beijing-Shanghai Rail Launches 60-Day Advance Booking

Passengers traveling on the Beijing-Shanghai high-speed railway and conventional railway can now book tickets up to 60 days in advance, following the launch of a new reservation pilot program on July 20. The initiative, announced by [China State Railway Group](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260720/4e31f78b8f1f4456a8f860a5a1ad9e0d/c.html), covers over 200 trains on one of China's busiest and most economically vital rail corridors.

## How the Reservation System Works

The new service, accessible through the railway 12306 mobile app's "Reservation Purchase" (预约购票) section, allows passengers to reserve tickets for travel between 60 and 17 days before departure. However, a reservation does not guarantee a ticket — it must first be fulfilled by the system.

According to [CCTV News](https://news.cctv.com/2026/07/18/ARTIl39o0os8gnzYHQCPYRAx260718.shtml), the 12306 system will fulfill reservation orders between 20 and 16 days before departure. Passengers who receive a successful fulfillment notification must complete payment before midnight on the same day, or the order is automatically canceled.

A railway official quoted by Xinhua explained: "60 days is only the maximum reservation period. After submitting reservation information, passengers do not yet have tickets. They need to wait for fulfillment between 20 and 16 days before departure."

## Two Booking Modes for Flexibility

The system offers two reservation modes. **Precise Reservation** allows passengers to select a specific train, departure and arrival times, seat class, and fare. **Fuzzy Reservation** lets passengers specify a time range, travel duration, and seat class without choosing a particular train, giving the system flexibility to match available capacity.

Each 12306 account can have up to three pending reservation orders simultaneously. Each order can include up to three precise reservations and two fuzzy reservations across two consecutive days, with a maximum of 19 passengers per order.

## Transition Period and Full Rollout

From July 20 to August 29, passengers can sequentially reserve tickets for travel on September 15 and later dates. Starting August 30, the 60-to-17-day advance booking window will operate on a normalized basis, allowing routine reservations for future travel.

## Intelligent Ticket Allocation

The 12306 system uses intelligent matching algorithms based on "long-distance priority, balanced with medium and short-distance needs" and "first-come, first-served" principles. To ensure fair ticketing, the system employs facial recognition and SMS verification codes.

## Penalties and Safeguards

To prevent speculative reservations, accounts that have three fulfilled orders go unpaid or are actively canceled after fulfillment within 30 days will lose reservation privileges for 90 days. Passengers can cancel orders before fulfillment without penalty.

## Refund and Change Rules

Reserved tickets come with specific refund and change policies. Refunds made between 20 and 8 days before departure incur a 5% fee. Refunds within 8 days follow standard advance-sale refund rules. Reserved tickets can be changed once within the advance-sale period, with a 5% fee on any fare difference if the new ticket is cheaper.

## Background: Evolution of China's Ticket Booking Window

The 60-day reservation window represents a significant departure from the current 15-day standard advance-sale period, which has been in place since the COVID-19 pandemic. China's railway ticket预售 (advance sale) period has undergone several major changes over the years. Before 2011, the window varied by season and route, typically 10 to 20 days. It was extended to 60 days between 2014 and 2015 before being shortened back to 30 days in November 2016 due to operational challenges.

During the pandemic, the window was further reduced to 15 days in 2021 and even temporarily to just 5 days in April 2022 during surge periods. The current pilot marks the first major expansion of the booking window since those reductions.

The Beijing-Shanghai high-speed railway, which opened in 2011, handles over 100 million passengers annually and serves as China's flagship high-speed rail line connecting the capital with the country's largest economic hub.

## Analysis: A Shift Toward Demand-Responsive Ticketing

This pilot represents more than just a longer booking window — it signals a broader shift in China Railway's approach to ticketing. The dual-mode system (precise and fuzzy reservations) leverages intelligent matching algorithms and big data to align passenger demand with available capacity more efficiently.

As [China News Service](https://www.chinanews.com.cn/gn/2026/07-20/10662651.shtml) reported, the move addresses long-standing passenger complaints about the limited 15-day booking window. Business travelers, tourists, and those planning trips during peak seasons stand to benefit most from the extended planning horizon.

However, the system's complexity — a multi-step process of reserving, waiting for fulfillment, and then paying — may pose challenges for some passengers, particularly elderly or less tech-savvy users. The penalty mechanism, which bans accounts for 90 days after three unpaid fulfillments, aims to prevent speculative reservations but could also affect genuine travelers with changing plans.

## What to Watch For

Industry observers will be watching closely to see whether the pilot is expanded to other major Chinese rail routes, such as Beijing-Guangzhou or Shanghai-Shenzhen. The system's performance during peak travel periods like the Spring Festival travel rush will also be a key test of its scalability and reliability. For now, passengers on the Beijing-Shanghai corridor can enjoy significantly greater flexibility in planning their journeys — a welcome upgrade on one of the world's busiest and most important rail routes.
