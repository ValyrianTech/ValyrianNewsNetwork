# Spain Wins World Cup for First Time in 16 Years

Spain has won the 2026 FIFA World Cup, defeating defending champion Argentina 1-0 in extra time at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey, on July 19. The victory, secured by a 106th-minute goal from substitute Ferran Torres, marks Spain's first World Cup title since 2010 and returns the nation to the pinnacle of world football after a 16-year drought.

According to [AP News](https://apnews.com/article/argentina-spain-world-cup-final-score-messi-fccc26aa12d9226e63d06b601b770617), Spain dominated the match from start to finish, taking the game's first 20 shots before Argentina managed its first attempt in extra time. The victory also makes Spain the first nation to simultaneously hold both the men's and women's World Cup titles, following the women's team's triumph in 2023.

## A Tactical Masterclass

Spain's victory was built on an extraordinary defensive record. The team allowed only one goal across eight matches in the tournament — the fewest ever conceded by a World Cup champion. Spain entered the final on a 38-match unbeaten streak (29 wins, 9 draws), the longest by any European men's team in history, surpassing Italy's previous record of 37.

Head coach Luis de la Fuente, at 65, became the oldest manager to win a World Cup. "I feel pride, to be honest," de la Fuente told reporters. "Accompanying them on this journey is an honor." The Spanish squad controlled possession throughout the final, employing their characteristic possession-based style to suffocate Argentina's attacking threats.

## The Decisive Moment

The match remained scoreless through regulation despite Spain's relentless pressure. Argentina goalkeeper Emiliano "Dibu" Martínez delivered a World Cup final-record 12 saves, keeping his team in the contest. The turning point came in the 89th minute when Argentina's Enzo Fernández was sent off after receiving a second yellow card for a reckless tackle on Spain defender Pau Cubarsí.

In the 106th minute, with Argentina down to 10 men, the breakthrough arrived. Pedro Porro crossed from the right, Nico Williams headed the ball back across goal, and Ferran Torres — a second-half substitute who had not scored all tournament — buried a left-footed shot just under the crossbar. "It was a goal scored by 47 million people," Torres said, referring to Spain's population.

## End of an Era for Messi

The defeat likely marks the end of Lionel Messi's World Cup career. At 39, the Argentine captain was playing in what he had indicated would be his final World Cup, seeking to become the first back-to-back champion since Brazil in 1962. Spain's defense marked Messi out of the game, limiting him to minimal touches and no clear scoring opportunities.

Argentina coach Lionel Scaloni acknowledged Spain's superiority. "We lost the game, and we accept that," Scaloni said. "That doesn't mean that we will forget everything we've done so far, so I'd like to thank my people, my players and the country. I can tell them that we played our hearts out."

## A Historic Tournament

The 2026 World Cup was the first expanded to 48 teams, co-hosted by the United States, Canada, and Mexico. The tournament featured 104 matches and more than 300 goals — the most in any World Cup edition. The final was attended by approximately 80,000 spectators at MetLife Stadium, with an estimated global television audience of 2 billion.

Spain midfielder Rodri was named the tournament's best player, winning the Golden Ball award. "I think now I'm in a cloud," Rodri said. "As I told the lads before the game, we have to go for the game, we need to face them, we have to look to their eyes and say we want this game. And then, life gives you the World Cup or not."

## What's Next

Spain's victory signals the emergence of a new golden generation, blending young talents like Lamine Yamal and Pau Cubarsí — both 19 and among the youngest players to feature in a World Cup final — with experienced leaders like Rodri. The team's defensive solidity and possession mastery suggest Spain could dominate international football for years to come.

For Argentina, the focus now turns to rebuilding after the likely departure of Messi and other veteran players. The nation's recent record — World Cup winners in 2022, Copa América champions in 2021 and 2024 — ensures a strong foundation for the future, but replacing an icon of Messi's stature will be the defining challenge for Argentine football in the years ahead.

As [FIFA.com](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/spain-argentina-final-report-highlights) noted, Spain are "champions of the world for a second time" — and they achieved it with a performance that will be remembered as one of the most dominant in World Cup final history.
