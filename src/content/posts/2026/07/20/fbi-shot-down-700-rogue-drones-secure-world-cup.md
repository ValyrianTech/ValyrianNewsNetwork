# FBI Shot Down Over 700 Rogue Drones During Unprecedented World Cup Security Mission

FBI Director Kash Patel revealed in an exclusive interview that the bureau successfully shot down over 700 unauthorized drones during a historic security operation to protect the 2026 FIFA World Cup, the largest commitment of FBI resources ever devoted to a single sporting event. The operation, which spanned 78 matches across 11 U.S. venues over 38 days, resulted in zero significant criminal attacks despite an attendance of 6.5 million people in the United States alone.

"That's what we executed on… We're proud to report that, thank God, and thankfully to the men and women of the FBI and our partners, we had no significant criminal attacks during this World Cup," Patel told [Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/politics/fbi-boss-kash-patel-details-how-agency-partners-secured-record-shattering-fifa-world-cup) in an exclusive interview published Monday. "And just to put that in perspective, we had 6.5 million people attend the 78 matches in the U.S. across 11 venues."

## A Security Operation of Unprecedented Scale

The tournament — described by officials as "78 Super Bowls in 38 days" — required the FBI to dedicate nearly 5,000 personnel, including more than 4,000 from field offices, 800 from FBI headquarters, and two legal attaché offices, along with support from 59 Homeland Security task forces. The bureau also conducted approximately 300,000 background checks on players, coaches, and other accredited personnel.

Nearly 7 million fans attended World Cup matches across the United States, Canada, and Mexico. Stadium entry often took fans less than 15 minutes for games averaging more than 65,000 attendees, according to the FBI.

## Aggressive Counter-Drone Response

A defining element of the security operation was the FBI's aggressive posture toward unauthorized drones. Recognizing that even low-cost unmanned aircraft could be weaponized for surveillance, disruption, or attacks with explosives or chemical agents, the bureau treated every incursion as a potential threat.

"If you're in the space, we're gonna take you down. And that's why we took down over 700," Patel said. "Some of these folks were maybe borderline nuisances, while many of them were criminal in terms of violations of FAA airspace and also failing to register their drones… We're not taking any chances."

The [Associated Press](https://apnews.com/article/world-cup-fifa-security-secret-service-trump-32f04baf3a242395f26816292a9dc7e2) previously reported that drones were among the greatest concerns for security planners, with New York City Police Commissioner Jessica Tisch stating, "If there is one threat that keeps me up at night, it is from drones."

## New Counter-Drone Infrastructure

The FBI established its first dedicated counter-UAS (unmanned aircraft system) training center at Redstone Arsenal in Alabama to prepare state and local officers for the mission. The National Counter-UAS Training Center (NCUTC), opened in November 2025 following Executive Order 14305 signed by President Trump, offers a two-week flagship course combining classroom instruction, hands-on training, and a capstone field exercise.

According to [C-UAS Hub](https://cuashub.com/en/content/fbi-opens-national-counter-uas-training-center-at-redstone-arsenal/), the center is modeled after the FBI's Hazardous Devices School and serves as the federal government's primary training and certification hub for counter-UAS operations. Congressman Dale W. Strong (AL-05), who championed legislation to establish the center, called it "a strategic investment in America's homeland security."

The FBI trained approximately 60 state, local, tribal, and territorial law enforcement officers in drone mitigation ahead of the World Cup, as [DroneLife](https://dronelife.com/2026/02/17/fbi-counter-uas-training-world-cup-2026/) reported in February. FEMA distributed $250 million in counter-UAS grants to the 11 host states and the National Capital Region to support the effort.

## International Coordination and Intelligence

The FBI hosted the International Police Cooperation Center in Northern Virginia, deploying more than 70 personnel to work alongside officers from U.S. host cities and international security officials. The bureau also assembled a Joint Operations Center with foreign police representatives from 46 of the 48 participating countries, enabling real-time threat intelligence sharing.

The FBI's Intelligence Coordination Center processed an average of 26 incidents per day and produced nearly 100 intelligence reports for federal, state, local, and private-sector partners, according to the bureau.

## Related Threat: White House UFC Plot

The security operation was tested early when investigators uncovered an alleged coordinated attack targeting UFC Freedom 250 at the White House involving explosive-laden drones. Patel said the FBI launched a six-state operation, tracked the alleged threat through online communications, and began a coordinated takedown "before the first punch was thrown," allowing the event to proceed without incident. Eight defendants have been charged, though they are presumed innocent until proven guilty.

## Leadership and Forward Look

Patel credited President Trump, who established the White House Task Force on the FIFA World Cup 2026 approximately 16 months before the tournament, and Executive Director Andrew Giuliani for bringing federal, state, and local agencies together under a unified security strategy.

"President Trump set the standard: Not only were we going to have the largest and safest sporting event in the history of the world, but we were going to make sure everyone had a great time," Patel said. "They wanted not just safety and security, but to show off America to the world. And I think we did it brilliantly."

The NCUTC at Redstone Arsenal is expected to become a permanent national asset, with potential expansion for future events including the 2028 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles. The operation also established an interagency security model that could serve as a template for future large-scale events, from political conventions to Super Bowls.

As Andrew Giuliani told the Associated Press ahead of the tournament: "There's never been a summer like this in American history from a security angle." The results suggest that preparation matched the scale of the challenge.
