# FBI: Federal Building Blast Was Anti-Government Attack

A man deployed an incendiary device outside a federal building in Lower Manhattan on Monday morning, prompting a major emergency response and an investigation by the FBI's Joint Terrorism Task Force. The incident occurred at approximately 8:30 a.m. EDT outside 26 Federal Plaza, a building that houses multiple federal agencies including the FBI, Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE), and an immigration court.

Four people sustained minor, non-life-threatening injuries, according to the New York Fire Department. One person was transported to a local hospital, while two others declined hospitalization. The suspect was taken into custody at the scene by Federal Protective Service officers.

## What Happened at 26 Federal Plaza

According to [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/newyork/news/incendiary-device-federal-building-nyc-manhattan/), the device involved commercial-grade fireworks placed in a garbage can. The explosion sent smoke and flames across the sidewalk in front of the building, drawing a swift response from the FDNY, FBI agents, and immigration officers who responded with guns drawn.

The suspect was found carrying an airsoft rifle, carbon dioxide cartridges, halogen lighting, and a helmet. Law enforcement sources told CBS News that two types of realistic-looking BB guns or airsoft guns were recovered, including a long gun carried in a grocery cart. No shots were fired. CBS News law enforcement analyst Richard Esposito described the materials as "things you might use in paintball-type events."

Video from the scene showed heavy smoke billowing from the sidewalk as officers restrained a suspect and took him into custody. The FDNY initially responded to a report of an outdoor rubbish fire before discovering the incendiary device.

## FBI Investigation and Motive

The FBI New York Joint Terrorism Task Force is leading the investigation. In a statement, the FBI confirmed that "an individual deployed an incendiary device outside of 26 Federal Plaza" and that the suspect had been taken into custody.

FBI Director Kash Patel [confirmed](https://www.yahoo.com/news/us/articles/1-person-arrested-explosion-outside-151017247.html) the incident on social media, noting two minor injuries. FBI official James Barnacle told CBS News that the JTTF is "investigating this as an anti-government attack on a federal facility."

Sources described the suspect as an anti-ICE activist. According to law enforcement officials who spoke with CBS News and the [Associated Press](https://apnews.com/article/incendiary-new-york-federal-building-immigration-e3b62b5cafb5ea777f5918670c48da72), the suspect had a small cart with the slogan "ICE off our streets" written on it and was carrying anti-ICE material. The suspect's name has not been released.

## Political and Official Reactions

New York City Mayor Zohran Mamdani called the incident "deeply disturbing" in a social media statement. "My team is in touch with the NYPD, and we will support the federal investigation," Mamdani wrote. "Our administration will continue to ensure that every New Yorker is safe in their city — and hold accountable any who threaten that."

The NYPD urged the public to avoid the area near Worth Street and Lafayette Street, warning of traffic delays, mass transit disruptions, and road closures.

## Broader Context

26 Federal Plaza has become a focal point for demonstrations against the immigration policies of the Trump administration. The building houses an immigration court where ICE agents have carried out arrests described by the Associated Press as "chaotic" and "sometimes violent" in hallways as migrants leave hearings. The building also houses offices of the FBI, ICE, U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS), the Department of Homeland Security, and the Social Security Administration.

The incident comes amid heightened national tensions around immigration enforcement. On the same day, dozens of Democratic U.S. senators sent a letter to the Department of Homeland Security demanding answers about ICE's vetting practices and body camera policies following two fatal ICE shootings in Maine and Houston.

Zero Hedge noted that the attack comes "days after Secretary of State Marco Rubio warned of far-left terrorism across the West," adding a layer of political significance to an event already being investigated as an anti-government attack.

## Analysis and Implications

The FBI's characterization of the incident as an "anti-government attack on a federal facility" signals that authorities are treating this as a potential act of domestic terrorism. The involvement of the Joint Terrorism Task Force — a multi-agency unit that includes NYPD detectives and FBI special agents — underscores the seriousness with which federal authorities view the threat.

The case intersects with broader national debates about the boundaries of political protest, the rise of domestic extremism, and the increasingly polarized discourse around immigration policy. 26 Federal Plaza has been a flashpoint for these tensions, serving as both a symbol of federal immigration enforcement and a site of ongoing protests.

## What's Next

The FBI investigation is ongoing, with authorities working to determine the suspect's full identity, background, and whether they acted alone or as part of a larger group. Federal charges are expected to be filed, and the case is likely to reignite debates about domestic extremism and the escalating rhetoric surrounding immigration enforcement in the United States.

Security at federal buildings in New York City and potentially nationwide is expected to be heightened in the wake of the attack. The incident also raises questions about how federal facilities balance public access with security, particularly those that have become focal points for political demonstrations.
