# Violent Incidents Rock Belgium: Four Major Cases in 24 Hours

Belgium has been shaken by a series of violent incidents over a 24-hour period spanning July 18-20, 2026, including a fatal nightclub shooting in Antwerp, a stabbing near the Gentse Feesten festival, a brutal attack on a Qatargate corruption investigator, and the arrest of a Belgian fugitive in Thailand. While not directly connected, the events have raised serious public safety concerns across the country.

## Fatal Shooting at Antwerp Nightclub

A 59-year-old Dutch national has been arrested following a fatal shooting at the Ikon nightclub in Antwerp that left a 23-year-old man dead. The shooting occurred shortly after 6:00 AM on Sunday, July 19, near the Straatsburgdok Noordkaai, according to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/19/twintiger-overleden-bij-schietpartij-aan-antwerpse-discotheek/).

The victim was critically wounded at the scene, resuscitated by emergency services, and transported to hospital where he later died. The suspected shooter fled but was later arrested. Police spokesperson Wouter Bruyns confirmed that during a house search in Lint, several additional people were detained. "Their involvement in the facts is being investigated," Bruyns said.

The suspect was already internationally wanted for a murder in Suriname, suggesting possible connections to transnational organized crime networks operating across the Benelux region.

## Stabbing Near Gentse Feesten Festival

On Monday morning, July 20, a man was seriously wounded in a stabbing at the intersection of Ham and Sint-Jorisbrug in Ghent, very close to the Gentse Feesten festival zone. [HLN](https://www.hln.be/gent/man-neergestoken-vlak-bij-gentse-feestenzone-bebloede-kledij-ligt-op-straat-daders-zijn-mogelijk-nog-niet-gevat~ac4cf261/) reported that blood-stained clothing was visible at the scene and police cordoned off the area for investigation.

According to Het Nieuwsblad, a manhunt is underway for suspects who reportedly wore balaclavas, though police could not confirm this detail. The East Flanders prosecutor's office stated it would communicate further details within hours. Public transport was disrupted in the area during the police operation.

The stabbing marks the third consecutive day of incidents at the Gentse Feesten, one of Europe's largest public festivals. The first evening saw a large brawl involving dozens of youths, while the second night featured further fights captured on video. Police have since increased permanent nighttime presence on the Veldstraat.

## Qatargate Inspector Severely Beaten

In what may be the most significant of the four incidents, an inspector from the Central Office for the Repression of Corruption (OCRC) was severely beaten in early June in the Hesbaye countryside of Liège province. The attack was disclosed publicly on July 18. According to [La Libre Belgique](https://www.lalibre.be/belgique/judiciaire/2026/07/18/une-inspectrice-du-qatargate-passee-a-tabac-le-parquet-de-liege-ouvre-une-enquete-pour-tentative-de-meurtre-2D7AHS6W4RBVBAFZPN3LOVRVB4/), the victim was attacked while walking near a pasture and was beaten with extreme violence, particularly to the face.

The Liège public prosecutor's office has opened a judicial investigation for attempted murder. The victim is one of the last remaining investigators on the Qatargate corruption case, a highly sensitive file involving alleged corruption at the European Parliament with alleged links to Moroccan intelligence services.

La Libre reported that as early as July 2022, Belgium's State Security Service warned federal police about risks to Qatargate investigators, but few protective measures were implemented. The federal prosecutor's office has confirmed it has not opened a separate file linking the attack to Qatargate, though all possibilities remain open.

## Belgian Fugitive Arrested in Thailand

Separately, a Belgian national wanted by Interpol for attempted murder was arrested in Patong, Phuket, Thailand, on July 19-20. According to [HLN](https://www.hln.be/buitenland/belg-die-door-interpol-gezocht-wordt-voor-poging-tot-moord-opgepakt-in-thailand~a28b3709/), the suspect faces charges including attempted murder as an accomplice, illegal possession of firearms, and involvement in a criminal organization.

Following a tip from Belgian authorities, Thai immigration police traced the suspect to an apartment complex in Patong. After surveillance, police arrested the man when he came outside. Immigration records show he entered Thailand on May 21 via Phuket International Airport with permission to stay until August 18. His Thai residence permit has been revoked and he remains in custody pending extradition to Belgium.

## Analysis and Implications

While these four incidents are not directly connected, their clustering in a single news cycle raises legitimate public concern about safety in Belgium. The Antwerp shooting suspect's international wanted status and the Belgian arrested in Thailand both point to cross-border organized crime networks operating in the region. The attack on the Qatargate investigator raises serious questions about witness and investigator protection in sensitive corruption cases, with potential institutional failures suggested by warnings that went unheeded. Meanwhile, the Gentse Feesten violence highlights challenges in securing large public gatherings that attract hundreds of thousands of visitors.

## What to Watch For

Investigations remain ongoing in all four cases. The role of those arrested during the Lint house search in the Antwerp shooting will be closely watched, as will any developments linking the Qatargate inspector attack to her work on the corruption case. The Belgian fugitive in Thailand faces extradition proceedings, while Ghent authorities are expected to announce additional security measures for the remaining days of the Gentse Feesten.
