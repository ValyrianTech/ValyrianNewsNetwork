# Three Boys Rescued from Drowning on Belgian Coast, Hospitalized

Three young boys were rescued from drowning in the North Sea at Ostend on Sunday afternoon after lifeguards spotted them in distress in a supervised swimming zone. The victims — aged 10, 11, and 16 — were pulled from the water near the Koninklijke Gaanderijen (Royal Galleries) and transported to hospital as a standard precautionary measure, according to [Het Laatste Nieuws](https://www.hln.be/oostende/drie-kinderen-raken-in-de-problemen-in-zee-redders-moeten-ingrijpen-jongste-10-werd-onderkoeld-naar-ziekenhuis-overgebracht~aaeb963c/).

## The Rescue

The incident occurred in a supervised swimming zone during a busy summer afternoon. Lifeguards on duty spotted the three boys struggling in the water and intervened immediately.

"The lifeguards saw that three boys, aged 10, 11 and 16, were in trouble," said Eline Goeminne, spokesperson for the Ostend police zone (Politiezone Oostende), as reported by [La Libre Belgique](https://www.lalibre.be/belgique/societe/2026/07/20/trois-garcons-sauves-de-la-noyade-sur-la-cote-belge-ils-ont-ete-transportes-a-lhopital-IIYOIIETNBAWVA2TYDLPOUFV2Q/).

Once pulled from the water, the youngest victim was found to be suffering from mild hypothermia — a common risk in the North Sea, where water temperatures remain relatively cold even during summer. All three boys were taken to hospital for evaluation.

"In accordance with standard procedure, all three were transported to hospital," Goeminne confirmed.

## Beach Safety Concerns

The rescue comes amid heightened concerns about beach safety on the Belgian coast during the peak summer season. Just days earlier, on July 15, a 44-year-old French man drowned in Knokke-Heist after swimming in an unsupervised zone, prompting frustration from the Coastal Rescue Service (IKWV).

According to HLN, in the first two weeks of July 2026 alone, 53 swimmers had already been rescued from the sea along the Belgian coast. The Flemish Rescue Federation (Vlaamse Reddingsfederatie) issued a public warning on July 16 about swimmer complacency, with chairman Karel Logghe noting that people have become "too nonchalant" and that swimming in open water is fundamentally different from swimming in a pool, as reported by [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/16/vlaamse-reddingsfederatie-pleit-voor-meer-aandacht-voor-zwemmen/).

An Beun of the IKWV noted that warm days do not necessarily mean ideal swimming days. Strong winds, heavy surf, and spring tides had created "unruly sea conditions" in the days leading up to the incident, meaning even experienced swimmers could get into trouble.

## Supervised vs. Unsupervised Zones

The Ostend incident occurred in a supervised zone, which likely contributed to the positive outcome. The IKWV has repeatedly emphasized the importance of swimming only in supervised areas, where lifeguards can monitor conditions and respond quickly to emergencies.

The Belgian coast uses a color-coded flag system to communicate conditions: green (safe swimming), yellow (swimming allowed but no inflatables), and red (swimming prohibited). On the day of the Knokke-Heist drowning, the red flag was raised at 15:00 due to strong currents.

## Analysis and Implications

This incident highlights a critical distinction between supervised and unsupervised swimming zones. While the outcome in Ostend was positive thanks to the rapid intervention of on-duty lifeguards, the broader pattern of rescues along the Belgian coast this summer underscores persistent risks.

The Flemish Rescue Federation has called for better swimming education, particularly in open water, and for increased parental supervision of children near water. Logghe emphasized that swimming in the sea involves challenges not present in pools — including turbidity, greater distances, currents, and cold water — and that basic swimming skills alone may not be sufficient.

## What's Next

All three boys are expected to recover fully, though their current medical condition has not been publicly detailed. The incident serves as a timely reminder for beachgoers as the summer season continues: swim only in supervised zones, heed flag warnings, and maintain constant supervision of children near the water.

As La Libre Belgique noted, "vigilance remains necessary on the Belgian coast during the summer period" — a message that rescue services hope will resonate as thousands more visitors head to the coast in the weeks ahead.
