# Belgian Fugitive Arrested in Thailand on Interpol Warrant

Thai immigration police have arrested a Belgian national in Phuket who was the subject of an INTERPOL Red Notice for attempted murder, illegal weapons possession, and leading a criminal organization. The suspect, identified only under the alias "Mr. Reid," was apprehended outside a condominium in the Patong district on July 19 and now faces extradition to Belgium, according to [HLN](https://www.hln.be/buitenland/belg-die-door-interpol-gezocht-wordt-voor-poging-tot-moord-opgepakt-in-thailand~a28b3709/).

## The Charges

The suspect is wanted by Belgian authorities on serious allegations including attempted murder as an accomplice, illegal possession and carrying of firearms, and involvement as a leader of a criminal organization linked to planned killings. The [Belgian Federal Police](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/20/belg-opgepakt-thailand/) confirmed the arrest but declined to provide further operational details, citing the ongoing investigation. Thai media described the suspect as a "fugitive gang leader" wanted for these serious offenses.

## How the Arrest Unfolded

According to the [Pattaya Mail](https://www.pattayamail.com/news/thailand-hideout-ends-after-interpol-fugitive-gets-too-comfortable-in-phuket-558227), the arrest followed a request from Belgian authorities seeking assistance in locating the suspect. The Special Crime Operations Division of the Immigration Bureau, together with Phuket Immigration Office, conducted the operation.

Immigration investigators traced the man's travel and accommodation records after receiving information from Belgium, discovering that he had been staying at a condominium in Patong. The suspect entered Thailand through Phuket International Airport on May 21, 2026, with permission to stay until August 18. Officers monitored the condominium location before moving in. When the suspect exited the building, they checked his passport details and confirmed his identity matched the INTERPOL notice.

Thai authorities revoked his permission to remain in the country under Articles 12 and 36 of the Thai Immigration Act (1979), citing national security concerns, as reported by the [Phuket Express](https://thephuketexpressnl.com/2026/07/19/Vermoedelijke-Belgische-bendeleider--gezocht-voor-moord--gearresteerd-in-Patong./). He was taken into custody without incident and remains detained pending legal proceedings for his deportation to Belgium.

## Thailand's Stance on Fugitives

Immigration Bureau Commissioner Pol. Lt. Gen. Phanumas Boonyalak emphasized Thailand's commitment to international police cooperation, stating that "Thailand continues to cooperate with domestic and international security agencies to track down individuals wanted on international arrest warrants, stressing that the country will not be a safe haven for fugitives or transnational criminals."

This case underscores Thailand's broader policy of cooperating with international law enforcement, particularly in high-profile cases involving INTERPOL Red Notices. The [Rawai.com](https://www.rawai.com/nl/belgische-onderdaan-op-interpol-opsporingsbericht-gearresteerd-in-patong/) report noted that the suspect's brief hideout in Phuket came to an end when Thai authorities acted swiftly on the Belgian request.

## Broader Context

Phuket, a major tourist destination popular with European expatriates, has occasionally been a destination for individuals seeking to evade justice. The island's large international community and relatively relaxed visa regime have made it an attractive, though ultimately unsafe, refuge for fugitives. Previous cases include the 2012 arrest of Marc De Schutter, a Belgian national wanted for the murder of his wife, and the more recent arrest of a French national wanted for attempted murder.

The suspect's alleged role as a "leader of a criminal organization" suggests links to serious organized crime networks operating in or from Belgium. Belgium has faced ongoing challenges with organized crime groups involved in drug trafficking, firearms, and violence, and this case highlights the international reach of such networks.

## The INTERPOL Red Notice System

A Red Notice is a request to law enforcement worldwide to locate and provisionally arrest a person pending extradition. It is based on an arrest warrant or court order issued by the requesting country. While not an international arrest warrant per se, Red Notices serve as a critical tool for international police cooperation, enabling countries like Thailand to act swiftly when fugitives cross borders.

## What's Next

The suspect remains in Thai custody while legal procedures for his return to Belgium are underway. His full identity has not yet been publicly disclosed by Belgian authorities, and several questions remain unanswered: What specific incidents of attempted murder is he accused of? What is the nature and scope of the criminal organization he allegedly led? And how long will the extradition process take?

What is clear is that the arrest represents a significant success for international police cooperation between Belgium and Thailand, and a clear message that even popular tourist destinations are not safe havens for those wanted for serious crimes.
