# Greene Accuses Hegseth of 'Bloodlust' Over Slain Troops

Former U.S. Representative Marjorie Taylor Greene has accused Secretary of War Pete Hegseth of "bloodlust" after the Pentagon chief described two U.S. service members killed in Iranian strikes as having made a "sacrifice," in an extraordinary public exchange that underscores deepening divisions within the Republican Party over the ongoing war with Iran.

In a profanity-laced post on X on Sunday, Greene responded directly to Hegseth's tribute to the fallen soldiers, writing: "Sacrifice?? What the f--- are you talking about?? They did not willingly sacrifice themselves. They were killed because of the war you, Trump, and the admin, are waging on behalf of a FOREIGN COUNTRY, Israel. You mean their deaths fuel your bloodlust for a senseless war."

## Context: The Iran War and Rising Casualties

The exchange comes more than four months into the U.S.-led war with Iran, which began on February 28, 2026, when the United States and Israel launched coordinated airstrikes against Iran. The conflict has since engulfed the Middle East, with Iran retaliating through missile and drone attacks against U.S. military bases, Israel, and Gulf state allies.

According to [Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/politics/mtg-pounces-hegseth-claiming-us-troop-casualties-fuel-bloodlust-senseless-war), two U.S. service members — 1st Lt. Tyler James Feehan, 25, of Ewa Beach, Hawaii, and Pvt. Isabella Gonzales, 19, of Carrollton, Texas — were killed on July 17 when Iranian ballistic missiles and drones struck Muwaffaq Salti Air Base in Jordan. A third service member remains missing, and unidentified remains have since been recovered at the site. A separate incident in northern Iraq on July 18 claimed the life of another U.S. service member during a controlled detonation of unexploded ordnance.

As of July 20, at least 19 U.S. soldiers have been killed and more than 580 wounded since the conflict began, according to casualty figures tracked by Wikipedia.

## The Exchange

The confrontation began on July 18 when Hegseth shared a U.S. Central Command post announcing the deaths, writing: "Godspeed, heroes. Their sacrifice only stiffens our resolve."

Greene initially responded to CENTCOM's announcement with a call for de-escalation, writing: "I'm praying for the families and friends of those two fallen soldiers and for the third missing soldier. This should not be happening. President Trump needs to bring our troops home and stop this senseless America LAST war!!!"

But the following day, she turned her ire directly on Hegseth, accusing him of using the troops' deaths to justify continued military action.

## Pentagon and White House Respond

Acting Pentagon Press Secretary Joel Valdez condemned Greene's remarks in a statement to Fox News Digital, calling them "disgusting" and accusing the former congresswoman of "using the death of our service members as fuel for her hatred of the Trump administration."

"Ideological disagreements are normal," Valdez said, "but using the death of our service members as fuel for her hatred of the Trump administration is out of line. We all serve at the pleasure of the President of the UNITED STATES, not a foreign nation or foreign leaders."

President Donald Trump, speaking to NewsNation on Sunday, expressed sadness over the deaths but defended the war as necessary to prevent Iran from obtaining nuclear weapons. "Well, we feel very badly. But you know those great people, those great patriots were out there fighting that Iran cannot have a nuclear weapon," Trump said, adding that the U.S. had struck Iran "very hard again tonight" in honor of the fallen.

## A Deeper GOP Fracture

Greene's attack on a sitting defense secretary from her own party represents an extraordinary break from party discipline — one that reflects a growing anti-war faction within Republican ranks. Greene, who resigned from Congress in January 2026 after a falling out with Trump, has aligned with conservative commentators Tucker Carlson and Megyn Kelly in arguing that the war was initiated to serve Israeli interests rather than U.S. national security.

In June 2026, both Greene and Carlson declared they would no longer vote for the GOP due to the Iran war. Democratic Rep. Ro Khanna of California has expressed openness to working with them on anti-war efforts, signaling the potential for an unusual cross-party coalition.

Even Vice President JD Vance has echoed some criticisms, suggesting on the Joe Rogan Experience that "some people within the Israeli government's system... are manipulating and trying to change American public opinion to keep the war going on indefinitely."

## What to Watch

With the war now in its fifth month, no ceasefire in sight, and the U.S. war effort estimated to have cost $113.3 billion as of June 2026, the Iran conflict is poised to become a defining issue in the 2026 midterm elections. Greene has previously warned of a "political revolution" if U.S. troops were deployed into Iran, and her latest broadside against Hegseth suggests she intends to make opposition to the war a central plank of her political identity.

The question now is whether her anti-war message will gain traction among other Republican lawmakers — or whether it will further isolate her from a party that remains, for now, largely united behind the president's war policy.
