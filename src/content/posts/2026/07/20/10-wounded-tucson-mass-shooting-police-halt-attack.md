# 10 Wounded in Tucson Mass Shooting; Police Halt Attack

Ten people were wounded early Sunday morning when a gunman opened fire in a crowded entertainment district in downtown Tucson, Arizona. Police on foot patrol nearby responded within moments, shooting and apprehending the suspect before he could inflict further harm. All victims, including the suspect, are expected to survive.

The shooting unfolded around 2 a.m. on July 19 in the 100 block of East Congress Street, a popular nightlife corridor known for its bars, restaurants, and music venues. According to [Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/us/mass-shooting-rocks-crowded-downtown-tucson-district-leaving-10-wounded), the incident began as a confrontation between two groups who knew each other.

## Rapid Police Response

Tucson Police Department officers patrolling the area on foot heard gunfire and immediately ran toward the sound. They encountered a male suspect fleeing the scene. After the suspect ignored repeated commands, a uniformed officer discharged his department-issued firearm, striking him.

"Those officers immediately began running towards the sound of the gunfire and, in doing so, they came upon the suspect," TPD Public Information Officer Frank Magos said during a press conference, as reported by the [New York Post](https://nypost.com/2026/07/19/us-news/hero-tucson-cops-stop-mass-shooting-that-left-9-people-critically-wounded/). "Officers gave that suspect repeated commands before, ultimately, one of those officers discharged his department-issued firearm at the suspect, striking him."

Officers provided emergency first aid to both the victims and the suspect before Tucson Fire Department personnel arrived. All were transported to local hospitals. Nine victims — all adults — suffered gunshot wounds primarily to their extremities, including arms and legs. The suspect was taken to a hospital in life-threatening condition.

## Witness Account

Anais Camacho was at Empire Pizza with her family around 1 a.m. when she noticed an argument between two groups outside. She told [AZFamily](https://www.azfamily.com/2026/07/19/10-critically-hurt-including-suspect-downtown-tucson-mass-shooting/) that she saw one individual lift his shirt, revealing what she believed to be a weapon.

"I said 'he has a gun, he has a gun.' As soon as we bent the corner, shots fired," Camacho recalled. "I'm feeling grateful that me and my family are all OK. And that we were all able to escape."

## Political Reactions Divided Along Party Lines

The shooting prompted sharply divergent responses from Arizona's political leaders, reflecting the state's deep polarization on gun policy.

Tucson Mayor Regina Romero condemned the shooting as "another serious and senseless act of gun violence" and called for stricter gun safety measures, including universal background checks and extreme risk protection orders. "Easy access to guns and Arizona's weak gun safety laws have continued to put all of us at risk," she said in a statement.

Former U.S. Rep. Gabby Giffords, who survived the 2011 Tucson mass shooting that killed six people, released a statement through her organization GIFFORDS. "Arizonans should be able to go get pizza on a Saturday night without fearing the next random act of gun violence," she said, as [AZFamily reported](https://www.azfamily.com/2026/07/19/arizona-leaders-demand-action-after-shooting-wounds-9-victims-downtown-tucson/). "Arizona continues to have laws that make it easy for dangerous people to access guns — the time to act is now."

Senate President Warren Petersen, speaking for the Senate Republican Caucus, emphasized the right to bear arms. "As this tragedy reminds us, evil exists, which is why we remain steadfast in supporting law enforcement and the constitutional rights of law-abiding Arizonans to protect themselves and others," he wrote.

Both sides praised the quick actions of Tucson police officers and first responders. Sen. Ruben Gallego thanked "the officers and first responders who ran toward the danger. Your courage saved lives."

## Investigation and Broader Context

The Pima County Sheriff's Department is leading the investigation into the officer-involved shooting, while TPD conducts a separate internal review for policy violations. Findings will be sent to the Pima County Attorney's Office for review.

This incident marks the 10th officer-involved shooting in the Tucson area in 2026 and the 52nd statewide in Arizona. It came just one day after two other officer-involved shootings in the Phoenix Valley.

Arizona has some of the most permissive gun laws in the United States. The state does not require a permit to carry a concealed firearm, does not mandate universal background checks for private sales, and does not have a "red flag" law.

## What's Next

Authorities have not yet released the suspect's identity. Investigators are asking anyone with information or video of the shooting to contact the Pima County Sheriff's Department. As all victims are expected to recover, attention will turn to whether this incident prompts any legislative action at the state level — a question that divides Arizona's political leadership along familiar lines.
