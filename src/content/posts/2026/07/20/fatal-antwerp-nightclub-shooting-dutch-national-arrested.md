# Fatal Antwerp Nightclub Shooting: Dutch National Arrested

A 23-year-old man was fatally shot in the early hours of Sunday morning outside Club Ikon in Antwerp, Belgium, following a dispute over parking. Belgian police have arrested a 59-year-old Dutch national, identified as Steven S., who was already wanted by Interpol for a murder in Suriname.

The shooting occurred at approximately 6:00 AM on July 19 at the parking lot of Club Ikon, located at Straatsburgdok Noordkaai in Antwerp's port district. The victim, Jonathan Pinas Sercinio, a 23-year-old resident of Geel, was attending a Surinamese music event called "Sranang Fest" at the nightclub with his brother and friends, according to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/19/twintiger-overleden-bij-schietpartij-aan-antwerpse-discotheek/).

## A Parking Dispute Turns Deadly

As the night drew to a close, Jonathan attempted to move his car closer to the exit. A verbal altercation erupted with a group of men in their 40s and 50s who claimed he had maneuvered too close to them. Jonathan stepped out of his vehicle to address the situation.

His brother, Donnevon Pinas Sercinio, who witnessed the incident, described the tragic sequence of events to [Het Laatste Nieuws](https://www.hln.be/binnenland/mijn-broer-kroop-nog-zelf-in-de-auto-maar-hij-zakte-meteen-in-elkaar-jonathan-23-wilde-enkel-dichterbij-parkeren-en-werd-toen-doodgeschoten~a6119f57/). "My brother got into an argument on the parking lot with a group that thought he maneuvered too close to them. He got out. And then I heard that shot."

According to witnesses, one man briefly left the group, returned, and fired a single shot in Jonathan's direction. Despite being hit, Jonathan managed to get back into his car but immediately collapsed. He was resuscitated at the scene and rushed to hospital, where he later died from his injuries.

## A Young Life Cut Short

Jonathan Pinas Sercinio had moved with his family from Suriname to Belgium in 2018. He completed his secondary education in Belgium, earned a diploma in electricity, and had been working at the sealant manufacturer Soudal for four years. His brother described him as a "very serious and hardworking guy."

The tragedy is compounded by the fact that Jonathan was about to become a father for the second time. His heavily pregnant girlfriend Zeresa, who works as a caregiver for the elderly, is expecting a son. The couple already had a two-year-old son named Zydan. "His two-year-old son doesn't understand that his daddy is gone," Donnevon told HLN.

## Suspect Arrested with Mocro-Maffia Ties

Later on Sunday, Belgian police's Rapid Response Team arrested Steven S. at a residence in Lint, a municipality in Antwerp province. During the house search, several other individuals were taken into custody, including the suspect's partner and his minor son. "Their involvement in the facts is being investigated," said Wouter Bruyns, spokesperson for the Antwerp police, as reported by [NOS](https://nos.nl/artikel/2623672-nederlander-59-vast-voor-dodelijke-schietpartij-bij-nachtclub-antwerpen).

Steven S., who goes by the nicknames "Red" and "Burru," is described as a known figure in the Mocro-maffia criminal circuit — organized crime groups in the Netherlands and Belgium involved in large-scale drug trafficking. He was already internationally wanted by Interpol for the 2014 murder of 37-year-old Melvin Zoutkamp in Paramaribo, Suriname, a case with striking similarities to the Antwerp shooting.

## A Chilling Parallel

The 2014 Suriname case bears an uncanny resemblance to Sunday's events. Melvin Zoutkamp was shot dead in Paramaribo after getting out of his car during a verbal altercation with occupants of another vehicle. Steven S., his son, and a third man were arrested at the time but released due to insufficient evidence.

Steven S.'s son, also named Steven, is currently serving a prison sentence in the Netherlands for large-scale cocaine trafficking. His name emerged from the cracked SKY ECC encrypted communications network, revealing that he had established cocaine routes to Antwerp and Hamburg with shipments ranging from 200 to 500 kilograms.

## Investigation Ongoing

The suspect's vehicle was found in the Heidelaan in Lint. Steven S. is known to Belgian police for several minor offenses. As of Monday, he was being interrogated by authorities as the investigation continues.

The case highlights the interconnected nature of crime between Belgium, the Netherlands, and Suriname, and raises questions about whether the shooting was a spontaneous act during a parking dispute or whether there were deeper organized crime dimensions at play. Legal proceedings will also need to address the international dimension, including the outstanding Interpol warrant for the 2014 Suriname murder.

For the family of Jonathan Pinas Sercinio, however, the focus remains on the devastating loss of a young father. As his brother Donnevon said: "We also don't know what to tell him. He won't understand that his daddy is gone."
