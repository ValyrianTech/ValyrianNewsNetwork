# Brussels Airport Sees Record Summer Crowds with 92,000 Passengers

Brussels Airport experienced its busiest day of the summer season on Monday, 20 July 2026, with approximately 92,000 passengers expected to pass through the terminal. Long queues formed at check-in counters and baggage drop-off points as a convergence of holiday travel, a bridge day, and the return of Tomorrowland festival-goers pushed the airport to near-record capacity.

According to [HLN](https://www.hln.be/zaventem/ik-heb-het-hier-nog-nooit-zo-druk-gezien-lange-wachtrijen-op-brussels-airport-waar-vandaag-92-000-reizigers-verwacht-worden~aa7e36f0/), the airport was expecting roughly 41,000 departing and 51,000 arriving travellers. Despite the heavy crowds, security and passport control for departing passengers reportedly ran smoothly, though arriving non-EU passengers faced approximately 45-minute waits at passport control due to the new European Entry/Exit System (EES).

## Why the Surge?

The record crowds were driven by several coinciding factors. "Due to the combination of the construction holiday, the summer vacation, the bridge day (for the national holiday) and the return of Tomorrowland visitors, all those travelers come together today," said Jeffrey Franssens, spokesperson for Brussels Airport, as reported by HLN.

Belgium's National Day falls on 21 July, creating a bridge day that allowed many workers to extend their weekend. The construction holiday (bouwverlof) — a traditional break in the Belgian building sector — and the ongoing school summer holidays added to the travel surge, while thousands of festival-goers returning from Tomorrowland in Boom further swelled passenger numbers.

## Passenger Experience

Travelers arriving at the airport reported mixed experiences. Mathijs, departing for Singapore with his family, told HLN: "We arrived three hours early, so we can leave without stress." Josée, travelling from the Netherlands to Gran Canaria, remarked: "I've never seen it so busy here."

Despite the congestion, most passengers appeared well-prepared. Families like Evi and Koen from Zandhoven, travelling to Japan via Hong Kong, arrived early and reported smooth check-in processes. "We were actually even too early," they said. "Our flight doesn't leave until one o'clock, but everywhere it was recommended to be well in advance."

## EES Delays for Non-EU Travellers

While departing passengers faced few issues at security, arriving non-EU travellers experienced significant delays. The new European Entry/Exit System (EES), which introduces additional border checks for non-European passport holders, resulted in waiting times of around 45 minutes at passport control. Belgian and other European travellers could use automatic e-gates and faced minimal disruption.

Brussels Airport has been preparing for the summer rush with extra staff deployed in the terminal, pop-up check-in desks, and a new Self Bag Drop zone for passengers of SAS, Finnair, KLM, and Transavia. Nearly 60 pre-registration kiosks have also been installed for EES registration, as [reported by Sidetrack](https://sidetrack.blog/2026/06/24/brussels-airport-is-expecting-5-2-million-passengers-this-summer-holiday-arriving-at-the-airport-in-good-time-and-well-prepared-for-security-screening-remain-key-for-a-smooth-departure/).

## Summer 2026 Outlook

The 92,000-passenger day approaches the all-time single-day record of 98,000 set in summer 2019. Across July and August, Brussels Airport expects to welcome 5.2 million passengers — a 4% increase over summer 2025. The airport handled 11.7 million passengers in the first half of 2026, up 3.6% year-on-year, according to [TripByTrip](https://tripbytrip.org/2026/07/18/brussels-airport-closes-first-half-of-2026-with-3-6-passenger-growth-despite-middle-east-strait-of-hormuz-conflict/).

## Broader Implications

The record crowds underscore sustained strong demand for air travel in Belgium, with Brussels Airport operating near its peak single-day capacity. The airport has demonstrated remarkable resilience in 2026, achieving 3.6% passenger growth in the first half of the year despite significant headwinds. Industrial action — including two national strike days in March and May affecting approximately 55,000 passengers, and a spontaneous strike by air traffic control provider Skeyes on 2 June disrupting another 25,000 travellers — has tested operations. The Middle East conflict has also led to the suspension of flights to Tel Aviv and reduced services to several Gulf destinations.

Cargo traffic has been a bright spot, with volumes rising 8.3% to nearly 420,000 tonnes in the first half of 2026, driven by an expanding cargo network and ongoing investments in modernising facilities.

The continued growth raises questions about long-term infrastructure needs. Renovation works at the airport are ongoing and expected to be completed by 2032. The EES implementation remains a friction point for non-EU travellers, though the deployment of nearly 60 pre-registration kiosks and increased staffing at border control are expected to gradually reduce wait times.

## What to Watch

Whether Brussels Airport will break the 2019 single-day record of 98,000 passengers before the summer ends remains an open question. With the peak travel season still underway, strong demand continuing, and the airport's network now offering 180 direct destinations served by more than 70 airlines, even busier days may lie ahead. The airport supports approximately 64,000 jobs in Belgium and serves as a critical economic hub for the region.
