# US and Iran Trade Strikes for Ninth Night as War Escalates

The United States military completed its ninth consecutive night of airstrikes against Iran on Sunday, as Tehran responded with retaliatory missile and drone attacks targeting American assets across the Gulf region. The intensifying exchange of fire has killed three more US service members over the weekend, bringing the total American military fatalities in the nearly five-month conflict to 17.

According to [US Central Command](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/20/us-iran-strait-of-hormuz-hostilities-casualties-.html), the three-hour operation targeted Iranian military command centers, air defense systems, coastal surveillance sites, maritime capabilities, and missile and drone launch sites. The stated objective was to "further diminish Iran's ability to attack commercial vessels and civilian mariners transiting the Strait of Hormuz."

## A Conflict in Escalation

The latest strikes mark a significant intensification of what began on February 28, 2026, when the United States and Israel launched coordinated strikes on Iran, killing Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei and sparking a major regional war. A 60-day ceasefire established through a Pakistan-mediated memorandum of understanding in June has effectively collapsed, with both sides accusing each other of violations.

[Al Jazeera reported](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/7/20/iran-war-live-us-military-carries-out-another-wave-of-strikes-on-iran) that Iranian media recorded explosions in multiple cities overnight, including Tabriz — targeted for the first time — as well as Bushehr, Hormozgan, Sistan and Baluchestan, and Khuzestan provinces. In response, Iran launched a new wave of missiles from Lorestan province targeting US military bases and assets across the region.

## Rising Toll on All Sides

The human cost of the conflict continues to mount. Three American service members were killed over the weekend: one died in northern Iraq during the controlled detonation of a downed Iranian drone, while two others were killed in an Iranian attack on a military base in Jordan on July 17. Human remains were recovered in Jordan after a service member was listed as missing in action.

Iranian authorities reported at least 50 people killed and more than 500 wounded in the renewed US strikes in July, according to CNBC. US strikes have also widened to target civilian infrastructure, including the Bonji desalination plant, which cut off water supplies to approximately 10,000 people.

## The Strait of Hormuz at the Center

The Strait of Hormuz — through which approximately 20% of the world's oil traffic passed before the war — has become the central battleground. Traffic has slowed to the lowest level in three weeks, with only eight transits recorded on July 16 compared to 15 a day earlier. Brent crude futures topped $90.7 a barrel, rising nearly 3%, while US WTI futures climbed 2.5% to $84.6.

US Energy Secretary Chris Wright claimed that roughly 14 million barrels per day — about two-thirds of pre-conflict traffic — is still moving through the waterway, down from approximately 20 million barrels per day before the conflict.

Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) claimed on Monday that two oil tankers "exploded" attempting to travel through what it described as an "unsafe and dangerous route south of the Strait of Hormuz," warning that oil, gas, and petrochemical shipments through the strait would remain at risk as long as US regional operations continue, as [The Jerusalem Post reported](https://www.jpost.com/middle-east/iran-news/article-903012).

## Regional Fallout

Iran's retaliatory strikes have targeted US military assets in neighboring countries, including Kuwait, Bahrain, Jordan, and Saudi Arabia. The Kuwaiti army confirmed it was intercepting "hostile" drone attacks from Iran. Air raid sirens sounded across Bahrain, and the US embassy in Manama warned of potential Iranian attacks in the capital.

[Press TV](https://www.presstv.ir/Detail/2026/07/16/772345/Iran-Armed-Forces-Army-IRGC-new-strikes-United-States-Kuwait-Jordan) reported that the IRGC claimed hits on the Ali Al Salem airbase in Kuwait, destroying an early-warning radar system and setting MQ-9 drones on fire. Iran's Army also said it launched drone strikes against US military facilities at Jordan's al-Azraq Airbase.

## Ceasefire Collapse and Diplomatic Impasse

The June 2026 memorandum of understanding, which established a 60-day ceasefire, has unravelled amid mutual accusations of violations. Michael Feller, chief strategist at Geopolitical Strategy, told CNBC that the agreement "said little about how disputed incidents would be investigated or how a breach in one area would affect obligations elsewhere."

Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi stated in an interview with state media that "the end of the war is possible either through absolute military victory or through negotiations," adding that "the right time to negotiate is precisely when you have achieved a reliable field and strategic achievement on the military front."

IRGC Spokesperson Brigadier General Hossein Mohebbi warned that "the enemy should not assume that it can sustain the current dynamics of the battlefield and turn the war into a war of attrition," adding that "Iran's operations are currently focused on destroying the US's offensive infrastructure in the region. The next phases will then begin."

## What to Watch

With both sides adopting maximalist positions and the ceasefire framework in tatters, the conflict shows no signs of de-escalation. US Secretary of State Marco Rubio has urged global powers to share the burden of protecting Strait of Hormuz shipping, while Iran continues to assert its control over the waterway.

Analysts warn that the widening of the conflict to include multiple Gulf states risks a broader regional war. Sina Azodi of George Washington University told Al Jazeera that Iran has accepted the risk of further alienating its neighbors because it views the conflict as "a war of survival and existence."

The economic impact continues to ripple through global markets, and the humanitarian toll — particularly from strikes on civilian infrastructure — is drawing increasing international concern. With negotiations appearing distant and both sides preparing for further escalation, the Middle East faces the prospect of a protracted and devastating conflict with no clear off-ramp in sight.
