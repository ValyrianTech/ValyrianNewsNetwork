# Li Qiang Chairs Economic Symposium, Signals H2 Support

Chinese Premier Li Qiang chaired a high-level economic symposium in Beijing on July 13, bringing together four leading economists and four prominent entrepreneurs to assess the country's economic situation and gather policy recommendations for the second half of 2026. The meeting comes at a critical juncture as China navigates the opening year of its 15th Five-Year Plan amid domestic consumption weakness and intensifying global uncertainties, as [reported by Xinhua News Agency](https://tv.cctv.com/2026/07/13/VIDEUVdpmVP9I1PZigrtG5QA260713.shtml).

## Context: A Pivotal Moment for China's Economy

2026 marks the first year of China's 15th Five-Year Plan (2026–2030), making economic performance this year particularly significant for setting the trajectory of the entire plan period. According to [People's Daily](http://politics.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0714/c1024-40759800.html), the symposium focused on analyzing new trends in economic operations and fine-tuning macroeconomic policy.

China's economy faces headwinds on multiple fronts. Total retail sales of consumer goods in May 2026 fell by 0.6% year-on-year, a concerning signal for the consumption-driven recovery. Meanwhile, the international environment is described as one of "intertwined and intensifying changes and turbulence," with ongoing geopolitical tensions and trade frictions affecting export-oriented sectors.

## Who Attended and Why It Matters

The eight participants who spoke at the symposium reflect the government's dual strategic focus. The four experts — Huang Hanquan (President, Chinese Academy of Macroeconomic Research), Yang Zhiyong (President, Chinese Academy of Fiscal Sciences), Wang Wen (Dean, Chongyang Institute for Financial Studies), and Zhang Jun (Chief Economist, China Galaxy Securities) — cover macroeconomics, fiscal policy, financial markets, and international relations.

The four entrepreneurs represent a carefully calibrated mix. Hou Qijun (Chairman, Sinopec) and Wang Zhijian (Chairman, Shandong Heavy Industry Group) represent traditional state-owned heavy industries undergoing transformation. Notably, Yu Donglai, founder of Pangdonglai Retail — a regional chain renowned for exceptional customer service and employee welfare — was invited, signaling the government's urgent focus on reviving consumer spending. Li Jun, President of Sugon (a leading high-performance computing and AI infrastructure company), underscores the priority placed on technological self-reliance and AI development.

As [China News Service](https://www.chinanews.com.cn/cj/2026/07-14/10658876.shtml) noted in its analysis, Yu Donglai represents the need to "stabilize the present and secure people's livelihoods," while Li Jun represents the drive to "upgrade industries, create new growth, and lay the groundwork for the future."

## Key Policy Signals from Premier Li

Premier Li Qiang's remarks, as reported by CCTV's Xinwen Lianbo, outlined several priority areas for the second half of 2026:

**Counter-cyclical adjustment:** Li emphasized the need to "maintain strategic focus on high-quality development, increase counter-cyclical adjustment, make good use of existing policies, and reserve incremental policies."

**Consumption and domestic demand:** The Premier called for systematic policies to "release domestic demand potential," including expanding the supply of quality services and accelerating the cultivation of new consumption growth points. This follows the State Council's approval on July 2 of the "Expanding Consumption 15th Five-Year Plan," which targets total retail sales of consumer goods reaching approximately 60 trillion yuan by 2030.

**Technology and industrial transformation:** Li stressed the need to "promote the smooth connection and transformation of old and new growth drivers," accelerate the digital and intelligent transformation of manufacturing, and facilitate the "large-scale commercial application of new technologies such as artificial intelligence."

**Employment and business support:** The Premier urged efforts to stabilize employment and support enterprises, calling on entrepreneurs to "strengthen their confidence in development, deeply explore opportunities from demand upgrades and industrial upgrades, and continue to strengthen the real economy."

## Analysis: A Dual-Track Strategy

The symposium's composition and messaging reveal a deliberate dual-track strategy. On one track, the government aims to stabilize the economy in the near term through consumption stimulus and support for traditional industries. On the other, it is laying the groundwork for long-term growth through technological innovation and industrial upgrading.

The presence of Yu Donglai — a retail entrepreneur rather than a traditional heavy industry figure — marks a notable departure from past symposiums and underscores the urgency of reviving consumer confidence. Pangdonglai's model of high-quality service, transparent pricing, and strong employee welfare is being held up as a template for the kind of consumption-driven growth Beijing wants to encourage.

Meanwhile, Sugon's Li Jun reinforces the message that AI, high-performance computing, and advanced manufacturing are central to China's economic transformation. As the country seeks to reduce reliance on traditional sectors like real estate and heavy industry, technology-driven growth is seen as the key to sustainable development.

## What to Watch For

Market observers will be watching for specific policy measures following this symposium. Premier Li's mention of "reserving incremental policies" suggests additional stimulus could be deployed if economic data continues to disappoint. Potential measures may include interest rate adjustments, targeted fiscal spending on infrastructure and social welfare, and further steps to boost consumer confidence.

The symposium also raises questions about how China will balance consumption stimulus with fiscal sustainability, and how the transition between old and new growth drivers will be managed without disrupting employment. With the 15th Five-Year Plan now underway, the policy direction set in the coming months will shape China's economic trajectory for the remainder of the decade.
