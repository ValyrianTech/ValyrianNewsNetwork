# Xi Jinping Issues Instructions on Basic Education Development

Chinese President Xi Jinping has issued important instructions on basic education, calling for comprehensive implementation of the Party's education policy and high-quality development of China's school system. The directives, reported on July 22, 2026, were conveyed at the National Basic Education Work Conference in Beijing, where Politburo Standing Committee member and Vice Premier Ding Xuexiang delivered a keynote address.

## Strategic Priority for National Development

The instructions mark a significant policy signal from China's top leadership, placing basic education — which encompasses preschool through high school — as a foundational pillar for building a strong education nation by 2030. According to [Xinhua News Agency](https://www.news.cn/politics/leaders/20260722/1f778d6335b647308d0e73380411e18f/c.html), Xi emphasized the need to "comprehensively implement the Party's education policy, fulfill the fundamental task of fostering virtue through education, and cultivate socialist builders and successors who are well-developed morally, intellectually, physically, aesthetically, and labor-wise."

The instructions come just weeks after the State Council issued the [15th Five-Year Plan for Education Development](https://www.gov.cn/zhengce/content/202606/content_7073640.htm) on June 29, 2026, which sets ambitious targets for advancing China's education system. The timing suggests a coordinated policy push, with the National Basic Education Work Conference serving as the implementation mechanism for translating the Five-Year Plan's goals into concrete action.

## Key Directives on Quality and Equity

Xi's instructions outlined several priority areas for China's education system. The president called on educators to "follow the laws of education, implement the concept of health first, improve the quality of education and teaching, and promote the physical and mental health of students." He also stressed the importance of education equity, urging authorities to "adapt to the new expectations of the people, promote the expansion and quality improvement of basic education, and firmly hold the bottom line of education fairness."

The [Ministry of Education](https://www.moe.gov.cn/jyb_xwfb/s6052/moe_838/202607/t20260722_1444692.html) confirmed that the conference was attended by senior officials including Shi Taifeng, Li Shulei, Wang Xiaohong, and Wu Zhenglong. Representatives from the Ministry of Education, Central Institutional Organization Commission, National Development and Reform Commission, Ministry of Finance, and several provincial governments delivered exchange speeches.

## Ding Xuexiang on Implementation

Speaking at the conference, Vice Premier Ding Xuexiang described Xi's instructions as "highly political, ideological, and guiding, pointing the direction for basic education work on the new journey of the new era." As reported by the [Chinese Government Portal](https://www.gov.cn/yaowen/liebiao/202607/content_7076265.htm), Ding emphasized the need to deeply study and understand the directives and ensure their implementation.

Ding highlighted several concrete measures, including strict enrollment management, enhanced care for rural left-behind children and children of migrant workers, and adaptation of education resource allocation to demographic changes — a reference to China's declining birth rate. He also called for improving teacher professional standards and compensation to attract more outstanding talent to the teaching profession.

## Broader Context and Policy Framework

The instructions build on years of sustained policy attention to education under Xi's leadership. A [People's Daily commentary](https://www.moe.gov.cn/jyb_xwfb/s5148/202607/t20260723_1444734.html) published on July 23 noted that "building a strong education nation starts with basic education. The more solid the basic education, the more stable and vigorous the pace of building a strong education nation."

China's basic education system is the world's largest, encompassing 430,000 primary and secondary schools and kindergartens, 220 million enrolled students, and 15.77 million full-time teachers. By the end of 2024, China's basic education had reached the average level of world high-income countries, according to official data.

The 15th Five-Year Plan for Education, which runs through 2030, targets significant advances in building a high-quality education system. Key priorities include improving education resource allocation in response to population changes, expanding and improving basic education quality, and strengthening the comprehensive cultivation system that integrates moral, intellectual, physical, aesthetic, and labor education.

## Analysis and Implications

The high-level attention signaled by Xi's personal instructions — combined with coverage on China's most-watched national news program, Xinwen Lianbo, and republication across all major state media — indicates that basic education is being elevated as a top national priority. This is not a routine policy announcement but a strategic directive from the highest level of leadership.

Analysts note that the emphasis on education equity, particularly the focus on rural left-behind children and migrant workers' children, reflects the government's concern about social inequality and its potential implications for stability. The mention of adapting to "population changes" directly addresses the challenges posed by China's declining birth rate, which has significant consequences for school planning, teacher deployment, and infrastructure investment.

## What to Watch For

As the directives from the National Basic Education Work Conference are implemented, observers will be watching for specific policy measures and funding allocations. Key questions include how the adaptation to demographic changes will affect school closures or mergers in areas with declining birth rates, what concrete steps will be taken to improve teacher salaries, and how the balance between ideological education and academic quality will be managed in practice. The conference represents a significant milestone in China's ongoing efforts to transform its education system from one focused on access to one emphasizing quality and comprehensive development.
