# Bike Service Station Opens at Edge of Brussels

A new "decarbonized service station" for bicycles has opened in Jezus-Eik on the southeastern edge of Brussels, offering maintenance, repair, and sales services to the growing community of commuters cycling into the Belgian capital. Operated by Belgian bicycle chain Bike Square, the facility is strategically positioned along the F204 bicycle highway (cyclostrade) connecting Overijse to Brussels, as [reported by La Libre Belgique](https://www.lalibre.be/belgique/mobilite/2026/07/23/une-station-service-pour-les-velos-ouvre-ses-portes-a-la-lisiere-de-bruxelles-ZV3Q2J5RCBDPXAZHTECJR2QG3A/).

## A Strategic Double Opening

The Jezus-Eik location, situated at the edge of the Sonian Forest at the gateway to Brussels, opened simultaneously with a second new Bike Square store in Woluwe-Saint-Lambert in eastern Brussels. The company, which already operates two stores in Ixelles and Woluwe-Saint-Pierre, now brings its total to four locations.

According to a press release quoted by La Libre Belgique, "This double opening, achieved following a recently completed strategic fundraising, marks the launch of an ambitious growth plan aiming to deploy seven stores by 2027 to cover the southeast of Brussels and its periphery." The expansion signals Bike Square's confidence in the region's cycling market and its commitment to serving the rapidly growing community of cyclists.

## The F204 Cycle Highway: A Growing Corridor

The Jezus-Eik location is no coincidence. It sits directly along the F204 bicycle highway, a major infrastructure project by the Flemish government agency De Werkvennootschap that will ultimately connect Overijse to Brussels, continuing to Rosières and Wavre across the Walloon border. According to [Werken aan de Ring](https://www.werkenaandering.be/en/working-on/cycling-infrastructure/f204-bicycle-highway), construction began in February 2023 and is expected to be completed by mid-2028, with the first section already in use and the second phase beginning in August 2026.

The current cycle path between Jezus-Eik and Herrmann-Debroux metro station is described as "uninviting, with significant changes in elevation, very narrow, and running alongside a busy motorway." The new F204 promises comfortable cycle paths, dedicated cycle bridges, priority at junctions, and separation from motorway traffic.

## More Than a Bike Shop: A "Decarbonized Service Station"

Bike Square's concept goes beyond traditional bicycle retail. In a [blog post published on June 2, 2026](https://www.bikesquare.be/en_GB/blog/news-that-keeps-you-rolling-1/bike-highway-services-mobility-hub-jezus-eik-7), the company articulated its philosophy: "For too long, cycling infrastructure has been reduced to a simple matter of civil engineering: drawing lines, pouring asphalt, and separating traffic flows. A bike path without services is dead infrastructure. It's just a route, not a solution."

The company frames the relationship between infrastructure and services as a duality: "Infrastructure is the skeleton. Service is the muscle. A commuter who worries about breaking down halfway through their Schuman-Overijse ride is a commuter who, out of caution, will end up taking their car." The Jezus-Eik location is positioned as an "Active Mobility Hub" and "intermodal node," offering specialized expertise for electric bikes, speed pedelecs, and cargo bikes alongside traditional bicycle repair.

## Cycling Boom in Brussels

The opening comes at a time of remarkable growth in Brussels cycling. According to the [Brussels Cycling Observatory](https://www.provelo.org/observatoire-du-velo-bruxellois-les-resultats-2025/), produced by Pro Velo with support from Bruxelles Mobilité, the number of cyclists during morning peak hours grew by a record 15.3% in 2025 compared to 2024. The data reveals an almost equal split between electric (49%) and traditional (51%) bicycles, with e-bikes covering longer distances (7.6 km versus 5.2 km for traditional bikes). Notably, 22% of bicycles are equipped for child transport, reflecting the growing use of cargo bikes and longtails for family mobility.

Public and philanthropic support for cycling infrastructure remains strong. The Fonds Bikes in Brussels, managed by the [King Baudouin Foundation](https://kbs-frb.be/fr/velo-bruxelles-15-million-deuros-13-nouveaux-projets-dinfrastructures-cyclables), awarded nearly €1.5 million to 13 new cycling infrastructure projects in May 2026, including funding for the F204 cycle highway itself.

## Implications for Urban Mobility

The Jezus-Eik bike service station represents a significant step in creating a complete cycling ecosystem. By addressing the practical concern of breakdowns and maintenance — what Bike Square calls the "psychological barrier" — such facilities may help accelerate the shift from car to bicycle commuting. The concept of a "decarbonized service station" positioned along a cycle highway could serve as a template for other cities investing in cycling infrastructure.

The location at the edge of Brussels also highlights the importance of serving suburban commuters who face longer distances and greater reliance on e-bikes. As Bike Square concludes in its blog: "For the mobility transition to succeed, we need to stop just building roads and start building trust."

## What's Next

With the F204 bicycle highway's second phase beginning in August 2026 and full completion expected by mid-2028, the cycling corridor between Overijse and Brussels will continue to improve. Bike Square's target of seven stores by 2027 suggests the company is betting that the cycling trend in and around Brussels is not a passing phenomenon but a permanent shift in how people move.
