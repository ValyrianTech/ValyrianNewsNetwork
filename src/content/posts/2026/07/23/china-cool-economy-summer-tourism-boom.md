# China's 'Cool Economy' Turns Summer Heat into Tourism Gold

As sweltering temperatures grip much of China, a growing number of travelers are heading for the hills — literally. Summer escape tourism, or "cool economy" travel, has become the dominant trend in China's summer cultural tourism consumption, transforming traditionally cooler regions into powerful economic engines. From the mist-shrouded gorges of Guizhou to the alpine meadows of Hunan, destinations with naturally cool climates are seeing record visitor numbers and significant economic gains.

## What Is the 'Cool Economy'?

The "cool economy" (清凉经济) refers to the economic activity generated by summer tourism to cooler destinations across China. Regions with naturally cooler climates — mountainous areas, northern latitudes, and high-altitude plateaus — are transforming their "cool resources" into economic drivers during the hot summer months. According to [CCTV News](https://news.cctv.com/2026/07/23/ARTIjDtqTPBVX7h1URukSPGa260723.shtml), this trend has become the mainstream of summer cultural tourism consumption nationwide.

## Record Numbers at Key Destinations

Several destinations are experiencing unprecedented summer tourism surges:

- **Guizhou Xingyi**: The Malinghe Gorge, known for its "hundred waterfalls" spectacle and average summer temperature of 23°C, has seen tourist numbers increase by 35.86% year-on-year since summer began. Local hotels and民宿 are fully booked, and restaurant patronage has risen sharply.
- **Jilin Changbai Mountain**: Located on the "golden summer escape latitude belt" at 42°N, Changbai Mountain averages 22°C in summer. Single-day visitor numbers have exceeded 30,000 since July, with significant growth in inbound tourists thanks to visa-free policies for Russia and South Korea.
- **Guizhou Liupanshui**: Known as "China's Cool Capital," Liupanshui averages just 19.6°C in summer. Its summer season runs from May 5 to October 7, featuring over 20 themed events and 200 performances.
- **Shanxi Huguan**: The Taihang Mountain Grand Canyon is drawing an average of 25,000 daily visitors seeking refuge from the heat.
- **Yunnan Shangri-La**: Even in summer, this high-altitude destination remains cool. New initiatives include direct scenic buses, upgraded visitor centers, and medical emergency facilities.
- **Hunan Xinning**: The Golden Pasture, with an average elevation over 1,200 meters and summer temperatures below 23°C, has become a popular family destination.

## Surging Search and Booking Trends

The data confirms the scale of the trend. Meituan reports that "避暑" (escape heat) search volume was up 45% year-on-year in July, while "避暑游" (summer escape tourism) searches surged nearly 200% month-on-month. Tongcheng Travel data shows "避暑" searches up over 50% week-on-week, as [reported by China Tourism Network](http://www.cntour.cn/h-nd-4650.html).

Booking platforms are seeing similar momentum. Ctrip reports water-related activities — water parks, rafting, coastal islands, and beaches — up over 40% year-on-year. Tongcheng Travel's long-distance summer escape route bookings have risen over 20% month-on-month. Notably, flight bookings from European cities like Edinburgh and Madrid to Chinese summer destinations have surged over 80% year-on-year through Tongcheng's HopeGoo platform.

## Rural Tourism Gets a Boost

The "cool economy" is also driving a rural tourism renaissance. According to [Cover News](https://www.163.com/dy/article/L29TITRS0514D3UH.html), analysts note that many villages are using "coolness as their base color" and activating cultural elements to create immersive experiences. The Ministry of Culture and Tourism reports that Q1 2026 rural tourism reached 793 million person-trips, up 12.2% year-on-year, with total revenue of 457 billion yuan, up 10.9%.

This trend is supported by policy. The 2026 Central Document No. 1 emphasizes deepening agricultural-cultural-tourism integration and promoting rural tourism upgrading, developing "small but beautiful" cultural tourism formats.

## Changing Consumer Behavior

Industry insiders point out that summer escape tourism has evolved from simply "finding coolness" to a comprehensive experience integrating nature, culture, and health, as [People's Daily noted](http://ex.chinadaily.com.cn/exchange/partners/82/rss/channel/cn/columns/t40vsk/stories/WS688b131fa310a07bb590b359.html). Tongcheng Travel's 2026 Summer Escape Trends Report confirms that tourists are choosing destinations not just for cool temperatures but for ecological resources, cultural heritage, and unique activities.

The demographic profile is notably broad. Seniors aged 60-75 account for 60% of health and wellness tourism purchases in traditional summer destinations, while family travelers aged 30-45 make up nearly 40% of long-distance and weekend escape product bookings.

## Economic Impact and Forward Look

The "cool economy" is providing significant stimulus to traditionally less-developed mountainous and rural regions, creating jobs in hospitality, food services, and local crafts. Local governments are actively supporting the trend — Liupanshui has issued 12 million yuan in consumption vouchers, while Jingzhou, Hubei, has allocated 4.5 million yuan in cultural tourism subsidies.

However, challenges lie ahead. Balancing tourism growth with environmental protection in ecologically sensitive areas like Changbai Mountain and Shangri-La will be critical. Regions dependent on summer tourism may also need to develop year-round attractions to avoid seasonal economic volatility.

As the summer of 2026 unfolds, China's "cool economy" is proving that when it comes to tourism, sometimes the hottest trend is actually finding someplace cool.
