# Yunnan Mojiang 5.0 Earthquake: No Casualties Reported

A 5.0 magnitude earthquake struck Mojiang Hani Autonomous County in Yunnan Province on the evening of July 21, triggering a swift multi-agency emergency response that resulted in no casualties despite significant property damage. As of July 22 at noon, authorities had relocated 33 households (94 people) and reported 221 houses with cracked or damaged walls across Mojiang and Ning'er counties, according to the Pu'er City Earthquake Relief Command.

The earthquake, recorded at 19:43 local time with a focal depth of 6 km, was centered at Yutang Town's Jingping Village in Mojiang County, as reported by the [China Earthquake Networks Center](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260722/f45f25a62dd14182a423b548114af581/c.html). Yunnan Province activated a Level IV earthquake emergency response, with Pu'er City and Mojiang County following suit, dispatching rescue teams and government work groups to the epicenter within minutes.

## Swift Evacuations and Community Response

Village cadres began door-to-door evacuations shortly after the quake struck at around 20:00 local time. In Laohezhai hamlet, villager Yang Jiayun described the terrifying moment the earthquake hit: "First I heard a huge rumbling sound, and before long the wall cracked open." His production house, used for storage and cattle, was severely damaged, with roof tiles that had been laid just over a week ago scattered on the ground.

Elderly and vulnerable residents received priority attention. Li Wancai, an elderly villager from the nearby Mulaohu hamlet — which was not directly affected — was evacuated alongside his wife by village cadres concerned about their remote location. "I just had surgery, and the village cadres were worried, so they brought my wife and me over. I'm very moved," Li told reporters.

By midnight, villagers were being transported to temporary shelters. Yang Gaizhi, a resident of Laohezhai, recalled arriving at the Huitoushan shelter: "By the time I arrived at the shelter, the tents were already set up, there were blankets, and I could lie down and rest."

## Multi-Agency Coordination in Action

The response involved coordinated efforts across multiple government agencies and state-owned enterprises. The [China National Radio](https://www.cnr.cn/yn/gstjyn/20260721/t20260721_527722608.shtml) reported that the Yunnan Fire Rescue dispatched 2 vehicles and 9 personnel to the epicenter within minutes of the quake, with the Mojiang County fire station arriving shortly after.

Fire rescue teams conducted large-scale inspections of damaged houses overnight and door-to-door hazard checks the following morning. "Last night we conducted a large-scale inspection of severely damaged houses, and this morning we went door-to-door checking," said Yang Guangyun, Captain of the Hani Road Fire Rescue Station.

China Southern Power Grid Yunnan Co. deployed 67 personnel, 1 power generation vehicle, 5 generators, 30 power supply kits, and 40 emergency lighting units to ensure stable electricity at shelters and command centers. Power grid worker Ji Jianwei noted, "Today's main work is to ensure power supply at the shelters and to check one by one for electrical hazards."

## Infrastructure Resilience

The China-Laos Railway, a flagship Belt and Road Initiative project connecting Kunming with Vientiane, was temporarily closed between Yuanjiang and Pu'er for safety inspections. According to [China News Service](https://www.chinanews.com.cn/sh/2026/07-22/10663945.shtml), the inspections were completed by 23:40 on July 21, and the line was reopened with train services gradually resuming. No safety hazards affecting train operations were found.

Broader infrastructure inspections covered 732 primary and secondary schools and kindergartens, 189 hospitals, and 36 elderly care facilities across the affected region. The Ministry of Water Resources activated its earthquake relief command mechanism, using satellite remote sensing and drones to assess damage to water conservancy projects, reporting no damage to dams or water infrastructure.

## Relief Supplies and Forward Outlook

Emergency supplies deployed include 100 tents, 110 quilts, and 110 folding beds, with 200 million yuan (approximately $28 million) in emergency disaster relief funds allocated. Transport crews of 45 personnel cleared three damaged roads and restored main thoroughfares to ensure relief supply routes remained open.

At the Huitoushan shelter, 10 tents were set up on the village activity ground, each equipped with four simple beds, bedding, drinking water, and lighting. Hot meals were distributed at midday, with orderly queues forming as residents received food. Villager Zhi Jiayue praised the conditions, saying the shelter provided convenient living with three meals a day guaranteed.

Two aftershocks were felt by journalists on-site during reporting on July 22, and a forecast of light to moderate rain in the coming week could complicate relief efforts. However, with ample supplies in reserve — over 11,000 items of emergency supplies stockpiled — authorities remain confident in their ability to support affected communities through the recovery period.

## What to Watch For

As aftershocks continue and the region enters a potential rainy period, authorities are focused on preventing secondary hazards, monitoring weakened structures, and ensuring continued access to clean water and power. The coordinated response demonstrates China's established disaster management protocols, with the absence of casualties in a populated rural area attributed to moderate earthquake magnitude, construction standards, and rapid evacuation procedures.
