# PLA Navy Ship Jixiang Ark Embarks on First Overseas Mission

The Chinese PLA Navy hospital ship Jixiang Ark (pennant number 868) departed from a military port in Qingdao, Shandong Province, on July 22, 2026, embarking on its first overseas deployment — a landmark mission that underscores China's expanding capabilities in military medical diplomacy. The vessel will visit Djibouti and other countries in Africa and South Asia over approximately 200 days as part of "Harmonious Mission-2026," providing free medical services to local populations, according to [Xinhua News Agency](https://www.news.cn/photo/20260722/ed19dcb7ee82442da223818abd872bfb/c.html).

## A New Addition to China's Hospital Ship Fleet

Jixiang Ark — meaning "Auspicious Ark" — is China's third and newest 10,000-ton-class ocean-going hospital ship, independently designed and built by the country and commissioned in 2025. It joins two predecessors: Peace Ark (commissioned in 2008, pennant 866) and Silk Road Ark (commissioned in 2024, pennant 867). The [Ministry of National Defense](http://www.mod.gov.cn/gfbw/jswj/16475033.html) confirmed that Jixiang Ark is assigned to the Northern Theater Navy, meaning each of China's three major theater commands — Eastern, Southern, and Northern — now operates one large hospital ship.

Defense Ministry Spokesperson Senior Colonel Zhang Xiaogang described the vessel at a May 2025 press conference as "China's third 10,000-ton-class ocean-going hospital ship independently designed and built by China, with a functional layout adapted to the needs of combat readiness and equipped with advanced medical equipment," as reported by [People's Daily](http://military.people.com.cn/n1/2025/0529/c1011-40490664.html).

The ship is equipped with 18 clinical departments, five auxiliary diagnostic departments, and eight standard operating rooms. It can perform over 60 types of examinations, treatments, and surgeries, including general surgery, orthopedics, obstetrics and gynecology, and ophthalmology. A ship-borne medical rescue helicopter provides forward emergency medical evacuation capability. The medical team comprises over 100 personnel drawn primarily from the Northern Theater Navy, supplemented by staff from Naval Medical University and the Joint Logistics Support Force, as detailed by [Tencent News](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20260722A078M000).

## Harmonious Mission-2026: Scope and Objectives

Harmonious Mission-2026 marks the 12th iteration of the PLA Navy's overseas humanitarian medical service series, which began in 2010. The mission follows a service model combining shipboard treatment with forward medical teams conducting outreach visits to local communities. Beyond medical services, the mission includes exchange visits, professional seminars, and cultural and sports exchanges with host nations.

The Ministry of National Defense stated that the mission is "aimed at enhancing traditional friendship and practical cooperation between China and relevant countries, providing more public security products to the international community, better implementing the Global Security Initiative, and serving the construction of a maritime community with a shared future."

## A Legacy of Medical Diplomacy

The Harmonious Mission series has become a cornerstone of China's soft power strategy. Over 11 previous missions, the PLA Navy has provided medical services to more than 370,000 people across Asia, Africa, Latin America, and Oceania. The first 10 missions were conducted by Peace Ark, while the 11th was carried out by Silk Road Ark. Jixiang Ark now takes the helm for the 12th mission, [CCTV News](https://news.cctv.com/2026/07/21/ARTIztl7tmmBND9f3Aohhwl8260721.shtml) reported.

The departure ceremony on July 22 saw the ship sound its horn and slowly depart the dock as mission personnel and civilian staff stood in formation on deck, waving to military and civilian leaders and family members who gathered to see them off.

## Strategic Significance

The deployment of Jixiang Ark represents more than a routine humanitarian mission. It completes China's three-theater hospital ship coverage, giving the PLA Navy the ability to project medical support capabilities across all major maritime theaters simultaneously. The mission to Djibouti is particularly notable given that China operates its first overseas military base — the PLA Support Base in Djibouti — in the same region.

The mission aligns with China's broader foreign policy concepts, including the Global Security Initiative and the vision of a Maritime Community with a Shared Future. It provides a humanitarian counter-narrative to international concerns about China's military expansion, emphasizing Beijing's role as a provider of global public goods.

## What to Watch For

As Jixiang Ark begins its 200-day voyage, several questions remain. The full list of countries to be visited beyond Djibouti has not been publicly confirmed, and the mission's scale compared to previous Harmonious Missions will become clearer as it progresses. The deployment also proceeds against a backdrop of heightened geopolitical tensions, including the ongoing US-Iran conflict and South China Sea disputes, though the mission itself is framed as purely humanitarian.

The successful completion of this mission would mark another milestone in China's growing ability to project humanitarian assistance globally, while simultaneously demonstrating the operational reach of its expanding naval capabilities.
