# Arizona Coalition Cuts Social Security for Half of Savings

Nearly half of the budget savings planned by Belgium's five-party Arizona coalition government are expected to come from cuts to social security, according to a new analysis by the think tank Athéna. The findings, published on Thursday, reveal that social security accounts for approximately 46% of all net savings — roughly €32 billion over the full legislative term — while the government's overall deficit reduction targets remain far from being met.

## The Athéna Analysis

For its inaugural report, the newly formed think tank Athéna — which describes itself as "pluralist and progressive" and succeeds the Éconosphères network — examined the budget of Prime Minister Bart De Wever's government. According to [La Libre Belgique](https://www.lalibre.be/belgique/politique-belge/2026/07/23/pres-de-la-moitie-des-economies-de-larizona-portent-sur-la-securite-sociale-6VIXA5F2TRB4PL5HQZZ6QF2JSY/), the analysis found that social security is by far the largest category of cuts.

The €32 billion in social security savings breaks down across five main areas: freezing the "welfare envelope" — which allows social benefits to track the cost of living — saves €8.4 billion; time-limiting unemployment benefits accounts for €8.3 billion; measures targeting people on incapacity for work save €5.4 billion; pension reform contributes €5.4 billion; and healthcare savings add €2.8 billion.

## Deficit Projection Gap

The analysis also highlights a significant gap between the government's deficit targets and actual projections. When the Arizona coalition took office, the projected public deficit for 2029 stood at €43 billion. After applying all announced savings, the deficit was expected to fall to €18.6 billion (2.5% of GDP). However, as [7sur7](https://www.7sur7.be/belgique/pour-realiser-des-economies-larizona-a-largement-coupe-dans-la-secu~a81807ac/) reports, the March 2026 projections show a deficit of €35.8 billion (4.9% of GDP) — a €17.2 billion shortfall.

According to Athéna, macroeconomic parameters alone cannot explain the gap. The think tank points to overly optimistic revenue hypotheses and underestimated multiplier effects of austerity measures, which compress domestic demand and employment, in turn reducing economic activity and tax revenues.

## The Arizona Coalition

The "Arizona" coalition — named for the colors of its five member parties, which correspond to the Arizona state flag — brings together a broad ideological spectrum: the Flemish nationalist N-VA, the Francophone liberal MR, the centrist Les Engagés, the Christian democratic CD&V, and the Flemish social democratic Vooruit. Prime Minister Bart De Wever (N-VA) leads the government, which was formed in early 2025 after nearly eight months of post-election negotiations.

As [BX1](https://bx1.be/categories/news/pres-de-la-moitie-des-economies-de-larizona-portent-sur-la-secu-selon-des-experts/) notes, the cuts disproportionately affect vulnerable populations — unemployed workers, people with disabilities, pensioners, and those relying on inflation-indexed social benefits.

## Political and Social Fallout

The Athéna report is likely to intensify opposition to the government's fiscal consolidation plans. Belgium's two largest trade union federations, CSC and FGTB, have already announced plans for new protest actions after the summer break, having previously organized demonstrations against the Arizona government's policies.

Within the coalition itself, the progressive Vooruit party may face pressure from its base over the social security cuts, while N-VA and MR continue to push for fiscal discipline. The large gap between projected and actual deficit reduction also raises questions about the government's fiscal planning and could affect Belgium's credibility with EU institutions and financial markets.

## Broader Implications

The Athéna analysis raises a classic debate in fiscal policy: whether austerity measures may prove self-defeating if they compress domestic demand and employment, thereby reducing tax revenues. Belgium's extensive social security system has been a cornerstone of its post-war social model, and these cuts represent a significant shift that could reshape the social contract.

## What to Watch For

Key questions remain unanswered. Will the government adjust its plans in response to the Athéna analysis? How will the unions' planned protests affect the political landscape? Can the ideologically diverse coalition hold together under the strain? And what will be the response from EU institutions regarding Belgium's fiscal trajectory?

As the summer break draws to a close, the Arizona coalition faces mounting pressure from both expert analysis and organized labor — with the social security system at the center of the storm.
