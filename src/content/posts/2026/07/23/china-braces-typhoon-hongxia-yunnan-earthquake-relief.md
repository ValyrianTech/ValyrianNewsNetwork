# China Braces Typhoon Hongxia, Yunnan Quake Relief Ongoing

China is confronting two simultaneous natural disaster challenges this week, as a tropical depression in the western Pacific is expected to strengthen into Typhoon "Hongxia" and make landfall along the southeastern coast, while rescue and relief operations continue in Yunnan Province following a 5.0 magnitude earthquake that struck on July 21.

## Typhoon Hongxia Approaches

A tropical depression formed approximately 930 kilometers east of Manila, Philippines, on the morning of July 23 and is expected to develop into a typhoon within 12 to 24 hours, according to [Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260723/ed8ef8cd29514062b336ef1d9c021179/c.html). The National Meteorological Center forecasts the system will make landfall along the coast from central Guangdong to southern Fujian on the night of July 25 through early July 26.

Following the typhoon naming sequence, the storm will be designated Typhoon "Hongxia" (红霞), becoming the 12th named storm of the 2026 Pacific typhoon season and the third to make landfall in China this year. It follows Typhoon "Maysak," which struck Hainan on July 3, and Typhoon "Bavi."

The National Meteorological Center has warned that two to three typhoons may form in the Northwest Pacific and South China Sea over the next ten days, with one expected to enter the northern South China Sea around July 25. This active period comes during China's peak flood control season, which runs from May to September.

## Yunnan Earthquake Response

Meanwhile, in southwest China's Yunnan Province, authorities continue to manage the aftermath of a 5.0 magnitude earthquake that struck Mojiang Hani Autonomous County at 19:43 on July 21. The earthquake, with a focal depth of six kilometers, had its epicenter in the Laohezhai village group of Jingping Village, Yutang Town.

According to the Pu'er City Earthquake Relief Headquarters, no fatalities or injuries have been reported. However, 221 houses in Mojiang and neighboring Ning'er counties sustained wall cracking and structural damage. A total of 33 households comprising 94 people have been relocated to temporary shelters.

Yunnan Province activated a Level IV earthquake emergency response, and Pu'er City followed with its own Level IV response. Multiple government departments have coordinated efforts, deploying fire rescue teams, power grid maintenance crews, and medical personnel to affected areas.

## On the Ground: Stories from the Epicenter

Xinhua reporters visiting the earthquake zone on July 22 described a scene of coordinated relief efforts. Yang Guangyun, fire rescue station chief in Mojiang County, told [Xinhua](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260722/f45f25a62dd14182a423b548114af581/c.html) that his team of 30 firefighters had divided into three groups to conduct door-to-door safety inspections and clear debris from damaged homes.

Villager Yang Jiayun, whose production shed was damaged in the quake, described the moment it struck: "First I heard a huge rumbling noise, and before long the walls cracked open." He expressed gratitude for the swift evacuation, saying, "I feel very warm-hearted; these village and town cadres and rescue workers are like family to me."

Elderly villager Li Wancai, who had recently undergone surgery, was relocated to a shelter by local officials concerned about his remote location. "I had just undergone surgery, and the village cadres were worried, so they brought my wife and me here. I am very moved," he said.

## Infrastructure and Power Restoration

The earthquake caused temporary disruptions to the China-Laos Railway, a major infrastructure link connecting Kunming to Vientiane. The Yuanjiang-Pu'er section was locked down for safety inspections immediately after the quake, causing train delays, as reported by [China News Service](https://www.chinanews.com.cn/sh/2026/07-21/10663898.shtml). The section was inspected and cleared by 23:40 on July 21, with train operations gradually resuming, according to [CCTV](https://www.chinanews.com/sh/2026/07-22/10663945.shtml).

Mojiang County's power grid remained operational throughout the crisis. Yunnan Power Grid Company activated its own Level IV earthquake response, deploying 67 personnel, one power generation vehicle, five generators, and 40 emergency lighting units to ensure stable electricity supply at shelters and command centers.

## Broader Implications

The concurrent disasters test China's emergency response capacity at a time when extreme weather events are intensifying. Typhoon Maysak, which hit Hainan earlier this month, caused severe flooding in Guangxi and the collapse of two reservoirs in Hengzhou, underscoring infrastructure vulnerabilities. The approaching Typhoon Hongxia now threatens the densely populated and economically vital Guangdong-Fujian coastal region, home to major manufacturing and industrial centers.

As China navigates this challenging period, the effectiveness of its multi-department coordination and rapid evacuation procedures — demonstrated in the Mojiang earthquake response — will be crucial in mitigating the impact of the coming storm.

## What to Watch

All eyes are on the trajectory and intensity of Typhoon Hongxia as it approaches China's southeastern coast. Residents in Guangdong and Fujian have been advised to monitor forecasts and prepare for potential evacuations. Meanwhile, relief efforts in Yunnan continue, with aftershocks still being felt and ongoing assessments of structural damage in affected communities.
