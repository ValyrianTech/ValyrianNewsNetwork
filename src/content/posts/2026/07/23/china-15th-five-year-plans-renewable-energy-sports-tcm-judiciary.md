# China Unveils 15th Five-Year Plans for Energy, Sports, TCM

BEIJING — On July 23, 2026, the Chinese government released a coordinated series of sector-specific 15th Five-Year Plan (2026–2030) blueprints covering renewable energy development, sports powerhouse construction, traditional Chinese medicine (TCM) revitalization, and judicial system reform. The simultaneous rollout of multiple sectoral plans signals Beijing’s comprehensive approach to implementing the overarching national 15th Five-Year Plan approved in March 2026, as China pushes toward its 2035 socialist modernization goals.

## Renewable Energy: Ambitious Green Targets

The National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC) and the National Energy Administration jointly issued the renewable energy plan, which [Xinhua News reported](https://english.news.cn/20260723/14f04827e4dc4a80b62b68ade949c4ce/c.html) marks a "new development stage featuring scale expansion, quality improvement and reliable substitution." By 2030, China aims for total renewable energy consumption to reach approximately 1.8 billion tonnes of standard coal equivalent, with total installed renewable capacity projected at about 3.5 billion kilowatts. Combined wind and solar capacity is expected to exceed 2.8 billion kilowatts, generating over 4 trillion kilowatt-hours annually.

These targets represent a significant acceleration from the 14th Five-Year Plan period (2021–2025), during which China already made substantial strides in wind and solar capacity expansion. The plan deploys seven key tasks around its main target indicators, reinforcing China’s position as the world’s largest renewable energy producer while advancing its carbon peak (2030) and carbon neutrality (2060) commitments.

## Sports Powerhouse: Aiming for 7 Trillion Yuan Industry

The sports plan, described by [Xinhua](https://english.news.cn/20260722/432ea398021143cfb5611ab954e1dcf6/c.html) as the first national-level special plan themed "Sports Powerhouse," was issued by the General Administration of Sport following State Council approval. Its headline target is a sports industry total value exceeding 7 trillion yuan (approximately $1.03 trillion USD) by 2030, nearly doubling from the 3.84 trillion yuan recorded in 2024.

Other key 2030 targets include increasing per capita sports venue area to about 4 square meters (up from 3.11 square meters at end-2025), raising the proportion of regular exercisers to approximately 40% of the population, and achieving a national physical fitness excellence rate of 31.1%. The plan identifies 38 key tasks across national fitness, elite sport, youth sport, sports industry development, sports culture, and anti-doping efforts, supported by eight complementary initiatives including venue construction, talent development, and sports technology innovation.

## Traditional Chinese Medicine: Universal Access by 2030

The TCM revitalization plan, jointly issued by the National Administration of TCM and the NDRC with State Council approval, sets a goal of universal access to TCM services covering the entire population and full lifecycle by 2030. According to [Xinhua](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260723/7f8c43466b1541e3ab525db51d7c2841/c.html), the plan envisions new breakthroughs in major disease prevention and treatment, grassroots service upgrades, enhanced basic research, stronger industry innovation, and improved international influence.

The plan outlines 10 key tasks, including promoting high-quality integrated development of Chinese and Western medicine, building high-quality TCM talent teams, advancing TCM digital intelligence empowerment and standardization, and expanding TCM’s open development internationally. This reflects Beijing’s strategic view of TCM as both a cultural heritage asset and a cost-effective healthcare alternative.

## Judicial Reform: Targeting Business Environment Improvements

The Supreme People’s Procuratorate released its own 15th Five-Year Plan, describing the period as critical for basically realizing socialist modernization and improving legal supervision quality. As [CCTV News reported](https://news.cctv.com/2026/07/23/ARTIpoHPvh23S0ICFdC6hd2Y260723.shtml), the plan targets more complete institutional mechanisms, improved judicial fairness evaluation, and a technology-enhanced procuratorate.

Notably, the plan includes 21 key tasks addressing issues such as regulating law-related enterprise enforcement and preventing illegal cross-regional and profit-driven enforcement. These provisions directly address long-standing complaints from the business community about arbitrary law enforcement, suggesting the government is taking business environment concerns seriously.

## Analysis: Coordinated Policy Signaling

The simultaneous release of these four sectoral plans on a single day is significant. It demonstrates the Chinese government’s integrated approach to policy implementation and signals to both domestic stakeholders and international observers that the 15th Five-Year Plan is being executed in a coordinated manner across multiple domains.

The economic implications are substantial. The sports industry target alone represents a major economic opportunity, while renewable energy investment signals continued state-directed capital flows into green technology. The TCM plan’s universal access goal could reshape China’s healthcare delivery model, and the procuratorate reforms address critical business environment concerns.

## What’s Next

With these four plans now public, attention turns to implementation details, including specific funding mechanisms and enforcement mechanisms at local levels. The renewable energy targets will have global implications for clean energy supply chains and climate commitments, given China’s dominant position in solar and wind manufacturing. Additional sectoral plans may follow in the coming weeks as the 15th Five-Year Plan framework continues to roll out across all policy domains.
