# Patrick Kane Returns to Chicago Blackhawks in $16M Deal

CHICAGO — Patrick Kane is returning to the Chicago Blackhawks. The franchise icon agreed to a two-year, $16 million contract on Thursday, bringing one of the NHL's most decorated American-born players back to the team where he built his Hall of Fame legacy.

The deal, which carries an $8 million annual cap hit and includes a full no-movement clause, was announced by the Blackhawks on July 23, 2026. Kane, 37, spent the last three seasons with the Detroit Red Wings after being traded from Chicago in February 2023.

## A Homecoming for a Franchise Legend

Kane ranks among the greatest players in Blackhawks history. Over 16 seasons in Chicago, he amassed 446 goals, 779 assists and 1,225 points — placing him third in franchise goals, second in assists and second in points. He is also third in games played (1,161) with the organization.

According to [AP News](https://apnews.com/article/blackhawks-patrick-kane-c9077f9857263007383b484270e109a9), Kane helped the Blackhawks win three Stanley Cup championships (2010, 2013, 2015) and was named one of the 100 Greatest NHL Players in 2017. His individual accolades include the Hart Trophy as league MVP (2016), the Art Ross Trophy as scoring champion (2016), the Conn Smythe Trophy as playoff MVP (2013) and the Calder Trophy as rookie of the year (2008).

"Day in and day out for 16 seasons, Patrick captivated the city of Chicago with his dazzling skill, creating some of the most memorable moments in Blackhawks history as he helped bring our storied franchise back to the pinnacle of our sport," general manager Kyle Davidson said in a statement. "He's shown on countless occasions that he knows what it takes to win at the highest levels."

## The Road Back to Chicago

Kane's departure from Chicago in 2023 marked the end of an era. As the Blackhawks embarked on a full rebuild, Kane was traded to the New York Rangers alongside defenseman Jake McCabe. He signed with Detroit as a free agent the following summer and spent three productive seasons with the Red Wings, recording 163 points in 189 games.

This past season, Kane posted 16 goals and 41 assists (57 points) in 67 games with Detroit. According to [NHL.com](https://www.nhl.com/news/topic/free-agency/patrick-kane-signs-two-year-contract-with-chicago-blackhawks), he passed Mike Modano in January 2026 for the most NHL points by a U.S.-born player and now has 508 career goals and 1,400 points in 1,369 regular-season games.

Kane spent three weeks of free agency weighing offers from the Blackhawks and his hometown Buffalo Sabres. The Sabres offered a stronger chance at playoff contention, while Chicago presented a larger role alongside the team's young franchise center, Connor Bedard.

## Bedard's Pitch and Davidson's Pivot

The return was made possible in part by a strategic reversal from Davidson, who had traded Kane in 2023 to give the Blackhawks' new generation a "blank slate" to grow without deferring to franchise legends. In 2024, Davidson said he didn't foresee revisiting that decision. But with Jonathan Toews' retirement in June 2026 clearing the way, and Bedard's enthusiastic endorsement, Davidson changed course.

Bedard, who signed a five-year, $75 million contract extension on July 18, had publicly campaigned for Kane's return just days earlier. As reported by the [Chicago Sun-Times](https://chicago.suntimes.com/blackhawks/2026/07/23/blackhawks-patrick-kane-returns-nhl-free-agency), Bedard said: "I can't imagine his first game back at the (United Center), the reaction he'd get and how much juice that would bring to not only our team but our fans, as well. That would be incredible to play with him and learn from him."

Kane's agent, Pat Brisson, confirmed that Kane is "extremely excited" about the return, adding that Chicago is "where he belongs."

## What Kane Brings to a Rebuilding Team

The Blackhawks have finished 31st in the NHL for three consecutive seasons and haven't made the playoffs since 2020. Last season, they went 29-39-14 — an 11-point improvement but still among the league's worst records.

Kane, while no longer the dominant force of his prime, remains a productive top-six winger. He has recorded at least 50 points in 18 of his 19 NHL seasons. The $8 million annual cap hit is considered an overpay relative to his recent production (he earned between $2.75 million and $4 million annually in Detroit), but the Blackhawks have ample cap space to absorb it in the short term.

Kane is expected to eventually play alongside Bedard once the 21-year-old center returns from left shoulder surgery, which is projected to keep him out until approximately November. The pairing of a future Hall of Famer with a generational talent could accelerate Chicago's offensive development and provide the mentorship that Bedard has openly welcomed.

## A New Chapter for Showtime

Kane's return signals that the Blackhawks' rebuild is entering a new phase — one where the team is ready to add veteran talent around its young core. The Blackhawks' social media post, declaring "two more years of SHOWTIME," captured the marketing and fan engagement value of the signing.

For a fanbase that has endured six years of losing, Kane's homecoming is a moment of pure celebration. The Blackhawks open the season Sept. 29 at the Vegas Golden Knights, with Kane's first home game at the United Center scheduled for Oct. 6 against the St. Louis Blues. His first game against his former Red Wings team will follow on Oct. 29 in Detroit.

As Blackhawks ambassador and former defenseman Chris Chelios told NHL.com: "You saw the reception (Kane) got a couple of years ago during the jersey retirement. He still belongs in Chicago."
