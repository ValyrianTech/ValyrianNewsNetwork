# Brussels Emergency Meeting as Firefighter Strike Worsens

The Brussels regional government convened an emergency cabinet meeting at noon on Thursday as an escalating indefinite strike by firefighters left just 22% of normal emergency crews on duty, prompting warnings that public safety in the Belgian capital is at risk.

Minister-President Boris Dilliès (MR) called the urgent online session to address the structural understaffing and underfunding of the Brussels Fire Brigade (SIAMU), according to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/23/staking-brusselse-brandweer-minister-president-dillies-roept-h/). The meeting comes one day after negotiations between the government and the joint union front collapsed without an agreement.

## Strike Escalates Rapidly

The industrial action began on Tuesday 21 July — Belgian National Day — as a 24-hour protest but quickly intensified. After a failed meeting with Dilliès on Wednesday, unions declared an indefinite strike. By Thursday morning, only 38 of 172 firefighters were on duty, representing just 22% of normal capacity.

Steven Gilissen of the Christian trade union ACV warned that this means only two fire engines and two ladder trucks can be deployed for fires across the entire Brussels-Capital Region. "Today, the safety of the Brussels resident is in danger because of a political misstep," Gilissen told [The Brussels Times](https://www.brusselstimes.com/belgium/2242211/brussels-fire-brigade-strike-continues-as-government-talks-collapse-tbtb).

Pablo Nyns of the socialist CGSP union said Thursday's walkout was "the biggest since the start of the action" and warned that Friday's "may well be even stronger," with officers considering joining the strike.

## Emergency Services Under Strain

The Brussels fire department has formally warned the public to avoid high-risk activities and exercise extra caution, as emergency services cannot be guaranteed at normal levels. The medical emergency plan has been activated, and reinforcements from outside the region — including Wallonia — have been requested.

Walter Derieuw, spokesperson for the Brussels Fire Brigade, assured the public that "essential missions are assured and available resources are deployed as a priority for the most urgent interventions," as reported by [DH Net](https://www.dhnet.be/regions/bruxelles/2026/07/23/la-greve-des-pompiers-bruxellois-se-poursuit-boris-dillies-convoque-un-conseil-des-ministres-en-urgence-MDCCBEXLK5HSJCL6THEJDXVPLM/). He urged residents not to hesitate to call the emergency number 112 in case of need.

## Deep-Rooted Structural Crisis

The strike exposes years of chronic underfunding and understaffing at SIAMU, the service responsible for both firefighting and emergency medical assistance in Brussels. Unions are demanding 200 additional firefighters, while the government has promised 40 new recruits by the end of 2026.

However, according to the unions, new language requirements for recruitment will not be finalized for approximately two months, meaning no new staff can begin for at least five months. Full operational capability for new recruits is not expected until 2029.

A further point of contention involves €10 million that Federal Interior Minister Bernard Quintin (MR) transferred to Brussels for the fire department. Union representative Yves De Vogelaere claimed the money "was used to fill the financial gap. The fire department saw none of it." The government's current proposal to allocate approximately €1 million to SIAMU has been dismissed by unions as wholly insufficient.

## Political Pressure Mounts

The emergency cabinet meeting places significant political pressure on Dilliès, who must balance union demands with budgetary constraints. In a statement following Wednesday's failed talks, Dilliès said: "I hear the anger and concerns expressed today. Some of the problems mentioned are real and must be addressed. Various measures have already been set in motion."

According to [La Libre](https://www.lalibre.be/dernieres-depeches/2026/07/23/greve-des-pompiers-bruxellois-greve-des-pompiers-boris-dillies-convoque-un-conseil-des-ministres-en-urgence-453CAUGPQNARVOLOJKYPYONT44/), it remains unclear whether the political parties within the Brussels government will issue a statement following the emergency meeting. "That depends on how the meeting goes," a spokesperson for Dilliès said.

## Broader Implications

The strike comes during Brussels' peak tourist season, raising concerns about economic impact and public confidence. The [Euro Weekly News](https://euroweeklynews.com/2026/07/23/brussels-firefighters-indefinite-strike-could-affect-emergency-response-times/) noted that while day-to-day life is continuing largely as normal, visitors are advised to keep up to date with local safety information.

Gilissen captured the urgency of the situation, warning: "The government doesn't have much room to maneuver anymore. I hope the need to take targeted measures in the short term also sinks in with the politicians. Currently, they are playing with fire."

## What to Watch For

As the emergency cabinet meeting unfolds, several key questions remain: Will the government produce concrete proposals that unions can accept? Can a short-term solution be found while long-term recruitment plans are pending? And how will the public react if a major emergency occurs while firefighting capacity remains critically reduced?

The crisis has also highlighted deeper tensions between Brussels regional and Belgian federal authorities over fire service funding — tensions that are unlikely to be resolved in a single emergency meeting.
