# US Coast Guard Deploys to South China Sea as Tensions With Philippines Spike

Six US Coast Guard patrol vessels have been redeployed to the South China Sea, arriving at Subic Bay in the Philippines on the same day China accused Philippine government vessels of trespassing near Huangyan Island, marking a significant escalation in regional maritime tensions. The developments, reported on July 23, come just days after a violent clash at Second Thomas Shoal left a Philippine Navy sailor injured, and as ASEAN foreign ministers gather in Manila for high-level talks.

## US Coast Guard Deployment

The six "Sentinel"-class fast response cutters, each 154 feet long and displacing 300 to 400 tons, were redeployed from the Middle East, where they had been conducting maritime security and drug interdiction missions amid the ongoing US-Iran conflict. According to [The Paper](https://www.thepaper.cn/newsDetail_forward_33642339), the vessels are authorized to operate from Singapore and rotate through Subic Bay through at least September 2026.

Military commentator Li Yaqiang, quoted by The Paper, noted that the choice of Coast Guard vessels rather than Navy warships reflects a calibrated US approach. "Why not warships, but the Coast Guard? This shows that the US has concerns in the use of military force, especially in East Asia," Li said. "Sending the Coast Guard demonstrates military presence, maintains alliance relations, and shows commitment to maritime security affairs, while reducing military sensitivity."

## Huangyan Island Incident

On the same day, [Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/world/20260723/0127d2fb8a514aa2a82def8bfda86ec5/c.html) reported that China Coast Guard spokesperson Jiang Lue accused two Philippine government vessels, BRP 3012 and BRP 3018, of forcibly intruding into Chinese jurisdictional waters near Huangyan Island, known internationally as Scarborough Shoal. China claims to have taken "follow-and-monitor, forced-out, block-and-control" measures to expel them.

"Huangyan Island is China's inherent territory. We urge the Philippines to immediately stop its infringements and provocations," Jiang said in a statement, adding that the China Coast Guard will continue to lawfully carry out rights protection activities in the area.

## Violent Clash at Second Thomas Shoal

The latest incidents follow a dangerous escalation on July 20, when a Philippine Navy sailor was struck on the head with a wooden baton by China Coast Guard personnel near the grounded BRP Sierra Madre at Second Thomas Shoal (Ayungin Shoal). [Philstar](https://www.philstar.com/headlines/2026/07/20/2543443/struck-baton-navy-sailor-injured-china-coast-guard-encounter-near-ayungin) reported that the Philippine National Security Council condemned the actions, stating: "This latest act of aggression against personnel performing their lawful duties is unacceptable and serves only to heighten tensions and increase the risk of dangerous miscalculation at sea."

The Philippine Department of National Defense struck a defiant tone, declaring: "We simply cannot trust this belligerent neighbor. We will not back down."

## Diplomatic Context

These events unfolded as Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi and Southeast Asian foreign ministers gathered in Manila for ASEAN talks, where maritime tensions were a central agenda item. US Secretary of State Marco Rubio met with Philippine President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. in Manila on July 22, on the sidelines of the meetings, as reported by Philstar.

[Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/21/china-and-philippines-trade-accusations-after-sailor-injured-at-sea) quoted a US State Department spokesperson condemning "China's troubling pattern of provocation against lawful Philippine maritime operations," which they said "undermines regional peace and stability."

## Analysis and Implications

The convergence of these events represents a calibrated but dangerous game of strategic signaling. The US deployment of Coast Guard vessels rather than Navy warships provides symbolic backing to the Philippines while reducing the risk of direct military confrontation. However, as military commentator Li Yaqiang noted, China maintains what it describes as "overwhelming strategic advantage" in the South China Sea, operating 280 law enforcement vessels totaling nearly 400,000 tons.

The July 20 clash at Second Thomas Shoal, involving physical violence with a baton strike, marks a dangerous departure from previous incidents involving water cannons and ramming. The risk of future incidents resulting in fatalities has increased significantly, potentially triggering more serious consequences, including possible invocation of the US-Philippines Mutual Defense Treaty.

## What to Watch For

The ASEAN foreign ministers' meeting in Manila will be closely watched for any joint statement addressing the escalating tensions. The Philippines may seek stronger collective action from ASEAN, while China is expected to push back against external interference. The six US Coast Guard cutters are authorized to remain in the region through September, ensuring a continued American presence as the diplomatic and military dynamics continue to unfold.
