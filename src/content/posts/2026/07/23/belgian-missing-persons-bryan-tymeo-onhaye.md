# Missing Persons Unit at Dead End in Search for Bryan, Tyméo

Twenty-six days after Bryan Brigou (29) and his five-year-old son Tyméo disappeared from the small town of Onhaye in Belgium's Namur province, investigators admit they are no closer to solving the mystery. Despite more than 100 investigative actions, three public witness appeals, and round-the-clock efforts, every lead has gone cold.

"Every trail we investigate leads to nothing," Gerry Van Loock, head of Belgium's Missing Persons Unit, told [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/23/gerry-van-loock-verdwijning-bryan-en-zoontje-tyme/). "We are searching for the last puzzle piece."

## A Disappearance Without a Trace

Father and son were last seen alive on Saturday, June 27, 2026. That afternoon, CCTV footage captured Bryan purchasing two bottles of soda at the Sunset de Biesme shop in Mettet, in the early afternoon. It was an extremely hot day, with temperatures reaching 35 degrees Celsius in the shade.

Earlier that day, Bryan and Tyméo had attended the Belgian Air Force Days at the Florennes military base. Bryan's brother Simon received a Snapchat photo from him late that morning, and Tyméo called his mother around midday. A receipt later confirmed they had also visited a toy store.

But later that afternoon, Bryan's mobile phone stopped transmitting. It was the last digital trace of either of them.

The next day, Sunday, June 28, Bryan's dark Volkswagen Golf 6 (license plate 2-FBK-880) was found on the parking lot of the local football club in Onhaye. Tracker dogs brought to the scene stopped at the vehicle and went no further.

## All Pistes Remain Open

Van Loock emphasized that investigators are keeping every possibility on the table. The scenarios under consideration include an accident, a criminal act, voluntary departure, a hit-and-run, or abduction abroad.

"Anyone could have left that car there: Bryan himself, but equally someone else," Van Loock explained in an interview on Radio 1's *De Ochtend*, as reported by [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/22/verdwijning-wallonie-vader-zoon/).

Adding a layer of complexity to the case, Bryan was wearing an electronic ankle monitor at the time of his disappearance as part of an ongoing judicial investigation. Van Loock confirmed the monitor is not traceable and has not aided the search.

"In any case, we are very worried, especially about five-year-old Tyméo," Van Loock said. "Even if this was a voluntary departure, he did not choose that for himself."

## A Family's Theory

Tyméo's aunt, Samantha, has offered a theory based on the behavior of the tracker dogs. Speaking to [RTL Info](https://www.rtl.be/actu/belgique/faits-divers/disparition-de-tymeo-et-bryan-onhaye-une-proche-pense-quils-ont-ete-emmenes/2026-07-07/article/793913), she explained: "When the dogs smelled Bryan's car, they stopped dead. They didn't go any further. It's a sign that Bryan and the little one got into another car and were taken somewhere."

## Urgent Appeal for Footage

The Namur Public Prosecutor's Office launched a third witness appeal on July 22, specifically targeting Flemish tourists who may have been in the area during the weekend of the disappearance. A village festival, the St-Walhère kermesse, was taking place in Onhaye that weekend, drawing visitors from across Belgium.

"Flemish people who happened to be walking or cycling in the area may have unconsciously taken images that we are looking for," Van Loock said, as quoted by [L'Avenir](https://www.lavenir.net/regions/namur/onhaye/2026/07/23/disparition-de-bryan-brigou-et-de-son-fils-tymeo-a-onhaye-le-27-juin-apres-trois-semaines-dintenses-devoirs-les-enqueteurs-butent-sur-le-vide-4DSV5DDDDNGOBGOIIKASN7KPFE/).

Investigators are urgently seeking dashcam, drone, GoPro, and smartphone footage from the Onhaye, Dinant, and Waulsort areas. They are particularly interested in any images captured on the football club parking lot between Saturday afternoon and Sunday afternoon.

## Analysis: A Case That Defies Conventional Investigation

The Bryan and Tyméo case stands out for the sheer absence of usable evidence despite exhaustive efforts. Over 100 investigative actions — including neighborhood inquiries, phone analysis, CCTV reviews, door-to-door searches, tracker dogs, helicopters, and drones — have yielded nothing concrete.

"We have been searching around the clock for 26 days, together with all possible agencies, but every trail goes cold," Van Loock told [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/fr/2026/07/23/mystere-autour-de-la-disparition-de-bryan-et-tymeo-son-fils-de/).

The presence of the electronic ankle monitor adds a significant unanswered question. Bryan was under judicial supervision at the time of his disappearance, yet authorities have disclosed nothing about the nature of the underlying investigation. Whether this context is connected to the disappearance remains one of the case's central mysteries.

## What Lies Ahead

As the one-month mark approaches on July 27, the case continues to grip both Dutch-speaking Flanders and French-speaking Wallonia. The investigation, led by a federal police team under the direction of an investigating judge, remains at a standstill.

"The disappearance is even more worrying now than at the beginning," Van Loock admitted. "It remains a five-year-old child and his dad."

The coming weeks may see renewed media attention, particularly if the case is featured on national television programs. Investigators remain hopeful that a member of the public reviewing their photos or videos from that weekend may unknowingly hold the key to breaking the case open.

Authorities are urging anyone with information to contact the police at opsporingen@police.belgium.eu, Child Focus at 116 000, or the free tip line at 0800/30 300.
