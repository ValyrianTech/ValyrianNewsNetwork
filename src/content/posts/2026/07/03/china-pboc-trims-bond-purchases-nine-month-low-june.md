# PBOC Trims Bond Purchases to Nine-Month Low in June

The People's Bank of China (PBOC) injected a net 10 billion yuan (approximately $1.47 billion) through open-market government bond operations in June 2026, marking the smallest monthly injection since September 2025, according to data released by the central bank on Friday.

The figure represents a significant deceleration from the average monthly pace of roughly 50–60 billion yuan seen in the preceding months. Cumulative net injections through this channel during the first half of 2026 amounted to 300 billion yuan, as [Xinhua News reported](https://www.news.cn/fortune/20260703/d1eed6faf03b4d1fa5ae9917d348d0db/c.html).

## Context: A Normalized Policy Tool

The PBOC first began trading government bonds in the secondary market in August 2024, following a proposal by President Xi Jinping. The program was paused in January 2025 when surging demand and record-low yields created market distortions, before resuming in October 2025 with a net injection of 200 billion yuan.

By the start of 2026, the central bank had declared these operations "normalized." In its first-quarter monetary policy implementation report, the PBOC stated it had been conducting government bond operations on a routine basis, flexibly adjusting the scale in response to base-money supply needs and bond-market conditions, as [the English edition of Xinhua noted](https://english.news.cn/20260703/c81c02d59f6247d894c7e6444c35a580/c.html).

## The June Pullback: A Signal of Caution?

The sharp reduction in June has drawn attention from market analysts. The pullback was foreshadowed on June 2, when the PBOC executed its smallest daily open-market operation on record, lending just 200 million yuan ($27 million) through seven-day reverse repos — a rounding error for the world's second-largest economy, as [CryptoBriefing reported](https://cryptobriefing.com/pboc-cuts-bond-purchases-nine-month-low/).

Chinese benchmark bond yields had dropped to their lowest level since August 2025 during early June, driven by a bond rally. Bloomberg characterized the reduced purchases as a sign of the PBOC's wariness over further yield declines, suggesting policymakers are turning cautious about potential excessive liquidity or financial stability risks.

## Broader Liquidity Picture

Despite the reduced bond purchases, overall liquidity conditions remained ample in June. The PBOC deployed a range of other tools:

- **Medium-term Lending Facility (MLF):** Net injection of 200 billion yuan
- **7-day reverse repos:** Net injection of 582.6 billion yuan
- **Other maturity reverse repos:** Net injection of 300 billion yuan
- **Other structural tools:** Net withdrawal of 137.2 billion yuan

This diversified approach reflects what analysts at [China Securities Journal](https://jnzstatic.cs.com.cn/zzb/htmlInfo/7d48c3c58c7243ab8b55f7c4e958b7a1.html) have described as "fine-tuned cultivation" (精耕细作) in liquidity management. Huaxi Securities Chief Economist Liu Yu characterized the 2026 policy framework as one of "ample quantity, stable price" (量宽价稳), with the PBOC using a combination of outright reverse repos, MLF, and treasury bond trading to release monthly medium- to long-term funds.

## Analysis: What the Reduction Signals

The June data point is significant for several reasons:

**1. Yield Curve Management:** The PBOC appears to be closely monitoring long-term bond yields. By scaling back purchases, the central bank can signal that it does not endorse excessively low yields, which could indicate market overheating or create financial stability risks.

**2. Flexible Tool Use:** The reduction demonstrates that the PBOC will use its bond-trading tool flexibly — scaling up or down based on market conditions rather than maintaining a fixed trajectory. This is consistent with the "normalized but flexible" framework outlined in the Q1 2026 Monetary Policy Report.

**3. Composition Shift:** While bond purchases fell, other liquidity tools more than compensated. The PBOC is shifting the composition of its liquidity provision rather than tightening overall conditions, maintaining its "moderately loose" (适度宽松) policy stance.

## What to Watch Going Forward

Market participants will be watching several indicators in the coming months:

- **July operations:** Whether the reduced pace of bond purchases continues or reverses will be a key test of policy direction.
- **Bond yield trajectory:** If yields continue to decline, further reductions — or even net sales — could follow.
- **Economic data:** Growth, inflation, and credit demand figures will influence the PBOC's next moves.
- **Global context:** With the US Federal Reserve's policy stance affecting global capital flows, the PBOC must balance domestic liquidity needs with external considerations.

The PBOC's June operations suggest a central bank that is comfortable with its new bond-trading toolkit but cautious about its implications for market pricing. For now, the message appears to be one of fine-tuning rather than a fundamental shift in policy direction.
