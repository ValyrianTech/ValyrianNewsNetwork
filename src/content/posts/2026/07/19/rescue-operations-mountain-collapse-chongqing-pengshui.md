# Rescue Operations Underway After Mountain Collapse in Chongqing

A massive mountain collapse struck Pengshui Miao and Tujia Autonomous County in Chongqing on the morning of July 17, burying multiple residential buildings and triggering a large-scale emergency response. As of the latest reports, eight people have been confirmed dead, 34 remain missing, and 10 others were injured, according to [Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/local/20260718/d9aad0f74f66428480102ff4ee8f9ab5/c.html). Vice Premier Zhang Guoqing has been dispatched to the disaster site to oversee search and rescue efforts.

## Context

The collapse occurred on the morning of July 17 along the Wujiang River at Hanjia Street in Pengshui County, a region characterized by steep mountains and fragmented rock layers in the Wujiang River Gorge core area. The area is highly susceptible to landslides and collapses due to its geological composition of sedimentary rock formations with weak interlayers.

This disaster comes just 10 days after a deadly landslide in Dangchang County, Gansu Province, which killed 21 people on July 7, raising concerns about geological hazard preparedness across the country.

## Key Developments

Community grid workers Peng Di and Gong Baodong detected falling rocks and unusual sounds roughly one hour before the main collapse and immediately began evacuating residents. As [CNR (Yangguang Net)](https://china.cnr.cn/yaowen/20260718/t20260718_527716292.shtml) reported, Peng Di described the moment: "When we grid workers were patrolling, we found碎石 falling from the mountain behind us. We immediately evacuated the masses to safe areas. We went building by building, shouting for everyone to leave." Their quick actions enabled the evacuation of over 60 residents from the immediate danger zone, and approximately 1,100 people were safely evacuated from surrounding areas.

Vice Premier Zhang Guoqing, representing the CPC Central Committee and State Council, arrived at the disaster site on the evening of July 17. According to [People's Daily](http://politics.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0719/c1001-40763407.html), he visited the disaster site again on the morning of July 18, went to the county hospital and temporary shelters to看望 injured and displaced residents, and conveyed instructions from President Xi Jinping and Premier Li Qiang emphasizing the priority of protecting people's lives.

Multiple emergency response levels were activated: Chongqing initiated a Level I geological disaster emergency response, the Ministry of Emergency Management activated a Level II national geological disaster emergency response, and the National Disaster Reduction Commission activated a Level IV national disaster relief response. A total of 872 personnel and over 1,100 pieces of equipment were deployed to the scene.

## Analysis

Geological experts have clarified that the collapse was a natural disaster. Li Li, Head of the Municipal Expert Group from the Chongqing Bureau of Planning and Natural Resources, explained the mechanism: prolonged rainfall had infiltrated weak rock layers, and when the weather suddenly cleared, the rock mass contracted and deformed, ultimately resulting in sliding, overturning, and collapse. He also confirmed that a firecracker warehouse explosion at the riverside was a secondary consequence of the collapse, not its cause.

Rescue operations face significant challenges. Qin Gang, Chief Engineer of the China Aneng Chongqing Rescue Team, reported that by the early hours of July 18, teams had cleared approximately 6,000 cubic meters of debris. However, the collapsed boulders are extremely large, the work area is narrow, and heavy rain — including a Torrential Rain Red Alert issued at 00:10 on July 18 — has complicated efforts. Rescue teams are using blasting to clear large boulders and employing slope radar, 3D laser scanners, and drones for real-time monitoring to prevent secondary collapses.

In response to the back-to-back geological disasters in Gansu and Chongqing, four central government departments — the National Disaster Reduction Commission Office, the Ministry of Natural Resources, the Ministry of Water Resources, and the Ministry of Emergency Management — jointly issued an emergency notice on July 17 calling for nationwide geological disaster risk inspection. As [Xinhua](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260719/38e8c54f4d1a4071ab3c02ebf16de4fc/c.html) reported, the notice highlighted systemic concerns including incomplete coverage of routine geological hazard inspections and inadequate detection of disaster precursors.

## What's Next

Rescue operations continue under the "golden 72 hours" window, with teams working around the clock despite challenging conditions. The search and rescue phase is expected to continue for several more days, with a particular focus on the debris pile where missing persons are believed to be trapped. The national notice on geological disaster risk inspection will likely lead to enhanced monitoring and early warning systems across China's high-risk regions, particularly in areas with steep slopes, riverside construction, and slope-side housing. The final death toll and full extent of property damage remain to be determined as rescue efforts progress.
