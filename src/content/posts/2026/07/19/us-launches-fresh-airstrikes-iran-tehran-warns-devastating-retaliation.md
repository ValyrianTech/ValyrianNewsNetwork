# US Launches New Airstrikes on Iran; Tehran Warns Retaliation

The United States military launched a new wave of airstrikes against Iran on Sunday, escalating the already volatile conflict in the Middle East after an Iranian attack on a US base in Jordan killed two American service members and left one missing. Iran's Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei responded by declaring President Donald Trump's signature "worthless and invalid," suspending Tehran's commitments to the June 17 Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) and warning of "unforgettable lessons" for the US and its allies.

## Escalation After Deadly Attack on Jordan Base

The latest round of strikes, announced by US Central Command (CENTCOM), were designed to "further degrade Iran's ability to restrict the traffic of oil tankers through the Strait of Hormuz" and to "swiftly punish" Iran for the deaths of US service members, according to [the Associated Press](https://apnews.com/article/iran-us-hormuz-strait-war-july-18-2026-5adfef67554652580963684d9a663af2). The strikes targeted southern Iran, particularly Hormozgan province bordering the Strait of Hormuz, hitting locations near Sirik, Hajiabad, Bandar Abbas, and Qeshm Island.

The US action came after Iran launched ballistic missile and drone attacks on a US military base in Jordan on July 17, killing two American service members, leaving one missing, and injuring four others who were evacuated to Jordanian hospitals and later discharged. This marked the fourth attack on US bases in Jordan in five days, following previous strikes that had damaged aircraft and injured personnel at multiple facilities.

## Iran's Supreme Leader Declares Trump's Signature 'Worthless'

In a strongly worded statement, Iran's Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei accused the United States of "repeated breaches of agreements" and declared that Trump's signature on the June 17 MoU was "worthless and invalid," as reported by [The Jerusalem Post](https://www.jpost.com/middle-east/iran-news/article-902879). Khamenei warned that "the noble Iranian nation and the Axis of Resistance have unforgettable lessons in store" for the US, referencing Iran's network of regional proxies including Hezbollah, the Houthis, and Iraqi militias.

Iran's Deputy Foreign Minister Kazem Gharibabadi subsequently announced that Tehran was suspending its commitments to the MoU, which had been signed just over a month ago with the aim of permanently ending the fighting. The deal had already collapsed on July 8 after attacks by both sides, but the formal suspension marks a significant diplomatic setback.

## Seventh Consecutive Night of Strikes

The US-Iran exchange of fire has now continued for seven consecutive nights, with the conflict expanding beyond bilateral strikes. According to [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/7/18/iran-war-live-us-and-iran-exchange-attacks-for-7th-consecutive-night), the IRGC and Iranian Army launched multiple waves of attacks against US military positions across the region, targeting facilities in Jordan, Kuwait, and Bahrain. The IRGC claimed to have destroyed US aircraft at a Jordanian base and a US military radar installation in Kuwait under Operation Nasr-2.

US strikes have caused significant damage to Iranian infrastructure. A desalination plant in Bunji village, Jask county, was hit, disrupting water supply to 20 villages with a population of approximately 10,000 people, according to [Press TV](https://www.presstv.ir/Detail/2026/07/18/772503/US-Targets-Iran-Water-Supply-Terrorist-Attack-Hormozgan-Hamsezpour). Abdolhamid Hamzehpour, CEO of the Hormozgan Water and Wastewater Company, described the attack as a "terrorist act" and said the pumping plant and electrical power transformer station were "completely destroyed."

Additional strikes damaged a bridge and two tunnels near Bandar Abbas, disrupting the main highway to the Strait of Hormuz. Iran's Health Ministry reported that at least 38 people had been killed and more than 400 injured in the past week alone, while Iranian authorities say at least 50 have been killed and more than 500 wounded in US strikes over the past three weeks.

## Regional Fallout and Growing Humanitarian Crisis

The conflict is increasingly drawing in regional actors. Kuwait reported that Iranian strikes hit a water desalination plant and oil facility, with several firefighters injured. Kuwait briefly closed its airspace due to missile threats. Air sirens sounded multiple times in Bahrain, while Saudi Arabia activated its air defenses. Jordan's air defense systems successfully downed Iranian missiles.

The secretary-general of the six-nation Gulf Cooperation Council, Jasem Mohamed al-Budaiwi, accused Iran of war crimes for strikes on infrastructure and civilian facilities. Meanwhile, the European Union and Gulf states rejected any "illegitimate claims" of sovereignty over the Strait of Hormuz and called for Iran to reopen it unconditionally.

## Strategic Implications

The suspension of the June 17 MoU represents a major setback for peace efforts. The core strategic objective remains control of the Strait of Hormuz, through which approximately 20% of global oil supplies transited before the war. Iran's effective closure of the strait has caused the largest supply disruption in global oil market history, with an estimated $113.3 billion cost to US taxpayers as of June 2026, according to [Wikipedia's comprehensive overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Iran_war) of the conflict.

US strikes on civilian infrastructure — including desalination plants, bridges, tunnels, and power stations — represent a significant escalation, especially as summer temperatures in the region reach approximately 50°C (122°F). Human rights experts have noted that strikes on civilian infrastructure not being used for military purposes could constitute war crimes.

## What to Watch For

The conflict shows no signs of de-escalation. President Trump has previously threatened to target Iran's power plants and bridges to compel Tehran to loosen its hold on the Strait of Hormuz. Iran, meanwhile, has demonstrated sustained missile and drone capability despite months of war, with the ability to strike targets across multiple countries simultaneously.

Key questions remain: Can diplomatic channels be revived after Iran's suspension of the MoU? How will Gulf states respond to continued Iranian strikes on their territory? And what is the impact on global oil prices and supply chains as the world's most critical energy chokepoint remains effectively closed?

As [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2026/jul/17/us-launches-seventh-night-of-iran-strikes-as-hormuz-conflict-escalates) noted, the renewed fighting has focused on the Strait of Hormuz, and despite the escalating conflict, Trump has stated that the war is "going well" for the US. With both sides dug in and diplomacy in tatters, the prospect of a broader regional conflict looms larger with each passing night.
