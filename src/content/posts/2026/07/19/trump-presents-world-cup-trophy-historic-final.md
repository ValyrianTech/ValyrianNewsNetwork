# Trump Presents World Cup Trophy as U.S. Hosts Historic Final

EAST RUTHERFORD, N.J. — President Donald Trump attended the FIFA World Cup final on Sunday at MetLife Stadium, where defending champion Argentina faced Spain in the culmination of the largest World Cup in history. Trump, as is customary for heads of state of host nations, was set to present the trophy to the winning team.

Trump declared the tournament "the most successful sports event, maybe in the history of the world," according to [AP News](https://apnews.com/article/trump-world-cup-final-ad67ea3f25163bbb1d779d17b04da115), and asserted that the United States had proven itself "a soccer country." The president arrived by Marine One helicopter, with the stadium placed under a security lockdown involving drone surveillance, restricted airspace, and elevated police monitoring.

## The Tournament at a Glance

The 2026 World Cup was the first to be co-hosted by three nations — the United States, Mexico, and Canada — and the first expanded to 48 teams, featuring 104 matches across 16 cities. The tournament offered a record $655 million prize pool, with the winner receiving $50 million. FIFA also introduced championship rings for the first time, with 30 rings for the winning team and 1,996 available for fans, as [BBC Sport](https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/articles/cgmk7mnnyjeo) reported.

## A Final Packed with Storylines

The match carried extraordinary significance on multiple fronts. For Argentina, a victory would make them the first back-to-back World Cup champions since Brazil in 1958 and 1962. The final was widely expected to be Lionel Messi's last match with the Argentine national team, adding an emotional weight to the occasion.

For Spain, a win would make them the first nation to hold both the men's and women's World Cup trophies simultaneously, following their women's team's triumph in 2023. The Spanish squad also features 19-year-old star Lamine Yamal, who was famously photographed as a baby being held by Messi in 2007 — a full-circle moment that captivated fans worldwide.

## Heads of State and Diplomatic Dimensions

The final drew an extraordinary gathering of world leaders. Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum and Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney attended at Trump's invitation, a notable gesture given the significant trade tensions and tariff disputes between the three nations. Spanish Prime Minister Pedro Sanchez and King Felipe VI also attended, while Argentine President Javier Milei stayed home citing superstition, according to [AS USA](https://en.as.com/soccer/world-cup/donald-trump-turns-metlife-into-a-bunker-for-2026-world-cup-final-f202607-n/).

## The Balogun Controversy

The tournament's most significant controversy involved Trump's direct intervention with FIFA. The president called FIFA President Gianni Infantino to request a review of a red card given to U.S. star forward Folarin Balogun. FIFA subsequently reversed the suspension, drawing criticism about tournament integrity. BBC reported that no other red or yellow cards were suspended during the tournament, despite appeals from France and England.

## Security and Logistical Challenges

MetLife Stadium was designated a National Special Security Event due to Trump's attendance. The Secret Service conducted individual body and bag searches of every fan, raising concerns that all 82,500 ticket holders might not get into the ground before kickoff, as [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2026/jul/19/world-cup-2026-spain-v-argentina-final-buildup-england-win-10-goal-thriller-live) reported. New Jersey Transit train prices from Manhattan were increased to $98, adding to the logistical challenges.

## A Spectacle for the Ages

The closing ceremony featured Post Malone and Swae Lee, while Jennifer Hudson sang the U.S. national anthem. For the first time in World Cup history, the final featured a Super Bowl-style halftime show with an extraordinary lineup including Madonna, Shakira, BTS, Justin Bieber, and Coldplay.

## What's Next

The successful hosting of the World Cup positions the U.S. favorably for future sporting events, including the 2028 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles and the 2034 Winter Olympics in Salt Lake City. The U.S. is also expected to be awarded the 2031 Women's World Cup, though Andrew Giuliani, executive director of the White House FIFA Task Force, indicated that part of securing those hosting duties would involve "ensuring that, in fact, women and only women will play" — an allusion to the administration's opposition to transgender women in women's sports.

FIFA is already considering further expansion to 64 teams for the 2030 tournament, which will be hosted across Morocco, Spain, and Portugal. Trump, for his part, made clear his desire for the U.S. to host again soon. "We have to do this again, and we have to do it while I'm around," he said on Fox Sports.
