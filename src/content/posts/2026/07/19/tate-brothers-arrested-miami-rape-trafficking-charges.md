# Tate Brothers Arrested in Miami on Rape and Trafficking Charges

Social media influencers Andrew Tate (39) and Tristan Tate (38) were arrested in Miami on Saturday by the U.S. Marshals Service on a sealed warrant, following an extradition request from the United Kingdom. British prosecutors announced 38 new charges against the brothers — bringing the total to 59 — alleging rape, human trafficking, sexual assault, and offenses relating to indecent images of a child and extreme pornography, involving seven alleged victims. The brothers are expected to appear in Miami federal court this week as the extradition process begins.

## Background: Who Are the Tate Brothers?

Andrew and Tristan Tate are dual U.S.-British citizens and former professional kickboxers who have built a massive online following through content promoting wealth, male dominance, and misogyny. Andrew Tate has amassed more than 10 million followers on X (formerly Twitter) and has been banned from YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram for violating hate speech guidelines. He runs an online academy where he claims to teach young men how to get rich and attract women, according to [AP News](https://apnews.com/article/tate-brothers-andrew-tristan-arrest-us-britain-0e0cb09047b75ce10ae210c86092c287).

The brothers are vocal supporters of U.S. President Donald Trump and have cultivated relationships with Trump allies. The New York Times reported in December 2025 that the Tates had communicated with Trump's youngest son, Barron Trump, over Zoom.

## The Charges Against Them

The Crown Prosecution Service (CPS) announced the new charges on Saturday, which relate to alleged offenses that took place between July 2010 and August 2017 in an area north of London where the brothers grew up. According to the [Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/news/2026/jul/18/andrew-tristan-tate-arrested-miami), Andrew Tate faces seven further counts of rape, three counts of arranging or facilitating trafficking for sexual exploitation, three counts of assault occasioning actual bodily harm, and 19 charges for offenses relating to indecent images of a child and extreme pornography. Tristan Tate is charged with one count of sexual assault, two counts of rape, and three counts of arranging or facilitating trafficking for sexual exploitation.

Malcolm McHaffie, head of the Special Crime Division at the CPS, said: "These charging decisions followed receipt of a further file of evidence from Bedfordshire Police and bring the total number of alleged victims in this case to seven." He emphasized that criminal proceedings are active and urged against any reporting or commentary that could prejudice the proceedings.

## A Long-Running International Legal Saga

The arrests mark the latest chapter in a multi-year legal battle spanning three countries. The Tates were first arrested in Romania in December 2022 on charges of human trafficking, rape, and forming a criminal gang to sexually exploit women. That case has stalled due to legal and procedural problems, as [USA Today](https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/world/2026/07/19/andrew-tate-extradition-what-to-know/90972596007/) reports.

In February 2025, Romanian authorities lifted travel restrictions, and the brothers flew to Florida on a private jet. Romania's foreign minister said at the time that a U.S. official in the Trump administration had expressed interest in the brothers' legal case. In June 2026, a U.K. High Court judge threw out the Tates' legal bid to be told the names of their accusers.

## The Extradition Process

The brothers are expected to appear in Miami federal court early this week for an initial determination. Extradition requests to the U.S. involve two phases: a judicial phase where courts determine if the allegations merit extradition, and an executive phase where the Secretary of State makes the final decision. The brothers are unlikely to get bail, which is rarely granted to those held for international extradition.

The final decision on whether to extradite the Tates will rest with U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio. The U.S. and U.K. have a strengthened extradition treaty ratified in 2007, and the U.S. has granted nearly every British request since then. One notable exception was the Anne Sacoolas case in 2019, when then-Secretary of State Mike Pompeo refused extradition for a U.S. diplomat's wife involved in a fatal crash in England, citing diplomatic immunity.

## Reactions and Defense

Joseph McBride, the brothers' attorney, called the charges "filth and slander" designed to counter defamation lawsuits filed by the Tates in the U.S. "The world knows Andrew and Tristan Tate are innocent. Their enemies know it best of all. That is exactly why they have been attacked," McBride said in a statement. "America does not do Britain's political dirty work."

Matt Jury, a lawyer representing four alleged British victims of Andrew Tate, urged authorities to ensure the case proceeds quickly. "Andrew and Tristan Tate are accused of some of the most serious offences, including multiple counts of rape and human trafficking. It is time they face justice," Jury said.

Bedfordshire Police Assistant Chief Constable Karena Thomas said: "There is no place for male violence against women and girls, and we will continue to work tirelessly to support victims and investigate all reports made to us."

## What to Watch For

The coming weeks will be critical in determining whether the Tates will face trial in the United Kingdom. The initial court appearance in Miami will set the stage for what could be a lengthy extradition battle, with potential political implications given the brothers' connections to the Trump administration. The case also highlights broader societal questions about the influence of "manosphere" content creators and the legal accountability of social media personalities for alleged crimes.
