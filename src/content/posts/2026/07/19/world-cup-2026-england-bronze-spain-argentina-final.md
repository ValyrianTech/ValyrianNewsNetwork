# World Cup 2026: England Win Bronze, Spain Face Argentina in Final

England defeated France 6-4 in a thrilling bronze medal match at the 2026 FIFA World Cup on Saturday, while Spain and Argentina prepare to face off in Sunday's final at MetLife Stadium. In a parallel story of cultural significance, three young Chinese artisans have brought centuries-old traditional crafts to the world's biggest sporting event through officially licensed FIFA products, marking the first time Chinese intangible cultural heritage has been integrated into World Cup merchandise at scale.

## England's Bronze Triumph

England secured third place with a remarkable victory over France at Miami Stadium, recovering from their semi-final heartbreak against Argentina. Bukayo Saka scored a hat-trick, including a penalty, while Jude Bellingham added a goal after coming off the bench. Declan Rice and Ezri Konsa also found the net in a first half that saw England race to a 4-0 lead, as [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/football/2026/jul/19/england-france-world-cup-third-place-playoff-match-report) reported.

France mounted a spirited second-half comeback, with Kylian Mbappé scoring twice and Bradley Barcola and Ousmane Dembélé adding goals, but England held on to claim the bronze medal. The match was played in front of a packed crowd at Hard Rock Stadium in Miami, with [Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/sports/20260719/96cb97bd32b84541a70d2a510a31a2dc/c.html) reporting that Saka's performance was the highlight of an extraordinary contest.

## Final Preview: Spain vs Argentina

All eyes now turn to MetLife Stadium in New Jersey, where Spain (FIFA No. 2) and Argentina (FIFA No. 1, defending champions) will clash in what promises to be a historic final. Argentina are seeking to become the third nation to retain the World Cup, after Italy (1934, 1938) and Brazil (1958, 1962). Spain, the reigning European champions, are chasing their second World Cup title following their 2010 triumph.

According to the [Opta supercomputer](https://theanalyst.com/articles/spain-vs-argentina-prediction-world-cup-final-2026-match-preview), Spain are favored in 59.6% of 25,000 simulations, though Argentina have won all seven of their matches at this tournament. The match also marks the first time the reigning Copa America and European champions have met in a World Cup final, and the first time the world's top two ranked nations have faced each other at any stage of the tournament.

Lionel Messi, at 39 years old, is set to become just the second player in history to appear in three World Cup finals, while Spain's teenage sensation Lamine Yamal could become the third-youngest player to feature in a final.

## Chinese Intangible Heritage Meets the World Cup

Beyond the pitch, the 2026 World Cup has become an unexpected stage for Chinese cultural diplomacy. For the first time in the tournament's history, traditional Chinese handicrafts have been integrated into FIFA's officially licensed product ecosystem through a project called "Intangible Heritage Meets World Cup" (非遗遇见世界杯).

Three young artisans born after 1990 are at the heart of this initiative, as [People's Daily](http://ent.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0719/c1012-40763483.html) reported. Gao Jia, a 95后 (post-1995) third-generation inheritor of Yuxian paper-cutting from Hebei Province, created World Cup mascot designs — a moose, a jaguar, and a bald eagle — in the intricate carved-paper style that defines her craft. "They want to see more diverse, younger products," Gao said of FIFA's expectations, noting that she has been shrinking her paper-cuttings to fit phone cases and refrigerator magnets to make them more accessible.

Qiao Lin, a 90后 (post-1990) 29th-generation inheritor of Qiao's Fahua ceramics from Shanxi, expanded her family's glaze palette from just five colors to over 150, a breakthrough that proved essential for meeting FIFA's exacting color standards. "The World Cup is in full swing, and Qiao's Fahua Workshop is really 'burning up'!" she said. Her workshop produced coffee cups, aromatherapy holders, and pen holders designed to "accompany people watching the matches."

Long Yu, a 90后 Miao embroidery promoter from Guizhou, is working to "let intangible heritage better connect with modern life." He collaborated with a surfskate company to produce three board designs combining World Cup elements with classic Miao embroidery patterns. Long is also developing an AI-powered platform to digitize Miao embroidery patterns and stitches, helping match designs to brand requirements more efficiently.

## A New Model for Cultural Export

The project, authorized by FIFA's master licensee for Greater China, Hangzhou Fu De Brand Management Co., represents a new model of cultural export — not through government campaigns but through commercial licensing and consumer products. The artisans have had to balance the standardization requirements of a global sporting event with the authenticity of centuries-old craft traditions.

For these young inheritors, the collaboration has been transformative. "I never dared to be so bold, to cross boundaries this far before," Qiao Lin admitted. Gao Jia added that the experience taught her how international partnerships should work: "Our core culture and elements should be respected and cannot be arbitrarily dismantled or interpreted."

## What to Watch For

As the football world focuses on Sunday's final between Spain and Argentina, the cultural legacy of this World Cup may extend beyond the pitch. The success of the "Intangible Heritage Meets World Cup" project could pave the way for more Chinese cultural elements in future international sporting events, while the AI-powered approach to preserving traditional crafts — particularly Long Yu's Miao embroidery platform — could serve as a model for heritage preservation worldwide.

The World Cup final kicks off at MetLife Stadium on Sunday, with Argentina looking to defend their title against a Spanish side that has kept six clean sheets in the tournament and remains unbeaten in 37 matches across all competitions.
