# China's Student Loan Program Reaches 620.7 Billion Yuan in 20 Years

China's student-origin credit-based student loan program has cumulatively disbursed **620.7 billion yuan** (approximately US$85.5 billion) over its 20-year history, providing financial support to **68.21 million student-visits** from low-income families, according to the [Ministry of Education](http://www.moe.gov.cn/jyb_zzjg/huodong/202607/t20260706_1442838.html). The milestone was announced as the 2026 application season officially launched on July 6 in Lanzhou, Gansu Province.

## A Two-Decade Commitment to Educational Equity

Established in 2006, the "student-origin credit助学贷款" program is a government-backed financial aid initiative administered primarily through the China Development Bank. Unlike traditional loans, it requires no collateral, operating as a credit-based system where students and their parents or guardians serve as co-borrowers. Applications are processed in the student's household registration county before university enrollment, making it particularly accessible to students from rural and less-developed regions.

The program's 20-year anniversary coincides with the start of China's "15th Five-Year Plan" period (2026–2030), signaling continued government prioritization of education access as a driver of social mobility and economic transformation.

## 2026 Application Season Kicks Off in Gansu

The 2026 Student-Origin Credit助学贷款 Application Kickoff Meeting was held in Lanzhou, Gansu Province, a western region that has historically benefited from the program's focus on underserved areas. Vice Minister of Education Wang Guangyan delivered the keynote speech, while Vice Governor of Gansu Wang Xu offered opening remarks. Representatives from the Ministry of Finance and China Development Bank also participated, alongside education departments from Henan and Gansu provinces, CDB's Guizhou branch, and Sichuan University, who shared their experiences with the program.

As [CCTV News](https://tv.cctv.com/2026/07/12/VIDEhuUFH3V3ix3wNKaRaqsy260712.shtml) reported, Vice Minister Wang highlighted the program's transformative impact, stating: "Over 20 years, the program has cumulatively disbursed 620.7 billion yuan, supporting 68.21 million student-visits, making significant contributions to promoting educational equity and building a strong education nation."

## Policy Evolution and Recent Reforms

The loan program has undergone significant policy adjustments in recent years to better serve students. In 2023, loan limits were raised to 12,000 yuan per year for undergraduate students and 16,000 yuan per year for graduate students. In 2024 and 2025, the government introduced interest-free policies with principal repayment deferral options, providing critical relief during economic uncertainty.

According to [Jiemian News](https://www.jiemian.com/article/14709381.html), the meeting emphasized a shift from a "guarantee-type" financial aid model toward a "development-type" approach, focusing not just on ensuring access to education but also on supporting student success and personal development outcomes.

## Digital Transformation and Future Directions

The Ministry of Education's emphasis on "数智赋能" (digital intelligence empowerment) signals an increasing role for technology in loan administration. From streamlined application processing to improved risk management, digital tools are expected to enhance the program's efficiency and reach.

The [Economic Observer](https://www.eeo.com.cn/2026/0706/945702.shtml) noted that the meeting called for scientific planning, quality improvement, and risk prevention as key priorities moving forward, reflecting a maturing program that must balance expanded access with financial sustainability.

## Broader Implications for China's Education Strategy

The 620.7 billion yuan figure represents one of the world's largest state investments in higher education access through loan programs. For millions of rural and low-income students, particularly in western provinces like Gansu, the program has been a lifeline—enabling university attendance that would otherwise be financially out of reach.

The choice of Lanzhou as the venue for the national kickoff meeting underscores the government's continued focus on regional equity. As China navigates demographic challenges and economic transformation, investments in human capital through programs like this remain central to the country's long-term development strategy.

## What to Watch

As the program enters its third decade, key questions remain: How will default rates evolve as loan volumes grow? What percentage of eligible students actually apply for and receive loans? And how will the transition to "development-type" aid reshape the student experience? The answers will shape the next chapter of China's ambitious educational equity project.

For now, the 20-year milestone offers a moment to assess a program that has touched tens of millions of lives—and the 2026 application season opening marks another step forward in China's ongoing effort to make higher education accessible to all.
