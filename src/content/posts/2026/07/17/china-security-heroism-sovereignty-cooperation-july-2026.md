# Heroism, Sovereignty, Cooperation: China's Security Week

Three distinct but thematically connected stories emerged from China this week, painting a multifaceted picture of the nation's security landscape: the heroic sacrifice of a young police officer in Shenzhen, a firm reaffirmation of Beijing's unification stance on Taiwan, and the launch of the seventh China-Croatia joint police patrol. Together, they highlight themes of individual sacrifice, national sovereignty, and international cooperation.

## A Young Officer's Ultimate Sacrifice

On July 12, 2026, 25-year-old police officer Wu Long made the ultimate sacrifice while protecting civilians in Shenzhen. According to the [Shenzhen Public Security Bureau](https://news.sina.com.cn/c/2026-07-17/doc-inihzmmu4446139.shtml), Wu Long and auxiliary officer She Jiabin confronted a knife-wielding criminal. Despite being severely wounded during the struggle, Wu Long continued fighting alongside his partner and bystanders to subdue the attacker. He ultimately succumbed to blood loss.

Born in October 2000 in Kunming, Yunnan Province, Wu Long served at the Liuyue Police Station in Shenzhen's Longgang District. Colleagues remembered him as an upright and dedicated officer who frequently volunteered for missions, including pursuing fugitives during the Spring Festival holiday. His funeral on July 15 drew over 800 mourners, including leaders from the Guangdong Provincial Public Security Department, Shenzhen municipal officials, and representatives from the Ministry of Public Security, which sent wreaths in tribute.

The incident has reignited public discussion in China about the equipment and training provided to grassroots police officers. Many online commenters noted that frontline officers often lack firearms and must rely on batons and physical confrontation when facing armed suspects, raising questions about whether adequate protective measures are in place.

## Beijing Reaffirms Unification Stance

On the political front, the State Council Taiwan Affairs Office issued a forceful statement on July 16, pushing back against recent remarks by Taiwan's leader Lai Ching-te. Speaking through spokesperson [Chen Binhu via Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/20260716/fa8431e982db4800941bc7a912d3d13e/c.html), Beijing rejected Lai's characterization of China's Anti-Secession Law and the Law on the Promotion of National Unity and Progress as instruments of "long-arm jurisdiction" that create a "chilling effect on democratic society."

Chen Binhu defended the Anti-Secession Law as both a safeguard for national sovereignty and a framework for peaceful cross-strait development, reflecting "the common will of all Chinese people." He accused the Lai administration of deliberately distorting facts to intimidate Taiwanese citizens and manufacture a "chilling effect" of its own.

"No matter what the Lai Ching-te administration says or does, it cannot change the basic格局 of cross-strait relations, let alone stop the historical trend that the motherland will ultimately and must be reunified," Chen stated. He urged Taiwanese compatriots to recognize what he described as the "ambitions and selfish desires" of Taiwan independence forces and to stand on the "correct side of history."

The statement is the latest in an ongoing pattern of rhetorical escalation between Beijing and the Lai administration, which took office in May 2024. The Democratic Progressive Party has historically advocated for Taiwan's separate sovereignty, a position that places it on a direct collision course with Beijing's core policy.

## International Cooperation: China-Croatia Joint Patrols

Striking a contrasting tone of international collaboration, the seventh China-Croatia joint police patrol was launched on July 15 at the Chinese Embassy in Zagreb. As [Xinhua News reported](https://www.news.cn/world/20260716/313bff349ef64f72bb06992fbf974705/c.html), eight Chinese officers from provinces including Hunan, Shaanxi, Jiangsu, and Guangdong, as well as the municipalities of Shanghai, Tianjin, Chongqing, and Beijing, will patrol alongside Croatian counterparts for one month.

The joint patrols will cover popular Chinese tourist destinations including Zagreb, Plitvice Lakes National Park, Zadar, and Dubrovnik. Croatian State Secretary of the Interior Irena Petrijevčanin welcomed the Chinese officers, expressing hope that the cooperation would contribute to maintaining security in Croatia.

Zhao Xuan, commander of the Chinese team, pledged that his officers would strictly abide by Croatian laws and respect local customs while assisting Chinese citizens and international tourists with their security needs. The program, which began in 2018 as part of Croatia's "Safe Tourist Destination" initiative, reflects the growing bilateral relationship between China and Croatia, including cooperation under the Belt and Road Initiative.

## Connecting the Threads

While these three stories may appear unrelated, they collectively illuminate key priorities in China's security and governance approach. The honoring of Wu Long reinforces the value placed on public service and individual sacrifice for the collective good. The Taiwan statement underscores Beijing's unwavering position on territorial sovereignty. And the Croatia patrol demonstrates China's expanding role in international law enforcement cooperation.

Together, they present a nation simultaneously focused on domestic security, assertive in its sovereignty claims, and increasingly engaged in cooperative international policing—a multifaceted approach to security in an interconnected world.

## What to Watch

In the coming weeks, the public discourse around grassroots police equipment and training in China is likely to continue, potentially prompting policy discussions at the local level. On cross-strait relations, further rhetorical exchanges between Beijing and Taipei appear inevitable as both sides hold firm to their positions. Meanwhile, the success of the China-Croatia patrol program may pave the way for similar initiatives with other European nations.
