# Gentse Feesten 2026: Lajoie's Farewell, New Crowd Limits

The 183rd edition of the Gentse Feesten, Europe's largest city festival, opened on July 17, 2026, with a series of historic firsts: five neighborhood processions marking the start, the final performances of legendary cabaretier Dirk Lajoie, and the introduction of visitor capacity limits on the Vlasmarkt square.

## A New Kind of Opening

For the first time, the festival kicked off not with a single grand show but with five processions converging on the city center from different neighborhoods. According to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/16/gentse-feesten-2026/), three groups departed on foot from Kapitein Zeppospark, Keizerpark, and Begijnhofdriespark, while one procession traveled by boat from the Coupure to Baudelopark and another took the tram from Brusselsesteenweg to Duivelsteen. All processions departed at 16:30.

This approach, organized by the pleinorganisatoren (square organizers), saved the city tens of thousands of euros compared to previous years and returned to a more traditional, community-driven opening format.

## Dirk Lajoie: An Era Ends

One of the most poignant stories of this year's festival is the farewell of Dirk Lajoie (79), the legendary Ghent-born cabaretier who is retiring after more than 60 years on stage. Lajoie is performing his final three shows titled 'Coupe Cabaret - Volume Twieje' at Nova Centrum in Sint-Amandsberg, alongside Theater Charlekijn and a four-piece live band.

"I have always loved being on stage, because that's where I truly feel at home and free," Lajoie told [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/17/dirk-lajoie-cabaretier-pensioen-gentse-feesten/).

A particularly emotional moment will come when Lajoie shares the stage with his son Sam Lajoie, who is making his debut as a cabaretier. Together, they will perform 'Mijn Gent' (My Ghent), a song originally written by Dirk's father Gust Lajoie. A puppet of Gust, created by Marino Lomme of Theater Charlekijn, will join them on stage, creating a three-generation tribute.

"And that will be very special, because he will stand next to his dad on stage," Lomme told [HLN](https://www.hln.be/gent/coupe-cabaret-volume-twieje-wordt-het-afscheid-van-dirk-lajoie-79-hij-speelt-nog-een-keer-zijn-bekendste-typetje~a4b405a0/). "Dirk Lajoie is taking his final steps as a cabaretier."

Lajoie's most famous character, the punker Melita ("Vloekende Punker"), will appear one last time. On Friday July 24 at 15:00, he will be honored at the Ghent City Hall. His final performances take place on July 24 at 20:00, July 25 at 15:00, and July 26 at 15:00.

Despite retiring from the stage, Lajoie has no plans to slow down entirely. "My mother just turned 100 and I plan to at least match that," he said. "I want to keep supporting young theater makers for as long as possible, free of charge."

## Visitor Limits on the Vlasmarkt

For the first time, the Vlasmarkt — one of the festival's most popular squares — will be subject to a visitor capacity limit. According to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/06/27/gentse-feesten-vlasmarkt-dicht-bij-4000-bezokers/), the square will be closed once more than 4,000 visitors are present. "Then no one else can enter unless other people leave," the city explained. Visitors can move through Koningstraat or Sint-Jacobsnieuwstraat to other streets. Similar measures already existed on other squares.

## Safety, Sustainability, and Accessibility

This year's festival introduces several other innovations. A mobile team to address transgressive behavior will patrol on Friday and Saturday nights from 23:30 to 3:30, with a fixed reporting point at the corner of Vlasmarkt and Steendam open daily from 23:30 to 8:30. An AI-powered smart LED screen in Volmolenstraat analyzes ambient sounds and displays messages when noise levels are too high — without recording conversations or identifying individuals.

On the sustainability front, improved security measures including camera monitoring have been introduced for reusable cups after thefts last year. The city is also exploring cups with embedded chips for future editions. A special GF tram from De Lijn runs between Flanders Expo, the city center, P+R, Gentbrugge, and Melle Leeuw, with daytime frequency every 7.5 to 10 minutes and nighttime service every 15 minutes.

Accessibility has also been improved: a wheelchair lift at the Cirq festival terrain allows artists with disabilities to reach the stage, and dance opportunities for blind people are available in Baudelopark.

## A Festival in Transition

With nearly 1.6 million visitors expected over ten days, the Gentse Feesten continues to evolve. Lajoie himself has been critical of the festival's direction. "I don't really consider that the Gentse Feesten anymore," he said. "It has actually become more of a commercial party."

His departure marks the end of an era for Ghent's cabaret tradition. As one of the last performers deeply rooted in the Ghent dialect and folk theater, his exit raises questions about preserving local cultural identity amid growing tourism and commercialization.

## What to Watch For

The festival runs through July 26. Key upcoming events include Lajoie's city hall tribute on July 24 and his final performance on July 26. The new crowd management measures on the Vlasmarkt will be closely watched as a potential model for future editions, and the AI noise monitoring system may be expanded to other locations depending on its effectiveness.

For now, Ghent is celebrating — with a mix of nostalgia for its past and cautious optimism about its future.
