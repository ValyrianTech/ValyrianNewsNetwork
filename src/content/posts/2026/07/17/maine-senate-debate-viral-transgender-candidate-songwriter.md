# Maine Senate Debate Goes Viral Over Songwriter Answer

A televised Democratic Senate debate in Maine erupted into a national firestorm on Thursday night after candidate Ashley Webb, asked about her qualifications for office, cited being a songwriter and author — a response that drew widespread mockery and amassed over 750,000 views on social media within hours. The viral moment has become the latest flashpoint in an already tumultuous race to replace former Democratic nominee Graham Platner, who withdrew this month amid sexual assault allegations.

## Context

The debate, held at the WCSH-6 studio in Portland, was organized to help Democrats select a replacement for Platner, a progressive populist who won the Democratic primary in June with a record-breaking 150,000 votes. Platner withdrew from the race on July 8-9 after a woman he had previously dated accused him of rape, allegations he denies. The [Maine Democratic Party](https://www.bangordailynews.com/2026/07/09/politics/elections/democrats-graham-platners-base/) now faces an unprecedented situation: a convention of approximately 600 delegates will select a new nominee on July 25, with a final deadline of July 27.

Eight candidates participated in the two-hour debate, including Maine Secretary of State Shenna Bellows, former Maine Senate President Troy Jackson, former Maine CDC Director Nirav Shah, and Webb, a transgender and intersex activist who has run for office multiple times without success.

## Key Developments

When asked by a moderator, "What qualifications do you have to serve in the U.S. Senate?" Webb responded: "I ran for office several times, didn't win, but I did run and then, I'm a songwriter and then I write my own books." She added, "I suppose my transparency, I wouldn't lie to people and I wouldn't deceive the people, like, we're being deceived right now."

According to [Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/politics/transgender-maine-senate-candidate-sparks-online-frenzy-response-debate-question-cant-be-serious), the response ignited immediate mockery from conservative commentators. Dave Rubin posted on X: "Surely you can't be serious…" while author Bethany Mandel wrote: "You cannot and will not convince me that this is real." The [New York Post](https://nypost.com/2026/07/17/us-news/dem-trans-activist-vying-to-replace-candidate-graham-platner-for-senate-goes-viral-over-absurd-debate-answer/) reported that the clip had surpassed 750,000 views on X by Friday.

Webb also stated: "I suppose the biggest thing is like I'm just an angry citizen right now, and if I get elected, I'd be an angry senator. And I think that would probably be their worst nightmare."

## Analysis

The viral moment serves multiple political purposes. For Republicans, it provides ammunition to paint the Democratic field as unserious at a critical juncture. Incumbent Republican Sen. Susan Collins, seeking a sixth term, is widely viewed as the most vulnerable Republican senator in 2026 — the only GOP senator representing a state that Donald Trump did not win in any of his three presidential campaigns. As [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_United_States_Senate_election_in_Maine) notes, Collins has historically outperformed other Republicans in Maine, defeating Democratic nominee Sara Gideon by nine points in 2020 despite polls predicting her loss.

For Democrats, the episode underscores the chaos of the post-Platner replacement process. The party must quickly coalesce around a credible challenger while navigating the cultural divides that Webb's candidacy has highlighted. Several more established candidates — including Bellows, Jackson, and Shah — offer traditional governing experience, but none have Platner's populist appeal or grassroots energy.

## What's Next

The Maine Democratic Party convention on July 25 will determine whether the party can unite behind a consensus candidate capable of challenging Collins in November. With the general election just over three months away, the eventual nominee will have limited time to build statewide name recognition and fundraising infrastructure. The question remains whether the Democratic field can recover from this turbulent chapter — or whether the viral debate moment will become a lasting symbol of a party struggling to find its footing in one of the year's most consequential Senate races.
