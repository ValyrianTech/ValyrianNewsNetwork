# Belgian Photographer Bart Herreman Dies at 81 in Milan

Bart Herreman, the celebrated Belgian-born photographer who built an international career from his base in Milan, has died at the age of 81. Herreman passed away unexpectedly in the night from Sunday, July 12 to Monday, July 13, 2026, in his studio in Milan, according to [Het Laatste Nieuws](https://www.hln.be/antwerpen/bekende-fotograaf-bart-herreman-81-in-milaan-overleden~a4e6e66d/). Known for his evolution from fashion photography to architectural documentation and ultimately to surrealist digital art, Herreman was described as a photographer who bridged documentary precision and artistic imagination for decades.

## A Life Between Belgium and Italy

Born in 1945 in Antwerp, Belgium, Herreman grew up in the districts of Ekeren and Kapellen. He studied at the Sint Joost Academy of Art in Breda, Netherlands, before moving to Italy in the 1960s — a decision that would define his career. As his [official biography](https://www.bartherreman.it/page/1/biografia) notes, he began as a fashion photographer, collaborating with prestigious Italian and international magazines before transitioning to architecture, interior, and art photography.

Herreman established his studio in Milan at via Brioschi 26, where he worked for renowned architects, designers, and international publications. His architectural portfolio includes iconic projects such as La Darsena di Milano, Università Bocconi, the Centre Pompidou-Metz, and the Cantina Antinori in Tuscany. His work also extended to documenting cultural landmarks like the Palazzo della Regione in Milan and the historic Teatro Gerolamo, as well as the sculptural works of artists such as Eduardo Chillida and Umberto Cavenago.

This transition from fashion to architecture marked a pivotal moment in his career. As his biography notes, Herreman channeled his talents into interpreting space through the lens, approaching each environment with a painterly touch — not merely documenting reality but reshaping it according to his personal vision.

## The Turn to Surrealism

With the advent of digital photography, Herreman developed a distinctive surrealistic visual language. His later works combined reality and imagination, bringing together people, animals, and landscapes in impossible, dreamlike scenes. Notable series included *Smoke*, *The Teacher*, *On the Rock*, and *Metaflora*.

As [MilanToday](https://www.milanotoday.it/eventi/mostra-fotografica-bart-herreman.html) reported during his 2022 exhibition at the Archivio Iginio Balderi, Herreman drew inspiration from surrealist masters including Miró, Ernst, Magritte, and Dalí. He described his approach as "pure psychic automatism," through which he expressed the real functioning of thought, free from rational control.

## Exhibitions and Recognition

Herreman remained active well into his later years, with numerous solo and group exhibitions across Italy. Notable shows included "Esefossevero 2024/2025" at Banca Generali Private in Genoa, "Esefossevero 2022" at the Archivio Iginio Balderi in Milan, and "L'Irreale Realtà" at Palazzo Ducale in Massa in 2018. His work was also featured at Seravezza Fotografia, one of Italy's prominent photography festivals, and at the exhibition "Una certa idea di sogno e di viaggio" which explored his dreamlike visual narratives.

According to [Artribune](https://www.artribune.com/artista-mostre-biografia/bart-herreman/), Herreman's exhibitions consistently drew attention for their technical mastery and imaginative scope. His 2022 Milan show featured 25 photographs, some in large format, that traced his creative journey and demonstrated his command of digital manipulation techniques.

His last Instagram post, shared on his account @herreman_bart, was embedded in the HLN obituary — a final glimpse into the artist's ongoing creative output even in his final days.

## A Cultural Bridge

Herreman was widely recognized as a cultural bridge between Belgium and Italy. His work was exhibited in both countries, and news of his death was prominently covered by Antwerp regional outlets. Gazet van Antwerpen reported that he died "unexpectedly" in his studio, though the exact cause of death has not been publicly disclosed.

Junior Verhelst, the HLN journalist who wrote the main obituary, captured his legacy succinctly: "With the death of Bart Herreman, the Belgian and Italian art world loses a photographer who for decades bridged documentary precision and artistic imagination."

## Legacy and Unanswered Questions

Herreman's career spanned more than six decades, from the fashion runways of Milan to the surreal digital landscapes of his later years. His unique position as a Belgian photographer who became an integral part of the Milanese art scene made him a distinctive voice in contemporary photography. He maintained strong ties to his hometown region of Antwerp throughout his life, even as he built his career in Italy.

As of this writing, no official statements from family members or Belgian cultural institutions have been published. The exact cause of death has not been disclosed, and questions remain about memorial arrangements — whether they will be held in Milan or Antwerp — and the status of his estate and unpublished works.

What is certain is the void his passing leaves in both the Belgian and Italian art worlds. Herreman was a photographer who refused to be confined to a single genre, moving seamlessly from the precision of architectural documentation to the boundless freedom of surrealist imagination. Art circles in both countries are expected to pay tribute to a photographer whose work defied easy categorization and whose creative spirit remained undimmed until the very end.
