# China and ASEAN Business Leaders Forge Deeper Economic Ties in Guangzhou

More than 1,000 business leaders, government officials, and industry representatives from China and ASEAN countries gathered in Guangzhou on July 15-16 for the 2026 China (Guangdong)-ASEAN Trade Promotion and Supply Chain Cooperation Mechanism Matchmaking Meeting, signaling a major push to upgrade bilateral economic cooperation amid surging trade volumes.

The event, co-hosted by the People's Government of Guangdong Province and the ASEAN-China Centre, drew 250 overseas guests from ASEAN government departments, consulates, and business delegations — including major firms such as Charoen Pokphand Group — alongside 750 representatives from Guangdong’s state-owned enterprises, private companies, and industrial parks, according to [Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/fortune/20260717/fa69b364779341df991d85e571b36a10/c.html).

## A Deepening Partnership Backed by Record Trade

The meeting comes at a moment of remarkable momentum in China-ASEAN economic relations. Bilateral trade surged 18.2% year-on-year to US$641 billion (4.34 trillion yuan) in the first half of 2026, with ASEAN retaining its position as China’s largest trading partner. Indonesia, Vietnam, and Thailand recorded the strongest increases, each expanding by more than 20%, [The Nation Thailand](https://www.nationthailand.com/news/world/40068754) reported, citing China’s General Administration of Customs.

Xu Ningning, chairman of the RCEP Industry Cooperation Committee, described the results as demonstrating “the resilience and dynamism of the region’s economic and trade links,” noting that a mature division of labor has enabled intermediate goods to move extensively between both sides.

## Key Outcomes: Reports, MoUs, and New Trade Centers

A centerpiece of the conference was the release of the “China (Guangdong)-ASEAN Supply Chain Cooperation Report,” which highlighted three defining features of the partnership: deep integration of regional production and supply chains, mature coordination models for key industries, and continuous improvement of cooperation platforms.

Guangdong CCPIT signed cooperation memorandums with the Malaysia Chinese Chamber of Commerce, Myanmar Federation of Chambers of Commerce and Industry, Philippine Chamber of Commerce and Industry, and Thai Industrial Federation. Additionally, trade service centers for Guangdong businesses were established in Laos, Myanmar, and the Philippines, building on five existing centers in Cambodia, Malaysia, Singapore, Indonesia, and Vietnam, as reported by [China Daily](https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202607/08/WS6a4e0074a310986e2b464306.html).

## Focus on New Economy Sectors

Discussions centered on three strategic themes: AI-empowered industrial upgrading, new energy industry cooperation, and industrial chain and supply chain coordination. Zhang Lizhong, Secretary-General of the China-ASEAN Centre, called on both sides to “proactively build a more stable, efficient, and sustainable regional supply chain system,” urging a focus on “digital economy, green economy, and artificial intelligence.”

“Both sides can focus on cutting-edge fields such as digital economy, green economy, and artificial intelligence, jointly build digital infrastructure and green supply chains, promote the extension of industrial chains to the high end, and jointly create a benchmark for regional green transformation,” Zhang said.

Sanuson Ponnama, Vice Chairman of the Lao National Chamber of Commerce and Industry, highlighted Laos’s unique geographical advantages and expressed interest in exploring cooperation in rail-sea intermodal transport corridors, agricultural product processing, AI and digital skills training for Lao SMEs, and new energy manufacturing.

## The Guangdong-ASEAN Complementarity Model

Ou Shaobin, Deputy Director of the Trade and Investment Promotion Department at Guangdong CCPIT, articulated the complementary economic relationship that has emerged between the province and ASEAN. “The two sides have forged a sound complementary model featuring ‘Guangdong manufacturing plus ASEAN resources’ and ‘Guangdong technologies paired with ASEAN markets,’” Ou said.

Guangdong offers mature industrial chains in photovoltaic power, wind power, energy storage, new energy vehicles, and hydrogen energy, while ASEAN nations hold upstream resource advantages in materials such as nickel ore and bauxite, alongside growing demand for clean energy infrastructure.

Zhou Haiying, Head of the Secretariat of Guangdong CCPIT, noted the strategic alignment between the Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area and Thailand’s Eastern Economic Corridor, calling the prospects for coordinated development “broad.” He also highlighted emerging cooperation in traditional medicine and health technology as new growth areas.

## Institutional Framework and Forward Look

The matchmaking meeting, now in its second year, follows the inaugural 2025 conference which facilitated over US$3.1 billion in project cooperation. It also aligns with the China-ASEAN Free Trade Area (FTA) 3.0 Upgrade Protocol signed in Kuala Lumpur on October 28, 2025, which is expected to further reduce barriers and expand market access.

Xu Ningning urged participating economies to implement the upgraded FTA more quickly and expand cooperation into digital trade, the green economy, and new energy, while strengthening services to help small and medium-sized enterprises enter regional industrial chains.

As global supply chains undergo restructuring amid geopolitical uncertainties, the deepening institutionalization of Guangdong-ASEAN economic cooperation — moving from ad-hoc business deals to systematic, government-supported mechanisms — signals a strategic bet on regional integration. The coming months will reveal how quickly the FTA 3.0 provisions translate into tangible benefits for businesses on both sides.
