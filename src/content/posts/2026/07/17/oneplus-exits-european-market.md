# OnePlus Exits European Market After 12 Years

Chinese smartphone manufacturer OnePlus has officially confirmed its withdrawal from the European and North American markets, ending a 12-year presence in the West. The announcement, made on July 16, 2026, follows months of speculation and marks a significant shift in the continent's competitive smartphone landscape. Parent company OPPO confirmed the decision, stating it aims to consolidate resources and strengthen global product strategy.

## Context & Background

OnePlus entered the European market in 2014 with the OnePlus One, a device that disrupted the industry by offering premium specifications at roughly half the price of comparable flagships from Samsung and Apple. The company's "flagship killer" strategy and invite-only sales model created a devoted following among tech enthusiasts. According to [Tweakers](https://tweakers.net/nieuws/250092/oneplus-stopt-na-twaalf-jaar-met-verkoop-producten-in-europa.html), the brand quickly became popular for its clean software experience and focus on performance.

However, over successive generations, OnePlus gradually increased prices, moving into premium territory where it competed directly with established giants without the brand recognition to command those prices. The company's integration with parent company OPPO accelerated after 2021, when research and development operations were consolidated and OxygenOS began sharing a common platform with OPPO's ColorOS.

## Key Developments

Existing OnePlus customers in Europe and North America will retain warranty coverage and continue to receive software updates, as [Android Authority](https://www.androidauthority.com/oneplus-north-america-europe-exit-plans-reasons-future-3687095/) reported. However, devices will transition from OnePlus's signature OxygenOS to OPPO's ColorOS in the coming months. OPPO Europe CEO Elvis Zhou confirmed to [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/tech/966404/oneplus-oppo-us-europe-withdrawal-realme) that users will have the option to roll back to OxygenOS if they prefer, though this would mean giving up future updates.

OPPO plans to expand its presence in Europe, offering switch incentives to OnePlus customers in select markets including Belgium and the Netherlands. The company described Europe as a strategic market and said it would increase investment across the region, with a stronger emphasis on flagship smartphones and expanded retail partnerships.

OnePlus will continue operations in China and India, where it has stronger market positions. OPPO's other sub-brand, Realme, is also scaling back operations in China to focus on overseas markets, as [WIRED](https://www.wired.com/story/oneplus-is-all-but-dead/) reported.

## Analysis & Implications

The exit marks the end of a remarkable rise and fall. OnePlus launched in 2013 with a disruptive ethos, but abandoned its "flagship killer" pricing strategy over time. The merger of OxygenOS with ColorOS removed one of the brand's key differentiators — a clean, stock-Android-like experience that formed the core of its appeal to enthusiasts.

Market pressures also played a significant role. OnePlus's US market share dropped from 1.8% in 2021 to just 0.1% by 2025, with shipments falling from 1 million to approximately 130,000 units annually. The company lost its T-Mobile partnership in 2023, a severe blow in a market where carrier partnerships drive 66% of smartphone sales. Global smartphone shipments declined 11% year-over-year in Q2 2026, according to Counterpoint Research, creating an unfavorable environment for mid-tier players.

OnePlus joins a growing list of smartphone brands that have exited or drastically scaled back Western operations, including HTC, LG Mobile, and HMD. The market is increasingly consolidating around Apple and Samsung, who together held 80% of the US market in 2025.

## What's Next

For Belgian consumers, OnePlus phones will no longer be available for purchase. Existing users will retain warranty and software update commitments, with devices transitioning to ColorOS. OPPO will offer switch incentives in select markets, potentially filling the gap left by OnePlus.

The OnePlus brand may eventually be phased out entirely, with reports suggesting it could leave India by 2027. As the smartphone market continues to consolidate, consumers may increasingly turn to alternatives such as Nothing, founded by OnePlus co-founder Carl Pei, or other emerging brands in the mid-to-premium Android segment.
