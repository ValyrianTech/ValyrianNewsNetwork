# Trump Vows Prosecution After Reflecting Pool Damage, Seeks Vandal-Proof Materials

President Donald Trump vowed on Friday to prosecute individuals accused of damaging the Lincoln Memorial Reflecting Pool "to the MAX," calling them "scum" and "deranged vandals" in a series of posts on Truth Social. The White House is now searching for "vandal proof material" to protect the iconic National Mall landmark, which has been drained for a second time this year amid a politically charged dispute over the extent and cause of the damage.

According to [Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-white-house-searching-vandal-proof-material-reflecting-pool-attack), Trump wrote that the pool had been "so badly damaged by Deranged Vandals" and emptied for repairs, adding: "We are looking for a Vandal Proof material, but such a thing should not have been necessary. The Scum in Court will hopefully be prosecuted to the MAX."

## Background: A Troubled Renovation

The Reflecting Pool, dedicated in 1923 and situated between the Lincoln Memorial and the Washington Monument, underwent a multimillion-dollar renovation this year ordered by Trump. The project was intended to repaint the pool's bottom in a shade the president called "American flag blue" in time for the nation's 250th anniversary on July 4.

However, the renovation was beset by problems from the start. The [Associated Press](https://apnews.com/article/reflecting-pool-damage-trump-david-hearn-c2f8e1d689d8cd3cd4f9aade65c674ee) reported that the initial public cost estimate of $2 million ballooned to more than $14 million. A Virginia-based contractor received a no-bid contract, and the [Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/jun/11/washington-dc-reflecting-pool) reported that Trump had steered the contract to a company that previously worked on his golf resort in the state.

Shortly after the pool was refilled, it turned green due to a natural bloom of algae from the genus Desmodesmus. Aquatic ecologists told [NPR](https://www.stlpr.org/npr/2026-06-21/trump-claims-vandals-damaged-d-c-reflecting-pool-and-says-it-will-be-drained-again) that the discoloration was a common process in shallow, sun-exposed water, and a George Mason University professor confirmed the algae was not toxic.

## The Vandalism Claims and Legal Proceedings

Trump has alleged that vandals used "some form of knife or blade" to cut a "250-foot-long gash" in the pool's new coating and poured "corrosive and destructive chemicals" into the water. However, neither the White House nor law enforcement agencies have released photographic evidence of such damage, and Trump made the claims "without substantiation," according to the AP.

Multiple arrests have been made. Former U.S. Olympic canoeist David Hearn, 67, was indicted on a felony destruction of property charge and pleaded not guilty on July 9. Hearn's defense attorney, Norm Eisen, a former White House ethics czar under President Obama, stated: "Every American should be alarmed about this prosecution. It is not a crime to touch the Reflecting Pool."

Hearn claims he stopped at the pool during a 64-mile bike ride, reached in to examine peeled coating, and briefly touched a chunk attached to the pool's side. At least three other people have been charged with misdemeanors for allegedly removing pieces of paint.

The case is being prosecuted by U.S. Attorney Jeanine Pirro, a former Fox News host appointed by Trump. She stated that vandalizing national monuments is "an affront to our shared history."

## The Tire Tracks Controversy

Following the July draining, social media users claimed that photos showed tire tracks from Trump's May 7 motorcade tour of the drained pool. [Snopes](https://www.snopes.com/fact-check/reflecting-pool-tire-tracks/) rated this claim "Miscaptioned," finding that the visible lines were debris, not tire tracks. Photographer Joe Flood, who took the photos, told Snopes the lines were "irregular lines of debris," likely from July 4th fireworks residue.

## Analysis: A Flashpoint in a Broader Debate

The Reflecting Pool story has become a political flashpoint. Supporters view the vandalism claims as legitimate attacks on a national monument requiring strong prosecution. Critics argue the administration is using the vandalism narrative to deflect from project mismanagement, cost overruns, and contracting irregularities.

The Cultural Landscape Foundation, a nonprofit, has sued the federal government, arguing the administration bypassed required historic preservation reviews for the renovation. A federal judge has not yet ruled on the case.

## What's Next

The pool remains drained for repairs, with no announced timeline for completion. The legal proceedings against Hearn and others will likely test the administration's evidence for its most dramatic vandalism claims. A status hearing for Hearn is scheduled for August 5.

As the White House searches for vandal-proof materials, the broader questions remain: What caused the coating failure? Will the administration present evidence of the alleged 250-foot gash in court? And how will the $14 million renovation project be remembered — as a patriotic restoration or a cautionary tale of cost overruns and political controversy?
