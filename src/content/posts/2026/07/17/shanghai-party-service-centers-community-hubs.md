# Shanghai Party Service Centers Go Viral as Community Hubs

Shanghai's Party service centers (党群服务中心) have become an unlikely social media sensation, drawing residents and visitors alike to "check in" at what were once seen as purely political spaces. With over 12,000 centers across the city, these facilities have evolved far beyond their traditional functions into comprehensive community hubs offering dining, education, recreation, healthcare, and business support services — a transformation that [CCTV News](https://news.cctv.com/2026/07/17/ARTIFmsbWQEsP3L5y5P7B9ih260717.shtml) describes as a "破圈" (breaking out) phenomenon.

## From Political Spaces to Community Anchors

The roots of this transformation trace back to May 2019, when the General Office of the CPC Central Committee issued the "Opinions on Strengthening and Improving Urban Primary-level Party Building," calling for the construction of "extensive coverage, intensive and efficient Party service centers." The [policy document](https://www.gov.cn/zhengce/2019-05/08/content_5389836.htm) envisioned these centers as "common spaces for Party members and the masses," integrating Party building, government, and social service resources.

Shanghai took this mandate and ran with it. The city converted old Party activity venues, repurposed public facilities and old factories, and embedded centers within the "15-minute community life circle" — the urban planning concept ensuring residents can access essential services within a quarter-hour walk. As Zhang Jie (张洁), Deputy Director of the Shanghai Party Building Service Center, explained to CCTV News: "We build them denser so that the masses can reach them in the shortest time possible."

## A Menu of 300+ Services

What makes these centers so popular is their sheer utility. They offer over 300 types of services, from community canteens and libraries to medical services, youth evening schools, and even livestreaming studios. According to the [Shanghai Government](https://www.shanghai.gov.cn/nw4411/20260706/14b3597ada97446f98431604ba5f1b3a.html), approximately 1,700 centers are open all week, and 950 extend their hours into the evening.

The Butterfly Bay (蝴蝶湾) Riverside Party Service Center in Jing'an District exemplifies the model. Housed in a beautifully renovated red-brick villa along the Suzhou Creek, it features a community canteen with discounted meals — seniors over 60 receive 15% off, while delivery workers can get a hearty meal for just 9.9 yuan. "I come every day, three times a day," one Shanghai resident told CCTV News. Director He Hanqing (何涵卿) added: "We open every public space, all to the citizens."

## Serving Diverse Needs

The centers cater to a remarkably broad demographic. Seniors find affordable dining and social spaces. Children have dedicated activity areas. Young professionals attend evening schools. Entrepreneurs receive business support, from licensing assistance to mentorship programs.

He Jialin (何佳琳), a solo entrepreneur, described how the Party service center gave her crucial support during a period of uncertainty: "The Party service center gives me a lot of support. As an ordinary entrepreneur, I have a backing to rely on." Li Jiawei (李加为), a fruit tea shop owner whose business license was expedited through the center's coordination, expressed similar gratitude: "We are very grateful to these grassroots Party organization members. We can feel the warmth."

## Grassroots Governance Innovation

The phenomenon represents more than just popular community centers — it signals a significant evolution in Chinese grassroots governance. The centers have shifted from purely political propaganda spaces to service-oriented community anchors, potentially strengthening Party-community bonds through tangible benefits.

"In the past, some people heard the word 'Party building' and felt it was serious and distant," He Hanqing told the Liberation Daily. "Now, through Party service center construction, Party spaces have become public spaces where people are willing to stay."

This approach extends to new employment groups as well. With 3,100+ Party building service specialists serving 23,000+ emerging-sector Party organizations, the centers actively reach out to delivery workers, self-media creators, and solo entrepreneurs. Cao Lei (曹蕾), Director of the Jiangsu Road Street Community Party Service Center, noted: "New employment groups are highly mobile. Only by truly entering their daily lives and seeing their needs can we more stably establish connections with them."

## The Numbers Behind the Trend

The scale of activity at these centers is substantial. In 2025 alone, over 10 million person-times watched Party education livestreams hosted at the centers. More than 523,000 Party education events and 201,000 organizational life sessions were held, along with approximately 99,000 consultation and deliberation events.

## What's Next

As Shanghai's Party service centers continue to gain popularity — both online and in daily life — questions remain about long-term sustainability and whether the model can be replicated in smaller cities with fewer resources. For now, however, the centers have achieved something notable: they have transformed the perception of Party institutions from distant bureaucratic entities into welcoming, indispensable parts of neighborhood life. As one resident put it simply, they have become the "caring helper at the doorstep."
