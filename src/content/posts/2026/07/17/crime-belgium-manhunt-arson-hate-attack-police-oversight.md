# Crime in Belgium: Manhunt, Arson, Hate Attack, and Police Oversight

Belgium is grappling with a series of serious criminal incidents on Friday, July 17, ranging from an internationally wanted arson suspect to deliberately set wildfires, a transphobic assault, and fresh revelations about police conduct. Here is a roundup of the key developments.

## Genk Arson Suspect Internationally Wanted

A 43-year-old Afghan man from Genk who was convicted on Tuesday to 10 years in prison for setting his wife on fire has gone into hiding and is now the subject of an international manhunt. According to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/17/genkenaar-die-vrouw-in-brand-stak-staat-internationaal-geseind/), the man poured heating oil over his wife and set her alight three years ago. He was not present in court when the verdict was announced, and the judge ordered his immediate arrest.

Prosecutor Marijke Teunis confirmed that the suspect is now "internationaal geseind" (internationally wanted). However, his lawyer, Vanessa Penninger, told VRT NWS that her client remains in Belgium and plans to report to police. "We have agreed that he will report to the police today. He is just in Belgium, so he certainly hasn't fled," Penninger said, adding that she considers the international warrant excessive. The suspect denies the charges and has filed an appeal.

## Deliberate Wildfires in Limburg Nature Reserves

The public prosecutor's office has opened an investigation into deliberate arson after two wildfires broke out simultaneously on Wednesday evening in the Lanklaarderbos in Dilsen-Stokkem and the Opglabbekerzavel in Genk. [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/17/natuurbranden-in-genk-en-dilsen-stokkem-zijn-aangestoken/) reported that a fire expert discovered multiple ignition points: four in Genk and three in Dilsen-Stokkem, confirming the fires were deliberately set.

The fire in Genk was quickly contained, but the blaze in Lanklaar required more effort and flared up again on Thursday morning. The affected area is estimated at several hectares. No suspects have been identified yet, and local police CARMA, together with the prosecutor's office, have launched an investigation.

Flemish Environment Minister Jo Brouns (CD&V) condemned the acts in strong terms. "Whoever deliberately sets our nature on fire not only causes irreparable damage to valuable nature, but also endangers hikers, residents and our emergency services. That is completely unacceptable," Brouns said. The Agency for Nature and Forest is filing a complaint and joining the case as a civil party.

## Transphobic Attack in Westmalle

A 50-year-old transgender woman named Ann was physically attacked on Thursday evening in Westmalle, Antwerp province, in what police have classified as a hate incident. According to reports, a neighbor referred to as "Luc" began harassing her, calling her a "dirty trans" and telling her friends, "Don't let such a lunatic sit with you." He then jumped over a fence and struck her multiple times.

Ann, who has been in transition for three years, told reporters this was the first time she has been physically attacked for her gender identity, though she had experienced verbal harassment before. "Just leave me alone. Let me live like you would let anyone else live," she said. The incident occurred just three weeks before Antwerp Pride.

Police from zone Voorkempen confirmed the facts and filed a report for incitement to discrimination. The suspect, a 46-year-old local man, could not be immediately arrested. Ann plans to file a formal complaint and will be examined by a forensic doctor. The incident was captured on video and by a security camera.

## Molenbeek Social Housing Manager Accused of Fraud

In Brussels, the co-coordinator of the M.AI.S. (Agence Immobilière Sociale) in Molenbeek is facing multiple allegations of abuse spanning from 2023 to early 2026. According to [RTBF](https://www.rtbf.be/article/la-gestionnaire-de-l-ais-de-molenbeek-s-octroyait-des-avantages-et-logeait-sa-propre-fille-11758847), she used AIS workers to perform work on her own property, benefited from discounted rates, and used the agency's van for personal purposes for three years.

More seriously, her daughter was allocated a social housing unit under questionable circumstances, with priority points added without supporting documentation just 11 days before allocation. The manager was also hired before obtaining her social worker diploma and was paid at a higher salary scale than her qualifications warranted for 13 years. The board of directors has not yet ruled on her dismissal, but it appears likely. The case follows a similar scandal at the AIS of Saint-Josse, which went bankrupt.

## Comité P President Acknowledges Excessive Police Force

As the Comité P — Belgium's independent police oversight body — celebrates its 35th anniversary on July 18, its president Kathleen Stinckens acknowledged in an interview with [RTBF](https://www.rtbf.be/article/violences-policieres-parfois-on-constate-que-les-policiers-utilisent-trop-rapidement-la-force-affirme-la-presidente-du-comite-p-11759103) that police officers sometimes use force too quickly. "There are many rules before using force. And sometimes we see that police officers use it too quickly to achieve their goal," Stinckens said.

In 2025, the Comité P received 2,783 admissible complaints out of 3,213 total, a 9.4% increase year-on-year. Stinckens attributed the rise to large-scale protests in 2025 rather than a general deterioration of police behavior. She also emphasized that police must adapt their approach when dealing with minors, applying what she called the "child reflex." Less than 10% of complaints are forwarded to the prosecutor's office.

## Looking Ahead

These five incidents paint a picture of a country confronting multiple challenges simultaneously: violent crime and judicial follow-up, environmental crime during dry conditions, hate crimes against the LGBTQ+ community, institutional corruption in public housing, and ongoing debates about police conduct and accountability. In each case, investigations are ongoing, and further developments are expected in the coming days and weeks.
