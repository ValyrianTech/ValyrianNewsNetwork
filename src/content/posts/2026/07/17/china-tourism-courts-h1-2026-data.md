# China Tourism Surges; Courts Handle 21.3M Cases in H1 2026

China recorded 22.91 million foreign visitor entries in the first half of 2026, a 20.4% year-on-year increase, while Chinese courts nationwide received 21.3165 million cases of various types during the same period, according to official data released on July 17. The twin data points paint a picture of a country experiencing both a sustained tourism boom and an actively functioning legal system.

## Inbound Tourism Surge Continues

The tourism figures, released by the National Immigration Administration and [reported by CCTV News](https://news.cctv.com/2026/07/17/ARTIb4OQUUnc5ecQXlLQo0el260717.shtml), mark the latest milestone in China's remarkable inbound travel recovery. The momentum builds on strong foundations: in the first quarter of 2026 alone, China recorded 21.33 million foreign entries, up 22.3% year-on-year, with visa-free entries reaching 8.315 million — accounting for 77.9% of all foreign entries, according to [China.gov.cn](https://english.www.gov.cn/archive/statistics/202604/10/content_WS69d88c62c6d00ca5f9a0a539.html).

Shenzhen has emerged as a bellwether for the trend. At Shenzhen Baoan International Airport, inbound-outbound foreign travelers reached 1.034 million by July 15 — a record high achieved 71 days earlier than in 2025. Ma Xiaoyu, deputy captain of the Shenzhen Airport Border Check Station, told CCTV News that since June, cumulative foreign visitor inspections increased over 20.6% year-on-year, with sightseeing and leisure travelers accounting for over 45.2% of the total, up 25.7% year-on-year.

European visitors to Shenzhen have surged 31.6% year-on-year since June. Flight ticket data reveals particularly sharp increases: sales from Finland to China jumped 12.4 times year-on-year, while Greek ticket sales doubled. Shenzhen theme parks reported a 50% year-on-year increase in foreign visitors.

## From Business to Leisure: A Shift in Travel Patterns

The composition of inbound tourism is changing. Lu Jingyue, a hotel manager in Shenzhen, noted that foreign visitors were previously mainly business travelers, but 2026 has seen a significant increase in vacationers. "Foreign travelers' consumption needs have shifted from basic accommodation to immersive experiences of local cuisine and culture," Lu told CCTV News, adding that more foreign guests are choosing Cantonese morning tea and showing keen interest in the stories and culture behind the food.

Popular destinations extend beyond the major hubs of Shanghai, Beijing, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Chengdu, and Qingdao. Shi Ke, a researcher at the Qunar Big Data Research Institute, noted a "northwestward trend," with foreign visitor numbers to Yining and Linzhi surging 6.3 times year-on-year, while destinations like Datong, Yiwu, Jiamusi, and Dunhuang saw numbers double.

## Visa-Free Policy Driving Growth

China's systematic visa liberalization — expanding from a handful of countries in late 2023 to 50 nations by February 2026 — remains the primary driver. The 240-hour transit visa-free policy, upgraded from 144 hours, now covers 60 ports across 24 provincial regions. Hainan's visa-free policy covers passport holders from 86 countries, as [China Travel Plus](https://www.chinatravelplus.com/blog/China-Travel-Boom-Continues-Q1-2026-Foreign-Entries-Hit-21-33-Million-Up-22-3_b24212) reported in its Q1 analysis.

In 2025, China received approximately 150 million foreign entries, with inbound tourist spending exceeding $130 billion, according to the Ministry of Culture and Tourism.

## Courts Handle 21.3 Million Cases

On the judicial front, the Supreme People's Court reported that Chinese courts nationwide received 21.3165 million cases in the first half of 2026, up 1.15% year-on-year, as [People's Daily](http://society.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0717/c1008-40762481.html) reported. The data reveals several notable trends.

Criminal cases declined significantly: 730,000 criminal cases were received, down 5.82% year-on-year, with criminal first-instance cases falling 11.18% to 471,000 and sentenced defendants dropping 10.11% to 634,400. Civil and commercial cases totaled 13.6269 million, down 1.32%, while administrative cases rose 6.41% to 405,500.

Enforcement activity saw the sharpest increase, with enforcement cases rising 10.12% to 6.1767 million. The enforcement completion rate stood at 40.86% and the realization rate at 51.14%, indicating courts are prioritizing the conversion of judgments into monetary recovery.

## Notable Trends in Case Categories

Company-related disputes rose 14.78% year-on-year to 111,100 cases, and unfair competition disputes increased 9.59% to 7,484 cases, reflecting heightened commercial activity. Environmental resource civil cases grew 4.22% to 110,400 cases. However, intellectual property civil cases declined 18.41% to 250,600, which may indicate improved IP compliance or shifts in dispute resolution.

Human trafficking cases saw a dramatic decline. First-instance human trafficking cases fell 39.46% year-on-year to just 135 cases, according to [Worker.cn](https://www.workercn.cn/c/2026-07-16/8848701.shtml). The Supreme People's Court reported that heavy sentence rates for human trafficking were 34.55 percentage points above the overall criminal rate, reflecting targeted enforcement.

By the end of June, 25,700 grassroots courts had connected with township and street comprehensive governance centers, reflecting a policy push toward resolving disputes at the local level.

## What to Watch

For tourism, the key question is how quickly China's service ecosystem can scale to meet growing demand. The May Day 2026 holiday saw foreign entries surge 98.7% compared to the same period in 2024, suggesting continued momentum. On the judicial side, the rising enforcement and administrative caseloads bear watching as indicators of economic activity and citizen engagement with legal channels.
