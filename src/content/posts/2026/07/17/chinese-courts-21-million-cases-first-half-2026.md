# China's Courts Handled 21.3 Million Cases in H1 2026

Chinese courts nationwide processed **21.3 million cases** in the first half of 2026, marking a modest 1.15% year-on-year increase, according to data released by the Supreme People's Court (SPC) on July 16. The figures, published by [Xinhua via Yangtze Evening News](https://news.10jqka.com.cn/20260717/c678250706.shtml), reveal a significant structural shift: criminal cases are declining while enforcement-related cases are rising sharply.

## A System in Transition

The H1 2026 statistics paint a picture of a judicial system undergoing notable transformation. Criminal case receipts fell 5.82% year-on-year to 730,000, with first-instance criminal cases dropping 11.18% to 471,000. A total of 634,400 defendants were sentenced, down 10.11% from the same period last year.

Civil and commercial cases — the largest category — totaled 13.63 million, a slight decline of 1.32%. Administrative cases, however, rose 6.41% to 405,500, suggesting growing citizen engagement with administrative litigation.

The most striking trend was in enforcement: courts received 6.18 million enforcement cases, a surge of 10.12% year-on-year. First-instance enforcement cases totaled 5.84 million, up 9.90%. The enforcement completion rate stood at 40.86%, with a recovery rate of 51.14% — both described by the SPC as "operating at high levels."

As the SPC noted, these figures show that winning parties' rights are being converted from "paper rights" into "real money" more effectively than before.

## Enforcement: From Paper Rights to Real Money

The sharp rise in enforcement cases reflects a decade-long campaign by the SPC to address what was once described as a crisis of enforcement — where court judgments went unexecuted. The "basically solving enforcement difficulties" initiative, launched years ago, has transformed how Chinese courts handle judgment enforcement.

The 51.14% recovery rate means that for every yuan awarded by courts, more than half is now actually recovered by the winning party — a significant improvement from historical levels.

## Business Disputes and Economic Signals

Company-related disputes rose 14.78% to 111,100 first-instance cases, while unfair competition cases increased 9.59% to 7,484. These figures align with China's push to build a "unified national market" and intensify competition enforcement.

Intellectual property cases, however, fell 18.41% to 250,600 first-instance civil cases — a decline that may reflect improved IP awareness and more effective pre-litigation resolution mechanisms.

Environmental resource cases rose 4.22% to 110,400, while traffic accident disputes showed mixed trends: motor vehicle accident cases fell 7.01%, but non-motor vehicle accidents rose 4.20%.

## Grassroots Dispute Resolution Expands

A notable development is the integration of courts with community-level dispute resolution bodies. By the end of June, 25,700 township and subdistrict-level comprehensive governance centers had been connected to grassroots people's courts, facilitating "one-stop" dispute resolution.

This expansion is part of China's broader "governance of litigation sources" policy, which aims to resolve disputes at the community level before they escalate to formal court proceedings — potentially easing the caseload burden on the judicial system.

## Human Trafficking Cases Continue to Decline

In a separate but related data release, the SPC reported that first-instance trafficking of women and children cases fell to just 135 in H1 2026, a decline of 39.46% year-on-year, as [China News Network reported](https://www.workercn.cn/c/2026-07-16/8848701.shtml).

The court emphasized its strict approach to trafficking crimes, with the severe punishment rate — sentences of five years or more — running 34.55 percentage points higher than the overall criminal case rate. The continued decline continues a long-term trend from a peak in 2012.

## What to Watch

The H1 2026 data suggests several trends worth monitoring in the second half of the year. The divergence between declining criminal cases and surging enforcement actions points to a maturing legal environment where the focus is shifting from adjudication to execution. The rise in company disputes and unfair competition cases may accelerate as China's economic restructuring continues under the 15th Five-Year Plan. And the expansion of grassroots governance centers could begin to show measurable impacts on court caseloads in the coming quarters.

Full-year 2025 comparative data was not included in this release, making it difficult to assess whether the H1 trends represent an acceleration or moderation of longer-term patterns. The SPC's next quarterly update will provide further clarity.
