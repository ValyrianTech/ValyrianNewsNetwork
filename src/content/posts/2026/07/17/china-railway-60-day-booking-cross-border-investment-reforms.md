# China Extends Train Booking 60 Days in Investment Reform

China announced two significant policy developments on July 17, 2026, aimed at improving travel convenience and deepening financial liberalization. Railway authorities will pilot a 60-day advance ticket booking service on the Beijing-Shanghai corridor, while the State Administration of Foreign Exchange (SAFE) unveiled a package of measures to facilitate cross-border investment and financing.

## Railway Booking Pilot: A Leap in Travel Convenience

Starting July 20, 2026, passengers on more than 200 trains along the Beijing-Shanghai High-Speed Railway and the Beijing-Shanghai conventional railway can submit reservation requests 60 to 17 days before departure, according to [Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260717/f87cdc54f6b14f6eb1ead89c5d719219/c.html). The 12306 system will confirm orders 20 to 16 days before departure, marking a substantial expansion from the current 15-day advance booking window.

The pilot introduces two booking modes: "Precise Booking," where passengers select specific trains, times, and seat classes, and "Fuzzy Booking," which allows travelers to specify time windows and preferences without committing to a particular train. Each 12306 account can hold up to three pending reservation orders simultaneously, with each order accommodating up to 19 passengers.

A transition period runs from July 20 to August 29, 2026, enabling sequential booking for tickets on or after September 15. From August 30 onward, the 60-to-17-day advance booking will operate as a routine service. [IT之家](https://www.ithome.com/0/977/975.htm) reported that the service is available exclusively through the Railway 12306 mobile app, with daily submissions opening in the morning for bookings 60 days ahead.

A China State Railway Group official described the initiative as a key step in market-oriented reform, enabling "on-demand train scheduling" to better match capacity with passenger demand. The approach mirrors airline revenue management systems, allowing railway authorities to optimize capacity allocation based on actual demand patterns.

## SAFE Unveils Cross-Border Investment Facilitation Package

On the same day, SAFE announced a comprehensive policy package at a State Council Information Office press conference, as reported by [The Paper](https://www.thepaper.cn/newsDetail_forward_33603836). Xiao Sheng, Director of SAFE's Capital Project Management Department, outlined three categories of reforms developed after extensive consultation with banks and enterprises.

The first category focuses on direct investment facilitation, simplifying capital account settlement procedures for foreign-invested enterprises and streamlining fund remittance reviews for Chinese companies investing abroad. The second expands cross-border financing access, broadening the scope of facilitation policies to cover more technology-innovation and green finance beneficiaries while rolling out a nationwide green foreign debt pilot. The third optimizes capital account registration by transferring certain procedures to banks and expanding online processing capabilities.

SAFE Deputy Director Li Bin emphasized that the agency "always promotes facilitation while preventing risks, and firmly guards the safety bottom line under open conditions."

## Broader Economic Context

The policy announcements come amid robust economic activity. China's goods trade total exceeded $25 trillion in H1 2026, up 16.9% year-on-year, while net cross-border capital inflows from the non-bank sector reached $247.2 billion. Foreign investment inflows from January to May totaled approximately $160 billion, significantly outperforming the same period last year. As of end-March 2026, China's external assets reached a record high of approximately $12 trillion, with net external assets exceeding $4 trillion — the second-largest globally.

SAFE also confirmed it is working to issue a new round of QDII quotas soon, signaling continued support for outward investment.

## What to Watch

The railway booking pilot's limited scope — confined to 200+ trains on two routes — suggests a cautious, test-and-learn approach before any potential nationwide expansion. For the forex policies, implementation timelines remain pending as measures complete relevant procedures. Together, these initiatives signal China's continued commitment to infrastructure modernization and gradual financial sector opening, even as global economic uncertainties persist.
