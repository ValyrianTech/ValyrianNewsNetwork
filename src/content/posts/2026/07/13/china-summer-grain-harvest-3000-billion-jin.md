# China's Summer Grain Harvest Breaks 3000 Billion Jin Barrier

China's 2026 summer grain output has reached a record **3014.9 billion jin (150.75 million tons)**, surpassing the 3000 billion jin threshold for the first time despite challenging weather conditions, according to data released by the [National Bureau of Statistics](https://www.stats.gov.cn/zwfwck/sjfb/202607/t20260710_1964090.html). The achievement represents a year-on-year increase of 20 billion jin (1 million tons), or 0.7%, and marks a significant milestone in the country's food security efforts.

## Context: A Harvest Against the Odds

The record comes after one of the most difficult planting seasons in recent memory. In autumn 2025, rare continuous rainfall hit the Huang-Huai-Hai region — China's primary wheat belt encompassing Henan, Shandong, and Hebei provinces — with precipitation exceeding 1.5 times the seasonal norm. The deluge left soils waterlogged, delaying both the autumn harvest and winter wheat sowing by 10 to 15 days across vast areas.

According to [Xinhua News Agency](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260710/0225a73480364e109658709bc74f7499/c.html), a staggering **44.2% of winter wheat was sown late**, with some plots not planted until mid-to-late November — well past the optimal window. This created a complex situation characterized by weak seedlings, high pest基数, and elevated frost risk.

## How China Turned Crisis into Record Output

Facing these adverse conditions, agricultural authorities implemented a comprehensive "Four Supplements, One Promotion" strategy — supplementing with improved seeds, higher planting density, additional fertilizer, and better quality, while promoting seedling strengthening. The central government also advanced subsidy funds for emergency harvesting and sowing.

Wei Fenghua, Director of the Rural Department at the National Bureau of Statistics, noted that favorable weather after the overwintering period played a crucial role. "After entering the overwintering period, the meteorological conditions in the main wheat-producing areas were generally favorable, with higher temperatures and sufficient precipitation. No large-scale spring drought, 'late spring cold,' or 'dry-hot wind' disasters occurred, and pest control measures were implemented effectively," he said, as reported by [China News Service](https://www.chinanews.com.cn/cj/2026/07-10/10656463.shtml).

Per-unit yield reached a historic high of **378.8 kg per mu (5,681.6 kg per hectare)**, up 0.8% year-on-year. Wheat production alone reached 2779 billion jin, with winter wheat output increasing by 21.7 billion jin. Notably, 22 out of 25 summer grain-producing regions recorded output increases, with Henan, Jiangsu, and Shaanxi seeing restorative growth.

## Technology and Policy: The Twin Engines

Sun Tan, Vice President of the Chinese Academy of Agricultural Sciences, credited the success to a large-area yield improvement program that expanded from 300 pilot counties in 2023 to **1,000 counties in 2026**, covering five major crops including corn, wheat, soybeans, rapeseed, and rice. "The large-area yield improvement action ensured the 2026 summer grain harvest," Sun told [CCTV News](https://news.cctv.com/2026/07/13/ARTI2pwZkHUQ3TzIlKR1fttU260713.shtml).

Lv Xiutao, Deputy Director of the Planting Management Department at the Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs, highlighted the role of high-standard farmland. Speaking to [CNR (央广网)](https://www.cnr.cn/news/20260712/t20260712_527705532.shtml), he said: "Relying on the 'hard power' of high-standard farmland, we accelerated drainage and soil moisture dispersion to create conditions for sowing. Leveraging the 'soft power' of technological support, we implemented key technologies to tap yield potential, effectively promoting per-unit yield increase."

Agricultural insurance also provided a critical safety net. In January-May 2026, agricultural insurance payouts reached 37 billion yuan, with grain crop insurance paying 6.047 billion yuan to 5.46 million affected households, as reported by [People's Daily](https://paper.people.com.cn/rmrb/pc/content/202607/08/content_30167436.html).

## Implications for China's Food Security Goals

The record harvest provides a strong foundation for China's ambitious "15th Five-Year Plan" target of raising comprehensive grain production capacity to approximately **1.45 trillion jin by 2030** — an increase of 600 billion jin from the 2025 baseline of 1.39 trillion jin.

Tu Shengwei, a researcher at the Academy of Macroeconomic Research of the National Development and Reform Commission, explained the strategic rationale in an interview with [China News Service / People's Daily Overseas Edition](https://www.chinanews.com.cn/gn/2026/07-09/10655651.shtml): "Setting the target of approximately 1.45 trillion jin is to use the stability of production capacity to cope with environmental uncertainty, provide more space for macro-control, and effectively resist various risk impacts."

## What to Watch

While the harvest is a clear success, challenges remain. Sown area declined slightly by 0.2%, meaning the entire output increase came from yield improvements rather than area expansion — a positive sign for productivity but one that underscores the importance of continued technological investment. Hubei province saw output decline due to heavy rainfall, and Xinjiang's output dropped significantly due to planting structure adjustments.

Looking ahead, the 15th Five-Year Plan emphasizes a shift from quantity-oriented to quantity-and-quality-oriented production, focusing on优质 rice, strong and weak gluten wheat, and high-yield high-oil soybeans. As Sun Tan noted, "Scientific and technological innovation must face the development of climate-resilient agriculture, mainly around stress resistance issues such as extreme weather, pests and diseases, lodging, and pre-harvest sprouting."

With global food security concerns mounting — driven by El Niño phenomena and geopolitical tensions — China's ability to maintain and expand its grain production capacity carries significance far beyond its borders. The 2026 summer grain harvest demonstrates that even in the face of mounting climate challenges, strategic policy intervention and technological innovation can deliver results.
