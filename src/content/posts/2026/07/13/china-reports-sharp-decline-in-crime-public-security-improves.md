# China Reports Sharp Crime Decline in Public Security

China's public security situation has continued to improve in 2026, with the Ministry of Public Security reporting double-digit declines in both criminal and public security cases during the first five months of the year. The latest figures, released on July 11, show criminal cases filed down **16.1%** year-on-year and public security cases down **10.6%**, according to [Xinhua News Agency](https://www.chinanews.com.cn/gn/2026/07-11/10657673.shtml).

## Data-Driven Policing Delivers Results

The Ministry of Public Security attributed the improvements to a nationwide deployment of enhanced prevention, control, and targeted strike capabilities. "Since the beginning of this year, the Ministry of Public Security has deployed public security organs nationwide to continuously improve precise prevention, fine-tuned control, and targeted strike capabilities, striving to safeguard high-quality development with high-level security," the ministry stated, as [reported by CCTV News](https://news.cctv.cn/2026/07/12/ARTIVtnNWTgg0pgmVfxmalYc260712.shtml).

Central to this effort has been the adoption of a "Professional + Mechanism + Big Data" policing model, which integrates advanced technology into everyday law enforcement. In Nanjing's Confucius Temple scenic area, for example, "AI police" assistants now handle tourist inquiries at service desks while command centers monitor real-time crowd flows on large screens. Similar innovations in Anhui's Lu'an city include smart traffic models capable of detecting distracted driving automatically, while Jiangxi's Pingxiang has implemented a "police-network integration" model that recruits citizens as "peace volunteers" to strengthen community security.

## Telecom Fraud Declines for Eighth Consecutive Month

One of the most significant achievements highlighted in the report is the sustained reduction in telecom and internet fraud — a crime type that has long plagued Chinese society. Since October 2025, the number of telecom fraud cases has declined for **eight consecutive months**, according to the [CCTV Xinwen Lianbo broadcast](https://tv.cctv.com/2026/07/12/VIDEuMNFultn5cCD2vorOywU260712.shtml). Public security organs have maintained a high-pressure crackdown on organized crime, mafia-style gangs, and telecom fraud, the broadcast noted.

In a notable display of international cooperation, Chinese, American, and UAE authorities jointly dismantled nine telecom fraud dens in Dubai, arresting 276 suspects — the first trilateral operation of its kind. Domestically, police have worked with the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology and the financial sector to implement full-chain countermeasures, including the regular interception of overseas calls and fraudulent software, while monitoring abnormal capital flows for early intervention.

## Economic Crime Crackdown and Business Protection

Since 2025, Chinese authorities have solved **128,000** economic crime cases, recovering approximately **¥37.5 billion** (US$5.2 billion) in economic losses. In a notable case, Nanjing police cracked a seed technology trade secret leak in May 2026, arresting 15 suspects and involving over 30 million yuan in damages. The city's Intellectual Property Criminal Protection Center has reduced case processing times by 40% by bridging administrative and criminal enforcement.

Specialized business protection initiatives have been established nationwide, including Beijing's "Jingwuwei" program, Shanghai's "Blue Whale" enterprise protection stations, and Zhejiang's "11087" service brand. In Inner Mongolia, over 520 "project police chiefs" have been assigned to major projects, creating a tailored security network for economic development.

## Public Service Reforms Benefit Nearly 100 Million

The public security improvements extend beyond crime fighting. Nearly **100 million** people have benefited from cross-province and nationwide document processing reforms, while vehicle registration is now available through a single online portal. These measures have significantly reduced processing times for citizens, eliminating the need for individuals to return to their registered hometowns for routine administrative procedures.

## Ecological Policing Network Expands

China has also strengthened its environmental protection efforts through policing. The country has established over **3,000** ecological police stations and appointed **73,000** river, lake, forest, and ecological police chiefs, embedding environmental protection into the public security framework. This aligns with China's broader ecological civilization agenda and its commitment to environmental governance.

## Grassroots Governance: The Fengqiao Experience

Underpinning many of these reforms is the "Fengqiao Experience" — a community-based dispute resolution model originating from Zhejiang Province in the 1960s. Adapted for the modern era, it emphasizes grassroots governance, mediation, and preventive policing. Cities like Shanghai have deepened "three-agency" mediation mechanisms, while Jilin's public security bureau has launched specialized youth protection programs.

## Analysis: A Coordinated Campaign

The consistent messaging across multiple state media platforms — from CCTV News to Xinwen Lianbo and People's Daily — indicates a coordinated information campaign highlighting the government's achievements in public security. The emphasis on technology-driven policing aligns with China's broader push for technological modernization, while the economic crime statistics frame security as an enabler of economic growth. The international cooperation element, particularly the China-U.S.-UAE operation, suggests areas of collaboration even amid broader geopolitical tensions.

## What to Watch

While the reported statistics paint a positive picture, the data covers only January through May 2026, and independent verification from non-government sources remains unavailable. Questions remain about potential seasonal or regional variations not captured in the aggregate figures, and how changes in crime classification or reporting practices may affect the numbers. Observers will be watching for full-year figures and whether the downward trends in crime can be sustained across all regions and seasons.
