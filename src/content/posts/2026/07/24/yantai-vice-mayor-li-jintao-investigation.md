# Yantai Vice Mayor Li Jintao Under Investigation in China Anti-Corruption Drive

Li Jintao, a senior official in the coastal city of Yantai, Shandong Province, has been placed under investigation for alleged "serious disciplinary and legal violations," Chinese authorities announced on July 24, 2026. The case marks the latest development in China's ongoing anti-corruption campaign targeting government officials at the municipal level.

## Investigation Details

According to a statement from the Shandong Provincial Commission for Discipline Inspection, published by the [Central Commission for Discipline Inspection](https://news.cctv.com/2026/07/24/ARTIqtpf8KqgbTFLduqsMg1P260724.shtml), Li voluntarily surrendered himself (主动投案) to authorities and is currently undergoing disciplinary review and supervisory investigation. The announcement was made on Friday, consistent with the Chinese government's typical practice of releasing politically sensitive news late in the week.

Li, 56, serves as a member of the Standing Committee of the Yantai Municipal Committee and Vice Mayor of Yantai, as well as a member of the Yantai Municipal Government Party Leadership Group.

## A Career Built in Yantai

Born in March 1970 in Rushan, Shandong Province, Li's entire 35-year career has been within Yantai's administrative system, making him a locally-grown official with deep roots in the city's political establishment. According to his [biography on Baidu Baike](https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E6%9D%8E%E9%87%91%E6%B6%9B/2656982), Li began his career in 1991 as a staff member at the Yantai Municipal Planning Commission after graduating from Yantai University.

Over the following decades, Li rose through the ranks, holding key positions including:

- Deputy Director and later Party Secretary of Laishan Town, Laishan District
- Standing Committee Member and Vice District Mayor of Laishan District (2007–2015)
- District Mayor of Fushan District (2016–2021)
- Party Secretary of Fushan District (2021–2023)
- Vice Mayor of Yantai (2023–2025)
- Standing Committee Member of Yantai Municipal Committee and Vice Mayor (2025–2026)

His long tenure in Fushan District, where he served as district mayor and later party secretary for approximately eight years, may be a focus of the investigation, as these positions typically involve oversight of land development, construction projects, and personnel appointments.

## Broader Anti-Corruption Context

Li's case is part of China's long-running anti-corruption campaign, which has intensified under the leadership of Xi Jinping since 2012. The campaign has targeted officials at all levels, from village cadres to senior national leaders.

Shandong Province has seen a significant number of officials investigated in recent weeks. According to the Shandong Provincial Commission for Discipline Inspection, several other officials were investigated in July 2026 alone, including Huo Taiying, a former senior inspector at the provincial ecology department, and Han Ning, a standing committee member and vice county mayor of Dongping County.

## Significance of Voluntary Surrender

The fact that Li "voluntarily surrendered" is noteworthy within China's anti-corruption framework. As [Jiemian News](https://www.jiemian.com/article/14820950.html) reported, voluntary surrender can potentially lead to more lenient treatment under Chinese law. It also suggests that the investigation may have been triggered by evidence gathered against him, leading him to preemptively confess. This pattern has been observed in many recent cases, including Song Zhenbo, Vice Mayor of Zibo, who also voluntarily surrendered earlier in 2026.

## Analysis and Implications

Li's investigation carries several significant implications. First, it demonstrates that China's anti-corruption campaign continues unabated, targeting officials at the prefecture and municipal level. Second, given that Li spent his entire career within Yantai's administrative system, his case may signal a broader probe into networks of officials in the Yantai area, particularly those who served in Laishan and Fushan districts.

As reported by [Sohu News](https://www.sohu.com/a/1054158495_121347613), the official announcement did not specify the nature of the violations. However, common areas of investigation for officials at Li's level typically include corruption and bribery during land development and construction projects, abuse of power in personnel appointments, and embezzlement of public funds.

## What to Watch For

Further developments in this case will likely emerge as the investigation progresses. Key questions include whether Li's case will lead to additional probes of other Yantai officials, what specific violations he is alleged to have committed, and how this will affect Yantai's municipal administration. The outcome of the investigation may also provide insight into the effectiveness of the CCDI's strategy of encouraging voluntary surrender as a tool for uncovering corruption networks.
