# Typhoon Hongxia Forms, China Issues Orange Alert

China's Central Meteorological Observatory issued an orange typhoon alert on Friday morning as the 12th typhoon of the season, named "Hongxia" (Red Cloud), formed over the northwestern Pacific Ocean and began moving toward the country's southeastern coast. The orange alert, the second-highest in China's four-tier warning system, signals the potential for significant weather impacts across multiple coastal provinces.

According to [Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260724/2a404e054e89473b842323853a1f4e04/c.html), the typhoon was located approximately 560 kilometers northeast of Manila, Philippines, early Friday morning, moving west-northwest at 25 to 30 kilometers per hour while gradually intensifying.

## Forecast Path and Landfall

Meteorologists project that Typhoon Hongxia will enter the northeastern South China Sea between late Friday night and early Saturday morning, tracking toward the coastlines of Guangdong and Fujian provinces. The storm is expected to make landfall between Zhuhai in Guangdong and Zhangpu in Fujian sometime between Saturday evening and Sunday morning.

After making landfall, the typhoon is forecast to turn northward and gradually weaken as it moves inland, though authorities warn that the storm could still bring dangerous conditions well beyond the immediate coastal impact zone.

## Widespread Weather Impacts

The Central Meteorological Observatory has warned of heavy rainfall across parts of eastern and southern Taiwan, with some areas expected to receive torrential downpours. The storm system is also forecast to bring gale-force winds to a wide swath of maritime regions.

Sea areas expected to experience winds of force 6 to 8 on the Beaufort scale, with gusts reaching force 9 to 10, include the northeastern South China Sea, the Bashi Channel, waters east of Taiwan, the Taiwan Strait, the southern East China Sea, and coastal waters off Taiwan, Fujian, and central-to-eastern Guangdong. Particularly dangerous conditions are anticipated in the Bashi Channel and the eastern part of the northeastern South China Sea, where winds could reach force 9 to 11 with gusts of force 12 to 13.

## Preparedness and Response

The issuance of an orange alert triggers a series of emergency preparedness measures across affected regions. Local authorities in Guangdong and Fujian provinces are expected to activate disaster response protocols, including monitoring of vulnerable coastal areas, preparation of emergency shelters, and coordination of relief supplies.

Residents in the projected impact zone have been advised to take precautionary measures, including securing loose objects, avoiding coastal areas during the storm's passage, and staying informed through official weather updates. Fishing vessels in the affected sea areas have been urged to return to port or seek safe anchorage.

## Historical Context and Seasonal Patterns

Typhoon Hongxia arrives during the peak of the Western Pacific typhoon season, which typically runs from July through October. China's southeastern coastline is frequently impacted by tropical cyclones during this period, with provinces such as Guangdong, Fujian, Zhejiang, and Hainan bearing the brunt of seasonal storm activity.

China's four-tier color-coded warning system — blue, yellow, orange, and red — provides a graduated framework for public alerts, with orange representing the second-most severe level, indicating the likelihood of significant damage and requiring substantial preparedness measures.

## What to Watch For

In the coming days, authorities will closely monitor the typhoon's trajectory and intensity as it approaches the Chinese coast. Key factors to watch include any shifts in the storm's path that could alter the expected landfall location, the rate of intensification over the warm waters of the South China Sea, and the potential for storm surge and inland flooding.

Residents in the affected regions are advised to monitor updates from the Central Meteorological Observatory and local authorities as the situation develops. The storm's impact is expected to be felt from late Friday through early next week.
