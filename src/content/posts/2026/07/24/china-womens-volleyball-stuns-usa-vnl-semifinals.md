# China Volleyball Stuns USA to Reach VNL Semifinals

The Chinese women's national volleyball team delivered a dramatic come-from-behind victory over the United States on July 23, winning 3-2 in a five-set quarterfinal thriller at the Macau East Asian Games Dome to advance to the semifinals of the 2026 FIVB Volleyball Women's Nations League (VNL).

According to [Xinhua News](https://www.news.cn/sports/20260724/399e13ff2985412092be72bcda195570/c.html), China dropped the opening set 15-25 before rallying to take the next two sets 25-23 and 25-20. After the USA forced a deciding set with a 25-17 victory in the fourth, China sealed the match 15-13 in a tense fifth set that saw the host nation fight back from a 10-8 deficit.

## A David vs. Goliath Upset

China entered the VNL Finals as the host nation after finishing ninth in the preliminary round, while the USA arrived as the top seed and three-time VNL champions. The victory represents a significant upset and marks China's first VNL semifinal appearance in two years, as [reported](https://finance.sina.com.cn/jjxw/2026-07-23/doc-iniivihw9351854.shtml) by China Youth Daily.

The win also extends China's undefeated record against the USA in VNL competition over the past four years, underscoring the intensity of the rivalry between two of women's volleyball's traditional powerhouses.

## Key Performers

Outside hitter Zhuang Yushan led China with 17 points, while captain Gong Xiangyu contributed 15 points as the second-highest scorer. Speaking after the match, Zhuang said the team gradually found their rhythm after a slow start, adding that the victory gave the squad tremendous confidence. Gong emphasized the team's resilience, stating that everyone was prepared for a difficult match and no one gave up when they fell behind.

China's starting lineup featured a blend of experienced veterans and emerging talents: Zhuang Yushan and Tang Xin at outside hitter, Wang Yuanyuan and Guo Zhongnan at middle blocker, Gong Xiangyu at opposite, Xie Shengyu as setter, and Wang Mengjie as libero.

## Tournament Context

The 2026 VNL, the eighth edition of the annual tournament, features 18 teams from four confederations competing from June 3 to July 26. The final round is being held at the Macau East Asian Games Dome, marking the first time in 15 years that Macau has hosted women's volleyball finals, according to [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIVB_Women's_Volleyball_Nations_League).

On the other side of the bracket, Turkey defeated Canada 3-1 in their quarterfinal match to set up a semifinal clash with China. As [reported](https://www.dailysabah.com/sports/turkiyes-sultans-of-net-rally-past-canada-to-reach-vnl-semifinals/news) by Daily Sabah, the "Sultans of the Net" dropped the opening set before taking control with three consecutive set wins, continuing their rise as a global volleyball power since capturing both the 2023 VNL title and the 2023 CEV European Championship.

## Looking Ahead

China will face Turkey in the semifinals on July 25, while the other semifinal features Italy against Brazil. July 24 serves as a rest day before the final four matches. The gold medal match is scheduled for July 26.

Turkey, led by star players Melissa Vargas and Zehra Güneş, presents a formidable challenge. The 2023 VNL champions finished fourth in the preliminary round with nine wins and enter the semifinals with momentum after their quarterfinal victory. However, China will enjoy home-court advantage in Macau, where the team has already demonstrated remarkable resilience.

## Significance

This victory validates China's status as a top-tier volleyball nation and showcases the successful transition of the program, blending experienced players like Gong Xiangyu and Wang Mengjie with younger talents stepping up in high-pressure situations. A potential finals appearance would be China's second VNL final after their runner-up finish in 2023.

The official [match result](https://en.volleyballworld.com/volleyball/competitions/volleyball-nations-league/schedule/26658/) confirms the final score: USA 2-3 China (25-15, 23-25, 20-25, 25-17, 13-15), with the host nation advancing to keep their championship hopes alive.
