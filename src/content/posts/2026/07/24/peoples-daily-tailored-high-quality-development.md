# People's Daily Urges Tailored High-Quality Development Paths

**Beijing** — In a front-page commentary published on July 24, 2026, [People's Daily](http://opinion.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0724/c461529-40766828.html), the official newspaper of the Chinese Communist Party, issued a significant policy signal urging regions across China to pursue high-quality development strategies tailored to their specific local conditions, rejecting a one-size-fits-all approach to economic growth.

The commentary, attributed to the newspaper's editorial commentator, argues that China's diverse regions must leverage their unique advantages and resources rather than copying uniform development models. Published on Page 01 — the front page — the article signals high-level policy emphasis as China navigates the early stages of its 15th Five-Year Plan period (2026-2030).

## The Yiwu Model as a National Template

The commentary centers on the "Yiwu Development Experience," a development model that has transformed the small inland city of Yiwu in Zhejiang Province from a resource-poor, land-scarce town into the world's largest small commodities market. The article opens by citing President Xi Jinping's April 2026 directive, in which he described Yiwu's transformation as "a successful practice of developing county economies according to local conditions."

As [Xinhua News Agency](https://m.mp.oeeee.com/a/BAAFRD0000202604231563416.html) reported, Xi emphasized the need to "further summarize and apply the Yiwu Development Experience" in conjunction with the ongoing party-wide education campaign on establishing a correct performance outlook.

This year marks the 20th anniversary since Xi Jinping, then Zhejiang Party Secretary, personally summarized and promoted the Yiwu Development Experience in 2006. During his tenure in Zhejiang, Xi visited Yiwu 11 times, shaping the six-pillar framework of the model: commerce-based city development, industrial linkage, urban-rural coordination, harmonious development, cultural enrichment, and capable governance.

## Economic Transformation in Numbers

Yiwu's transformation is striking. According to the research compiled for the commentary, the city's market now covers **8 million square meters** — confirmed by the [People's Daily feature](http://zj.people.com.cn/n2/2026/0723/c186327-41647115.html) which states the market's operating area is 800万平方米 (8,000,000 sqm) — with over 80,000 shops selling more than 2.1 million types of goods globally. Its export value reached 730.69 billion yuan in 2025, while GDP grew from 30.48 billion yuan in 2005 to 269.33 billion yuan in 2025. Urban per capita disposable income hit 101,816 yuan in 2025, and the urbanization rate reached 83.7 percent.

A People's Daily feature published the previous day detailed how Yiwu evolved from a "chicken feather for sugar" barter economy to a global trading hub, with the city's sixth-generation market — the Global Digital Trade Center — opening in October 2025.

## Policy Context: Correct Performance Outlook

The commentary is strategically timed. In February 2026, the CCP Central Committee issued a notice launching a party-wide "Study and Practice of Correct Performance Outlook Education," as [reported by Study Times](https://www.studytimes.cn/tbzt/slhjxzqzjgxxjy/202602/t20260223_86252.html). The campaign targets leading cadres at or above the county and department level and runs through July 2026.

The commentary explicitly links the Yiwu model to this campaign, arguing that the Yiwu experience represents a "vivid practice of correct performance outlook in county governance." This framing suggests that local officials will increasingly be evaluated not by GDP growth alone but by the quality and sustainability of their development approaches.

## Three Pillars of the Commentary

The People's Daily editorial identifies three key principles for high-quality development:

**First, establishing a holistic perspective.** The commentary argues that counties must position themselves within the national development landscape, serving both local and national interests. Yiwu's success in becoming a "world supermarket" that buys and sells globally exemplifies this approach.

**Second, adhering to local conditions.** The article stresses that regions should "set reasonable goals, find the best path" based on their actual circumstances — whether suited for agriculture, industry, or commerce. This echoes Xi's longstanding emphasis on adapting measures to local conditions.

**Third, deepening reform and innovation.** The commentary highlights that Yiwu has generated over 40 national and provincial "firsts" since launching a new round of comprehensive trade reforms, from pioneering customs inspection models to expanding economic management authority.

## Implications for China's Development Strategy

The commentary carries significant implications for China's economic governance during the 15th Five-Year Plan period. Analysts suggest that local officials across China will be expected to study and adapt elements of the Yiwu model to their own regions, with a shift away from GDP-centric metrics toward quality-of-development indicators.

A separate [People's Daily theoretical article](http://paper.people.com.cn/rmrb/pc/content/202606/29/content_30165412.html) from June 29 had already previewed this theme, presenting a detailed investigation of the Yiwu experience and its lessons for county-level economic development nationwide.

As the commentary concludes, drawing on Xi's words: "High-quality development is the primary task in building a modern socialist country in all respects, and adhering to high-quality development must become an important part of leading cadres' performance outlook."

## What to Watch

Observers will be watching how different regions interpret "tailored development paths" and whether the emphasis on local conditions leads to greater regional autonomy in economic decision-making. The extent to which Yiwu's unique model — built on grassroots entrepreneurship, government facilitation, and integration with global trade networks — can be replicated in regions with different economic structures remains an open question.

The commentary signals that China's leadership is prioritizing quality over quantity in economic development, with the Yiwu experience serving as a benchmark for how local governments can balance market forces with effective governance to achieve sustainable growth.
