# Russia Extends Visa-Free Entry for Chinese to 2027

Russian President Vladimir Putin has signed a decree extending visa-free entry for Chinese citizens to Russia until December 31, 2027, marking the latest milestone in deepening bilateral ties between Moscow and Beijing. The decree, published on Russia's legal information portal on July 20, replaces the previous expiry date of September 14, 2026, with the new extended deadline, according to [TASS](https://tass.com/politics/2162605).

Under the policy, Chinese citizens may enter and stay in Russia without a visa for up to 30 days for tourism, private visits, or business purposes. The simplified entry regime also covers participation in scientific, cultural, socio-political, economic, and sporting events, as well as transit travel.

## A Reciprocal Partnership

The extension follows China's own decision in May 2026 to extend its visa-free policy for Russian citizens through the end of 2027. Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesperson Guo Jiakun announced the reciprocal measure on May 20, stating that the decision was made "to continuously facilitate personnel exchanges between China and Russia," as reported by [Xinhua News Agency](https://www.news.cn/20260720/fe6a3eb3e6b34eb59e99ad7a2396ae59/c.html).

The coordinated timing reflects close diplomatic coordination between the two capitals. China introduced its reciprocal visa-free regime for Russian citizens on September 15, 2025, and Putin signed the first reciprocal decree on December 1, 2025, allowing Chinese citizens visa-free visits for up to 30 days until September 14, 2026. The current extension builds on that foundation, as noted by [China Daily](https://cn.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202607/20/WS6a5e1911a310d709c2fbea35.html).

## Deepening People-to-People Ties

The visa-free agreement for group tourist trips was originally signed by the governments of China and Russia in February 2000. After a period of reduced implementation, the group visa-free mechanism was restored in August 2023, leading to a significant surge in cross-border tourism. According to [Interfax](https://interfax.com/newsroom/top-stories/104752/), nearly 580,000 tourists from both countries used the visa-free mechanism in the first year after its restoration.

Chinese tourism to Russia has grown substantially. In 2024, Chinese visitors made an estimated 1.2 million tourist trips to Russia, making China the top source country for inbound tourism. In the first quarter of 2025 alone, over 106,700 Chinese tourists entered Russia — an almost 8% increase compared to the previous year.

## Hospitality Sector Adapts

Russia's hospitality industry has been actively preparing for an influx of Chinese visitors. Maria Grishina, Head of Federal Projects at Cosmos Hotel Group, told [CGTN](https://news.cgtn.com/news/2025-12-11/VHJhbnNjcmlwdDg3ODY1/index.html) that the group is implementing "China Friendly" elements across its properties, including Chinese-language signage, restaurant menus in Chinese, and hiring Mandarin-speaking staff.

Alexander Lvov, Head of the "World Without Borders" Travel Association, noted that significant work has been done at the state level to prepare Russian regions for increased Chinese tourism. "A lot of work on the part of the state and state structures has been done throughout the year to prepare the regions to welcome more tourists including from China," Lvov said.

Daria Domostroeva, a representative of Intourist, described the visa-free decision as "era-defining for the market," predicting that it would increase individual tourism by 50% and group tourism by 10-15% in the months following its announcement.

## Geopolitical Context

The deepening of Russia-China tourism ties occurs against a backdrop of shifting geopolitical alignments. With many Western countries imposing sanctions on Russia following the Ukraine conflict, Moscow has been actively pivoting toward Asia, particularly China. The visa-free policy is part of broader efforts to strengthen economic, cultural, and people-to-people ties between the two nations.

[Jingsh Law Firm](https://jingsh.ru/en/blog/blog/russia-has-extended-visa-free-entry-for-chinese-citizens/) noted that for businesses, the extension facilitates short-term visits by Chinese representatives for negotiations, site visits, exhibitions, and other commercial activities, potentially boosting bilateral trade and investment.

## What to Watch

With the policy now secured through 2027, both countries have a multi-year window to develop tourism infrastructure, cultural exchange programs, and business travel networks. Key questions remain: whether the visa-free policy will be made permanent beyond 2027, how it will affect Chinese investment in Russia, and whether other countries in the region will pursue similar visa-free arrangements with China.

For now, the doors remain wide open — and the welcome sign is set to stay that way for years to come.
