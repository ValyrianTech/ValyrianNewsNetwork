# 'Village Super League' Brings Grassroots Football to Macau

China's grassroots football phenomenon, the "Village Super League" (村超), expanded into the Macau Special Administrative Region on July 18, 2026, with an invitational match held at the Macau University of Science and Technology football field. The event, organized by the Macau-Guizhou Association General Association and co-organized by the university, featured a friendly match between two local Macau teams — MGM FC and MUST Elite FC — alongside cultural performances that highlighted the unique heritage of both regions, according to [People's Daily](http://ent.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0721/c1012-40764588.html).

## A Cultural Bridge Through Sport

The Macau leg embodied the tournament's guiding theme: "With football as the medium, with culture as the bridge" (以足球为媒，以文化为桥). Beyond the match itself, spectators were treated to performances by a Guizhou intangible cultural heritage team, which presented the Dong Grand Song (侗族大歌), and a Macau local team that performed a Portuguese folk dance (葡萄牙土风舞), reflecting Macau's distinctive Chinese-Portuguese cultural fusion.

Ou Baihe (区柏来), Chairman of the Macau-Guizhou Association General Association, described the event's significance in an interview with the [People's Daily Greater Bay Area Channel](http://gba.people.cn/n1/2026/0721/c42272-40765063.html): "The 'Village Super League' is not just a series of sports competitions, but an important cultural bond, allowing Macau residents to experience the charm of Guizhou's ethnic culture, and also allowing Guizhou friends to experience the unique Chinese-Western cultural fusion of Macau, promoting deeper cultural and sports exchanges between the two regions."

Reporter Fu Zimei of People's Daily noted that the event "continues the 'Village Super League' craze, showcasing the close cooperation and exchange achievements between Guizhou and Macau in the fields of sports, culture, and tourism," as reported in the [People's Daily print edition](http://paper.people.com.cn/rmrb/pc/content/202607/21/content_30169984.html).

## From Local Village Event to National Phenomenon

The "Village Super League" first captured China's imagination in the summer of 2023, when it went viral from Rongjiang County in Guizhou Province. What began as a community-organized tournament — with players drawn from local farmers, vendors, and students — quickly grew into a nationwide sensation. Matches are accompanied by vibrant performances of Miao and Dong ethnic traditions, and prizes famously include local specialties like Xiang pigs, carp, and ducks rather than cash.

Since its inception, the league has expanded dramatically. Over 50 countries have sent 28 international teams and more than 1,000 players to participate. The league has forged partnerships with the English Premier League and hosted visits from international football figures including Brazilian legend Kaká. Plans are underway for 2026 "Belt and Road" friendly matches and an ambitious 2028 "Village Super League" World Cup.

The economic impact has been substantial. As of December 2025, the Village Super League had driven nearly 30 billion yuan (approximately US$4.1 billion) in tourism revenue for Rongjiang County alone, according to a [Guangming Daily](https://news.gmw.cn/2026-04/07/content_38692517.htm) feature on the broader "Village Brand" phenomenon.

## Macau's Role in the Village Super League

The Macau leg represents a significant milestone in the league's expansion into China's Special Administrative Regions. Both MGM FC and MUST Elite FC will jointly represent Macau at the national "Village Super League" tournament in August 2026, where they will compete against teams from across China.

The event also highlights Macau's unique position as a bridge between Chinese and Portuguese-speaking cultures. The Portuguese folk dance performance, paired with Guizhou's Dong Grand Song, symbolized the cultural exchange at the heart of the tournament's mission.

## What's Next

With the Macau invitational declared a success, attention now turns to the national tournament in August, where Macau's representatives will face off against grassroots teams from across the country. The league's organizers have signaled ambitions for further international expansion, including the proposed "Belt and Road" friendly matches that could leverage Macau's role as a platform connecting China with Portuguese-speaking nations.

As the Village Super League continues its trajectory from a local village competition to a national and international cultural export, the Macau leg stands as a testament to the power of sport to build bridges — between regions, between cultures, and between communities.
