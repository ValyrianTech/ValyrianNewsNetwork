# Man Seriously Injured in Blankenberge Stabbing Incident

A 33-year-old man from Charleroi was seriously injured in a stabbing incident in the Belgian coastal town of Blankenberge on Monday evening, after a dispute over toilet access at a local chip shop escalated into violence. Police arrested a 41-year-old suspect at the scene, and the victim was rushed to hospital for emergency surgery, according to [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/21/man-zwaargewond-na-steekincident-in-blankenberge/).

## The Incident

The altercation began late on the evening of July 20 at a frituur (chip shop) on Malecotstraat, near Blankenberge's seafront promenade. The victim, visiting the coastal town from the southern Belgian city of Charleroi, was denied access to the toilet after the establishment had closed for the evening.

According to reports, the customer became disruptive after being refused, and the situation escalated when he struck the employee. The 41-year-old employee then took a knife and chased the customer from the shop. The attack occurred at the nearby Charlierhelling intersection, where the victim was stabbed at least once.

## Emergency Response

The victim managed to stumble into a nearby café, where he asked staff to call emergency services. He was rushed to hospital for emergency surgery. His condition was initially described as life-threatening but was later reported as stable.

Police cordoned off the intersection for several hours while forensic teams and a medical examiner conducted evidence collection at the scene. The suspect returned to the frituur after the incident and was arrested by police.

"A suspect has been arrested," confirmed Jan Maertens, Commissioner of the Police Zone Blankenberge/Zuienkerke.

## Investigation

The West Flanders prosecutor's office has opened an investigation into the incident. Griet De Prest, spokesperson for the prosecutor's office, stated that according to initial findings, the stabbing arose after a discussion between those involved.

"Further investigation will have to determine the exact circumstances of the incident," De Prest said.

The suspect faces potential charges of attempted manslaughter (poging doodslag). A forensic doctor has been appointed to assist with the investigation, and authorities are examining whether any security camera footage from the area captured the incident.

## Context

The incident occurred during the peak summer tourist season in Blankenberge, a popular coastal destination in West Flanders with approximately 20,000 residents. The timing — on the eve of Belgium's National Day (July 21) — may have contributed to the late-night activity at the frituur.

The victim had traveled approximately 150 kilometers from Charleroi in Wallonia to the coast, suggesting he was likely a tourist visiting for the holiday period.

Blankenberge has experienced other stabbing incidents in recent years. In August 2023, a 15-year-old boy was stabbed at the town's train station, sparking political debate about knife crime in Belgium. More recently, in March 2026, a stabbing in a home in Blankenberge left a 7-month-old baby lightly injured and a grandmother seriously injured.

## Legal Proceedings

The case raises questions about self-defense claims, as reports indicate the employee was struck first before retaliating with a knife. The suspect was arrested after voluntarily returning to the frituur, suggesting no flight risk. The prosecutor's office is expected to determine specific charges in the coming days.

## What's Next

Authorities continue to investigate the exact sequence of events. Key questions remain, including whether the suspect's claim of being struck first will affect legal proceedings, and whether any witnesses beyond the café where the victim sought help have come forward. The victim's current condition is reported as stable following surgery.
