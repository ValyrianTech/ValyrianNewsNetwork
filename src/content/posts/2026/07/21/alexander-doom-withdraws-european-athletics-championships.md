# Doom Withdraws from European Athletics Championships

Defending European champion Alexander Doom has withdrawn from the 400 meters at the upcoming European Athletics Championships in Birmingham due to a hamstring tendon injury, the Belgian sprinter announced on Tuesday. The 29-year-old's absence represents a significant blow to Belgium's medal hopes at the championships, which run from August 10 to 16 at Alexander Stadium.

## Context

Doom, born in Roeselare in 1997, is one of Belgium's most accomplished track athletes. His career reached its zenith in 2024 when he became the first male athlete ever to win both the individual 400m and the 4x400m relay at the same World Athletics Indoor Championships in Glasgow. He replicated this extraordinary double at the European Championships in Rome later that year, setting a Belgian national record of 44.15 seconds in the process, as [HLN reported](https://www.hln.be/meer-sport/geen-alexander-doom-op-de-400-meter-titelverdediger-geeft-forfait-voor-ek-atletiek-na-hamstringblessure~a76c4285/).

However, injuries have repeatedly derailed Doom's momentum since that peak. At the Paris 2024 Olympics, he was forced to pull up in the 400m semi-final due to pubalgia. He missed the entire 2025 indoor season due to injury, and while he returned to claim silver at the World Indoor Championships in Toruń in March 2026, his season's best of 45.47 seconds — set in Ostrava on June 16 — remained well below his Belgian record of 44.15.

## Announcement and Reaction

Doom broke the news via an Instagram post on July 21, expressing his devastation at being unable to defend his title. According to [Sporza](https://sporza.be/nl/2026/07/21/alexander-doom-kan-door-blessure-zijn-titel-niet-verdedigen-op-e~1784621214218/), the Belgian public broadcaster, Doom wrote: "I am devastated to share this. Due to a minor injury to the hamstring tendon, I cannot participate in the European Championship in August. I am extremely disappointed and sad to have to miss this."

The decision was made in consultation with medical staff. "Together with the medical staff, I came to the decision not to take unnecessary risks," Doom said. "It is frustrating, but the smartest choice with an eye on the long term. Time to recover, catch my breath, and come back stronger."

## Impact on Belgian Teams

Doom's withdrawal has cascading consequences for the Belgian athletics contingent. As the defending champion, he was automatically qualified for the 400m, which had freed up an extra spot for another Belgian athlete. [ATNI](https://www.atni.be/2026/07/21/alexander-doom-zal-zijn-europese-titel-in-birmingham-niet-verdedigen-door-hamstringblessure/) noted that a "dream scenario" of four Belgians starting in the 400m — Doom, Dylan Borlée, Daniel Segers, and Jonathan Sacoor — had seemed within reach after all three teammates dipped under the qualifying standard of 45.15 seconds.

More critically, the Belgian Tornados, the men's 4x400m relay team, lose their anchor leg and team leader as they prepare to defend their European title. The Belgian Waffles, the mixed 4x400m relay team that finished fourth at the 2024 European Championships, also lose a key component of their lineup. [DHnet/Belga](https://www.dhnet.be/dernieres-depeches/2026/07/21/euro-dathletisme-blesse-alexander-doom-declare-forfait-et-ne-defendra-pas-son-titre-sur-400-m-LUPN5KYYXNHSVM5TG6OBSVG3EY/) described the withdrawal as a heavy blow for both relay squads.

## Analysis and Implications

Doom's recurring injury problems raise legitimate concerns about his long-term durability. Since his historic 2024 season, he has been unable to maintain consistent training and competition without physical setbacks. The pattern — pubalgia at the Paris Olympics, a missed indoor season in 2025, and now a hamstring injury — suggests a body struggling to withstand the demands of elite-level 400m running.

The European Athletics Championships, being held in Great Britain for the first time, will now proceed without one of its headline acts. For Belgium, the focus shifts to whether the Tornados can defend their relay title without their talisman, and which athlete will step into the void left by the defending champion.

## What's Next

Doom's decision to prioritize long-term health over short-term competition is prudent, but questions remain. Will he be fully fit for the 2027 outdoor season? Can he rediscover the form that made him world and European champion? For now, the Belgian star has chosen the cautious path — resting, recovering, and aiming to return stronger. The athletics world will be watching closely.
