# China Adds 27 Vocational Programs for AI, Robotics, Energy

China's Ministry of Education (MOE) has expanded its national vocational education catalog with 27 new programs designed to align workforce training with the country's strategic industrial priorities. Announced on July 16, the additions — comprising 9 higher vocational (college) specialties and 18 vocational undergraduate programs — span 14 disciplinary categories and will begin enrolling students in 2027, according to [CCTV News](https://news.cctv.com/2026/07/16/ARTIL348g5OAKyWPYMB3IMeY260716.shtml).

The new programs target emerging sectors including artificial intelligence, humanoid robotics, low-altitude transportation, digital economy, green energy, and high-end manufacturing, as well as critical livelihood services such as elder care and early childhood education.

## Strategic Alignment with National Plans

The additions are explicitly tied to China's "15th Five-Year Plan" (2026–2030) and the "Education Development 15th Five-Year Plan," which emphasize modernizing the industrial system and fostering "new quality productive forces." The MOE stated that the new programs serve three overarching principles: serving national strategic needs, filling skill gaps created by technological iteration, and serving people's livelihoods, as detailed in the [MOE press release](http://www.moe.gov.cn/jyb_xwfb/gzdt_gzdt/s5987/202607/t20260715_1443823.html).

Peng Binbai, Director of the Department of Vocational Education and Adult Education at the MOE, explained in an [official Q&A](http://www.moe.gov.cn/jyb_xwfb/s271/202607/t20260715_1443829.html) that vocational undergraduate programs account for 66% of the new additions, aimed at producing skilled talent capable of solving complex problems in production environments.

## Robotics and AI Take Center Stage

A significant portion of the new programs targets cutting-edge technology fields. The humanoid robot engineering technology undergraduate program responds to 2026 being viewed as the global mass production year for humanoid robots, with an estimated 50,000 units expected to ship worldwide. Tangshan Industrial Vocational Technical University is leading a consortium of 14 institutions and 26 enterprises in this field.

Zhang Jianjun, President of Tangshan Industrial Vocational Technical University, told CCTV that the program "integrates mechanical, electrical, artificial intelligence, and information technology disciplines around the needs of humanoid robots." Graduates will be prepared for roles including embedded systems debugging, robot system integration, and field maintenance across manufacturing, healthcare, and service sectors.

Other technology-focused additions include programs in digital twin engineering, computing-network convergence, embodied intelligence computing, and industrial software engineering — all designed to support China's push for integrated computing power networks and digital economy transformation.

## Addressing Critical Skill Gaps

The new programs are grounded in documented industry needs. Huang Xiaodong, Deputy Director of the Machinery Industry Talent Development Service Center, noted that a survey of 381 non-destructive testing (NDT) institutions revealed a 59% talent gap in technical process roles, with 51% of current practitioners holding only college-level education or below. The new intelligent NDT program aims to address this shortfall.

Similarly, the marine intelligent robot application technology program — expected to serve an industry projected to exceed 10.9 billion yuan in 2026 with 25–30% annual growth — establishes 229 core skills mapped one-to-one to enterprise job requirements, according to Xu Yanming of Tianjin Maritime Vocational College.

## Service Sector and Demographic Response

The additions also reflect China's demographic challenges. With 323 million people aged 60 and older and a silver economy exceeding 8 trillion yuan, new programs in travel-wellness operations management and infant family care target critical gaps in social services. The travel-wellness program, developed by Shanghai Tourism College, will train graduates for roles in senior living communities, wellness tourism planning, and age-friendly service design rather than traditional hospitality positions.

## Cultural Heritage Meets Formal Education

In a notable departure from the technology focus, the catalog includes a program in theatrical costume and prop design and production — a national intangible cultural heritage skill. This marks an effort to preserve traditional crafts through formal vocational education, moving beyond the traditional master-apprentice model that has struggled with slow training cycles and high personnel turnover.

## Cumulative Impact

Since the release of the first Vocational Education Professional Catalog in 2021, China has added 169 new programs cumulatively, representing 11% of the total catalog. The MOE has warned against "blindly following trends" and emphasized that local institutions should carefully assess their capacity before launching new programs, particularly in highly specialized fields like humanoid robotics and computing-network convergence.

## What to Watch

As enrollment opens in 2027, key questions remain: Can vocational institutions rapidly develop qualified faculty for these highly specialized fields? Will enterprises be prepared to absorb graduates in emerging job categories still evolving? And will these programs reach less developed regions where skill gaps may be even more acute? The answers will determine whether China's ambitious vocational education reform can deliver on its promise of bridging the gap between classroom training and industrial innovation.
